Indo-Palestine society goes on day-long strike

(IRNA, 14 October 2000)

New Delhi, Oct 14, IRNA -- Activists of the Indo-Palestine Friendship Society today went on a day-long hunger strike outside the Israel Embassy here to demand withdrawal of Israeli army from the entire Palestine area including Jerusalem.

The activists, led by Jammu and Kashmir Panther's party chief Bhim Singh, demanded implementation of resolutions passed by the Security Council of United Nations and the agreement reached at Oslo. They also submitted a memorandum for Israeli Prime Minister Ehud Barak asking him to apologise for the massacre of Palestinians and pay for the death and destruction caused by Israeli army.

Urging Barak to give peace a chance, the memorandum asked for a complete withdrawal of Israeli troops from all occupied areas, including Golan heights.

The activists were, however, detained by police in the afternoon and later released.
