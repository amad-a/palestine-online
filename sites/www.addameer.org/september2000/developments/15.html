Latest Developments No. 15 - Friday 6 October 2000, Web posted 7:00 PM (GMT+2), Ramallah, West Bank

***Please note, the information being received is in a very raw format, there may be discrepancies in personal information listed. We are reporting to the best of our knowledge and trying to reconfirm all information as it comes it***

In Jerusalem, reports state that there are 4 Palestinians in extremely critical condition, with a further 3 in critical condition. Clashes continue throughout East Jerusalem. Reports state that Israeli forces have raided the Al Aqsa Mosque Compound, trapping people within the mosques. Majdi Samir Maslamani, 14 years old, from Jerusalem, one of the extremely critical cases reported earlier, is dead by live ammunition to his head.

The name of the second killed in the village of Salem, near Nablus is Iyad Abdel Halim Shteyeh, 24 years old. In the same village, 2 Palestinians are in extremely critical condition. In the Nablus area, 20 injuries have been reported thus far, with clashes erupted near Joseph's Tomb. Reports are coming in of Israeli air attacks to neighbourhoods near the area of Joseph's Tomb. Mohammad Khalid Ihsein Awwad is reported to be in extremely critical condition after being hit by live ammunition to the heart.

In Gaza, Marwan Abdel Razaq Shamlakh, 25 years old from Sheikh Ijlin, Gaza was killed near Beit Hanoun by live ammunition to his chest. Marwan's cousin Ismail Shamlakh, 27 years old, was one of those killed in Ramallah on Tuesday 3 October. Issa Saleh Al Riyati, 22 years old from Nusseirat Refugee camp, died today of injuries sustained four days ago. Sharif (Rashad) Najjar, from Gaza and Wajid Mousa Abu Awwad, 22 years old, have also been reported dead. At least 1 Palestinian is reported to be in extremely critical condition. Armed confrontations have erupted near the Israeli settlement of Kfar Darom, outside of Khan Younis. No further details are available.

In Hebron, clashes continue in Halhoul, Sa'ir, Fawwar and Arroub Refugee Camps and within the city of Hebron itself. Radio reports state that 14 year old Ala Muhammad Mahfouz Al Jawabreh from 'Aroub Refugee Camp near Hebron, was hit in the head by live ammunition, damaging the right side of his skull and tearing out his right eye, and is in extremely critical condition. Another child, Gevara Mohammad Al Halahleh, is reported to be in extremely critical condition due to live ammunition to his head. Khalil Abdel Jalil Shahateet, was injured with live ammunition to his head. Ahed Yousef Fakusi, a child from Hebron was also injured by live ammunition to his head.

Clashes in Ramallah have spread from the northern entrance of the city to Atara, outside of Birzeit village, Jalazoune Refugee Camp and Mughayar village. 33 injuries have been reported thus far.

In Jenin, clashes have spread throughout the area. The villages of Jaba', Fandaqomieh, Silt Al Thaher, Ya'bad, and Sannour report a total of 45 injuries thus far. 3 of these are in critical condition, 2 extremely critical from live ammunition to their heads. Armed confrontations have erupted in the city of Jenin. Kamal An Neirat from Maythaloun, Jenin is seriously injured by live ammunition to his head and Abed Muhammad has been seriously injured by live ammunition to his chest.

The Israeli Authorities have declared Qalqilya a closed military area. Following clashes that have been ongoing since the afternoon, a total of 80 injuries have been reported, with 27 from live ammunition, mostly to the head and chest. Midhat Miskawi was hit by an exploding Dum-Dum bullet to his knee, amputating his leg from the knee down.

In Salfit and Kuful Harres, and Deir Istya, a total of 25 injuries from live ammunition have been reported.

In Tulkarem, following the death of 18 year old Mohammad Khalid Mahmoud Tammam, clashes intensified throughout the area. Thus far, 30 injuries have been reported, the majority of which have been to the head and chest from live ammunition.

In the Bethlehem area, 7 injuries have been reported from live ammunition and one injury from shrapnel from explosives thrown at protesters by Israeli soldiers.

2 medical personnel have been injured in the face with rubber coated steel bullets, one in Hebron and one in Jerusalem.
