• Texas State Railroad
    Ride a steam driven train through the forest of East Texas and enjoy picturesque scenery. It is reminiscent of the 1800s when travel by rail was the new wave of the century. How many opportunities, and for how long, will you and your kids (or grandchildren) have to ride such a train? It runs March through November. Call 1-800-442-8951 for details and reservations.

  • Texas Dogwood Trails Festival
    Held the 3rd and 4th weekends in March and the 1st weekend in April. Enjoy the splendorous beauty of the dogwood trees as you travel through the Davey Dogwood Park and surrounding area. Various activities are scheduled each weekend to compliment the Dogwood Trails Festival, such as an arts and craft fair, melodrama theater, BBQ cook off, and train robbery on the Texas State Railroad. The trail ride from the Do Little Ranch joins the parade on the first Saturday of the festival. March 23-25, 30-April 1, April 6-8
    The Legend of the Dogwood At the time of the Crucifixion, the dogwood attained the size of the oak and other forest trees. So strong and firm was the wood that it was chosen for the timbers of the cross. To be thus used for such a cruel purpose greatly distressed the tree, and Jesus smiled upon it, sensed this and in His gentle pity for sorrow said to it: "Because of your regret and pity for my suffering, I make you this promise; never again shall the dogwood grow big enough to be used for a cross. Henceforth it shall be slender and bent and twisted, and its blossoms shall be in the form of a cross - two long petals and two short petals - and in ther center of the outer edge of each petal will be nail prints, brown with rust and stained with blood. In the center of the flower there shall be an image of the crown of thorns and all who see it will remember it was upon a dogwood tree that I was crucified, and this tree shall not be mutilated or destroyed, but cherished as a reminder of my death upon this cross."

  • Texas Rangers Company D, Frontier Battalion
    The Texas Top Guns is a reenactment group of Company D. They perform at the Old Town Palestine the three Saturdays of Dogwood Trails and the Saturday of the Hot Pepper Festival. Show times are at 1pm, 2pm and 3pm on March 24, 31, April 7 and October 27th. ". . . one riot, one ranger . . ."


  • Go Texan BBQ/Trade Show
    Held in conjunction with the Dogwood Trails Festival, this is the opportunity BBQ cookers are looking for; show off your special BBQ recipe and win a prize. Call 1-800-659-3484 for current information.

  • Elkhart Pioneer Day
    A celebration of the pioneer heritage of Elkhart. It features arts and crafts, a parade, food booths and much more, fun for ages 0-99. March 17

  • Senior Citizen Doll Show
    Held in conjunction with the Dogwood Trails Festival. This is a showcase of local Senior Citizen talent where you can pick up a special token of your Palestine visit. March 24-25

  • Palestine Library Friends Melodrama Production
    Held during the Dogwood Festival weekends. March 23-24, 30-31 and April 6-7

  • Anderson County Youth Livestock Show
    This annual April event allows area youth the chance to display their success in livestock care and breeding. Proceeds are used to assist youth within the association achieve their dreams in the livestock arena. Call 1-800-659-3484 for current information.

  • Neches Heritage Day
    This small East Texas town celebrates its heritage with a one day festival of arts and crafts, a parade, food booths and other fun events. Held in April. Call 1-800-659-3484 for current information.

  • Chamber Golf Tournament
    Held in May, this golf tournament, sponsered by the Chamber of Commerce, will excite avid linksters and casual players. Call the Chamber for details. 903-729-6066

  • Crawfish Boil
    This event offers a genuine taste of crawfish heaven. Spicy eating and great summer weather mark this event. Calling 1-800-659-3484 will get you the dates.

  • A Taste of Palestine
    The fun and excitement of sampling food from an array of Palestine's eateries. Call the Chamber for details. 903-729-6066

  • Palestine Predicament
    Spend a weekend playing Sherlock Holmes! Wander around downtown looking for clues to an exciting murder mystery, then compare notes at the Mystery Ball to solve the case. Ride the train the next day and enjoy the scenic beauty of East Texas. The dates are July 7-8, 14-15, 21-22, and 28-29.

  • Wilderness Canoe Race
    A 22 mile canoe race down the Neches River through beautiful river country. The trip takes 4 to 10 hours. First Saturday in August. August 4th


  • Hot Pepper Festival
    Hot pepper enthusiasts will not want to miss this event. The festival features a classic car show, street dance, parade, arts and crafts and food booths, Tour De Pepper Bike Ride, Chili Cookoff, hot pepper eating contest, and much more. 'Hot Lips Houlihan' watch out! October 27th

  • Palestine Community Theater
    A wide variety of productions are offered each year from drama to comedy to musicals. Production dates and times vary each year so please Call us at 1-800-659-3484 for the latest information.

  • Museum for East Texas Culture
    They host many local events; a local artist exhibit, a dulcimer concert and BBQ are scheduled for the Dogwood Trails in March. Then there is an annual auction held in August and a Happy Haunted House in October. Call 1-800-659-3484 for the specific dates.

  • Murder on the DisORIENTed Express
    Hosted by the Texas State Railroad and Palestine Library Friends, this annual October event features a specially penned murder mystery that is enacted on the train. Passengers dine before boarding the train, and while it travels the inky blackness of the East Texas countryside, they are asked to solve a murder. Period costumes and actors lend a special touch to the entire evening. Proceeds benefit the Palestine Public Library and the Anderson County Literacy Council. Third Saturday in October. October 20th

  • Cattle Baron's Ball
    A very successful fund raising event benefitting the American Cancer Society. Call the Chamber for details. 903-729-6066


    C H R I S T M A S in P A L E S T I N E

  • Christmas Parade of Lights
    A crisp December evening, twinkling lights, marching band music and Santa Claus is all that is required for the perfect Christmas parade. Add clowns, mounted horsemen, antique cars and a beautiful downtown area and you have our Christmas Parade of Lights on the first Saturday in December. December 1st

  • Christmas Tour of Homes
    Each December the local gardening clubs sponsor three homes for this tour. Each home is an exquisite example of various period architecture. Special decorative touches are donated by the gardening clubs to complement the superb homes. First weekend in December. December 1-2

  • Victorian Christmas Train Ride
    A Christmas of yesteryear awaits you at the Texas State Railroad in December. Old world refreshments, strolling carolers and a train ride on a genuine Victorian stream powered train. Come dressed in your favorite Victorian costume and enjoy a gracious trip into yesteryear. (Not all of us have Victorian costumes, so join us and just 'come as you are'.) First three Saturdays in December. December 1, 8, & 15.
    Call us at 1-800-659-3484 for information regarding ticketed Christmas events and other details.
