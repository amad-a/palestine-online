Palestine is said by some to be as gracious and beautiful as a Southern Belle. She displays her magnificence through numerous restored Victorian homes and yards filled with magnolias and dogwoods.
Downtown buildings with architecture from the early 1800s are filled with specialty shops, antique stores and unique merchandise.
On the soft breezes that kiss the tall pines and dogwoods, you may hear the whistle of a distant train. Often called the Iron Horse, but better known as the Texas State Railroad, it makes weekend excursions through the splendor of the countryside taking you on a trip back in time when the steam engine was the ruler of transportation.
Within the arms of this Southern Belle is the world famous Eilenberger's Bakery, offering - for 100 years - old world recipe cakes, cookies and pastries. Or if festivals are your forte, then check out the Hot Pepper or Dogwood Trails Festivals, or any one of the numerous special events which happen year round.
Enter through the gates of yesteryear and visit Palestine; you'll find it to be a city which beckons you back time and time again.
