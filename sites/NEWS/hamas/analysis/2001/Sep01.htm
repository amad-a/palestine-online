(26 September 2001)

Political Analysis

American policies and the helpless bet

Introduction:

The USA has played a pivotal role in international politics after the Second World War. This role gained added importance and centrality after the disintegration of the Soviet Union and the socialist bloc countries represented in the Warsaw Pact. Hence the USA became the sole leader of the world. The entire world has become relatively governed by results of the American elections and the consequent changes in the new administration’s economic, military and political policies. This has a direct impact on the Middle East situation and the American handling of the Arab-Zionist conflict. Which means that parties to this conflict have to take into account the prevailing American political atmosphere when setting their agendas  to realize their goals.

Constants and variables in American policy:

The USA is essentially a two party democracy where the Republican and Democratic parties take turns in power, the change of party in power entails a change of the administration team. This alteration is normally followed by a change in policies despite the absence of essential differences between the two parties in political tenets. The president’s personality, his team and their political, economic and social beliefs greatly reflect on the overall policy of the administration. The American voters are more concerned with internal issues such as social insurance, taxes and economic policies than any other question.

As for the foreign policy: it is governed by USA’s fundamental interests and any change in this policy is linked to American interests in the world in addition to internal considerations in the American society and influence of pressure groups.

The American international policy is based on certain interests that envisage boosting the country’s international status in a way preserving its pioneering role in leading the world. It seeks to maintain America as the sole power ruling supreme on global politics and economy. It also seeks to retain the state of military supremacy through developing defensive and military capabilities to ensure its hegemony while endeavoring to neutralize military potentials of other big countries.

American policy in the Middle East is based on four vital interests:

1- Oil: the Middle East, especially the Arab Gulf countries, is the main source of oil supplies to the USA. This is the lifeline of the American economic activity where American machines consume 30% of world oil despite the fact that its population constitutes no more than 6% of the world population. Furthermore, oil is the most important international commodity on demand and has no alternative at times of peace and of vital strategic importance at times of war.

2- Water routes: the Middle East controls a group of the most important water routes in the world including the Mediterranean, the Red Sea and the Arab Gulf if Turkey is excluded. The region thus gains added military, strategic and economic importance since most of the oil trade and others pass through those water routes.

3- Preserving the Zionist entity’s security, existence and supremacy in various spheres in its capacity as the West’s advanced base in the Middle East especially after the USA took over leadership of western colonialism from Britain.

4- The region, especially the Gulf States, is and essential market for American goods that helps keep the American industrial machine running. The region is considered the biggest importer of American arms, an industry that supports many other industries.

Based on these interests constant American policies were drawn, most important of which:

1- The establishment of strategic alliances in the region that work for protecting and realizing American interests. They would serve as advanced basis for the USA in the region. The most preferred state in the region for that mission is the Zionist entity, yet the USA is keen on maintaining its alliance with Egypt and continuation of its assistance to that Arab country in a way almost equaling the aid presented to the Zionist entity to ensure retaining Egypt as a satellite to American policy in the region.

2- Guaranteeing freedom of navigation in waterways for American navy and commercial vessels. This is one of the reasons for the presence of American forces in the Gulf and Red Sea waters since the Iraqi-Iranian war.

3- Ensuring stability in the region so that no Arab-Zionist dispute would turn into a comprehensive regional war, since such a probability would threaten the USA’s vital interests in the region.

The variable policies deal with the means and mechanisms that contribute to protecting American vital interests or the partial interests linked to them. American economic, political and military pressures fall under this category with the aim of realizing specific goals. Those pressures are coupled with political initiatives and related negotiating tactics on various issues. The team that deals with the Middle East crisis in the American administration sets out these polices.

American policy from Clinton to Bush:

The American involvement in the Arab-Zionist conflict reached a peak during the reign of president Clinton when the peace process occupied the topmost priority in the American foreign policy. All political settlements to the conflict were signed under the supervision and patronage of Clinton and his administration. Those efforts reached a climax in the Camp David summit (2) in a bid to end the conflict after which Clinton concluded his efforts in what came to be known as Clinton’s paper that was tabled at the end of Barak’s rule.

During those years, the USA employed all means available to impose a peaceful settlement to end decades of Arab-Zionist conflict. However, those colossal efforts that included the personal involvement of Clinton only led to partial agreements essentially on security issues. Those efforts reached a deadlock and the possibility of concluding a comprehensive settlement faded away after eruption of the Aqsa intifada and election of Sharon.

Under these circumstances, American president Bush Jr. took over the presidency with a new Republican administration that started an evaluation of the American performance in the Middle East especially regarding the Arab-Zionist conflict to benefit from experience of the Clinton administration. The new American administration’s performance in dealing with political developments in the Middle East was confused in the absence of a clear vision. More often than not conflicting statements were voiced by officials of the new administration towards the crises in addition to political and field developments especially between vice president Dick Cheney and secretary of state Powell. However, features of the American policies started to take shape after figures and parties specialized in Middle East affairs started to draft those policies. Observers and analysts noted that the core of the American policy towards parties to that conflict have not changed, however the involvement in the details when dealing with crisis in the region that was prevalent during the Clinton administration has disappeared.

Features of the new American policy in the region:

1- The assumption that the Zionist entity is the main pillar to guarantee the USA’s interests in the region. The American-Zionist alliance in words and deeds is the main effective tool in the Middle East and is the solid base for America’s plans and policies in the region. Consequently that relationship should never suffer from tension. Furthermore, relations with Arab countries in the region should not come anywhere near the special relations that the USA and the Zionist entity have.

2- Easing American administration’s direct intervention in the region’s crises and explaining to both parties that the USA’s interest in a final settlement between the two parties is limited and that any real American interference should be based on two factors:

A-Both parties should ask for American intervention asking for settlement proposals, thus pointing out that negotiations are the responsibility of both parties and America is not a party to that.

B-The two parties must pledge to accept the tabled formulas.

3- The presidency should be kept aloof from dealing with the crisis to preserve its esteem leaving the mission to the state department in addition to activating role of the CIA. In general, the administration prefers reducing the rank of American employees involved in dealing with Middle Eastern crises.

4- There should be absolute keenness not to escalate the crises into regional war and pressures should be practiced on various parties to avoid such a possibility. Pressures must involve all available methods to halt security deterioration in Palestine and bring back both parties to the negotiating table.

5- The friendly Arab countries should continue to table political initiatives to settle the conflict even if the initiatives’ success opportunity was slim because otherwise a political vacuum would occur in the region leading to escalation of events.

6- Pressures would continue on the Palestinian Authority to return to its security role and to vow to return to the negotiating table in its capacity as the sole mean of settling the conflict with the Zionist entity.

7- Pressures would be also exerted on Syria to calm down the South Lebanon’s front and curb Hizbullah’s moves. At the same time Washington would declare absolute support to any Zionist measure against Syria or Lebanon in retaliation to Hizbullah attacks.

8- There should be clear-cut separation of the Arab-Zionist conflict from the relations with the Gulf countries to ensure America’s oil interests in the Arabian Peninsula in their capacity as two different strategic files with each having its own policies and plans that should not be mixed up.

9- Pressures must be maintained on countries rebelling against American hegemony topped by Iran, Iraq, Syria and the Sudan.

The American policy in dealing with the current crisis:

Coping with the aforementioned political broad lines, the new American policy can be summarized in the following points:

1- Intensifying American secret and declared pressures on the PA to return it to its security role in favor of occupation through the following methods:

a) Holding the PA directly responsible for escalation of events and demanding more efforts to end the intifada and return to negotiations.

b) Dealing with Arafat’s visit to Washington as a pressure card to offer more political and field concessions.

c) Activating a number of Arab countries to pressure the PA into claming things down.

d) The Congress’ condemnation of the PA, charging it of backing terrorism and threatening to label the PLO as a terrorist organization and barring American assistance to the PA.

2- Involving the PA in understandings and initiatives to abort the intifada politically, to lower ceiling of its demands, from one of independence and liberation to marginal questions, and to deal with the crisis as a security problem.

3- Seeking to strip the Palestinian party from its source of strength (resistance) and returning it to the negotiating table in a weaker position than before on the basis of renouncing violence as means of achieving political goals.

4- Avoiding any condemnation of the Zionist entity in international platforms especially in the Security Council in view of its legally binding resolutions.

5- Foiling any international efforts or initiatives that do not win approval of the Zionist entity as evident in aborting the proposal of deploying international observers despite its shortfalls.

6- Continuing declared political, informational and military support to the Zionist entity despite its crimes against the Palestinian people.

7- Lowering the rank of employees supervising the crises from secretary of state to his assistant William Burns then to his envoy David Satterfield in a way affirming the American trend to ignore the region’s crisis while curbing any escalation through the CIA to avoid a regional war.

8- Allowing the European Union and the European NATO members to play an assistant political role that would complement the American role in the region in a way serving American policy and interests of its Zionist ally.

9- Employing internal pressure cards on a number of Arab countries in accordance with American political needs.

Arab stand towards American policy:

The Arab stand towards the USA is governed by certain factors most important of which:

1- The repercussions of the second Gulf War on Arab solidarity and the ensuing American military presence in the region and the political, economic and military consequences attached to that presence.

2- Priority of bilateral interests with the USA since most of the Arab states set their individual interests and relations above their Arab national duty especially those receiving economic assistance and custom facilities or enjoy direct American military protection.

3- A number of Arab countries are committed to certain roles in line of serving American interests in the region especially countries described by America as moderate.

4- Some Arab states fear that the USA might stir internal troubles through opening certain files concerning internal affairs,  charges of terrorism, slavery and other issues that the Americans could use to interfere in the internal affairs of or pressure those states. Hence those countries turned into a hostage to such issues and files.

5- The impotence of  Arab institutions in the face of the Zionist entity and the USA particularly that the Arabs shackled themselves when they decided that negotiations is their sole strategy to solve the conflict, add to that Arab division and national weakness.

These factors directly affected Arab performance in face of the American policies, allied with the Zionist entity, leading to the state of Arab paralysis and helplessness in dealing with the American administration. Such a situation is reflected in the following:

1- Refraining from any denunciation of American stands while merely calling on Washington not to be biased in favor of the Zionist position.

2- Asking the USA to interfere to end the Zionist aggression on the Palestinian people.

3- Urging the USA to shoulder its political and ethical international responsibilities towards the Zionist repression of the Palestinian people.

4- Dispatching Arab delegations to explain the Arab situation and begging for an American intervention to solve the dispute.

5- Ruling out any attempt or threat against American interests in the region especially the return to using the oil weapon since that would harm the Arabs themselves. The Arabs argue that they are not capable of taking such a big step (oil embargo) because it would threaten their common interests with the USA.

6- Some Arab countries and leaders continue to assist the American role in the region especially through efforts of countries establishing relations with the Zionist entity and others. They do not wish for a political vacuum that would lead to further escalation in the region. Hence came the numerous Arab initiatives to return to the pre 28 September 2000 and a return to the negotiating table.

The PA stand towards American policies:

The PA position could not be separated from the Arab stand since they are essentially the same with a mere relative tactical difference and certain specialty due to the PA’s situation and political trends. The most prominent factors affecting the PA’s stand are the following:

1- The Oslo team believes that 99% of the cards of the political game in the region are in the hands of the USA.

2- The team fears that an American step to strip the PA off its political legitimacy would mean the beginning of the end of that team.

3- That team also focuses on importance of harmonizing with American political and security initiatives due to the aforementioned two factors.

4- Personal considerations of a number PA leaders who linked their fate to the USA whether due to being employed by its intelligence services or their need for American support in face of internal competition on important positions in the PA and the PLO after Arafat’s absence. This explains the numerous visits of different PA political and security officials to the USA. The USA recently leaked information that six PA ministers and 11 legislative council members were on the CIA seasonal payroll.

5- The PA is determined to continue along the Oslo path considering it the only strategic option available, which necessitates the intervention of an international party to help in finding a political settlement and to serve as the guarantor for implementation of any signed agreement.

6- The PA acknowledges American tilt towards the Zionist entity and its sole leadership of the world without presence of any other alternate international party.

Such considerations have their impact on the PA’s stand towards the USA and its policies in the region, examples of such influence:

1- Continuing positive interaction with the USA’s political and security role in the region whether due to real desire or in response to political and economic pressures.

2- Continuing to beg for an American role and even seeking to involve the American administration into the Middle East quagmire in its capacity as the sole party capable of pressuring the Zionist government.

3- Denouncing the American stands that support the Zionist entity’s repressive policies and the American unlimited support to occupation with all means while retaining open bridges with the American administration. The PA tries to voice protests to satisfy the Palestinian street while at the same time its maintains its relationship with the US.

4- Trying to encourage international parties such as Russia, the European union and finally China to play a role in the peace process to break the American monopoly on the Middle East file. It also tried using that method to pressure the USA into returning to play an effective role in the settlement fearing to lose its weight in the region especially in the light of the recent lapses of American foreign policy in international platforms.

5- Urging certain Arab countries to mediate with the American administration to activate its role or to explain the PA’s stand or to table the PA’s ideas with the Americans in the form of initiatives to extricate the PA, parties to the peace process and America’s allies in the region from the current ordeal.

The losing bet:

The American president’s statements that declared absolute support to the Zionist entity and asked the PA to exert 100% efforts to halt resistance along with the vice president Dick Cheney’s support of Zionist assassination policy wiped out all speculations that Bush was less influenced by Zionist lobby that supported his presidential opponent Al Gore. This proves that betting on a neutral American role was unrealistic and illogical and that dragging the USA into the region was not and will not be in favor of the Palestinians and the Arabs. This was proven in Clinton’s era and later Bush. The matter thus is not linked to an administration, party or the person ruling the USA in as much as other factors most important of which:

1- The importance of the USA’s vital and strategic interests in the region topped by security and existence of the Zionist entity since the Zionist project was the brainchild of the colonialist West. This project would have never seen the light had it not been for absolute western support. The USA had taken over leadership of the West after World War II. One of its most important responsibilities was preserving the existence and security of the Zionist entity in Palestine in its capacity as the West’s advanced military and culture base.

2- The American law and election rules give the various pressure groups a considerable political influence. Top of those groups comes the Zionist lobby that gains strength from two main elements:

The first its financial resources in the money market (Wall Street) and other giant companies in arms industry, technology and banks in addition to huge informational resources in the media making, audio-visual production and the cinema.

The second its presence in the White House and various political decision-making apparatuses, which can be clearly witnessed in decisions on the Middle East.

3- The American political system, in theory, is based on balanced pluralism where lobbies representing various interests are the instrument used to insure the political leadership is representative of their collective views. The balance of interests of various parties composing the society leads to balance of policies. However, strength of the Zionist lobby turned the American political system into an unbalanced system in view of its supremacy over other pressure groups. Thus we find the American Congress in the past few months adopting several decisions and draft laws affirming unlimited support to the Zionist entity and others condemning the PA on unanimous basis with the exception of only one deputy. This raises the question on absence of an Arab pressure group in the USA.

4- The absolute American support to the Zionist entity is not limited to certain sectors but rather it is a popular trend as affirmed in opinion polls that showed an 85% support to the Zionist entity. Furthermore, more than 70% supported the Zionist policy of assassination, which is an indication that the American-Zionist alliance was supported by the American society. Backing Israel is a popular, cultural, religious, American and western phenomenon linked to a belief in the return of the Messiah that is deeply rooted in the western culture based on their belief in the Old Testament. Church in the USA has a big influence on the society and the American political figures despite the talk of segregating religion from the state.

The aforementioned, other than numerous phenomena that could not all be listed here, affirm that betting on the American role in the region was the helpless choice of a party that linked itself, program and future to enemies of its people and Nation and is thus trekking an absurd road that would not lead to anything tangible or worthwhile.

Any effective and practical dealing with the USA must be based on the following:

1- Giving less weight to the USA role in the region and trying to contain and isolate it, which requires disregarding its initiatives and not begging for its solutions.

2- Making it clear to USA that its interests in the region and the world would not be served in the light of its unconditional support of the Zionist entity.

3- Maintaining open channels with the American public to inform them of  what is truly taking place in Palestine and to explain the dangers that the Zionist project poses to American stability and her interests in the long run through activating the role of Arab societies and coordinating their efforts.

4- Endorsing the resistance program as the Palestinian people and the Arab and Islamic Nation’s strategic option along with developing elements of power in the Nation and giving up once and for all the political settlement option, which represents the main weak point through which the USA and the Zionists penetrate our people and Nation’s ranks.

6- Reviving the committees formed to boycott American goods and presenting national alternatives.

In short: logical, practical and realistic stands necessitate dealing with the American administration as the patron and participant in the Zionist project in the region and thus all policies and work programs should be based on that assumption while having deep understanding of the political, social and doctrinal situation in the USA along with an understanding of America’s interests and policies in the region and the world.
