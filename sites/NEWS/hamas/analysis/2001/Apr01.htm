(April 2001)

 

Political Analysis  

17/4/2001

Intifada escalates in confrontation of Sharon government’s terrorism

Zionist premier Ariel Sharon did not need 100 days to complete execution of his plan, main points of which were declared on his victory of the election. The past two months were enough to display his plan in practice without succeeding in actualizing his promise to the Zionist audience that is thirsty for security and without implementing his expectations that the intifada would end in the first weeks of his ascension to power.

The martyrdom operations and mortar shells were the early Palestinian methods in confronting Sharon and his criminal government. Still there is more creative resistance operations that would continue alongside popular confrontations until defeat of occupation.

Sharon’s terrorism did not succeed in defeating resistance

Sharon started his campaign against the Palestinian people with tightening the siege laid to cities, towns and villages. He also refused to transfer collected custom dues to the Palestinian Authority in a bid to force it return to security coordination. He demanded the PA to re-detain leaders of Jihad and Islamic Resistance who were released at start of the Aqsa intifada. However, the campaign failed in the light of escalation of resistance offensives especially the martyrdom operations that coincided with the Arab summit in Amman. Sharon later shifted to leveling painful blows to Palestinian security centers especially the Force-17, which he accused of facilitating the resistance attacks and even taking part in some of them. The attempts, which aimed at terrorizing the PA, did not bear fruit so far in the required form due to absence of any progress on the political level along with Sharon’s rejection of holding negotiations with the PA as long as resistance continued. The Zionist savage shelling was coupled with field liquidation of resistance and intifada activists especially those of Hamas and Islamic Jihad, four of whom were so far martyred in Zionist operations. This campaign also failed due to continuation of daily resistance operations particularly the escalation in firing mortar shells at Zionist settlements in Gaza. The Zionists considered such shelling as a serious threat to their security and expressed fears of resultant big losses in lines of the settlers. They claimed that the attacks inflicted three casualties so far.

When Sharon realized that his iron-fist policy did not succeed in breaking the Palestinian people’s determination, he moved in two adjacent methods:

First: carrying out terrorist storming operations of certain cities and suburbs under control of the PA especially in the Gaza Strip and Al-Khalil (Hebron) in view of the presence of Jewish settlement areas within them.

Second: intensifying security contacts with the PA to reap the fruit of the military pressures and push the Authority into returning to security coordination after luring it with resumption of negotiations.

Sharon coupled such a policy with voicing statements accepting establishment of a disarmed Palestinian state on 42% of the West Bank and Gaza Strip areas.

On the other hand, he entrusted his partner (Peres) with launching a diplomatic campaign to picture the Palestinian people’s struggle as violence and terrorism in addition to justifying Zionist repressive measures in their capacity as retaliation to such terrorism and an attempt to protect the Zionists.

Despite continuation of the Zionist security campaign, yet the Palestinian people’s insistence on continuance of intifada and resistance led to failure of Sharon’s goal of subduing the Palestinian people into accepting nothing more than his predecessor Barak had offered.

Meanwhile, the PA’s secret and public negotiations with Sharon’s government was met with strong opposition in the Palestinian street that expressed its rejection of such practice through marches and demonstrations in addition to continuation of confrontations with the occupation. This opposition pushed the PA to be very cautious in dealing with such negotiations particularly when the maximum limit offered by Sharon was less than that tabled by Barak that was rejected on the Palestinian, Arab and Islamic levels. Furthermore, hopes that the new American administration of Bush would pressure the Zionists into resuming negotiations from the point, which they had reached, died out. This administration was totally biased in favor of the Zionists as was evident in condemning Palestinian resistance operations and giving the green light for Sharon’s campaign against intifada and resistance activists during Sharon’s visit to the USA earlier this month. However, fears of the PA’s submission to Sharon and his security demand were still there. Information indicated that the meeting held in Gaza between PA chairman Yasser Arafat and Avy Dachter, head of the Zionist general security apparatus (The Shabak) to discuss the most dangerous issue that annoyed the Zionists lately, i.e. the firing of mortar shells that slammed into Zionist positions and settlements within and at the outskirts of the Gaza Strip, had led Arafat to acquiesce. He agreed to work for halting the mortar attacks in response to Zionist threats that the Zionist occupation army would directly target his Authority if he did not adopt practical measures to stop those assaults.

PA and the hesitation between both options: steadfastness or submission

Sharon continued in his campaign against the Palestinian people and concentrated on striking Palestinian security forces in addition to destroying Palestinian houses and buildings, which he claimed were used by the Palestinian resistance. Zionist hesitation over the best method of dealing with the PA was apparent. The Zionists were hesitant whether to preserve and avoid the collapse of the PA or to prevent it from coordinating with the Palestinian people in resisting occupation.

Sharon conveyed a message to Arafat namely that he did not intend to reoccupy PA areas. Sharon’s son Omray, who secretly met with Arafat in Ramallah, carried the message. On the other hand, he launched a practical step by occupying a number of areas categorized as (A) zones for limited hours during which his soldiers destroyed Palestinian targets and buildings. The operation indicated that Sharon similar to his predecessor Barak was not interested so far in ending that Authority so as to avoid confronting more difficult and stronger foes. However, Sharon was interested in pushing that Authority into the brink of collapse to compel it to accept a formula for security coordination and achieve political gains. No doubt Sharon and his security machine were primarily targeting Arafat in person who they believe is the key to relations with the Zionist entity ignoring the fact that Arafat and the PA could not bypass the Palestinian people’s will.  Even if they managed to bypass the Palestinians’ will at a certain previous stage, yet it was not possible this time after all those sacrifices and blood offered by the Palestinian people. The PA feared that it would be charged with treachery if it sought to terminate the intifada in return for gains that would not convince a small Palestinian child.

The PA was trying to cope with the popular leanings, including its organization the Fatah Movement, while at the same time trying to preserve its presence and gains secured through winning satisfaction of the Zionists. The PA’s options was moving in between as long as it saw that its presence was not threatened and as long as it continued to believe that it could secure more gains through negotiations, a matter surely not accepted by the Palestinian people.

Growing and continuing intifada despite pains and wounds

The intifada, after six months of its eruption, secured numerous gains whether on the level of asserting the Palestinian people’s insistence on their rights, lands and holy shrines or whether through asserting the option of steadfastness and resistance. It further succeeded in activating popular support on the Arab and Islamic levels in addition to restoring conflict with the Zionist enemy to its early and real beginnings that is based on contradiction of the Zionist project with the project of the Nation’s renaissance and progress.

Though the Aqsa intifada did not lead to big changes in the official Arab stand as represented in the Amman summit late last month, a number of relatively positive developments took place on that level namely supporting the resistance program and granting it Arab legitimacy after it was rejected or at least not supported in the past.

We may quote a German expert as saying in an article published in his country on 6 April that the “second intifada displayed pointers on the possibility that (Israel) might lose the image of an invincible state and even (accept the idea) that it could be defeated in the end.” We also would like to refer to the Zionist statistics on intifada activities, which are naturally less than the real figures, stipulating that the intifada led to the killing of 75 Zionists and more than 550 wounded in six months compared to less than 400 Zionist wounded during six years of the first intifada, which started in 1987. It is a sure thing that the Zionist human losses were increasing in addition to economic losses. The most important loss, however, was the psychological factor, which directly affected the Zionist society’s ability of steadfastness. Continuation of Zionist losses in the next few years would lead to changes in the Zionist position that could accept giving up occupied areas similar to its withdrawal from South Lebanon following concentrated resistance strikes.

As for the Palestinian losses, they are expected and known but the Palestinian people backed with their Arab and Islamic Nation have always proven that they were capable of steadfastness and withstanding repercussions of resistance for the sake of achieving their noble goal of ejecting occupation. Surely this people’s ability to resist will continue and increase despite Sharon’s arrogance and  .
