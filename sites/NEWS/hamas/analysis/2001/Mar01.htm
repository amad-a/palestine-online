(March 2001)

 

Political Analysis  

Continuation of intifada and resistance in face of the national unity government

3/3/2001

It is true that Ariel Sharon’s significant victory over his contender Ehud Barak posed as a Zionist referendum towards the settlement process especially the final status issues. However, the ensuing formation of a Zionist national unity government on the basis of Sharon’s program along with wiping out all understandings reached in the Camp David II and Taba served as the mercy bullet that finished off the settlement process and initiated a state of war against Palestinians and Arabs. This program could be only confronted through escalation of the intifada and all forms of resistance to eject occupation from the occupied Palestinian territories.

Sharon and the unity government … pointers and indications

The election of the mass murderer General Sharon with 25 points more than the other mass murderer General Barak did not merely end confidence in the latter as some tried to picture the matter. Sharon’s sweeping victory was an indication that the Zionist street was heading towards more extremism and fanaticism, which that man can express more than any other. Consequently the mass murderer of Sabra and Chatilla and commander of the Zionist terrorism gangs won a big endorsement by the Zionist street never dreamt of by many leaders of the Hebrew state since its creation. The authorization was given to Sharon to quell the intifada after his predecessor failed in this mission. In other words the Zionist street panicked by the intifada and resistance wanted to restore its lost security on both the individual and collective levels especially when the Aqsa intifada had led to the killing of scores of Zionists and wounding of hundreds other than the thousands who call on psychiatric clinics following the return of booby-trapped car explosions in Tel Aviv, Al-Quds (Jerusalem) and Khadera.

No doubt the other message addressed through Sharon’s election was the Zionist stand towards the settlement process namely rejecting all proposals over Al-Quds and the refugees, which were tackled in the Camp David II and Taba negotiations and which paved the way for Barak government’s downfall in the Knesset and Barak’s subsequent defeat in the premiership elections. Consequently results of the election represented a Zionist referendum on understandings of the final status talks and dealt a coup de grace to the Oslo agreement that was staggering in the past years and entered the stage of clinical death following the Aqsa intifada.

The intifada had started an alarm bell in the Zionist entity while the elections broke loose its extremism and wish of imposing hegemony on the Palestinian people. For its part, formation of the national unity government with the approval of the two big parties and with participation of most Zionist parties, if not all, amalgamated all elements of force in the Zionist society in face of an unarmed people that only owned solid belief in victory by the grace of Allah and in justice of its cause and rights in addition to strong determination and support of the Arab and Islamic Nation.

The Labor party’s approval to participate in the national unity government with the Likud reflected the Zionist street’s aspirations and wishes. It furthermore achieved the party’s interests and aspirations after it started to suffer from internal disintegration and retreat in its political program based on realizing a settlement with the Palestinian side ensuring Zionist security. The Labor party, through participating in one government with the Likud based on a stringent political program, is trying to beautify its image before the Zionist street. The participation could further be in response to the US Jewish lobby’s wishes and the American administration’s inclinations that did not want to leave Sharon rule supreme and drag the region into a state of instability.

As for Sharon, his alliance with the Labor party would ensure persistence of his government more than allying with religious and rightist parties. It would also present him in a better image before the Palestinians, Arabs and the world.

The national unity government’s program is based on the following:

  • Continuation of the settlement process and commitment to signed agreements.
  • Conclusion of transitory accords with the Palestinians rather than final agreements.
  • Introduction of changes on redeployment in the West Bank and Gaza Strip.
  • Non-establishment of new settlements unless both major parties in the coalition agreed.

The program clearly realizes Sharon’s demands, which declare that he would only abide by signed agreements and which consider understandings reached in Camp David II and Taba as non-binding. The new American administration supported that step. The program further ignores importance of reaching a final agreement in the near future as Barak had sought but rather concentrates on transitory accords. Such a shift would prolong negotiations for many years to come and would change understandings reached over Al-Quds and the refugees in addition to reducing the third redeployment stage to the minimum. As for the settlements, the program did not prevent its fattening or expanding, which is enough for Sharon who was the one that called for planting “political” settlements everywhere in the Palestinian lands.

Sharon government … and the intifada

The most important issue in the government’s program was the one dealing with means of confronting the intifada. There is a general consensus on continuing security measures including use of maximum force against intifada activities and persecution of Palestinian activists. However, Sharon is also seeking to expand the list of liquidation to group senior officials in the Palestinian Authority if the Zionists found out they were involved in encouraging or planning resistance operations. He is also of the opinion that personal and economic interests of PA leaderships should be endangered to pressure them into restoring security coordination to its former level and to employ its influence to calm down the intifada. Sharon already started his previous program through tightening the economic siege on PA areas. Zionist chief of Staff Shaul Mofaz also charged that the PA was turning into a terrorist entity, a statement that was backed by Sharon. The statement paves the way for leveling selective strikes against a number of PA figures or reoccupying certain PA controlled areas for a period of time so as to execute terrorist operations in them. Despite the reservations voiced by Peres (foreign minister in Sharon’s government) towards Mofaz’s statement, yet Sharon is determined to strengthen his security grip on the intifada. Sharon intends to turn his defense minister Benjamin Ben Eliazer, chosen by the Labor party to the post, into a repressive tool to quell the intifada especially when Eliazer is considered one of the right wing within the Labor party and his ideas are close to those of Sharon.

However, Sharon does not own any magical (terrorist) solutions to end the intifada despite his repeated threats to wipe it out. He would be no more bloodthirsty than his predecessor Barak who failed to stop the intifada and the resistance. Furthermore, any more tightening in the security and economic siege on the Palestinian people might lead to the political and economic collapse of the PA, a matter not favored by any Zionist government because the alternative would be facing all Palestinian forces without any commitment or control in addition to shouldering burdens of occupation anew, which would be far more costly than the current situation that followed the Oslo agreements. Hence, Sharon is not expected to occupy the Palestinian self-rule areas but rather to corner the PA in them without allowing it to expand control over other areas even within transitory accords. Sharon also would not give up political negotiations through which he would try to drag the PA into resuming security coordination under the pretext of winning political and economic gains in return.

The required Palestinian and Arab stand

Sharon’s election and formation of a national unity government reflected the Zionist stand towards the settlement process and the intifada. In return the Palestinian stand should be based on ending all forms of negotiations with the enemy, which only understood the language of force and only submitted to armed threat and gun smoke especially when the political settlement process failed to restore the Palestinian people’s rights and failed to convince the Zionist street to recognize the minimum limit of Palestinian aspirations.

The intifada must be escalated and the anti-occupation struggle methods must be developed to confirm the Palestinian people insistence on their rights under all circumstances and to declare that Zionist terrorism and iron-fist policy would not affect the Palestinian people’s determination or lessen their insistence on ejecting occupation from their lands. What is required here is a Palestinian national program of confrontation against occupation that does not limit itself to coordinating or organizing intifada activities but rather should comprise a political program of confrontation on the basis of resistance.

The Palestinian people have proven great ability of steadfastness and sacrifice regardless of the elapse of time or big losses. Thus the Palestinian people won support of the Arab and Islamic world and forced the enemy to accord them special attention. The Palestinian leadership should live up to the people’s will, give up the Oslo program, set the interests of the people above its own interests and lead a national unity program in a way achieving the Palestinian people’s aspirations and furnishing the road for a real Arab-Islamic interaction with the intifada and resistance. Such a situation would improve the official Arab stance driving it into adopting and supporting the resistance program with all available potentials.

Sharon does not wish to develop the dispute in the region into an all-out war with the Arab countries because the international situation does not allow it and because such a scenario does not serve the Jewish entity that is trying to merge into its surroundings and not get more isolated. At the same time, the Arab countries’ stands do not differ from Sharon’s wish due to internal and external calculations. However, the possibility of war breaking out or limited clashes between the enemy’s army and each of Syria and Iraq or even Iran still remains lingering. Thus the Arab countries should be prepared for such a scenario and not remain captive to fears of such a development. They should also back the Palestinian people’s struggle program considering it the advanced defense line of the Arab Nation and a constant attrition of the enemy.
