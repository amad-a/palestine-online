(January 2001)

 

Political Analysis

Clinton’s proposals … American-Zionist conspiracy doomed to failure

American president Bill Clinton did not wish to leave the presidency on 20 January before achieving a final accomplishment that would be registered in the black Zionist lobby’s files and without trying to save the stringent General Ehud Barak from falling in the forthcoming Zionist elections in the light of the continued progress of his more extremist and terrorist opponent Ariel Sharon.

Hence, came the proposals declared by the outgoing American president as a last ditch effort to endorse results and understandings reached in the Camp David II negotiations and compelling the Palestinian Authority to accept tabled solutions to the questions of Al-Quds (Jerusalem) and refugees, which were not completely finalized during those negotiations.

The unusual intensity of negotiations in the past few weeks over Clinton’s proposals harbingers new Palestinian concessions and threatens to abort the intifada that broke out of rejection of similar proposals in the first place.

Clinton’s proposals … background and motives

It was obvious long time ago that the Palestinian Authority wished to invest the intifada to achieve political gains but it was unable to do so at its early beginnings out of fear from popular Palestinian and Arab reactions. Furthermore, the PA estimated that the Barak government was not interested in reinvigorating the settlement process or improving its offers tabled in Camp David II negotiations. Two months after outbreak of the intifada and the accompanying decline in Barak’s popularity, he tabled his resignation from the premiership thus starting the race for the post, pointers of which indicate that the Zionist street was inclined to vote for the Likud candidate Ariel Sharon.

Faced with such a situation, Barak reached the conclusion that his only chance to defeat Sharon was concluding a final settlement with the PA after exploiting the American president Bill Clinton’s wish to accomplish an historic agreement before leaving the presidency. Thus Barak expedited his advisors and representatives to hold secret meetings with PA leaders to probe the Authority’s willingness to seize that opportunity as well. The meetings reaffirmed the PA’s wish to extend a helping hand to Barak after considering that the other alternative would be much worse than him, that he would drag the region into an atmosphere of war, that he would escalate repressive measures against the intifada and against the PA itself, which was starting to fear its status in the midst of such a terrified scenario.

When the in-camera talks succeeded in luring the PA into winning relatively better gains than those achieved in Camp David II especially regarding the area of land to be transferred to Palestinian sovereignty (around 95% of the West Bank in addition to the whole of Gaza Strip), the American administration announced its readiness to receive Palestinian and Zionist delegations to table its proposals on a lasting settlement.

The American proposals were based on the Zionist position tabled during marathon talks that were secretly and openly held in and outside Palestinian lands during the past month of December. Yet the American-Zionist wish to forge a speedy agreement with PA endorsement made Clinton officially table his proposals asking the Palestinian party in particular to accept them as they are without any amendments in their capacity as the gist of the Zionist stance and the best possible offer.

It was not strange that Barak would announce his principled acceptance of the proposals only few days after tabling them in Washington with Zionist and Palestinian delegations since they were originally Zionist ideas with minor amendments. However, the PA could not accept them because it needed more time to prepare and convince the public opinion not to display strong opposition to the negotiations based on those proposals since they do not guarantee return of refugees and do not allow real Palestinian sovereignty over Al-Quds!

However, Arafat’s visit to America including his meeting with Clinton was an attempt to throw the ball (of such burning proposals) into the Zionist court out of the wish not to shoulder the responsibility of rejecting them. He tabled a number of queries to the American administration as a hurdle in face of PA acceptance of them.

The PA paid a heavy price for dealing with those proposals namely accepting the resumption of security coordination and seeking to calm down the intifada in addition to showing readiness to accept them if certain Palestinian reservations were taken into consideration.

Clinton’s Zionist proposals and the Palestinian reservations

Despite the fact that Clinton’s proposals were not tabled as an official memorandum so far but rather verbal points tabled by Clinton in the presence of the Zionist and Palestinian delegations in Washington, yet their details were leaked to Zionist and Palestinian media means then Clinton divulgee them in a speech at the Israeli political forum on 8 January.

The following table reveals nature of the American proposals and both the Zionist and Palestinian explanations of them:

1

 Land

-The Zionist entity is to withdraw from 94-96% of the total West Bank area (5800 sq. Km and 100% from the Gaza Strip area (365 Sq. Km).

-The Palestinians are to be compensated with lands (1-3%) in addition to a permanent safe passage between the West Bank and the Strip in return for the areas that would be annexed to the Zionist entity.

-The two parties are to ponder exchanging lands and renting them.

 

-Withdrawing from (94-96%) of the West Bank after deducting the east Al-Quds (Jerusalem) area and settlements that amount to (5%) of the total West Bank area.

-The Barak government expressed readiness to grant the PA the Halutsa area, within the green line and adjacent to the Gaza Strip, with an area of (1%) of the total West Bank area. It is an area of low Zionist presence and the Zionist enemy has been thinking of getting rid of it a long time ago and is currently using it to bury nuclear waste of its Deymona reactor.

-Zionists wish to rent the settlements surrounding Khalil city for a long period.

  

2

 Settlements

American proposals are based on two issues:

1-Annexing the minimum possible areas of the West Bank to the Zionist entity with the biggest possible number of settlements.

2-The minimum possible number of Palestinians would be affected by that annexation.

-Hence the proposals stipulate annexing 80% of the settlements to the Hebrew state.

-It would comprise settlements that would be annexed: the settlement blocs surrounding Al-Quds and the northern settlements bloc.

That would necessitate the dismantling of 100 small settlements from 150 ones in addition to re-inhabiting 20% of its settlers in settlement blocs that would be annexed to the Zionist entity or granted financial compensations to build new houses.

The PA’s declared stand is the dismantling of all settlements but the negotiating stand is accepting the annexation of Al-Quds settlements only which comprise 5% of the West Bank area and restoring the remaining 95% area. But the PA is apparently prepared to accept 90% of the West Bank area because it had accepted 88% in Camp David II.
3 Withdrawal and security arrangements

-Zionist withdrawal from Palestinian territories to be completed in 36 months during which the international forces gradually deploy in areas under their supervision.

-At conclusion of the 36 months, Israeli forces would be stationed in specified positions in the Jordan Valley under authority of the international forces deployed in the Palestinian lands for a period not more than another 36 months that could be curtailed if security conditions were less threatening.

-The American proposals include formation of international forces led by the US with participation of Israeli forces along with forces from Europe and others that would not be evacuated unless the two parties’ approved.

-The Zionist entity is to retain three warning stations in the West Bank mountains, status of which would be revised after ten years.

-The Zionist entity keeps arms caches in the West Bank for emergency cases when it is threatened from the eastern borders. Emergency is defined as an imminent and clear threat to security of the Zionist entity on condition that it was of military nature and in the event a state of emergency was declared in “Israel”.

-The airspace of the Palestinian state would be under Palestinian sovereignty but the two parties would work for finding special arrangements for the Zionist entity’s needs in the fields of training and movement.

-The Zionist army demands renting the Jordan Valley for 12-25 years or controlling vital areas in that Valley.

-The state of emergency is to be determined by the Zionist entity, allowing it to interfere in the Palestinian state in the event it felt a security threat from the eastern areas.

-The occupation army’s explanations stipulate that air control should be in the hands of the Zionist entity while allowing the Palestinians to use specified air passages.

-The PA demands guarantees on completion of the withdrawal from all Palestinian lands or from the percentage agreed upon so that the Oslo experience would not recur.

-The PA expressed reservations on participation of the Zionist entity in the international forces because it would pose as a veil for continuation of Zionist occupation

4

 The Palestinian state

-The Palestinian state is to be established on lands evacuated from the Zionists and on lands exchanged in the Negev. Its borders would be Jordan River to the east and the green line to the west.

-The Palestinian state would not be equipped with heavy arms.

The state should have effective security forces.

-The international forces are to protect borders of Palestine (international control on the crossings).

    

5

 Al-Quds

-The solution is based on a general principle that the Arab quarters in the city would be shifted to Palestinian sovereignty and the Jewish quarters to Zionist sovereignty. This also applies to the old city where both parties should negotiate on means of achieving geographic connection between suburbs under control of each of them.

-Eastern Al-Quds would be capital of Palestine and the western area the capital of the Zionist entity.

-Two options for the Haram Al-Sharif proposed:

First: Palestinian sovereignty on the Haram and Zionist one on the western (Wailing) Wall and the sacred spot, which is considered a part of it (this refers to the area underneath the Haram).

Second: Palestinian sovereignty on the Haram and a Zionist one on the Wailing Wall in addition to a joint one on the digging whether under the Haram or behind the Wailing Wall.

 Barak refused any proposal granting sovereignty to the Palestinian party over the Haram Al-Sharif.

-The PA fears converting old Al-Quds into isolated Cantons as is the case in the West Bank.

-The PA refuses dividing sovereignty on the Haram and demands full sovereignty on it (It would accept shifting the sovereignty to any Arab or Islamic party) and authorizing it control on the Haram.

6

 The refugees

-The proposals recognize that the Zionist party could not agree on the right of return because it contradicts with its sovereignty and threatens the Jewish feature of the state.

-The solution is based on the two parties’ agreement on the right of return for all refugees to the Palestinian state with the exception of Al-Quds and proposes rehabilitation of refugees in host countries or resettling them in third countries or returning to the Zionist entity within its sovereignty conditions or granting them appropriate reparations.

-Establishing an international committee for whatever entails from such an agreement such as compensations, rehabilitation and resettlement or any other material or moral commitments. America, the European countries, Zionist entity and other countries would share in such measures.

-Both parties agree that tabled solutions are considered a crystallization of the UN resolution 194.

  

The PA declares that it could not approve a solution not guaranteeing the right of return for refugees but it realizes that it was impossible to achieve it practically through the current settlement. Hence it focuses on the return of part of them to the Palestinian state and resettling or compensating what remained of them. The PA also set as priority the refugees in Lebanon, which was mentioned in Clinton’s proposals.

7

 Ending the conflict

-The agreement includes an end to the conflict and its implementation would put an end to all demands.

-The agreement might be executed through a Security Council resolution pointing out that its application was in implementation of the Security Council resolutions 242 and 338.

-The agreement calls for the immediate release of all Palestinian prisoners to coincide with the agreement’s execution.

  

The PA considers that an end to the conflict should only take place after implementing the agreement on the ground.

The proposals on the scale

It is clear that the proposals took into consideration most of the Zionist demands especially those dealing with the issues of Al-Quds and the refugees. Hence came the Barak government’s approval of those proposals with some reservations. Furthermore, Barak was hoping to conclude a framework peace agreement that would help him in the elections scheduled for 6 February. However, the PA found itself in between American pressures to accept the proposals as is without any clarifications and the rejection of the Palestinian people with all its forces including the Fatah Movement, the backbone of the Authority.

In the light of the continuation of the blessed intifada against occupation, any initiation of political talks with the enemy would mean letting down the intifada and weakening the resistance option. The price wanted in those talks was the head of the intifada because the Zionist enemy would not sign any agreement as long as the intifada was still raging. The Zionists also demanded the PA’s return to full security coordination with the occupation authorities including exerting all possible efforts to halt what they call “violence” and “terrorism” i.e. the intifada and the armed resistance operations!

When it comes to core of the negotiations, what is tabled does not exceed the Camp David II proposals with the exception of certain improvements as far as Al-Quds is concerned. The proposals, however, retained Zionist sovereignty over the whole city of Al-Quds through converting it into an open city along with sovereignty on the old city and hegemony on passages and crossings and the real control on the Haram Al-Sharif. The most important accomplishment for the enemy would be winning the largest Quds known in history.

As for the issue of the refugees, the right of return was not accepted. The agreement attempts to reduce it to the minimum of allowing their return to PA controlled areas, compensations or resettlement.

There is no change towards the issue of the Palestinian state that would be enjoying limited sovereignty, disarmed and linked to Zionist security through the early warning stations, arms caches and Zionist military presence in the Jordan Valley. The enemy troops would be also present through participation in the international forces on the borders and border crossings. Furthermore, the Palestinian lands would be divided into three Cantons isolated from each other (the north and center Canton, the southern Canton and the Gaza Canton).

No doubt, taking six years to complete the withdrawals would return the same experience of the Oslo agreements as a result of the expected Zionist delays under various pretexts. Application of the accord would be dependent on the nature of the occupation government, its alliances, internal conditions, nature of its relations with the PA, persistence of the intifada and presence of resistance.

The agreement does not achieve the minimum of Palestinian ambitions and will attempt to abort the intifada prior to reaching its aspired goal of defeating occupation in all Palestinian territories. Furthermore, its details and application mechanics would convert it into another transitory agreement but this time for final status issues. In turn, that agreement would also need detailed accords, application of which would be subject to wishes of the stronger party in the equation namely the Zionist entity.

Surely, the PA realizes reality of such proposals and the serious repercussions of accepting them on present and future of the Palestine cause. However, this did not prevent the PA from accepting the proposals even if it tried to delay its clear response due to certain calculations pertaining to the Palestinian street’s rejection of them.

The PA tried to attain an Arab authorization to go ahead with the negotiations on the basis of Clinton’s proposals through tabling them at the recent meetings of the Arab follow-up committee, which emanated from the Arab League, in Cairo. However, the meeting did not give a green light to the Authority and insisted on the broad lines of Arab and Palestinian demands without passing a definite opinion accepting or refusing the proposals.

The Egyptian stand was the only one supporting a positive reaction to Clinton’s proposals while Syria and Saudi Arabia were still expressing reservations, asking the PA not to expedite an approval.

The marathon talks currently taking place on all political and security levels between the PA and the Barak government in addition to the concomitant announcement of resuming security coordination reflected a PA’s practical approval of Clinton’s proposals even if that stand was not officially declared. The current talks reflect an attempt to bargain and improve certain articles within those proposals for the sake of accepting them in future if appropriate internal circumstances were permissible (stoppage or retreat of the intifada) along with external conditions (improving the American stand with the advent of the new administration of president George Bush).

Despite the fact that no agreement was possible before end of Clinton’s term in office, the negotiations persisted with the aim of reaching an agreement before the upcoming Zionist elections to help Barak beat his opponent Sharon in the event of which the road would be open to implement that agreement. If an agreement were not reached, writing down points of concord would help both sides resume negotiations from the point that they had reached. Sharon, if he wins the election, will be forced to positively react to those understandings instead of starting new negotiations, results of which would not be favorable to the PA as the present ones.

The Palestinian people have decided and the intifada will persist

We have to point out that the Palestinian and Arab streets were against those negotiations and that the PA was standing alone and isolated in its decision that let down and weakened the intifada and resistance. Those decisions even pose as a conspiracy to abort and end the intifada and deal a blow to the Palestinian people’s national aspirations and goals.

Therefore, we greatly doubt the PA’s ability to market any political agreement with the enemy at a time when the intifada and resistance are continuing and at a time when all forms of Zionist aggression on the Palestinian people are still persisting. Furthermore, Barak could not present more than those offered in the Clinton proposals because the price would be weakening his stand in the upcoming elections. Hence, the chances of reaching an agreement between the two parties prior to the Zionist elections are slim and the issue will need

 a few more months before the negotiations resume after Bush assumes presidency and after the Zionist elections. The Palestinian people will remain resisting occupation and its repressive measures in addition to rejecting any bargaining over national rights and lands.
