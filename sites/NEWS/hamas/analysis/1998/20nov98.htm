Political Analysis - 20 November 1998

Wye Plantation Agreement: Between its Signing and its Application

The Wye Plantation agreement, signed on 23 October 1998, was signed after 18 months of Palestinian-Israeli compromise and as a result of the intense efforts by the American envoy to the Middle East in several meetings between the American Secretary of State, Arafat and Netanyahu. The agreement was also the outcome of an intensive nine-day-long series of negotiations held at Wye Plantation in Washington, D.C. With the effective participation of the American President, Bill Clinton, the agreement was reached after the Palestinians adopted the Israeli proposal concerning redeployment, also known as the "10+3" plan. This proposal was also adopted by the Americans after Netanyahu's cabinet rejected the initial American proposal of a 13% redeployment of the occupied West Bank. Consequently, the negotiations at Wye Plantation concentrated mainly upon issues of security, in addition to other matters relating to the ‘transitional period’, such as the Palestinian airport, the seaport, the safe passages, the release of prisoners held in Israeli jails, the amendment of the Palestinian National Charter and various other economic issues.

American Pressure, Palestinian Submission and the Limited Israeli Response

When Ori Safir, one of the architects of the Oslo Agreement, and the Director General of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs for the Labour Party described the Wye agreement as an American-Palestinian agreement, he was absolutely right, especially in regards to the obvious and effectual role played by the Americans in the formulation of the terms of the security plan. These terms must first be presented to the CIA, who will receive a daily report about the security procedures carried out by the PA, who has put all its confidence in the American ability to exert pressure upon Israel to fulfil their obligations. The American Government, in particular President Clinton, is very concerned with the realization of the Wye agreement regardless of its contents. To this end, it has pushed Arafat to give into all Israeli demands, without forcing Netanyahu to give anything substantial in return. Instead of working to ensure that the agreement is tenable, the American Government has been busy with the sex scandals of their President and the election of the States’ governors. The Democratic Party itself has been busy presenting their achievements to the American public in order to guarantee another presidential term in the year 2000 for both President Clinton and Vice-President Al Gore, who participated in signing the agreement, although he has had little to do with the whole affair.

Netanyahu himself threatened to create more problems between the Israelis and the Palestinians and he had to be pushed by Clinton into accepting the proposal. Realizing that the Americans were unable to exert any real pressure upon him, Netanyahu attempted to gain even more advantages from the agreement by insisting that the CIA play a significant role in controlling the PA security systems in accordance with Israeli interests. This is because Israel cannot perform the same role, as to do so would cause considerable tension between the Israelis and the PA. Netanyahu has also succeeded in getting the American Government to promise to give Israel the right to decide the area for the third plan regarding redeployment. To this end, the American’s have signed a partial agreement and they have also endorsed a number of issues for negotiation in the later stages of the peace process.

Soon after the Wye Agreement, Israel signed a further strategic cooperation agreement with America in order to improve Israeli defense capabilities especially against attacks from nuclear weapons. The agreement also ensures that Israel will receive direct and immediate aid, if and when they are attacked by countries that are known to have nuclear capabilities, such as Iran, Syria and Iraq. This is considered to be a remarkable addition to the strategic agreement signed by Israel and the USA as a result of the Israeli-Egyptian peace treaty of 1981.

According to Netanyahu, the Wye agreement has realized most of his demands, especially in relation to security issues. The agreement was signed despite the objections of the Israeli Rightists who have accused Netanyahu of treason, just as they accused Rabin before him. However, Netanyahu has prepared for this hostility with the appointment of Sharon as the Israeli Minister of Foreign Affairs. He himself participated in the Wye Plantation negotiations and supported Netanyahu's position. However, because Sharon is from the extremist Right, it is difficult for the Israeli Rightists to object to his position regarding Wye because of his well-known hostility towards the Palestinians.

As regards the PA, by adopting the American position, which is dedicated to solving all of the problems of the transitional period by any means necessary, especially the redeployment of 13% of the West Bank land, the PA have given in to a great deal while receiving little in return. The most pressing factor for the Palestinians was the realization of some kind of achievement concerning land for the Palestinians, as this was essential to justify compliance with the Oslo Agreement, which has received sharp criticism due to its impracticality. In addition, the deteriorating position of the PA has led them to fear that Netanyahu would cancel the agreement and reclaim Palestinian territories. To this end, the PA pleaded with the USA to do everything necessary to press Netanyahu into complying with the agreement.

The Items of the Agreement

The Wye Agreement dealt with the issues of redeployment, security and the previously suspended arrangements concerning the transitional period, such as the airport, the seaport, the industrial zone, safe passage, Israeli-held prisoners, legal and economic coordination and the permanent compromise talks, as well as the so-called unilateral activities. Most of the terms of the agreement concentrated upon security issues, thus redeployment has been made dependent upon whether the PA could achieve acceptable levels of security within 12 weeks, followed by a 3-month programme of activities with the objective of eradicating all types of terrorism as well as its infrastructure. This was to be achieved through the blanket arrests of all suspects and their presentation before the courts, under the supervision of the CIA and through coordination with the Israelis. In addition, there was to be an exchange of information concerning security matters with full cooperation, a prevention of all types of agitation, the control of all the armed groups, confiscation of all arms and weapons and the control of all arms smuggling, as well as the reduction in the numbers of PA policemen. The agreement has also classified the first and second stages of redeployment, which have been consolidated into one stage with three steps or ‘pulses’, all of which depend upon the extent to which the PA can comply with the security demands. Consequently, the redeployment will transfer 12% of area C to area B, including a development of 3% of this land into a nature reserve; then 15.2% of areas C and B will be transferred to area A so that eventually the PA will have complete control over 18.2%. In area A, 21.8% will also be controlled by the PA with the exception of security; in area B, 60% will be totally controlled by Israel-C. This should all take place within the first three weeks, and thus in reality the PA will control less than 20% of the West Bank, which is approximately 4% of Palestine. It is noticeable that Israel has not presented any charts for the redeployment plan, rather it seems that Israel is keeping this a secret for their own goals and interests. These charts were presented to the Israeli Cabinet for approval, and the Cabinet has insisted that the realization of each ‘pulse’ of the agreement is conditional on receiving the prior consent of the Cabinet, and that all the charts must be considered and studied by a special committee of Ministers.

With regards to the third stage of the redeployment, the Wye agreement suggested that an Israeli-American committee should be formed to discuss this matter in the light of the letter presented by Warren Christopher, the American ex-Secretary of State, enclosed in the Hebron Agreement, which granted to Israel the right to determine the area of redeployment. The meeting held by the Israeli Cabinet, which approved the Wye Plantation agreement, was evidence of this fact when the third stage of redeployment was determined for only a 1% area of land. The inauguration of the northern pass, which would connect Gaza with the northern part of the West Bank, was postponed and is to be dealt with during later negotiations, which will also include discussion of the seaport, and the economic and legal issues. Little was said on the issue of Israeli prisoners although there was an oral agreement to release 750 prisoners over a period of time, unless they have been involved in operations against the Israelis, or if they belong to the organizations of HAMAS or Al-Jihad Al-Islami.

According to the Wye agreement, the PA is required to permanently amend the Palestinian Charter through a series of sessions held by the Executive Committee and the Central Council, which must eventually be approved by both the PLO and the PA. On the other hand, the agreement does not oblige Israel to stop building new settlements or to stop expanding the existing ones. The most that has been said, in some letters sent by America to both Israel and the PA, is that there should be a cessation of unilateral procedures, indicating that the U.S. does not approve of either a unilateral declaration of an independent Palestinian State or the building of new settlements by the Israelis. It has also been decided that the permanent compromise negotiations should begin as soon as possible, even before the transitional compromise negotiations have been resolved. This has been the objective of Netanyahu even though it is something that is comprehensively rejected by the PA, as it is believed that it will destabilize the situation and thus make the PA less powerful.

Any study of the Wye agreement shows that it is in reality a security agreement for the benefit of Israel, and this can also be seen through the reactions of Israel, the PA, the Arabs and the world in general.

1. The Wye agreement is not a new agreement but rather it is a continuation of the Oslo Agreement. However, it can be considered as a basis for security procedures, agreement upon which has come to control the success of any agreements between the Israelis and the PA, and which the PA itself has little control over. Security has always been the core of the Oslo Agreement, although until the Wye agreement it was seen as a general issue, whereas now it is given much more importance, being supervised by the CIA through bilateral Israeli-Palestinian coordination as well as tri-lateral Israeli-Palestinian-American coordination.

2. The Wye agreement also suggested that there should be further meetings regarding what has been approved at Wye, because although it does not include any new issues, it has changed some matters relating to the area of redeployment, including the development of a nature reserve, acceptance of the CIA as a partner to supervise the PA’s efforts regarding security pledges and a new phase of voting with the objective of abolishing the National Palestinian Charter.

3. The Wye agreement did not cover all issues despite the wishes of the PA. It contains nothing regarding the third stage of redeployment, while other issues have been postponed, such as the seaport, the northern safe passage and the civil, legal and economic issues.

4. The agreement went above and beyond the main reason for the meeting at Wye Plantation, which had initially been the delays to the peace process caused by the continuous policy of the Israelis regarding the building of new settlements and the expansion of existing ones, such as at Abu Ghneim. However, Palestinian objections to such a policy ended in a remarkable bid to resume the negotiations, which can be seen as yet another victory for Netanyahu and his determination to suppress the existence of Palestinians.

5. Starting negotiations for the final solution before issues relating to the transitional phase indicate that these issues will be delayed, which will consequently have negative results for the Palestinians, while the Israelis will continue to insist upon the resolution of current issues and the continued postponement of the delayed issues. This will make the PA’s position weaker and even more fragile.

6. The Wye agreement shows that Netanyahu is attempting to put an end to Israeli isolation by appearing to be keen to realize a permanent compromise with the Palestinians, while at the same time refusing to give into any of the PA demands, except regarding the issue of redeployment, to which they have attached unbearable conditions, which the PA may not be able to fulfil and which will consequently become a direct obstacle to the peace process. Generally speaking, Clinton has benefited a great deal from the signing of the Wye agreement because it has deflected attention from his domestic and personal issues. In reality, the only loser is the PA because it has given in on so many issues while at the same time it has taken on heavy responsibilities relating to the success of the agreement. This will inevitably threaten the cohesion of Palestinian unity and may cause other Arab countries to cease their support and the payment of subsidies to the PLO due to the negative responses of some Arab countries to the agreement.

7. In addition, the dignity of the PA has been undermined through the allowing of the CIA to supervise the security plans of the PA, which indicates that the PA is seen as being solely responsible for any security infringements while Israel remains blameless.

Doubts concerning the application of the Wye agreement

The resistance organizations will almost certainly continue in their campaigns against the current peace process because the agreement is no different from previous agreements, especially regarding the submissive position of the PA and the focus on security issues. The Wye agreement was signed as a result of American pressure on both the Israelis and the PA. Even so, Netanyahu has tried to evade implementation of some of the aspects of the agreement by emphasizing the failure of the PA to fulfil their undertakings regarding the security issues, which indeed seem impossible for the PA to carry out successfully. All of these matters cast several doubts upon the viability of the agreement as well as its practicality, primarily because of the stance taken by Netanyahu as well as the reaction of the Israeli Cabinet to the agreement. The new additions to the Wye agreement, such as the approval of the National Council with a majority of two-thirds of the votes, realizing each stage of redeployment separately, and building more settlements such as at Abu Ghneim, along with the exclusion to date of matters concerning Jerusalem, have in reality added more complications than it has solved.

The most dangerous item of the agreement is the deprivation from the Palestinians of their only source of power, which lies in resistance to the Israelis, after the end of the Intifadah and the waning support from the former Soviet Union since the early 1990s. This deprivation of the right to resistance on the one hand removes the negative effects of any such resistance for the political elite in Israel who have been competing to claim success in this domain and hence win the support of its frightened people. While on the other hand it deprives the Palestinians of any authority or the ability to achieve a so-called "balance of terror", and instead forces the Palestinians to bow down to Israeli demands.

Both the PA and the Israeli forces have failed to suppress the resistance even with the cooperation of the U.S. as at Sharm Al-Sheikh. The resistance will continue and if the PA continues to pressure these groups, it will lead to a fierce civil war which can only benefit Israel. Finally, the position of the resistance organizations is stronger than before because of the objections of some of the influential Arab countries such as Syria, Egypt and Saudi Arabia. The growing objections to the peace process on both Arab and Islamic levels and Netanyahu's continued obstinacy has so far rendered the process a failure and made the resistance movements even more legitimate and urgent than before.
