Political Analysis - 14 October 1998

The Declaration of the Palestinian State:
New Bargaining Chip Used by the PA

As the span of five years during which time the Oslo agreement was to be realized comes to a close, most of the issues contained therein have not been achieved, especially the redeployment. The obstinate Israeli policy regarding this issue has forced Yasser Arafat to announce through the mass media that he will soon declare the independent Palestinian State on 4 May 1999, regardless of the outcome of the negotiations with Netanyahu’s Cabinet. According to the current events and conditions of the PA after four years, we can question these threats, which contradict what is going on now about the re-deployment and the deteriorating situation of the PA. Yasser Arafat’s frantic reaction was precipitated by Netanyahu’s rejection of the American proposal of the redeployment from 13% of the West Bank, by the failure of the efforts exerted by American envoy Ross to persuade Netanyahu to adopt the proposal, and by the fact that the PA has finally discovered that Israel will not compromise its attitude toward imposing harsh provisions on the PA in order for it to accept the establishment of a nature reserve in 3% of the redeployment area where Palestinians will not be allowed to build houses. All of this has been accompanied by the popular rejection and indignation amongst Palestinians, who have been frustrated because of the overall failure of the compromise. The situation has become worse because of the PA security procedures, the rampant corruption and favouritism (which Arafat himself is incapable of stopping because the involved persons have connections with Israel and they rival Arafat for his post). Consequently, Arafat was pushed to declare the independent State to hide the obvious failure of the agreement and to busy Palestinians with minor issues. The most important reason for these threats by Arafat was to make Netanyahu adopt the American proposals. However, Arafat has gone back to his initial threats since the USA forced him to adopt the American-Israeli-Palestinian compromise and to carry out these proposals AFTER Netanyahu adopted them with certain amendments. These amendments include establishing a nature reserve on 3% of the redeployment area and the rejection of the security protocol of the Israeli procedures concerning terrorism, provided the PA prevent the resistance operations from their side. The only thing Arafat said in his speech to the United Nations General Assembly was that the Palestinians are anticipating their independent State, which should be established according to their right to self-determination. His threatening tone became milder as the summit conference consisting of the Americans, the Israelis and the Palestinians would be held by 15 October 1998. Moreover, he announced during his visit to Norway that the Oslo agreement entitled him to declare the independent State just to avoid the direct threats and to make this issue based on the agreement, which is considered an obvious regression.

Some of the PA departments have promoted this declaration and considered it an attempt to fill the legal vacuum at the end of the third episode of the transitional period and to prevent extending this period without any restrictions on Israel in order to terminate the compromise. However, Ahmed Qurei’ announced that he has no objection to postponing the final solution for a few months provided the Israelis are more lenient in the current negotiations. This emphasizes the fact that this State will not be declared without the approval of Israel and only in accordance with its provisions. Furthermore, it confirms the current situation, leaving Palestinian land scattered and the PA unable to control these territories, because Israel will be controlling the water resources, the borders and the highways that connect these territories.

Theoretically, the PA can declare this State, but Israel will entirely control the Palestinians, and can abort any political reaction that would be taken by the Palestinians against the security, economic or political interests of Israel. The Labour Party rejected this State using their "veto" power because it will not be armed and powerful so it must be called an "autonomy" not an independent State, which would open the channels of normalization with the neighbouring Arab and Islamic countries and make Israel more active in the economic, political and security domains in the region under the umbrella of the new Middle East order, as well as having an axial role in the region. Arafat would have got this concession if the Labour Party had still been ruling, regardless of all other considerations; however, this will never be accepted by the Likud Party because of its security considerations. Netanyahu emphasized this notion when he was being interviewed by Sham’oun Shever, the Israeli journalist from Yed’iot Ahrenot on 18 September 1998 when he said, "The Palestinian State can be compared to a black box in which everything can enter such as Stinger missile batteries, which will make the Israeli air power ineffectual and is considered a strategic threat, not a tactical one, because the Palestinians can fire at the Israeli airplanes at Ben Gurion Airport. They will also have hideouts, missile launchers and other forces. Besides, they can be allied with powerful countries such as Iran and Iraq so the Iraqi and Iranian troops will not be far away from Tel Aviv. There is great fear that HAMAS may be able to overthrow Arafat and control this Palestinian State so we must preempt any potential ally for Iran and Iraq from becoming powerful in Palestine by all means."

This is an exaggeration because these fears are present at all times and they can be avoided through the agreements that make Israel superior in security issues. However, these fears become of greatest priority to those who do not adopt peace and its benefits for the Hebraic State, and over-emphasize security issues. Despite the conflict between the Labour Party and the Likud about the authorities which will be given to this State, Netanyahu does not ignore the formalities and he has not been fully convinced with the Oslo Agreement and its requirements. Netanyahu’s threats to annex the Palestinian land and abolish the Oslo Agreement have become more serious in the light of grasping his own attitude toward the agreement and his rejection of any attempt by Arafat to declare the Palestinian State in a feeble effort to force Netanyahu to fulfill the suspended items of the compromise.

This also includes the security news released by the Israeli army that everything is ready to recapture the PA territories in case the State is declared, although this notion is greatly doubted because of the essential role of the PA on behalf of Israel. Thus, the current conditions have continued to deteriorate since the declaration of Palestinian independence ten years ago (15 November 1988) and after it had lost the Russian support as well. As a result of the foregoing, the Palestinian headquarters has abandoned its most influential methods-the intifadah and the military struggle.

Consequently, the PA has been prone to all Israeli threats and so any declaration of a State will be equivalent to throwing ashes in the eyes and will establish a State that is entirely controlled by Israel. Despite the widespread and popular desire to obtain real independence, the end result has only amounted to a virtual teasing of the people in an effort to conceal an evidently miserable situation. It is remarkable that the proposed declaration has not been presented as a well-prepared policy of the PA or a comprehensive national plan to capture the land which requires all Palestinians to go back to resistance but rather it was just a political tactic to force Israel to fulfill its obligations toward the PA.. As a result, the threats disappeared when Israel agreed to do so. Moreover, these threats indicate that the PA is still adhering to the worn out agreements and insisting on ignoring the real expectations and anticipations of the Palestinian people. All of these threats have just been political and informational tactics to make the PA continue ruling the Palestinians against their will, interests and goals. The entirely independent State is required by all Palestinian nationals, which will never be realized through an aborted agreement that has failed in realizing even a few of these demands, so how would it enable the Palestinians to restore their homeland and get rid of the hegemony of Israel in all aspects of their lives? Finally, these threats will not work out unless the PA is capable of shouldering the consequences of this declaration and snatch it from Israel by force and through resistance. However, this will not be done by the PA given its virtual submission to all of the security demands of Israel instead of adhering and sticking to its popular and national background to confront the Israeli plans and stop them as well.
