Political Analysis - 17 September 1998

The Success (or Not) of the Oslo Agreement

We cannot ignore the series of failures that have defined the aftermath of the Oslo agreement, signed in 1993. Nevertheless, any logical assessment requires us to consider some vital remarks that may be helpful in understanding the future of the agreement and the Arab-Israeli conflict, particularly on the Palestinian level.

The First Remark: The fruits of the agreement have not been the same for both sides. The PA has received only a very limited autonomy. They are dependent on Israel while situated on a piece of land that is less than 8% of the West Bank and even lesser still than the total area of Palestine. This has scattered Palestinian land and likened it to islands in an ocean of Israeli colonies with hegemony over the natural resources. Economically, the employment rate is between 40%-50% among the Palestinians depending on the Israeli procedures whereas it was around 30% before the agreement. The income per capita has also gone down 30%. On the security level, the agreement has laid more restrictions and persecution on the Palestinians enforced by the PA, who were responsible for the murder of 20 people while in custody of PA soldiers as well as 14 more individuals who were shot down by PA policemen during demonstrations on 18 November 1994. Moreover, the PA has been carrying out arrest campaigns against HAMAS activists and disciples as well as other resistance formations just one month after Yasser Arafat entered his “kingdom”. The most severe campaign was on 25 February 1996, which lasted six months, during which time period more than 2,000 HAMAS activists were arrested while protesting the murder of Yahya Ayyash. The PA detention centres are still holding more than 200 active members without allowing their cases to be presented in court in order to harm the infrastructure of HAMAS and exhaust it in the long run.

Furthermore, more than 3,000 are still detained in Israeli prisons. However, a few were released as part of bargains between the PA and Rabin’s ineffectual cabinet, but most of the released were either about to finish their terms of imprisonment or had not been political detainees in return for which Israel obtained a lot of political yields from the PA. Consequently, the PA failed to realize security for the Palestinians, particularly since several individuals, namely Kamal Kaheel, Yahya Ayyash, Muhiyedeen Al Sharif, and Emad and Adel Awadallah, have been assassinated by the Israeli intelligence systems in the PA region, indicating that the PA has been collaborating with these systems.

The PA is the product of the corrupt Israeli occupation and its neighbouring systems, which in turn has led to a multitude of mal-administration and moral and financial corruption within the PA. The most recent examples of its systematic and moral corruption include fashion show exhibitions, nightclubs and gambling casinos in Jericho, which caused a great shock to the infrastructure of the resistance and left the door wide open for such corruption to affect the Palestinians.

On the Israeli side, there have been a lot of positive achievements as a result of the Oslo agreement, including the attraction of a lot of foreign investment during the reign of the Labour Party, the recognition of Israel as a State by a lot of Arab and Islamic countries, and the close cooperation with these countries at the political and economic levels. Instead of shouldering the financial responsibilities toward the Palestinians such as health, education and municipal services, Israel has transferred these duties to the PA but kept the security issues in hand throughout Palestine. This has been clearly evident over the past five years. In addition, the Jewish State has been able to force the PA into keeping the security for the benefit of Israel at the expense of the Palestinians, who have been suffering from insecurity because of the Israeli procedures carried out by its agents in the PA regions in the same way, but sometimes more brutal, than the Israelis. Furthermore, they attempted to diminish the role of the resistance at the popular as well as the military level by creating a civil war amongst all parties rejecting the agreement by simultaneously undermining these forces, especially HAMAS, and considering them as targets in the security issues. Moreover, they have dealt with the debate tactically to serve their own interests, and not to prepare the Palestinians to repel the Israeli attacks or even to solve their domestic problems.

The Second Remark: The agreement, which has been aborted ever since it was signed, has been used as the terms of reference for all Arab and Western States when dealing with the Arab-Israeli conflict. It is also considered as the means to an end of this crisis and will lead to peaceful co-existence. Therefore, the problem lies with the application of the agreement items, not the Israeli occupation and the continuous suffering of the Palestinians. On the other hand, the agreement has excluded the Palestinian Cause from its Arab and Islamic domain and has limited it to a conflict between Palestinians and Israelis. As a result, it considers the conflict a humane issue, the resolution of which would reduce the suffering of the Palestinians but not restore their homeland; this attitude was welcomed by all defeated Arabs, especially after the second Gulf war. The agreement had been pre-arranged at the time when most Arab leaders met in Sharm Al-Sheikh in 1996 to sign the treaty against terrorism and wipe out all the motives of Jehad (holy war) among Muslims.

The Third Remark: The agreement was also spoiled by those who rejected it in Israel after they had taken over authority by limiting it to the Hebron agreement. They have not fulfilled their commitments toward the PA’s ineffectual system. This attitude has revealed the shortcomings of the agreement and diminished the great expectations of the PA.

The big question is: Has the agreement failed or been disclosed?

The assessment of the agreement should be based on its purposes because it was not designed to restore the rights of the Palestinians-although the PA idols thought this was the case-but rather its aim was to isolate the Palestinian Cause from its Islamic/Arab domain and make it quite a domestic issue based on the security of Israel as viewed by Zionism. Consequently, we can consider the agreement as a severe catastrophe and disaster for the Palestinians as well as for the Arabs in general because it has failed to restore the Palestinian land and rights. On the contrary, it has established the PA as an Israeli ally. On the other hand, the agreement has been a great achievement for Israel because it ignored the occupation and put both Israel and the PA in one trench while side plans to destroy the other and the agreement have created an imbalance in some Palestinian attitudes about the conflict, although the attitudes of the extreme Rightists in Israel made the Palestinians, Arabs and Muslims recognize this bitter fact given the provocative nature of the procedures used by the Israelis.

Finally, the future of the agreement does not depend on its success or failure as has been propagated by some Palestinians. These misguided individuals have adopted the agreement because it has secreted a gang that is using its terms of reference for their own interests and to satisfy their lust for authority. This gang must be removed and isolated by both Palestinians and Arabs because this group will not learn from the long history of our struggle and experiences with Israel. All in all, the agreement has failed entirely because it did not lay a well-established base to solve the problem, and it has been designed to suit the American-Israeli strategies and their Arab allies in the region who support the agreement for the benefit of Israel and not for Palestine.
