23 October 1999

The Wye River agreement:
A prelude to the liquidation of the Palestinian Question

The Wye-2 agreement signed at Sharm el-Sheikh of Egypt on 5 September 1999 between the Israeli government and the PLO was considered to be the practical approach to found a base to the final resolution of the Palestinian Question. This was proven through linking the third and last phase of the agreement with the agreement of principles on the final solution, which is supposed to be reached in February 2000. The Zionist Prime Minister Ehud Barak succeeded in implementing some of the provisions of the Wye River Memorandum signed by his predecessor Netanyahu as his approach to play the role of a man of peace before the region and the World. He also wanted to show that Israel was serious in implementing the agreements. However, he achieved the philosophy of his spiritual father—Rabin—that is dates are not sacred in agreements. Barak used this as a way to make Israel politically and economically open to the Arab and Muslim worlds, and in pressuring Jordan and the Palestinian Authority to escalate their measures against Hamas in order to weaken it.

A deliberate study of the contents of the Wye River agreements, both the original and the amended one at Sharm el-Sheikh, proves that they lead to the physical separation between the Palestinian People and the Jewish settlers in the West Bank. But they do not achieve any real political or geographical independence for the Palestinians. For the redeployment will eventually conclude with only 5.4% of the total area of the West Bank under the Palestinian Authority’s total control and 29.3% under the PA’s civil control. However, about 94.6% of the West Bank will remain under the so-called Israeli security control.  

On the other hand, the Palestinian control will be on separated and isolated pockets of the West Bank surrounded by Jewish settlements and their bypass roads, which gives the upper hand to the Israeli Occupation authorities. Meanwhile, Israel will officially annex the Jewish settlements and concentrations, which amount to more than 60% of the West Bank. These settlements are well connected, covering the West Bank from north to south and from west to east; and suffocating the Palestinian populated areas. The Palestinians’ movement and mobility will be hindered by this situation. The Palestinians will also face difficulties in using the supplies of the underground water.

The Wye River agreement makes the so-called “safe passage” between the West Bank and the Gaza Strip under total Israeli control. The external communication of the West Bank and Gaza with the external world and their borders and ports all will remain under Israeli security. Moreover, the Occupation troops will not withdraw from any part of Occupied Jerusalem.

The final status talks are based, according to Israeli understanding of the Wye River memo, on these facts: Palestinian separated cantons, and complete Israeli control over the communication between the West Bank and Gaza as well as over the communication of each with the external world. Therefore, Barak can consider the declaration of the Palestinian State under these conditions and circumstances. He can also “invent” a new Jerusalem in any neighbouring Palestinian village to be made as a capital of the Palestinian State in accordance with Beilen-Abu Mazen agreement. Therefore, we can say that Barak succeeded, during his first three months in office, to establish and enhance the Israeli vision of the final resolution of the Palestinian Question, a thing that Netanyahu failed to achieve in three years.

On the other hand, the red lines set by Barak concerning the final solution which deny the right of return to Palestinian refugees and displaced persons, and which reject the complete military withdrawal from the Occupied Territories in accordance with United Nations resolution 242. These red lines also deny the Arab and Muslim right to Jerusalem, to Palestinians’ water sources, and to their right to have an independent State with real sovereignty, as well as to their right to live on their land like other people in the world. Barak wants to make sure the Palestinian People remain paralyzed and weak in the security, military and economic fields. He also wants them to decrease their population, to have poor natural resources, and to remain isolated from the external world except through the Israeli gate. This leads to the liquidation not the resolution of the Palestinian Cause. Therefore, Israel denies the human and political rights of the Palestinian People and it does not recognize or realize them.

Any Palestinian involvement in the final status talks based on the Wye River agreement and on Barak’s set of “Nos” will be considered a complete submission and abandonment of our rights in favour of Israel while the Zionist regime works to liquidate and weaken the Palestinian opposition and resistance led by Hamas. Dependence on the muscles of the Palestinian negotiator and his eloquence and on the “good” intentions of the Israeli Labour government is the first step towards defeat and surrender in these talks.

The experience with the Zionist movement and Israel over the last few decades proved that the physical and economic attrition of the Zionist regime and the resistance of the occupation are the most effective way to force Israel to give “relinquishments”.

  Today, Hamas is leading this resistance, which makes the crackdown on Hamas a service in favour of the Zionist plan and which weakens the Palestinian position in the final status negotiations.
