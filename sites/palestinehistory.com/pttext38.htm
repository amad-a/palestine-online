1954 - Nasser Takes Charges

For almost two years, Colonel Gamal Abdal Nasser had quietly directed Egypt's revolution from above, while General Muhammad Naguib served as president and prime minister.

February 1954, the colonel stepped to the fore. Citing Naguib's ties to the banned Muslim Brotherhood and his intention to restore the old system of government, Nasser forced him to resign. In April, Nasser took over the premiership.
