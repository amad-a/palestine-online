Day 21 : Wendesday 18.10.2000

Large marches took place in Palestinian towns in the West Bank and Gaza Strip , protesting Sharm el-Sheikh agreement which makes most Palestinians feel deeply disappointed. As a results of the demonstrations , clashes with Israeli occupation soldiers continued in many parts of the West Bank and Gaza Strip and the Israeli occupation soldiers continued to open fire at unarmed Palestinian demonstrators. Over a hundred Palestinian injures were reported .

In Deir al-Balah, near the Jewish settlement of Kfar Darom, in the Gaza Strip, Palestinian demonstrates throw stones in the direction of Israeli soldiers and the soldiers returned by live bullets and rubber coated steel bullets . (Photo 1) at least 15 Palestinians protesters were injured by Israeli soldiers fire.

In Gaza city , hundreds of Palestinian storm the streets in a huge funeral of a Palestinian policemen Nabel Kater, who was killed during clashes near Erez, the crossing point between Palestinian-ruled Gaza and Israel in the Gaza Strip . (Photo 2)

Thousands of Palestinians took to the streets on the West Bank voicing their rejection of the Sharm el-Sheikh agreement and vowing to keep up the Aqsa Intifada until the Palestinian people regain freedom and liberation from the Jewish occupation.

In the West Bank city of Ramallah , Israeli soldiers fired live and rubber coated steel bullets against the Palestinians demonstrators causing number of injuries.

Giorgio Giacomelli A U.N. investigator mandated by the U.N. Commission on Human Rights to the Palestinian territories reported the scale of Palestinian deaths at the hands of Israeli forces during a wave of violence over the past 20 days was " unprecedented ." He also expressed concern about what he called Israeli settler " paramilitaries " who he said were responsible for at least five Palestinian deaths in the West Bank.

" In some sense the scale of this violation is unprecedented. It is worthy of note that the number of deaths caused by Israeli forces so far approximate the number killed in the first four months of the intifada, in 1987-88" Giacomelli said in his seven-page report issued in Geneva. The report also said that Israeli forces " appear to have indiscriminately used excessive force in cases where there was no imminent threat to their lives. "

The report add that about 40 percent of an estimated 2,000 to 3,700 Palestinians wounded by Israeli occupation forces were under age 18 and that at least half of the injuries resulted from the use of live ammunition.

" Presently, the Israeli settler population has emerged as an increasingly obvious source of paramilitary activity. Numerous reports indicate that Israeli occupation forces have not acted to deter such paramilitary activities " Giacomelli report said.

According to the report, Israel, refused to cooperate with the U.N. investigator .
