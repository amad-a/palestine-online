Day 107-113 : From 12.01.2001 To 18.01.2001

Day 107 : Friday 12.01.2001

Israeli soldiers drag the body of Shaker Hassouni away, after he was shot dead by Israeli forces in the divided West Bank city of Hebron. Israeli soldiers shot and wounded five other Palestinians in the Hebron clash with rubber-coated metal bullets.


Day 108 : Satarday 13.01.2001

A Palestinian hurls a burning tire towards Israeli soldiers during clashes in the divided West Bank city of Hebron.


Day 109 : Sunday 14.01.2001

A group of Palestinian march during the funeral procession of Shaker Hassouni (22 years old), in the divided West Bank city of Hebron. Hassouni was shot dead last Friday by Israeli soldiers who had entered a Palestinian-controlled area of the city.


Day 110 : Monday 15.01.2001

Israeli soldier run past a large fire burning in the agricultural area of Kfar Yam in the Gush Katif Jewish settlement bloc in the Gaza Strip. The fire was set by Jewish settlers enraged after the killing of a Jew settler who was abducted and killed by Palestinians last night.


Day 111 : Tuesday 16.01.2001

The body of Madi Ishtayeh is escorted by Palestinian during his funeral procession in the West Bank village of Kfar Salem near Nablus. Madi was killed during by Israeli soldiers Monday.


Day 112 : Wednesday 17.01.2001

Palestinians try to make their way over a mound of dirt on a main road near the West Bank town of Hebron, set up by the Israeli army to keep Palestinian vehicles from entering Israeli-controlled areas.


Day 113 : Thursday 18.01.2001

Masked Palestinian demonstrators burn an effigy of Canadian Foreign Minister John Manley during a rally in the West Bank city of Nablus. The demonstrators were reacting to an interview in which Manley said Canada has offered to accept Palestinian refugees as part of a Middle East peace plan proposed by the United States. The U.S. plan would involve Israeli withdrawl from the West Bank and Gaza and a Palestinian renunciation of the right of millions of refugess to return to their old homes in Israel.
