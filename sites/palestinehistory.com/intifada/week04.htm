Day 86-92 : From 22.12.2000 To 28.12.2000

Day 86 : Friday 22.12.2000

A Palestinian youth strapped with explosives blew himself at an Israeli restaurant frequented by Israeli soldiers north of Jericho, killing himself and injuring ten Zionist, four seriously.

Palestinians in the West Bank and Gaza strip today buried four martyrs killed by occupation troops. The four are; Najib Ibedo (17 years old) from the village of Sa'ir near Hebron, Arafat Jabarin from the village of Sa'ir north east of Hebron, Nidal Oun from Khan Younis, The fourth Palestinian is the martyr who carried out the martyrdom operation at a restaurant frequented by occupation soldiers north of Jericho, his identity has not been disclosed.


Day 87 : Saturday 23.12.2000

Day 88 : Sunday 24.12.2000

Occupation troops raided the village of Asira el-Shamaliya near Nablus, attacking residential homes, vandalizing property and beating local residents. Fourteen local villagers were arrested for anti-Israeli activities and resistance.


Day 89 : Monday 25.12.2000

Israeli occupation army is still withholding the body of Najib Ibedo (22 years old) who was murdered by Jewish settlers near the settlement of Haggay, on Hebron’s southern outskirts. Israeli army told the bereaved family that it will hand over the body for burial only if the family undertake not to sue the Jewish settlers for murder.

Israeli occupation authorities uprooted over 200 fully-grown olive trees near the Palestinian town of Nablus. Israeli army bulldozers extirpated the trees at the two villages of Salim and Jama’ain.


Day 90 : Tuesday 26.12.2000

An opinion survey in Israel has shown that a majority of Israeli Jews are opposed to a peace deal with the Palestinian Authority based on the recent American proposals. The Israeli paper Yedeot Ahranot has shown that 48% of the respondents, who represent a random sample of the adult population, were opposed to "ceding sovereignty in Jerusalem to the Palestinians in the context of an agreement with the PA." Around 43% said they would support such an arrangement, while 9% expressed no opinion.

In response to a question on the proposed transfer of Arab neighborhoods in East Jerusalem to the Palestinians, 60% of the respondents said they supported the idea, while 39% per cent said they were opposed to it.

A staggering 72% of the respondents said they were firmly opposed to the repatriation of even a small number of Palestinian refugees to their historical homeland in what is now Israel. 27% of the respondents said they would support such an idea.
