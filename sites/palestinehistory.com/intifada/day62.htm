Day 62 : Tuesday 28.11.2000

One Palestinian was killed by Israeli occupation soldiers on in the West Bank. Ibrahim Abed Kahla (24 years old) from the village of Ramon, died of coronary arrest when Israeli occupation soldiers chased and fired on a group of Palestinian youths who were throwing stones at the Israeli occupiers.

Two other Palestinians died of wounds they had sustained earlier in confrontations with the Israeli occupiers. Karam al Kurd (14 years old) from gaza and Imad al Dayeh (17 years old) from the Shati’ refugee camp in Gaza.

Palestinian human rights watch group has declared that Israel was using ammunition containing depleted uranium against Palestinian civilian targets. Depleted uranium was used to add more penetration power to the shells. The material leads to serious health hazards to both the military and civilians topped by chemical poisoning and radiation dangers.

The Israeli army attacked with tear gas canisters the Zahra’ school, causing several children to faint as a result of gas inhalation. Around noon, the Israeli troops opened fire on school children at the El-Ram school.

A Palestinian Authority official has rejected recent Israeli and American proposals for a new interim agreement styled after the Oslo Accords.

In Hebron, the occupation army re-imposed curfew on the estimated 40,000 Palestinians living in the old town where nearly 300 fanatic settlers decide the tune for the entire city, under the protection of the occupation army.

The Israeli occupation army has sealed most of the villages and hamlets in the northern parts of the West Bank preventing inhabitants from bringing in food supplies from the main large towns.
