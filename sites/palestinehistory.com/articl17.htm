Open Letter to Mr. Jamal Al-Durra

Dear Mr. Al-Durra;

Please accept my sincere and profound sorrow at the death of your son, Mohammed. I know that my words are small compared to the terrible grief you and your family are feeling. It is my own shock and sorrow that compel me to reach out to you with my words—they are all the comfort I have to offer.

I am also the parent of a 12-year-old boy. He even looks a little like Mohammed. I know you loved, and continue to love, your son, just as I love mine. There is no way to measure the joy we feel in our children, nor the grief we endure at their loss. When I watched you and your son against that wall, with no safe place to go, and then saw the terrible outcome, I wept and pleaded with God to help us all.

Words are inadequate to express to you what I felt and continue to feel. But I can report to you that one small good thing has happened as a result. After days of sorrow and tears, I felt moved to take action. I could not watch a child get shot down in the street, even though he was thousands of miles away from me, and then go on with my life as if nothing had happened! I began to look around on the internet, to read the news from all points of view. I searched out sources for the history of the current conflict, and educated myself beyond what small information is offered in the news. I felt I owed it to little Mohammed, who so reminded me of my own boy, to find out as much of the true story as I could. I am still doing that, daily.

I have found out a lot that I was quite unaware of before. Now I know about injustices done to Palestinian people under occupation, about prejudice and humiliation they have suffered. Now I know about the breaking of international law by Israeli Defense Forces. (Anyone who doubts this needs to look at the report from Amnesty International at http://web.amnesty.org/ai.nsf/Index/MDE150412000?OpenDocument&of=COUNTRIES\ISRAEL. Now I also know that there are people of good conscience on both sides working for justice, dialogue and peace. For instance, I found on the internet Jewish Israeli groups who recognize the injustices, who abhor the way Palestinians have been treated, and who disagree with the Israeli government’s policies. None of this information has found its way into U.S. news reports, that I have seen.

As a result, I have dedicated my web site to your son, and I have filled it with all the information I have found, in the hope that I can help educate others about the whole truth in the Middle East. I hope you will be able to look at my site sometime, and read the tribute I wrote to Mohammed. http://www.pond.net/~myrrh/index.htm

I was glad when I heard that you are going to take the case of your son’s death to international courts. I encourage you to do so. Mr. Al-Durra, you and your family are in my thoughts and prayers. I hope for your speedy recovery and the continued health and wellbeing of the rest of your family. I will never forget your son Mohammed Jamal, and will work for peace and justice in his name.

Sincerely,
Myrrh Sagrada
Oregon, USA
