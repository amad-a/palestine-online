Sharon launches all-out war on the Palestinian people

Occupied Jerusalem – From Khalid Amayreh

Israeli Prime Minister and certified war criminal Ariel Sharon has apparently decided to launch an all-out war on the Palestinian intifada or uprising, now entering its seventh month.

On 30 March, and while Palestinians were commemorating the 25th anniversary of “Land Day”, which symbolizes the Palestinians’ clinging to their land in the face of unceasing Jewish efforts to wrest it from their hands, Sharon ordered his occupation troops throughout the West Bank to resume the “shoot-to-kill policy” against Palestinian protesters.

Consequently, the Israeli army, using high-velocity bullets and nerve-agent gases, which cause severe spasmodic reactions and often lead to an agonizing death, killed at least six Palestinian youths and minors.

The bloody suppression of Palestinian protests was especially ferocious in three Palestinian towns: Nablus, Ramallah and Hebron.

In Nablus, at least 5 young demonstrators were killed and over 50 injured when apparently trigger-happy Israeli troops stationed on hilltops overlooking the West Bank’s largest town opened fire on thousands of Palestinians who were marching to protest continued Israeli torment and occupation.

The shelling also targeted civilian neighbourhoods inside Nablus itself, including the Amman and Jerusalem neighbourhoods as well as downtown.

Of those killed, four were reportedly hit by gunfire in the head and neck, apparently by lethal high-velocity ammunition.

The martyrs are: Ayesh Zamel, 19, of the village of Deir al-Hatab village, near Nablus, who was hit by a bullet in his left eye, going through his brain and exited at the back of his head; Ahmad Marahil, a 16-year-old high school student, who was killed while walking on al-Qods street, by a bullet in the neck, Murad Saleh Sharay’a, 20, and Sha’aban Salloum, all three of whom are from the refugee camp Balata outside Nablus.

According to hospital sources, the impact of the high-velocity bullets was such that Murad Saleh Sharay’a’s head was smashed to pieces while Sha’aban Salloum had his head blown off.

Khalid Abu Nahleh, 28, from the El-Ein refugee camp succumbed to his wounds in hospital after being hit by gunfire in the back of his waist.

In Ramallah, when Palestinian demonstrators reached the northern entrance of the town, Israeli soldiers fired at them without warning, using live ammunition and rubber-coated steel bullets.

As a result, 21-year-old Muhammed al-Wawi from the Ama’ari refugee camp was killed with a sniper’s bullet in the head.

Over 20 others were injured, including Abu Ali Muqbul, 55, from Ramallah, who sustained critical wounds in the pelvis.

In Hebron, clashes between stone-hurling Palestinian youths and heavily armed Israeli occupation soldiers erupted again on 30 March along the so-called “friction line” at Bab-el-Zawiya and Shalala Street.

Hospital sources spoke of at least ten people hospitalized, including one, Ihab al-Hashlamoun, who reportedly sustained serious gunshot wounds.

In the afternoon, Israeli tanks and gunners pounded several civilian neighbourhoods with artillery shells, inflicting widespread destruction.

The ferocious shelling continued for over four hours and continued well into the night.

Meanwhile, Nazi-like Jewish settlers continued their week-old rampage against unprotected Palestinian civilians throughout the old town of Hebron.

Palestinian sources said as may as 20 stores, houses and businesses were torched by Jewish settlers, reportedly in the full view of Israeli occupation soldiers who made no attempt to stop settler vandalism.

Earlier, scores of settlers sought unsuccessfully to carry out a pogrom against the Abu Sneineh neighbourhood opposite the centre of the old town.

Palestinian defenders repulsed the settlers who retreated to their small enclave in the old town, where the Israeli occupation army brought in three tanks and an additional 3,000 troops ostensibly to launch a fresh onslaught on Palestinian civilians.

In 1948 Palestine, massive marches commemorating “Land Day” were organized throughout Arab towns and villages from Akka and Sakhnin in the north to Rahat and Beir al Sabb’a in the south.

The largest march took place in Sakhnin, where the first Land Day originated on 30 March 1976, when Israeli occupation soldiers opened fire on Palestinian peasants protesting the confiscation of their land by Israeli authorities seeking to expand Jewish settlements, killing six youths.

“The conditions that existed 30 years ago are still very much the same now; the Nazi Zionists are still trying to chase us out of our homeland. The seizure of our land has not abated a bit,” said one speaker.

Arab Knesset member Abdul Malik Dahamashe, speaking at a rally in Kufur-Kanna, stressed the unity of struggle and destiny between Palestinians at home and in the Diaspora.

“We reaffirm the umbilical attachment to the Palestinian people in Palestine and in the Diaspora,” he said.

The bloody events of “Land Day” followed an escalation of Israeli aggression against the Palestinian people on 28 March when Israeli Appache helicopter gunships struck several Palestinian targets, including power stations, in Ramallah and the Gaza Strip.

The blind bombardment resulted in the killing of three Palestinians, including a 40-year-old mother, Suad Etiwi, who was killed in Bitunya near Ramallah while seeking to move children to safety.

Earlier, on 27 March, a Palestinian boy named Mahmoud Ismael Darawish, age 11, was killed while eating supper inside his living room in the town of Dura.

The deadly bullet was apparently fired by Israeli occupation soldiers stationed on the opposite hilltops.

The Israeli media, which sought to exploit the accidental death of a Jewish toddler in Hebron a day earlier, made no mention of the killing of Darawish.

Much of the Zionist escalation of violence against the Palestinians is apparently attributed to a sort of “green light” which the Israeli war criminal and Prime Minister Sharon received from U.S. President George W. Bush during his recent visit to the United States.

On 30 March, Bush tacitly blamed the Palestinians for the “violence.”

“The signal I’m sending to the Palestinians is to stop the violence and I can’t make it anymore clear.”

Bush ignored, almost completely, Israeli aggression against the Palestinians, saying only that Israel should relax the siege on Palestinian population centres.

Since the outbreak of the Palestinian revolt against Zionist Nazism, more than 450 Palestinians, mostly children and minors, were killed, and over 15,000 were injured, many seriously.

During the same period, 73 Jewish soldiers and settlers were killed.

Sharon seeks to create more “facts” in the West Bank

Occupied Jerusalem – Special to PT

Despite claims to the contrary, the Sharon government has apparently endorsed plans to create at least one large Jewish settlement south of Jerusalem and significantly expand a number of existing settlements throughout the West Bank.

According to the Hebrew press, the Israeli Housing Ministry has already prepared plans for building a new Jewish settlement that would include 6,000 flats in an area located between Bethlehem and Hebron.

The new settlement, to be called Giva’ot, would be inhabited by Jews from the “national-religious camp,” considered among the most racist and virulently anti-Arab of Jews anywhere.

If built, the new settlement would further truncate the southern part of the West Bank, bisecting it into two main enclaves (Hebron and Bethlehem) cut off from each other and lacking territorial continuity with the Ramallah region in the central West Bank.

In addition to the planned settlement, a Jerusalem municipal committee has approved new plans to build another 3,000 apartments on confiscated Arab land at Jabal Abu Ghneim, or “Har Homa” as referred to by the Jews.

The governments of Benyamin Netanyahu and Ehud Barak had already constructed hundreds of settler units at Jabal Abu Ghneim on Jerusalem’s southern suburbs, mainly for the purpose of completing the chain of Jewish settlements around occupied East Jerusalem.

The political aim of this settlement, as was the case with other settlements, is unmistakably clear, namely to make returning the town to the Palestinians a remote or non-existent possibility.

Meanwhile, the grabbing of Palestinian land by armed Jewish settlers continued unabated throughout the West Bank.

Palestinian sources reported this week that Jewish settlers were seizing Palestinian land in the vicinity of their settlements and cordoning it off with barbed wire, with the Israeli army’s tacit approval.

In Hebron, for example, the Israeli army last week confiscated a large expanse of Palestinian farmland adjacent to the former armistice line of 1967.

The owners of the land, poor peasants from the nearby village of Beit Kahel, organized a sit-in on the land on 26 March to protest the illegal seizure. However, Israeli soldiers stationed at the site forcibly vacated the villagers, threatening to shoot the them if they didn’t disperse.

Israeli army bulldozers likewise were busy during the last week of March pulverizing Palestinian vineyards and olive orchards south of the town of Dura, 10 miles south-west of Hebron.

There the Israeli army is opening yet another bypass road that would link the tiny settlement of Otnael, inhabited by no more than 30 settler families, with inter-city roads in southern Israel more than 15 miles away.

The opening of the road, and the accompanying brutality of destroying verdant Palestinian fields on which villagers depend for their livelihood, underscores the brutal ugliness of the Zionist mentality and the extent to which the Sharon regime is willing to go to torment the Palestinians in order to appease a handful of North American immigrants who view Palestinian life as expendable.

And as the expansion of settlements continues, so do the often murderous attacks of Jewish settlers against Palestinian civilians throughout the occupied territories.

On 23 March, the bludgeoned body of 44-year-old Jabri Abu Ras of Dura was found dumped outside the Jewish settlement of Halmish north of Ramallah.

Palestinian hospital sources in Ramallah said Abu Ras, a father of five, was shot several times in the chest and head before his skull was crushed by stones.

His relatives affirmed that they had no doubt whatsoever that the settlers committed the gruesome crime.

On 16 March, settlers from the settlement of Navi Yaccoub north of Jerusalem reportedly murdered a 10-year-old Palestinian child who was playing soccer near the settlement, using very much the same gruesome method of murder.

In both cases, the Israeli occupation authorities made no arrests among the settlers nor even launched an investigation.

In fact, far from seriously investigating the two murders, an Israeli police spokesman sought to divert attention from the true culprits, claiming that the Palestinian Authority was behind the murders, an incredible claim scoffed at by Palestinians.

Apart from their murderous attacks which have come lately to assume a daily frequency, the settlers continued to carry out wanton acts of vandalism against Palestinian civilians often in the full view of Israeli army soldiers.

Earlier this week, the Israeli human rights group B’Tselem accused the Israeli army of tolerating and tacitly encouraging settler violence against innocent and unprotected Palestinians.

The report stressed that the settlers’ often wanton violence and vandalism against Palestinians and their property “occur against a background of leniency and prolonged impotence of the Israeli law-enforcement authorities.”

According to the report, the settlers’ violence took many forms, including firing at Palestinian civilian neighbourhoods, killing and wounding Palestinian civilians, firing and throwing stones at Palestinian cars, damaging property, uprooting trees, burning a mosque, harming Palestinian medical teams, attacking journalists and preventing farmers from reaching their fields.

The report mentioned two telling facts, which are known too well to the Palestinians.

First, the fact that Israeli soldiers and police officers are often present when settlers attack Palestinians but do absolutely nothing to stop or prevent the attacks. Indeed, in many cases, the report said, the soldiers respond “derisively” to Palestinian appeals for assistance against settler violence.

Second, and this is very significant, the fact that settler violence against the Palestinians is very often not investigated, with two thirds of the cases being closed, ostensibly owing to “lack of interest.”

The plague of Jewish settlers is not confined to physical violence and wanton rampage.

On 26 March, as much as 3.5 million cubic metres of partially treated sewage water originating at the Jewish settlement of Nahal Oz, east of Gaza, deluged several Arab neighbourhoods in Gaza town, causing a huge environmental disaster.

The huge torrent reportedly occurred when an Israeli water storage facility at the settlement broke down (or was deliberately broken down), causing the deluge to inundate much of the Shujaiya neighbourhood.

The flood reportedly caused incalculable damage to houses as well as to farmland and livestock in the area.

Palestinian officials put the damage and losses incurred at tens of millions of dollars.

One Palestinian official remarked on the incident, saying that the Jews have introduced a new weapon against us.

Siege continues despite Sharon’s PR

Occupied Jerusalem - From Khalid Amayreh
The stark dichotomy between Ariel Sharon’s repetitious claims about relaxing Israel’s suffocating siege on Palestinian population centres and the reality on the ground is still observed throughout the West Bank and the Gaza Strip.

Indeed, any traveller in the West Bank these days will be affronted by the fact that the nearly hermetic blockade of Palestinian towns, villages and even small hamlets is still firmly in place.

True, a slight mitigation in the severity of the siege might occasionally be observed here and there. But the basic structure of the siege remains intact and its onerous effects are ubiquitous and felt by the entire population, as the Israeli army continues to dissect the West Bank into small cantons, ghettos and closed areas, cut off from each other and virtually isolated from the rest of the world.

On Monday, 19 March, the Israeli army further tightened its blockade on Bethlehem following the killing of an Israeli settler near the settlement of Navi Danial a few miles south of the Arab town.

Israel accused the Palestinian Authority of giving safe haven to the perpetrators, vowing to retaliate harshly.

According to Israeli sources, the occupation army postponed or refrained from taking “stronger retaliatory measures” in order not to undermine Ariel Sharon’s visit to Washington.

Some Palestinians interpreted the ambush as a retaliation for the increasingly bold attacks by Jewish settlers against Palestinian civilians.

On 16 March, the Jerusalemite family and relatives of 10-year-old Muhammed Nassar, whose bludgeoned body was found in the vicinity of the Jewish settlement of Navi Yaccoub in occupied Arab East Jerusalem, accused Jewish settlers of murdering him in cold blood.

The family said the boy went to play soccer at a playground near the settlement but failed to return home.

“I don’t have the slightest doubt that they (the settlers) did it; who else would commit a gruesome crime as such?” said the boy’s uncle Amin Nassar.

The family scoffed at and vehemently denied Israeli insinuations that the boy might have been the victim of a vendetta killing by other Arabs.

“They just want to divert attention from the settlers by all means; our family has no feuds or problems with anyone; I found their insinuations to that effect disgusting,” said Nassar.

Palestinian economy on the brink of paralysis
In the Gaza Strip, the Israeli army hasn’t made things any easier for the estimated 1.3 million Palestinians, most of whom are languishing in abject poverty owing to the siege and lost jobs inside Israel.

For in addition to closing the roads and the continued truncation of the area within which the Palestinians are free to move without being targeted by Israeli bullets, the Israeli occupation army has effectively barred fuel imports to the Gaza Strip, pushing the Strip’s already ravaged survival economy to the brink of paralysis.

And to make things even worse, and despite Israeli public statements that Palestinian fishermen would be permitted to fish off the Gaza coast, Israeli navy boats on many occasions chased and fired live rounds of ammunition at the poor Gazan fishermen for violating the ban on fishing. According to Palestinian sources, a number of fishermen have been arrested and their boats were either seized or vandalized for entering “a closed military zone.”

The Draconian measures prove that recent Israeli claims and statements about relaxing the siege on the Palestinians were only public relations announcements with almost no substance and virtually no good will.

This is at least the message most Palestinians are getting.

During the third week in March, Palestinian intellectuals and artists marched through Ramallah, demanding the lifting of the siege which they contend reflects the ugly face of Israel’s racism.

The march was a novel and rather successful Palestinian experiment with non-violent and passive resistance.

The marchers, who included people of all ideological orientations, passed by the onerous Israeli roadblocks, and instead of hurling stones at the well-entrenched soldiers, they sang and chanted national anthems, glorifying freedom and denouncing apartheid and oppression of men by their fellow men.

Another non-violent march organized on Monday, 19 March, this time by the Palestinian women’s union, was harshly repressed by Israeli soldiers who fired rubber-coated bullets, tear gas shells and stun-grenades at the women activists as they sought to enter Jerusalem at the El-Ram roadblock, which the Israelis consider the border point between Jerusalem (e.g. Israeli-controlled territory) and the self-rule areas.

As many as 20 protesters were injured and hospitalized, including Palestinian law maker Hanan Ashrawi.

Two journalists were also injured, one Egyptian and the other Italian.

The harsh repression of the women activists’ march demonstrated that the Israeli government and army will not change their ill-treatment of the Palestinians, however non-violent and peaceful Palestinian protests against the Israeli occupation may become.

Indeed, the Israeli shoot-to-kill policy continued to be the standard Israeli response to Palestinian protests, resulting in the killing of an average Palestinian everyday.

Moreover, Jewish settlers seem to have been emboldened by the ascendance of Ariel Sharon to power in the Jewish State.

In addition to the reported murder of the child Nassar on Friday, Jewish settlers near Nablus attacked on 17 March two Arab cars, injuring six Palestinians, including a woman who sustained serious wounds as here car plunged into a ravine after settlers smacked a large rock on its windshield.

These and similar attacks by settlers on Palestinian civilians, including acts of murder, are routinely ignored by the Israeli police on the ground that “the intifadah is a war launched by Palestinians against Israel and in war, excesses are permitted and expected.”

It is believed that the settlers have been responsible directly or indirectly for the often murderous killing of as many as 40 Palestinian civilians since the outbreak of the uprising on 28 September.

Very few of them, if any, have been prosecuted or imprisoned for the crimes committed.

In Hebron on 11 March, Jewish settlers celebrating a Jewish religious holiday dressed up like Baruch Goldstein, the settler who in 1994 massacred 29 Palestinians praying in the Ibrahimi Mosque, and rampaged through Hebron’s Old Town, attacking Palestinians, vandalizing their property and shouting “death to Arabs.”

The settlers, some drunk and armed, also fired into the air, ostensibly in a gesture of defiance to the 180,000 Palestinians who constitute the population of Hebron.

The settlers pelted pedestrians with stones and empty bottles in full view Israeli soldiers.

Palestinian leaders in Hebron condemned the settlers’ wanton behaviour, arguing that peace couldn’t be restored to Hebron without removing the settlers from the city.

A few leftist Israeli politicians also denounced the Israeli army for allowing settlers to act wantonly in Hebron and for re-imposing a curfew on the Old Town’s nearly 30,000 Palestinians in order to allow “a handful of settlers to have a good time.”

“Thousands of Palestinians are under curfew to allow a smattering of settlers to dress up as Baruch Goldstein. It is immoral and will not assist in restoring quiet to the city,” commented Moshe Raz of the left-of-centre Meretz Party.

Nonetheless, with Sharon, Zevi and Liberman now at the helm of power in Israel, it is quite unlikely that Raz’s statements will find much of an echo among settlers or other Israelis.

Israel is interning 100 Palestinian
children in squalid conditions

Occupied Jerusalem - The Palestinian Authority accused Israeli security forces of interning as many as a hundred Palestinian children in conditions unfit for human dignity.

PA official Hisham Abdul Razek, who is in charge of the prisoners’ portfolio in the PA administration, said the children were all under 17 and being interned with Jewish criminal offenders, including rapists and drug addicts.

Abdul Razek said in an interview with the Palestinian semi-official daily, Al-Ayyam, that the children were also being beaten and continually harassed by Israeli prison officials.

Last month, the children smuggled out letters in which they complained of continuous maltreatment at the hands of Israeli prison authorities.

The children pointed out that imprisoned Jewish criminals often attack them, using sharp objects, and seize their belongings and food.

Meanwhile, Israeli occupation authorities in the West Bank and Gaza Strip continued to bar family visits to political prisoners.

Israel is still holding as many as 2,000 Palestinian political prisoners in its numerous jails and detention centres.

Moreover, according to human rights organizations, the Israeli army has rounded up hundreds of Palestinians since the outbreak of the Palestinian intifada nearly six months ago.

Most of the detainees are interned without charge or trial and are used as bargaining chips to exert pressure on the Palestinian Authority to offer concessions in return for their release.

Intifada causes huge losses to Zionist agricultural exports

Occupied Jerusalem - The agricultural sector of the Zionist economy has reportedly suffered huge losses stemming from a serious shortage of manpower.

The Israeli State-run radio reported during February that Israeli farmers were forced recently to destroy over 30,000 tons of harvested agricultural produce, which had been earmarked for transport to Europe and North America, because of a shortage of harvesters.

The radio estimated the losses to the agricultural sector emanating from the absence of Palestinian workers owing to the intifada at hundreds of millions of dollars.

In addition, the construction and tourism industries were even more hard-hit as a result of the intifada.

Israeli sources reported that tourism to the Zionist State was down by 50% and that thousands of employees in the tourist industry were laid off as a consequence of the unprecedented recession.

All in all, it is believed that Israeli losses in connection with the intifada has so far topped 1,500 million U.S. dollars.

Palestinian child dies after Israel barred
him from getting treatment abroad

Occupied Jerusalem - A four-year-old Palestinian child succumbed to his illness on 21 March after the Israeli occupation authorities refused to allow him to travel to Egypt for medical treatment unavailable in the Gaza Strip.

Palestinian sources in Gaza said the child, Abdul Fattah Jouhar al-Sebakhi, of Khan Younis, died as a result of his heart illness after Israeli authorities barred his family from taking him to Egypt for cardiac treatment.

Hospital sources in Gaza said the child was suffering from a congenital heart disease known as Ebstein Anomaly.

Israel reportedly denied him treatment in Israeli hospitals owing to the siege imposed on Palestinian population centres.

The child’s father testified that he went to Rafah crossing point four times for the purpose of travelling to Egypt and that every time he was turned back by the Israeli authorities at the border crossing.

Israeli occupation soldiers manning roadblocks in the West Bank and Gaza Strip routinely deny Palestinians access to hospitals, often resulting in their death.

Palestinian group accuses PA of corruption

Gaza - A Palestinian group calling itself the “Free Officers” said that purging the land of Palestine necessitated ending corruption of those elements that did not care for Palestinian pain and suffering.

The group, in a statement distributed in the streets of Gaza, named several Palestinian figures occupying official posts in the Palestinian Authority and charged them with financial, moral and administrative corruption in addition to opportunism.

It is believed that the group is comprised of several medium-ranked officers within the PA security apparatuses who view with dismay the deteriorating situation in the Palestinian society on various political, social, economic and moral levels, holding the PA leadership responsible for such deterioration.

The statement charged finance minister Mohammed Nashasheebi, director of his ministry Saeed Oweida, sport under-secretary Ahmed Yazji and rector of Azhar University–Gaza Branch—Riad al-Khudari of corruption.

The statement, after mentioning names of a group of prostitutes who corrupt the behaviour of Palestinian youth, said that it was important for everybody to know their names.

The statement emphasized that those corrupt elements were more dangerous than the occupiers. It charged those elements of child prostitution, which it said was never practised by occupation, at a time when children’s blood was being shed on a daily basis in the intifada.

The same group had issued previous statements dealing with similar topics but the PA never arrested any of the listed names.

Zahhar: PA television serves only one party

Gaza - Dr. Mahmoud al-Zahhar, one of the senior officials in the Hamas Movement, has criticized performance of the Palestinian Authority’s television for not coping with developments of the Aqsa Intifada.

He said that the Zionist occupation troops tried to strangle the Palestinian media through assaulting journalists and tried to link Palestinian news web sites on the Internet to pornographic sites.

He affirmed, however, that the intifada had imposed itself on the international media and hailed, in this respect, efforts of various reporters covering the intifada activities.

Zahhar castigated the PA official television for being absent during celebrations held in honour of the martyrs in addition to daily sufferings of Palestinians as a result of the Zionist siege.

The Hamas leader castigated some Arab satellite channels for practising normalization while others tried to drive a wedge among Palestinian factions through cheap means.

He refuted the Arab media use of the term “Israel” when referring to the Zionist entity, affirming that they should negate any recognition of that entity in the minds of the Arab public opinion.

Zahhar’s statements were made in an informational panel organized by the engineers’ syndicate in Gaza under the title of the “Israeli repression, American offensive and the role of Palestinian media and Arab satellite channels in confronting them.”

Tourism to Zionist regime down by 50%

Occupied Jerusalem - The Zionist regime’s ministry of tourism has announced that the number of tourists arriving in Israel during the last six months has dropped by 50% compared with the same period last year.

Ministry officials said hotel occupancy was also under 50%, pointing out that thousands of employees in the tourist industry have been laid off.

The officials attributed the depression in the tourist business to the continuation of the Palestinian intifada.

Several countries, including the United States, have warned their citizens against visiting the Zionist State for safety reasons.

Zionists accuse Palestinians of launching “injustice” virus

Occupied Jerusalem - Zionist circles accused Palestinians of launching an electronic virus around the world bearing the name “injustice.”

The Zionist press reported that the virus carried an attachment that, once opened, drops a copy of itself into the Windows system directory and displays a message box titled “Please accept my apologies for disturbing you. We need every possible help. Israel soldiers killed in cold blood 12-year-old Palestinian child Muhammed Durra as his father tried to protect him in vain…these criminal acts cannot be forgiven or forgotten. Help us to stop the bloodshed.”

The virus-worm is classified as medium risk and does not destroy files and is not likely to crash mail servers.

Report: Israeli violence against Palestinians recoils on Israeli society

Occupied Jerusalem - A recent report by the Israeli Ministry of Education has shown that the internecine confrontations between the Israeli occupation army and Palestinian protesters in the West Bank and Gaza are contributing to the growing violence within the Israeli Jewish society.

The report—the release of which was suppressed by the new right-wing education minister Limor Levnat—outlined the increasing use of physical violence resorted to by Jewish boys and youths against their peers and in many cases against their family members for solving petty day-to-day problems.

The reported cited many “incidents” of bullying and violence in Israeli schools, stressing that the rates have risen significantly since the outbreak of the Palestinian intifadah began more than five months ago.

During the month of February, the right-wing Israeli newspaper, the Jerusalem Post, reported the death of one Israeli schoolboy, a tenth-grader, at the hands of another Israeli boy, a minor, who maintained he killed the pupil in self-defence.
