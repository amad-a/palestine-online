Terrorism Breeds Terrorism

The 30 July suicidal bombing in West Jerusalem has once again confronted us with the reality of the enduring and essentially unresolved conflict between Palestinian Arabs and Israeli Jews. Moreover, the bloodbath seems to have exposed once again the utter and scandalous superficiality of the "peace process," which has so far failed miserably to provide "justice" for Palestinians and "security" for Jews.

It often seems that one should not unduly busy himself with the secondary task of ascertaining the identity of the perpetrators, who, along with their victims, are now in the other world. Nor with placing undue blame solely on Hamas, an essentially religio-cultural movement with hundreds of thousands of followers and supporters in Palestine and further millions of sympathizers all over the world.

For there are more important "genuine" questions that must be addressed and answered, convincingly and courageously, to prevent a repetition of the bloody scenes.

It is amply clear that the current Israeli government bears much of the responsibility for creating the "perfect environment" for "suicidal bombers and bombings."

Israel, one must have sufficient courage to point out , has been narrowing Palestinians' horizons by stealing their land on a daily bases, desecrating their holy places, profaning their religious symbols, and killing and mutilating hundreds of Palestinians with total impunity.

In the last three months alone, as man as five hundred Palestinians were riddled by Israeli live and rubber bullets in Hebron. Five Palestinians were killed, one cold-bloodedly at an Israeli roadblock outside Nablus on 28 July.

Moreover, the Israeli government has effectively turned its back on the peace process, offering the Palestinians either capitulation or eventual national extermination.

East Jerusalem, a city of utmost importance to Muslims and Christians, has been subjected to a frenzied campaign of "ethnic cleansing," a step towards having an all-Jewish Jerusalem.

Moreover, thanks to the Israeli policy, the Palestinian Authority has become virtually a pathetic version of the Israeli puppet militia in South Lebanon known as SLA.

Hence, all Palestinian dreams of freedom and deliverance from Israeli shackles have effectively evaporated. The Palestinian towns, nominally under PA control, have become "shut-in ghettoes" where undercover Israeli soldiers roam freely in the full view of subdued and powerless Palestinian policemen.

In short, the collective and individual life of the Palestinians has become an enduring hell so much so that many people, and their number is increasing steadily, have come to lament the days of the "direct Israeli occupation."

In the face of an ugly, nightmarish reality, one can hardly behave idealistically, and in a world of wolves one has to be either a wolf or a venomous, cunning snake in order to survive.

Indeed, when humanbeings are pushed to the borders of despair (and the Palestinians have been pushed to the edge of despair, thanks to Israeli measures) they tend to behave unpredictably. The unpredictable behavior here is not the cause; it is the effect. One doesn't have to be a great psychologist to understand that when people are bereft of hope, when their horizons are narrowed, they easily become suicidal, because then they have nothing to lose but "torment and suffering."

Now, will the bombing be the last? Let no one delude himself into believing that suicidal bombers and bombings would simply disappear just because the world doesn't like them.

Only when the ethics of justice replace the ethics of "power" can Israelis and Palestinians rid themselves of this cycle of "terrorism breeding terrorism." Only a genuine "political act" could really put an end to Palestinian-Israeli bloodshed. Any other sought-after alternatives would only perpetuate terrorism, the effect rather than the cause.

Profane posters should sound alarm bells for Muslims

On 28 June, two Jewish settlers pasted blasphemous anti-Islamic posters on Arab storefronts in Hebron featuring a caricature of the Prophet Muhammed (may peace be upon him) as a pig writing the Qur'an. The despicable act, which understandably drew angry Muslim reactions all over the world, was treated by the Israeli government as an "isolated act" and an "anecdotal event." However, Israeli officials, while ostensibly denouncing the act and branding it as "incompatible with Jewish traditions," turned a blind eye on the real issue that bred the sacrilegious incident and truly made it a "phenomenon" rather than an "exception."

It is amply clear that anti-Islamic (and anti-goyem) thinking is rife among a sizeable segment of the Jewish community in Palestine, the United States and some European countries. The list of morbid Jewish behaviour to that effect is too long to be reviewed in this brief sketch. However, the following examples may illustrate our point.

It is an established fact that Jewish film-makers, who have a virtual monopoly over Hollywood, have repeatedly insulted Islam and Christianity under the rubric of freedom of expression. In decades of tendentious pro-Israeli movies, Zionist producers never lost an opportunity to malign Islam, the Prophet of Islam, the Holy Book of Islam and Muslims at large. In fact, Zionist films insulted Islam in every conceivable way, beginning with starring dogs called "Muhammed" to making contextual remarks such as "Allah is on my ass." More to the point, Israeli leaders on many occasions insulted the religion of Islam and its prophet, Muhammed. In 1991, Israeli premier Yitshak Shamir said, while addressing the Knesset or Israeli parliament, that "Arabs were learning lies from their Prophet."

In Palestine, the settlers of Gush Emunim, who are represented in the Knesset and government of Benyamin Netanyahu through the National Religious Party (NRP), make no secret of their "contempt of Islam" and more so of "Christianity." Tearing up copies of the Qur'an has always been a favourite practice by Jewish settlers in many localities in the West Bank. Ask any Arab kid in downtown Hebron and he would tell you volumes of hair-raising accounts of anti-Islamic insults perpetrated by the settlers under the watchful eyes of heavily armed Israeli soldiers.

Anti-Islamic racism on the part of many Jews is not a tentative, passing phenomenon. It is rather a way of thinking that is deeply engraved in their upbringing and culture. Ask a settler kid how he views Muslims and he would readily and nonchalantly tell you "they are dogs and pigs."

This is very sad indeed because words can kill. Baruch Goldstein, who murdered 29 Muslim worshippers at the Ibrahimi Mosque on 25 February 1994, was indoctrinated in the same anti-Islamic, anti-goyem ideology that forms the collective morbid entity known as "Jewish settlers."

The problem does not stop at merely uttering injurious words that might seem innocuous to distant outsiders; it actually goes much further, because spiteful, virulent words could inspire and even encourage bloodshed. It is a fact, as any psychologist would tell you, that vilification and denomination usually precede genocide. The Jews themselves had had stark lessons in this which should make them think many times before deciding to walk in the footsteps of their former tormentors.

Muslims, too, should be vigilant. It is often said that the ultimate aim of Jewish settlers is the destruction of Al Masjidul Aqsa and the building on its ruinous site of the Temple, the ultimate symbol of Jewish redemption. Hence the pasting of the blasphemous posters by settlers in Hebron which could serve as a "water-testing precedent" to a would-be crime that is much more colossal. Muslims, therefore, must not take this possibility lightly because Jewish-Zionist insolence knows no bounds. The choice is simply one of life or death for the Muslim Umma. Hence the paramount significance of Muslim reawakening.
