Israel Smuggles "Sexual Gum" to Autonomous Enclave

Occupied Jerusalem- From: Khalid Amayreh (31/5/1997)

Palestinian Authority officials have accused Israel of responsibility for spreading the so-called "sexual gum" among young Palestinian women in the West Bank and the Gaza Strip.

According to PA's Food and Drug Board, Israel has been smuggling for some time defective and hazardous consumer products to the Occupied Territories, including the lust-arousing chewing gum, which reportedly cause sexual arousal to female teenagers and infertility to women of child-bearing years.

The report, published Saturday in major Arabic dailies in the West Bank, pointed out that seven kinds of elastic gum-like tablets were discovered of late in retail shops and roadside stands in several Palestinians towns, both in the Gaza Strip and the West Bank.

The report said the product, which didn't bear a trademark or any other information pertaining to its manufacturer or exporter, most likely originated in Israel and was smuggled by "suspicious elements" and "greed-driven merchants."

However, according to Nahed al Nakhal, Director-General of the PA Food and Drug Board, some of the gum coverings contained a warning saying "not recommended for girls below the age of 18." Other coverings bear the phrase "made in Spain," but board officials dismiss the label as deliberately misleading since it doesn't specify the name and address of the manufacturer.

Nakhal said it was unlikely that what he called "the suspicious product" came from abroad, adding that the most likely source of the gum is Israel.

Nakhal, who holds a Ph.D. in Microbiology, pointed out that Israel has always sought to "dump and smuggle all kinds of defective and potentially dangerous consumer products into the West Bank and the Gaza Strip."

"They seek to achieve two sinister goals, harming the Palestinian people and making money doing that," said Nakhal.

Last year, samples of the "sexual gum" were taken to Egypt for thorough analysis, and the results showed that the gum contained, inter alia, the progesterone hormone, a steroid hormone that induces sexual arousal in women. Higher levels of progesterone, however, could cause infertility to married women.

Palestinians have always suspected that the Israelis were after something wicked to control their high birth rates, mainly as a way to keep the Palestinians in a permanent numerical status.

The Palestinian printed and electronic media have treated the subject with ample attention, warning young girls to be careful. Moreover, School principals throughout the West Bank and the Gaza Strip have been exhorted to educate school girls on the matter, in the hope of increasing public awareness of this matter of paramount significance to the Palestinians.

Far from denying Palestinian charges, Israeli officials make no secret of their anxiety at the "worrying levels" of Palestinian population growth due to a combination of high birth rates and improved health care

Last week, Jerusalem mayor, Ehud Olmert, said unashamedly at a press conference that he was disturbed by the increase of Arab population in Jerusalem, saying that "this problem neutralized our continuous efforts to reduce Arab population to 'manageable' proportions."

Similar racist remarks were uttered virtually by most Israeli officials, including, first and foremost, Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu.

Last month, the Israeli human rights organization, B'tselem, published a report entitled "The Quiet Deportation" detailing frenzied Israeli efforts and measures, including especially-promulgated laws, to restrict and reduce the Palestinian population in East Jerusalem.

The report pointed out that official Israeli policy against the Palestinians in Jerusalem amounted to "ethnic cleansing."

Indeed, if the Israelis were indeed behind this "gum scam," and all indications say they are, it would mean that the Israeli intelligence services (the likely mastermind of something of this nature) would be indulging in a crime of genocide proportions.

"Hospital" or "Place of Torture"

Occupied Jerusalem- From Khalid Amayreh (30/5/1997)

Khalid Ayesh Abu Dayyeh, 37, who died on 16 May of brain hemorrhage at West Jerusalem's Share T'zedek hospital , was not the first Palestinian to die of torture at an Israeli hospital. However, the circumstances surrounding his death provided a fresh testimony to the utter savagery and bestiality with which Palestinians detainees are routinely maltreated at the hands of Israeli interrogators. Abu Dayyeh was actually beaten to death "inside the hospital" where he was supposed to receive treatment. Hence, rather paradoxically, the very people who were supposed to provide medical care for him, killed the helpless Palestinian deliberately, by beating him on the head until he was no longer alive.

Khalid's fatal saga began on 4 May when he headed for the Al Aksa Mosque for prayer. There at the Mosque's entrance, he was stopped by four Israeli soldiers who started making sarcastic and obscene remarks about Islam and the Prophet Muhammed. According to his mother, Khalid (a practicing Muslim) could not bear hear the Jews mock Islam's Holy Book and Prophet. And as he sought o defend his religion's dignity (verbally), the four soldiers attacked him savagely, handcuffed him, and took him to the notorious Russian Compound detention center, often referred to by Palestinian detainees as the "butchery of the Shabak."

There Khalid was reportedly subjected to various forms of severe torture, particularly beatings. Consequently, Khalid sustained serious injury in the neck which necessitated his transfer to the Share T'zedek hospital in West Jerusalem. Khalid had called his mother shortly before he was taken to the hospital. He told her "not to worry" and that he would be released in a few days. But that was the last time she heard his voice. Khalid died almost immediately after he arrived at the hospital.

The Israeli authorities initially sought to evade responsibility for his death. An Israeli Prison Authority spokesman claimed on 18 May that Abu Dayyeh "committed suicide at Share T'zedek, " adding that he had psychological problems, a charge vehemently denied by is family. However when an unidentified sanitary worker at the hospital intimated to the press the circumstances of Khalid's death, the Israeli spokesman was forced to abandon the "suicide story," stating that "the matter is being investigated."

According to Khalid's mother, the same eyewitness informed the family that the border policemen who accompanied her son the hospital provoked him and sought to humiliate him by cursing his "religion and Prophet." (deriding Islam, the Prophet Muhammed, and the Holy Qura'an lately became a widespread practice in Israeli jails and detention centers). Khalid, the eyewitness continued, screamed at them "stop, stop, stop," and sought to disentangle himself from their grip. Watching the site, hospital personnel, including several sentries, leapt on Khalid, beating him savagely on the head, using hand fists and unidentified objects until he lost consciousness. Khalid was pronounced dead two hours later. An autopsy on his body revealed that he died of brain hemorrhage caused by "external blow to the head."

Khalid's mother and bereaved wife expressed utter distrust in Israeli promises to establish the facts of his death.

"First they murdered my son cold-bloodedly, second they tried to cover up their crime, and now they tell us to trust them to carry out a fair investigation into what happened," his mother said.

"God will take our revenge," his wife added in sad serenity.

On 22 May, Khalid was buried in Bethlehem, to be added to the long list of Palestinian victims of Israeli torture and oppression.

Thousands of Islamist students swear allegiance

to Al Aksa Mosque and Jerusalem

Occupied Jerusalem- PT- 18/5/97

Thousands of mainly Islamist students at al Najah National University in Nablus vowed on 17 May to fight and die for the sake of al Aksa Mosque and al Quds al Sharif (Jerusalem).

The students who attended "the Jerusalem Loyalty Rally" shouted in unison "Jerusalem in our hearts," and "with Soul and Blood we shall recover al Masjidul Aksa."

At one point during the rally, 12 students draped in white robes, symbolizing readiness for martyrdom, mounted the large podium, holding aloft copies of the Holy Quran and vowing to give what it takes to recover the world's third most sacred Islamic Shrine from Jewish occupiers.

The huge audience shouted "God is Great" as Islamist leader Musa Abu Marzook spoke to them via telephone from Amman where he now lives following his deportation from the United States earlier this month.

Abu Marzook commended the Islamist students for "defending Muslim rights in Palestine."

"You are defending al Quds al Sharif on behalf of the Muslim Umma...We proudly follow your resistance against the colonization of our land and the Judaization of our most beloved city, Jerusalem," Abu Marzook through a huge loudspeaker.

Sheikh Jamal Mansur, a prominent Islamist leaders from Nablus, attacked Israel, the PA and the peace process which he labeled as "a conspiracy against our people fabricated by Israel, the United States and defeatist Arab regimes."

Mansur suggested that the PA learn the right lesson from Hizbullah in Southern Lebanon and how the Islamic Resistance there has forced Israel to seek a way out of Lebanon.

"We call on the PA to reconsider the entire path of Oslo, especially in light of Zionist arrogance and intransigence," the Islamist leader said, as several PA policemen listened attentively.

Elsewhere, Islamist leaders repeated their invitation to the Palestinian authority to seek other alternatives to the deadlocked peace process.

At the Islamic University of Gaza, pro-Hamas students organized a huge rally on 17 May in solidarity with jailed Hamas leader Sheikh Ahmed Yasin.

Sheikh Ismael Abu Shanab exhorted the PA to follow Yasin's footsteps (who despite his nearly complete paralysis, refused to budge to Israeli blackmail."

Observers in the occupied territories believe the Islamist movements, especially Hamas, have regained and even increased their strength throughout the West Bank and the Gaza Strip, especially in light of the stalemated political process and the dismal performance of the Palestinian authority in virtually all spheres of Palestinian life.

Crocodile kills Shekaki's killer

Occupied Jerusalem- 16/5/97

Shaul Robin, the Mossad agent believed to be responsible for the assassination of Palestinian Islamic Jihad leader Fathi Shekaki in Malta on 28 October 1995, was reportedly killed by a ferocious crocodile at a Costa Rican beach recently while vacationing there.

Robin was also implicated in the killing of several Palestinian activists, while serving in the notorious Israeli General Security Service (the Shabak) during the late years of the Intifada.

According to the Hebrew Press, a ferocious crocodile attacked Robin while swimming at a Costa Rican beach, killing him instantly. Israeli Hebrew papers reported on 11 May that Costa Rican life guards failed to "pull Robin from the crocodile jaws."

The papers quoted Israeli military sources as saying that Robin "had a long arm" in "liquidating" a number of Palestinian and Lebanese freedom fighters, including Islamic Jiahd leader Fathi Shekaki.

The same sources also revealed that Robin was " a high-ranking officer" in the undercover death unit known as "Duvidan" which operated in the Gaza Strip between 1989-1994, and later in the death unit "Samson" in the West Bank.

Following the Oslo Accords, Robin was transferred to an elite Mossad unit which operated in Southern Lebanon and was instructed to assassinate Lebanese resistance leaders and carry out acts of vandalism and terrorism against Hizbullah fighters and leaders.

Israeli officials have so far declined to elaborate on Robin's death.

Asked to confirm or deny press reports on Robin's death, an Israeli army spokesman refused to comment on the reports, saying "it is not our policy to publicly comment on reports of this nature."

Netanyahu is proud of approving settlement building in Jerusalem

Tel Aviv- 13/5/97

Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu said it would take some time before a U.S. envoy would get Israel and the Palestinian Authority (PA) to talk peace again. The Israeli leader also said he was proud of his decision that sent bulldozers to carve out a new Jewish settlement in Arab East Jerusalem. "I am proud that I approved the building in Jerusalem which clarified to the entire world our commitment to the unity of the city," said the Israeli Prime Minister.

He dismissed the idea the settlement stopped peace-making. "No process was stopped. If that stopped the process, there is no process," he said.
