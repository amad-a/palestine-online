Palestinians! Beware of what Arafat is doing

There is ample evidence that the autocratic Palestinian leadership is embarking on a new grand treachery, the biggest of them all, one that would have a profound effect on the Palestinian people and their enduring cause for many years to come.

According to impeccable sources, Arafat’s representative Ahmed Qrei has been discussing a draft framework agreement for the final status settlement whereby the three most fundamental issues of the Palestinian cause are virtually scrapped.

These include the Jewish settlements in the West Bank and Gaza, East Jerusalem and the paramount refugees’ right of return, all of which have already been dealt with through relevant United Nations resolutions, in particular 242, 338 and 194.

On the issue of the land and the Jewish settlements, dubbed the “border issue”, it is reported that Qrei, who is Israel’s most favourite Palestinian negotiator next to Arafat, has accepted (at Arafat’s instructions) an Israeli map whereby the Zionist regime would retain control over a 10-mile-wide strip of land extending along the River Jordan.

This means that border crossings from and to the West Bank, home of the contemplated Palestinian mini-State, would be firmly under Israel’s control.

Furthermore, Israel would still retain control over large mountainous areas throughout the West Bank, including the bulk of the settlements as well as strips of land truncating three Palestinian cantons, one in the north, a second around Ramallah and a third in the south including Hebron and Bethlehem regions.

The reported PA acceptance of this map, rejected earlier by PA negotiator Yasser Abed Rabbo, who resigned from the negotiating team in protest at the opening of a secret channel in Stockholm, would be a serious departure from the erstwhile declared position of the PA that insisted on full Israeli evacuation from all territories seized in 1967.

As regards East Jerusalem, which Arafat has been saying will be his capital, it has been reported that the PA leadership effectively consented to what was termed the Belen-Abu Mazen plan, whereby Israel would continue to have overall sovereignty over all of Jerusalem, with the Palestinian population accorded “internal autonomy” in some East Jerusalem neighbourhoods, along with a guaranteed “safe passage” to the Haram al-Sharif (Muslims’ third holiest site).

Finally, leaks from the Stockholm talks suggested that the refugee issue was nearly “finalized” in line with the declared Israeli position which rejects the implementation of UN resolution 194, which grants the refugees repatriation and restitution.

Last month, PA security official Muhammed Dahlan actually admitted that the PA leadership “was realizing that the return of some five million refugees to Israel was unfeasible.”

Dahlan reportedly told a refugees’ committee in Gaza that “the bulk of the refugees don’t want to return anyway, and that many of them are Jordanian citizens and are content with their present life.”

And in a threat reflecting the police-State mentality of the PA, he reportedly told the audience that “whoever opposes us, we’ll break his head.”

Needless to say, these statements, coupled with the increasingly diluted and vague phraseology being used by the PA leadership when referring to the right of return (such as substituting the right of return with phrases like “just solution” and “equitable settlement”) suggest that Arafat and all his ilk are actually kissing goodbye to the right of return, a grand treachery by all standards.

Now, it is understandable that many PA officials and propagandists will vehemently deny that none of this is taking place and that the Palestinian stance on the fundamental issues, the so-called national red lines, remains unchanged.

However, these and similar statements have been parroted for a very long time, and they were always proven wrong, as Arafat and his mafia continue unabated on their downward course.

In addition, the PA mouthpieces who routinely utter these denials either don’t know what is going on in Stockholm, as they didn’t know what had been going on in Oslo in 1993 until the scandalous scheme became public, or know but can’t face up to Arafat, the giver of jobs, money and privileges.

These stupid mouthpieces also realize quite well that all decisions pertaining to the talks with the Zionists are taken by one man, Yasser Arafat, who didn’t, doesn’t and won’t bother to consult his aides.

Interestingly, Qrei is the speaker of the Palestinian Legislative Council, and his job is essentially to monitor and hold to account the executive authority.

However, given Qrei’s suspicious role in the Stockholm talks, one is prompted to wonder how can he be expected to put up any opposition to a prospective sell-out of Palestinian rights by Arafat?

In short, it seems that the Legislative Council is an accomplice to the brewing crime.

It is likely that Arafat would seek in the next few months to obtain a “a ratification” of the expected sell-out from the so-called PLO institutions, including the Central Council, the Palestine National Council and the PLO Executive Committee.

Would the Palestinian people come to terms with this grand treachery in the name of peace?

The answer is a clarion “no”, no matter how large the duress, how inducing the bribery or how harsh the repression.

And should it reach the point of imposing the capitulatory deal by force, Arafat and his groups would just be treated as quislings…very much like the defunct South Lebanese Army.
