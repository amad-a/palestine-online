Mordechaei-Abu Mazen Meeting ended in Failure

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (28 February)

Palestinian sources said Saturday the latest rounds of talks between high-ranking Israeli and Palestinian officials ended in failure due to "continued Israeli intransigence."

On Friday, Israeli Defense Minister Yitzhak Mordechaei and the deputy head of PLO executive committee Mahmud Abbas met at the Dehniyya airport (yet to be opened pending Israel's approval) to discuss outstanding issues including the overdue Israeli army redeployment in the West Bank, the opening of the airport and the industrial zone.

The sources described the talks as (a waste of time," labeling the Israeli stance as "the same as usual," adding "there was no progress whatsoever."

Following the meeting, Abbas admitted the gap between the two sides' respective positions "was substantial and can't be easily bridged."

PA officials had earlier hoped the breakthrough of the Iraqi-American crisis would pave the way for more vigorous American involvement in the peace process.

However, Israel sources said on Saturday the US administration was not about to "present ideas of its own to end the present impasse in the peace process."

PA officials acknowledged "Israeli equivocation and American connivance with Netanyahu."

Hanan Ashrawi, who holds the higher education portfolio in the PA government conceded in a newspaper interview Saturday that the Palestinians were deceived by the Israelis and the Americans alike.

"At the culmination of the Iraqi crisis, the Israeli government began efforts to activate talks with us," said Ashrawi, adding, however, that "as long as the Iraqi crisis began to recede, the Israeli leadership reverted to its characteristic intransigence."

Meanwhile, a Palestinian Legislative Council Member accused PA Chairman Yasser Arafat of "not doing what should be done in the face of Israeli deception."

"They (the Israeli government) is beguiling the weak Palestinian leadership through holding recurrent rounds of insincere talks aimed at gaining time and exhausting the Palestinians," said Abu Omar in an interview with the Arabic daily, al Quds, on Saturday.

"Netanyahu will stall and stall and stall knowing that Arafat has no alternative but to wait and wait and wait because the United States is the only country that can exert real pressure on Israel and the Americans are not about to do that neither now nor in the foreseeable future," said Abu Omar.

Israel unhappy about agreement

Occupied Jerusalem -PT(23 February)

Israeli officials reacted with a combination of consternation and ambivalence to the agreement reached in Baghdad on Monday between U.N. Secretary-General Kufi Annan and the Iraqi leadership.

Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu told reporters that Saddam Hussein gave in to American demands, adding that there was no reason why the United States shouldn't go ahead with plans to attack Iraq if it emerged that the Iraqi leader didn't fully succumb to American conditions.

Netanyahu, who came under sever criticisms from the leftist opposition for showing poor brinkmanship, said the Israeli position throughout the crisis was right.

He added that "our enemies knew our force of deterrence before and after the crisis."

However, Netanyahu critics described his words as "verbal juggling."

Yossi Sarid, leader of the leftist party Merets described Netanyahu as a big impostor who shams statesmanship.

Sarid accused Netanyahu of creating false hysteria in the Israeli society, arguing that Israel was the main loser in the Iraqi crisis.

Government ministers didn't hesitate to express outright dismay at the agreement.

Health Minister Joshoua Matsa said he was not happy nor sad at the agreement, adding that a new crisis would unfold sooner or later.

He urged the United States to keep its armada of warships and aircraft carriers in the Gulf on a permanent basis.

The same call was also made by Avigedor Kahlani, Minister of Internal Security, who expressed ambivalence with the Baghdad agreement.

Kahlani told the Israeli radio "voice of Israel" that Saddam Hussein would continue to pose a threat to Israel.

He also alluded to Iran as "an important threat to Israel and American interests in the region."

For their part, the Palestinian Authority (PA) welcomed the agreement as "good news from Baghdad."

PA chairman Yasser Arafat told journalists in Gaza he hoped the world would now pay more attention to the stalled peace process.

More Palestinians protest US Saber-rattling against Iraq

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (22 February)

Sporadic protests against a probable American aggression on the Iraqi people continued to take place throughout the West Bank, Gaza and the pre-1967 Palestinian territories.

So far, demonstrations took place in Dura, Bani Naim, Hebron, Beit Ommar, Bethlehem, Dheishe, Ramallah, as well as many localities across the green line (Israel).

In Ramallah on Saturday, thousands of Palestinian youth hurled stones at Israeli soldiers manning military positions near the town's southern entrance.

Earlier, demonstrators burned Israeli and American flags despite police orders not to do so. Moreover, protesters shouted slogans against Palestinian Authority (PA) police chief Ghazi al Jabali, who had threatened to "break the necks of protesters."

"Ya Jabali Ya Amil" "Jabali (Israeli) collaborators" shouted hundreds of demonstrators at the al Manara Square in downtown Ramallah.

Nearby at Beir Zeit University, students marched through the university town, shouting anti-American slogans and carrying placards denouncing "cowboys' cannibalistic instincts."

In the meanwhile, Fatah, which has organized many of the protests since the onset of the American-Iraqi crisis, circulated a leaflet, saying the American aggression on Iraq would be an aggression on all Arabs and Muslims.

Moreover, the leaflet castigated American double standards in dealing with both Iraq and Israel.

In Bethlehem, over a thousand protesters marched through the town, shouting anti-American and anti-Israel slogans.

One placard read "Uncle Sam! how much more Iraqi blood would satiate your cannibalistic appetite.?"

Jewish settlers Commemorate mass murderer

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (17 February)

Hundreds of Jewish settlers in Hebron and the nearby settlement of Kiryat Arba have begun a "weak of commemoration" in memory of Baruch Goldstein, the Jewish mass murderer who on 25 February, 1994, machine-gunned 29 Arab worshipers at al Khalil's Ibrahimi Mosque before he himself was overpowered and subsequently killed by survivors.

In the last few days, scores of settlers, including children, laid wreaths at Goldstein's tomb which has become a pilgrimage site for many Israeli and Jews.

Sources close to settlers said thousands of "the lovers of the land of Israel and of the Tora of Israel" were expected to arrive at Kiryat Arba "to pay homage" to Goldstein.

Baruch Marzel, a prominent Kach (an outlawed terrorist movement in Israel and the United States) member and a fervent disciple of Goldstein, said in a radio interview that the number of Goldstein followers among the settler population and the larger Jewish community was rising.

"Many more Jews are subscribing to the ideas and ideals of Goldstein," Marzel said.

He added that contrary to the negative image Goldstein received in the media, his image among "real Jews" was that of " a national hero and a great lover of the land of Israel."

Concordant Israeli and Palestinian sources stress that while Goldstein is gone, "Goldsteinism" is well and alive among many Israelis, a vindication of Marzel's remarks.

Recently, a young bearded settler appeared on TV explaining to a foreign journalist "the glorious merits of what Goldstein did."

The young settler told the foreign journalist that "if what Joshua did to the Philistines and the Cannanites three thousand years ago was right, why shouldn't what Goldstein did four years ago right likewise?"

Baffled by the settler's hideous analogy, the journalist asked the settler "does this mean you support murdering innocent Arabs?" To this the settler retorted, "You are dead right."

Many pundits have repeatedly pointed out that Goldstein's "genocidal ideas" were in no way confined to a small segment of Israeli Jewish population as is often projected by Israeli media and officials.

The pundits site many opinion surveys showing disproportionately high numbers of Israelis calling for the expulsion of Palestinians from "the land of Israel" and maintaining the status quo of Arabs in Israel as " second class minority."

Palestinians saddened by philanthropist's death

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (17 February)

Palestinians in Jordan and the West Bank have been deeply saddened by the sudden death of Munif Hijjeh, a prominent businessman and philanthropist who was particularly known of his generous support for charitable institutions especially in the Hebron area.

Hijjeh died in Istanbul at 44, of a massive cardiac arrest, while on a business trip.

On Monday, Hijjeh's body was laid to rest in Dura, his home town, 10 miles south west of Hebron.

As many as ten thousand people took part in the solemn procession, including religious figures, civic leaders and PA officials.

Before burial, Hijjeh was eulogized by Sheikh Nayef Rajoub, Imam of the Grand Mosque in Dura, who described the deceased as "a glorious example of how a Muslim businessman ought to be."

Rajoub, an eloquent speaker, said Hijjeh helped the needy, fed the hungry, and comforted the inflicted, adding that he was immensely loved by those who knew him.

He prayed that "he will be loved by God as he was by man."

Hijjeh, a religious man, supported orphanages, schools as well as many poor families in the West Bank and in Jordan.

He also contributed generously in the construction of many mosques and Islamic centers, and paid the tuition on behalf of scores of poor college students in Hebron and Amman.

Hijjeh was also a fervent supporter of the recently-outlawed Islamist Refah Welfare party in Turkey headed by Necmeddin Erbakan.

Hijjeh is survived by his wife, two sons and three daughters as well as his mother and five sisters.

Palestinian Islamists condemn US "arrogance of power"

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (16 February)

Palestinian Islamists have urged Arab heads of state to hasten to save the Iraqi people from "the imminent American aggression," a leaflet signed by the Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) said on Monday.

The leaflet pointed out that the United States was "going too far in its insolence and arrogance of power by preparing to strike at the Iraqi people who have been debilitated by seven years of oppressive sanctions."

The leaflet went on: "with the cowboy mentality, America is launching a blind and evil crusade to protect its strategic interests...without ethics, save the ethics of naked military force, and without principles and humanitarian considerations."

The leaflet exposed the "stark double standards" adopted by the American administration vis-a-vis Iraq and "Israeli-Zionist brutal oppression and torment of the Palestinian people."

Moreover, the leaflet expressed appreciation for the Saudi Arabian refusal to allow American war planes to use Saudi soil as "a launching pad" for aggression against Iraq.

On Friday, Islamist speakers in mosques throughout the occupied territories called on the Muslim Umma to resist American hegemony, saying the US was not only after Iraq but after any and every country that refuses to budge to American imperialism.

Never the less, one speaker, Sheikh Nayef Rajoub of the Grand Mosque in Dura, opined that the "fault lies not in the United States but in the Muslim Umma itself."

"The fault is not with America which seeks to subjugate and humiliate us, but in our Umma and its leaders who readily accept American hegemony."

Rajoub said it was a foregone conclusion that Saddam Hussein is a brutal and ruthless despot, adding, however, that the United States was not after Saddam, whom he said, was serving American interests in the region, but was rather after "Iraq's recovery potentials."

The Friday imam at al Masjidul Aksa essentially repeated the same theme, urging Muslim leaders "to awaken from their deadly silence and cowardly posture."

"When Saddam Hussein struck Teheran with long-range rockets, the US and the West in general actually helped him, but when the prospect of his missiles reaching the "state of oppression" (Israel), the world wasted no moment in attacking and destroying him." the imam said during the Juma'a sermon on Friday.

On 12 February, Hamas military wing, the Izzidin al Kassam Squads, warned that any American attack on Iraqi civilians would not pass unanswered.

"Let the world know that we at al Kassam Brigades would not stand idle in case the brotherly Iraqi people were attacked...our response would be human bombs in the depths of the Zionist entity."

Jewish settlers warn government against removing terrorist's tomb

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (10 February)

Jewish extremists warned the Israeli government on Tuesday against removing the tomb of Baruch Goldstein from Kiryat Arba'a, threatening to "turn the world upside down in case the tomb was tinkered with."

Goldstein is the Jewish terrorist who in February 25, 1994 massacred 29 Palestinian worshipers at the Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron before he was himself killed by survivors.

Ever since his death on the day of the massacre, Godstein's tomb has become a pilgrimage site for Jews from places as far as California.

Baruch Marzel, a friend of Goldstein, said in a radio interview Tuesday that he along with his friends would "turn the world upside down" in case the remains of Goldstein were removed from Kiryat Arba'a.

Marzel, a leading member of the terrorist Jewish movement "Kach," added that any tinkering with "the sacred tomb of the saint Baruch Goldstein" would spark off unpredictable reactions from many Jews.

Marzel's vitriolic threats came in reaction to a Knesset proposal tabled by the Israeli parliamentarian Dudy Tsuker calling for the removal of a memorial plaque atop Godstein's tomb and transferring his remains to Tel Aviv or Jerusalem.

Moreover, the notion calls for the incrimination of activities and actions proscribing sanctity to terrorists and their places of burial.

Tsuker's notion was labeled as "evil and repulsive" by Kiryat Arba'a settlers.

The Settlement chief rabbi, Dov Leor, described Tsuker's proposal as "inimical to Jewish traditions."

Leor is considered among the leading advocates of "Goldsteinism" the Jewish equivalent of Nazism.

Last year, he published a book glorifying and lionizing the terrorist Goldstein. He described him as " a great saint" whose action would herald the age of redemption for the Jewish people.

Moreover, Leor teaches his many disciples that the blood of Jews is redder than that of non-Jews, suggesting that Jews are preferred by God than non-Jews.

Israeli government Okays "torture law"

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (8 February)

The Israeli cabinet has officially endorsed a draft law allowing the employment of physical and psychological torture by Israeli intelligence interrogators against Palestinian detainees.

The draft law, entitled "the General Security Service Law" stipulates that GSS interrogators may use "moderate physical pressure against detainees whenever deemed necessary for safeguarding the security of the state."

The new draft law doesn't stipulate what constitutes "moderate physical pressure" nor details the ways and means to be employed by Israeli interrogators.

This means the matter will be left for the individual interrogator or his immediate superior to determine if the case at hand warrants or not the use of torture.

The canonization of torture by the Israeli government has drawn sharp reactions from anti-torture groups in Palestine.

"This law is a stigma at Israel's forehead," said the Director of the "People Against Cruelty," an inter-confessional organization against torture, in a radio interview on Sunday.

According to human rights organization, the new torture law would effectively absolve interrogators indulging in torture from any legal responsibility even if the torture caused death.

The GSS currently uses a variety of officially-sanctioned means of torture including the notorious "shaking technique," which often causes brain dislocation and hemorrhage, severe beating, strangulation, electric shocks and hooding.

Moreover, numerous forms of psychological torture are used against Palestinians which experts say are often similar to the forms of torture used by the Nazis in the 1940s.

In the last two years, as many as 12 Palestinian detainees died of torture at the hands of Israeli interrogators.

While Israel is not the only country in the world that practices torture, it is the indeed the only country that officially sanctions the savage practice.

Israeli officials routinely seek to justify the use of torture as the only available means to extract confessions from Palestinian detainees.

However, human rights organizations, Palestinian and Israeli alike, argue that political detainees are also subjected to various forms of torture.

Last month, a public opinion survey conducted in Israel showed that over half the Israeli Jewish population supported the employment of a variety of physical and psychological torture against non-Jews.

HR groups urge PA to investigate death

at police center; authority of law nonexistent

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (7 February)

Palestinian human rights groups have accused Palestinian Authority police of "seeking to conceal evidence" concerning the death last week of a Palestinian detainee at a PA police lockup in Dura, 8 miles south West of Hebron.

The Palestinian Human Rights Monitoring Group (PHRMG) charged in a press release on Friday that the PA was "dragging its feet in clarifying the exact circumstances surrounding the death of Nasser al Hurub."

Al Hurub, 28 and father of five children, died in vague but suspicious circumstances on Monday 2 February only two hours after his arrest at the hands of PA policemen in Dura.

His family has insisted all along that his death resulted from torture at the hands of PA police, a charge denied by the police officials who claimed al Hurub committed suicide.

Two autopsies performed on al Hurub's body showed the death resulted from a massive rupture of a major artery in the brain.

However, it remains to be established what caused the hemorrhage.

The case is likely to remain shrouded in vagueness especially in the light of the fact that the PA lacks proper forensic facilities.

The PHRMG pointed out that shortly after his arrest, al Hurub started bleeding from the mouth and the ears.

Hebron police Chief Tareq Zeid told (PT) al Hurub strangled himself to death using a shoe string.

However, his wife stressed that her husband was wearing unlaced at the time of his arrest.

Al Hurub is the 19th Palestinian to dies in PA custody since the installment of Yasser Arafat's Palestinian Authority more than three years ago.

The latest prison fatality in the PA autonomous enclaves, say observers, is only an outer symptom of the dire state of the Palestinian judicial system.

On Saturday, Professor Ali al Jarabawi of the Beir Zit University, declared that "the rule of law within the Palestinian Authority is nonexistent."

Jarbawi, a professor of political science, wrote in the Ramallh-based Arabic daily al Ayyam that "there was a state of chaos in our legal system and one no longer knows who is responsible for what ."

Citing an example of the rampant judicial disarray within the autonomous enclaves, Jarbawi pointed out that the acting Palestinian minister of Justice, Freih Abu Maddin, dismissed the head of the PA Supreme Court, Qusay al Abadleh, while the latter was on an official visit in Cairo.

Last month, the PA dismissed a Ramallah judge after he ruled that a score of Beir Zit University students ought to be released as their continued detention by PA police was illegal.

Arafat losing faith in U.S. role in Peace Process

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (4 February)

In a sign of growing Palestinian impatience and disenchantment with the American role in the Middle East peace process, Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat began on Wednesday a new European tour aimed at mobilizing European pressure on Israel.

Palestinian sources said Arafat would hold talks with the leaders of the Netherlands and Spain and try to convince them to pressure Israel for implementing the stipulated Israeli army redeployment in the West Bank.

Last week, Arafat met with the leaders of France and the United Kingdom and reportedly obtained a promise that Europe would step up its peace efforts.

On Wednesday, a close aide to Arafat said the Palestinian leader "was losing the remaining confidence in the credibility of the American role in the peace process."

Ahmed Abdul Rahman, Secretary of the PA cabinet, said Arafat became hopeless of any genuine American pressure on Israel especially after the failure of the two White House meetings between U.S. President Bill Clinton and both Arafat and Netanyahu.

Abdul Rahman said the recent visit by U.S. Secretary of State Madeleine Albright to Palestine served to enforce the feeling of "helplessness on the part of the Palestinian leadership," citing Albright's soft tone with Netanyahu in contrast with her sharp tone on Iraq.

Paradoxically, Arafat reportedly had thought Albright would be seeking to strike some balance in the American policy in the Middle East by showing that the U.S. was serious in trying to achieve peace between Israel and the Palestinians as much as it was determined to "force Iraq to comply with U.N. resolutions."

However, having lacked the inclination and the will to pressure Netanyahu to carry out the long overdue redeployment, Albright sent a depressive message to Arafat, saying "don't expect America to pressure Israel."

Indeed, the bleak message must have prompted Arafat's spokesman, Nabil Abu Rudeineh, to declare the next two weeks to be "decisive."

Interestingly, Arafat's aides have already begun to label the American role in the peace process as "conspiratorial."

Palestinian exasperation with perceived American connivance with Israel coincided with the Israeli decision on Wednesday to build a new Jewish neighborhood at Ras al Amud in East Jerusalem.

The Israeli decision draw strong reactions from Palestinian leaders.

Faisal Husseini, who holds the Jerusalem portfolio in the PA administration, called the decision "a powder kerg" and warned Israel against putting it into effect.

Ahmed Abdul Rahman said the Israeli decision would deal a the death blow to the already moribund peace process.

Israel admits Hamas didn't plan bomb attacks

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (2 February)

Israeli authorities have admitted that earlier Israeli reports alleging the arrest of two Hamas fighters on their way to carry out a bomb attack in Israel were "inaccurate" and "lacking credibility."

On Monday, the Israeli Hebrew press reported that General Security Service (GSS) officials criticized Internal Security Minister Avigdor Kahlani for "blowing up the issue out of proportion by channeling false information to the international media."

According to the Hebrew daily Yedeot Ahranot, GSS officials rebuked Kahlani for "contributing to weakening GSS credibility."

Yedeot also reported one GSS official as saying that "this issue ought to teach Kahlani a lesson in how to keep his mouth shut."

Last week, news agencies and leading television networks quoted Israeli officials as saying that "two would-be Hamas suicidal bombers were arrested while on their way to carry out a suicide bomb-attack inside Israel."

The Israeli radio, often a mouthpiece of the GSS, even pointed out that large amounts of TNT were found in the two suspects' cars.

Moreover, Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu and other Israeli officials used the report to justify Israeli refusal to implement the long overdue Israeli army redeployment in the West Bank.

On 30 January, the people of the Bedouin village of Ksefeh in the Negev demonstrated against Kahlani, calling on him to resign and apologize for "the libelous and malicious accusations he made against the people of our village."

Knesset member Talab al Sanei, a Bedouin, said Kahalani should have known what he was talking about.

"He should have waited to know the truth before rushing to tell Reuters that two Hamas bombers had been detained." he said.

In a similar incident, Israeli police yesterday released three Palestinian university students who had been arrested two weeks earlier for "aiding Hamas."

Police sources said the charges were false.

The students, Fuaz Abu Ghosh and Yazid Yaghmour, both of Beit Hanina neighborhood near Jerusalem, were reportedly placed in solitary confinement for two weeks.

The two also told relatives their hands were tied and they were forced to sit on low stools with their hands and legs fettered.

In other development, Israeli troops arrested on Sunday six Najah University students while on their way to school.

Israeli occupation troops routinely erect roadblocks and checkpoints on all roads and routs from and to Palestinian towns under Yasser Arafat's PA administration.
