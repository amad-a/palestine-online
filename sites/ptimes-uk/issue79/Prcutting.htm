THE United States will be the ultimate arbiter in decisions over which Islamist militants should be detained or freed as part of moves to put the Middle East peace process back on track according to Israeli news reports yesterday. The agreement, worked out by Palestinian, Israeli and US security officials, is aimed at assuaging fears that Yasser Arafat's Palestinian Authority rounds up extremists after major atrocities and then quietly releases them... The Yediot Aharonot daily newspaper claimed that an agreement among the parties stipulates that the Palestinians will give the US Central Intelligence Agency's representative in Israel advance notice of the names of detainees they plan to release. The CIA will then inform Israel of impending releases and Israel will be given an opportunity to voice objections. In the end, the Palestinian Authority can release detainees only with US approval, Yediot said. The Guardian, December 20, 1997
