Concern Over Al Aqsa Mosque Rises

Occupied Jerusalem- Special to PT

Palestinian leaders representing various political orientations have warned that Jerusalem's al Aqsa Mosque is facing the danger of destruction at the hands of Jewish extremists, possibly in connivance with certain circles in the Israeli Government. The renewed Palestinian concern was voiced following the disclosure by the former Israeli Police Inspector Assaf Hefets on New Year's eve that extremist Jewish elements affiliated with the Messianic Gush Emunim movement were planning to destroy the al Aqsa Mosque and the Dome of the Rock for the purpose of "rebuilding the Temple of Solomon on their sites and accelerating the process of redemption for the Jewish people."

Hefets didn't give any details, but signalled that the "aspirations" of the "extremist elements" ought to be taken seriously by the political authority in Israel.

Meanwhile, the Hebrew press reported that extremist elements confessed of having rented a flat overlooking al Aqsa Mosque which they hoped to use as a launching pad for firing rockets on Muslim worshippers during the current holy month of Ramadan. The would-be-perpetrators reportedly hoped to kill as many Palestinians as possible, which would in turn spark off more violence and bloodshed, a condition viewed by the sages of Gush Emunim as a necessary requirement for "the appearance of the saviour" and achievement of "redemption."

Hefets' revelations coincided with the arrest of two other Gush Emunim members for plotting to launch a pig's head into the Haram al Sharif compound also during the month of Ramadan.

Haidar Abdul Shafi, a legislative council member and outstanding Gazan figure, said he was not surprised by the "grave" revelations because the "ambitions of these movements are well-known and border on madness." He argued that the vital question now is "what Palestinians, Arabs and Muslims ought to do to forestall their heinous designs."

"All our holy shrines are in danger and I can't really overestimate the gravity of the situation ... I'm afraid that one day we will wake up to the sad news that al Aqsa Mosque has been demolished," Abdul Shafi said in a telephone interview with Palestine Times.

Sheikh Muhammed Hussein, Director-General of the Haram al Sharif compound, described the foiled plot as "characteristic of Zionist thinking."

"They make no secret of their aspirations to destroy Islamic holy places in Jerusalem in order to rebuild a Jewish temple on their ruins."

For its part, Hamas reacted strongly to Hefets' revelations. Dr. Abdul Aziz al Rantisi, the Movement's principal spokesman in Gaza, accused "the Israeli State of harbouring definite designs toward Islamic holy places."

"The collective Israeli State is involved in this evil scheme ... the subterranean tunnels they are digging beneath the mosque testify to their sinister designs," Rantisi told PT.

Reflecting a sense of frustration and bitterness at the grim prospect, Rantisi demanded that Muslims "wake up before it is too late."

"The Israeli occupation of Jerusalem was a great catastrophe for Muslims; the destruction of the al Aqsa Mosque now in the full view of over a billion Muslims would be even a greater catastrophe," he said.

On 2 January, as many as 170,000 Palestinians descended on the al Aqsa Mosque to perform the first Friday prayer in Ramadan. There they listened to a fiery speech by Sheikh Hamed Beitawi, who warned the Israeli authorities and Jewish extremists against "any miscalculations."

He further warned that "the destruction of the al Aqsa would also mean the destruction of Israel."

"We would burn the ground under your feet if anything happened to our mosque," alluding to suicidal bomb attacks.

On the following Friday, around 200,000 people showed up, taking advantage of the unseasonably good weather and of relaxed Israeli restrictions on Palestinian entry into East Jerusalem during the month of Ramadan.

The two recent disclosures about Jewish plots against the al Aqsa Mosque coincided with a shocking revelation also pertaining to the mosque. The Hebrew daily Ha'eretz quoted the Israeli army commander of the Central Region during the 1967 war, Major General Uzi Narkis, who died recently, as saying that the army's former chief Rabbi, Shlomo Goren, suggested to him that he deposit 100 kg of TNT beneath the Dome of the Rock and get it destroyed "once and for all." According to Ha'eretz, Narkis intimated that Goren broached persistently on the matter, saying "Uzi, this is the time to get 100 kg of explosives inside the Dome of Rock and it will go once and forever." Shocked by Goren's persistence, Narkis reportedly told Goren "Rabbi, stop this."

Goren was a founding father of Gush Emunim, which later bred such cardinal terrorists and racists as Baruch Goldstein and Meir Kahane (the former murdered 29 Muslim worshippers at the Ibrahimi mosque in Hebron and the latter founded Kach)."

The movement fiercely opposed the peace process between Israel and the Palestinians on the ground that peace would postpone or slow down the process of redemption.

Hence, followers often seek to initiate attacks on Palestinians and Muslim holy places in East Jerusalem in the hope that this would generate violence, which in turn would induce redemption.

Rajoub weakened

There are increasing signs that the controversial head of the PA Preventive Security Force (PSF), Colonel Jebril Rajoub, is falling into disfavour with Chairman Arafat and much of the Fatah establishment. On 10 January, a street melee erupted in Hebron between high-ranking Preventive Security and members of the "Special Forces" responsible to Ghazi Jabali, a stalwart supporter of Arafat. The melee nearly evolved into an armed confrontation were it not for taxi drivers and street-goers who intervened to separate the factions vying for influence in the street.

Last week, Rajoub was reportedly harshly rebuked by Arafat following a similar incident in Nablus. According to Palestinian sources, Rajoub supporters forced two prominent Fatah leaders, Sakher Habash and Hakam Bala'awi, both members of the Fatah Revolutionary Council, out of a rally celebrating the annual anniversary of the movement. Habash and Bala'awi reportedly protested the conspicuous absence of Yasser Arafat's portraits, prompting Rajoub's supporters to "eject them."

Rajoub's membership in the Revolutionary Council was "suspended" for eight months last month after he reportedly used "obscene and derogatory" language against Habash.

Rajoub was until recently viewed as a major candidate for successor to Yasser Arafat. However, on 18 January, the Israeli media quoted "reliable Palestinian sources" as saying that Rajoub had submitted his resignation to Arafat. Rajoub denied the Israeli reports, saying they were part of an Israeli campaign aimed at "distorting the image of the Preventive Security and its leadership."

Sheikh Yasin: I won't recognize Israel; the Oslo Agreement was born dead

Sheikh Ahmed Yasin, founder and spiritual mentor of Hamas, is unrelentingly opposed to "any dialogue with the entity of oppression (Israel) because this would lead nowhere."

Sheikh Yasin spoke last week with the Israeli-Arabic weekly Sawt al Haq wal Hurriyya.

The following are excerpts of what he said:

Question: There is a lot of vagueness concerning the political course of Hamas. Does the Movement want to remain a military organization or combine armed struggle with political action?

Answer: To begin with, there is no movement the world over that adopts military struggle without having political goals because the former is always entwined with the latter. We at Hamas have a very clear policy. We want to recover our rights and establish our Palestinian State. Politics is the means with which we deal with all States and organizations. Hence, we adopted the principle of a step-by-step solution provided that Israel leave the West Bank, the Gaza Strip and East Jerusalem in return for a "hudna" or an extended truce.

Q: But from a political viewpoint, does Hamas absolutely reject the principle of talks or negotiations with Israel?

A: I object to the wording of your question for two reasons: first, I don't recognize Israel and am unwilling to talk to her because such a thing would imply recognition; second, we are careful not to allow the Zionist entity to exhaust us as they exhausted the PLO. Moreover, if the Israelis are serious about peace, why don't they just leave us alone and leave our land. Look, they have given the PLO nothing despite the huge concessions to them by the PA. This (negotiations with Israel) is a route fraught with deception and equivocation, and we are not willing to give it any consideration.

Q: I am saying in case an offer is made to Hamas?

A: I'm not willing to divide the Palestinian cause into small fragments. I won't recognize Israel.

Q: Do you support any progress in the talks such as extending Palestinian authority to more land and releasing Palestinian prisoners?

A: I don't mind releasing prisoners, but you have got to remember that the PA has conceded everything to the Israelis.

Q: Is there any room for reconciling military struggle against Israel with PA opposition to it?

A: Reconciling the irreconcilable is very difficult, indeed. We are supposed to resist the occupation and the PA is resisting the resistance. However, we are doing everything possible to avoid any unnecessary friction with the PA.

Q: So the PA is insisting on its rejection of any armed struggle against Israel?

A: The peace process has now reached a dead-end. In the past, the PA harboured certain hopes and therefore moved against us. Now, these hopes have evaporated, so, moving against us anew would be meaningless and unacceptable.

Q: Does the PA benefit from Hamas' military struggle?

A: Certainly.

Q: Don't you see a certain inconsistency in Hamas' policy? On the one hand you are continuing the struggle, on the other you are careful not to "collide" with the PA?

A: There is no inconsistency Our policy is very clear. We want to fight our oppressors; the PA rejects this. What we want from the PA is to realize that we are not against it and we won't fight it despite the fact the PA is siding with the enemy against us as demonstrated in the torture to which our brothers were subjected in PA jails and detention centres.

Q: The PA says you want to destroy its national dream?

A: This is not true; Hamas doesn't want to destroy the PA; the moment Hamas points its guns at or on the PA, you can say something like this. We are against the occupier and the PA is utterly unable to remove the occupier's oppression from our land. The right to resist the occupation is absolutely legitimate.

Q: Why won't Hamas give the PA a respite for one or two years to take its chance?

A: We didn't sign the Oslo Accords and were not a part to it. In any case, the Accords were born dead, so you can't give a dead body a chance. The only chance it deserves is to allow it to be buried.

Q: What is the alternative, then?

A: The PA has made a big blunder, hence it now faces an unenviable situation. Undoing Oslo and resuming the intifadah is the alternative.

Q: Since your release from prison, the PA is ignoring you.

A: We make our views known through the media.

Q: What is the national programme of Hamas?

A: Hamas is established on the ground with its social, educational and other institutions. Even Islamists, who work within the PA, are active in building for the future. However, the PA doesn't accord us freedom to serve our people.

Q: Hamas leaders claim there is total separation between the political and military wings of the Movement, is this true?

A: This is not true. Our political line is unequivocal, and our brothers in the military wing understand that.

Q: Will Hamas continue to be a secret movement?

A: Hamas is a fighting movement, and Jihad requires a great deal of secrecy.

Q: Do you have a message for Arafat?

A: Reconsider your calculations.

Fourth Anniversary Of Hebron Massacre

By E. Yaghi

To assume that the man who so cold-bloodedly massacred Muslim worshippers during the dawn prayer at the Ibrahimi mosque on February 25, 1994 in Hebron was demented is an easy way of dismissing one of the major factors contributing to the obstacles preventing peace between the Israelis and Palestinians.

The massacre occurred shortly before 5 a.m. when the mosque was crowded with worshippers for the dawn prayers during the Holy month of Ramadan. And though the former Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin called this heinous crime, "a murderous act by a psychopath," as if it were only the evil deed of a crazed man and nothing else, it was so much more than that. Further, let us not assume Baruch Goldstein, who was an American immigrating to Israel from New York was a deranged person. He had carefully calculated what he did and was in fact a major in the Israeli army as well as a medical doctor. Medical doctors take an oath to save lives yet Goldstein was responsible for the deaths of more than 50 people, including children.

When he attacked the worshippers at dawn that fateful Friday four years ago, he indiscriminately fired two clips of a machine-gun into the faithful who bent their heads in prayer. More than 300 people were injured during the attack. Israeli soldiers positioned at the mosque at the time of the incident fired teargas and guarded the entrances, making the evacuation of dead and injured impossible. It is for this reason that no ambulances were able to reach the scene of this massacre until nearly one hour later, making the number of fatal casualties even higher.

But let it be remembered that Goldstein knew very well what he was doing. Hebron is the only place in the West Bank where Jewish settlers live in the heart of an Arab city. Those Israelis who choose to live outside Israel proper do so because of generous government subsidies intended to encourage the colonization of Arab lands seized in the 1967 war. Most of these settlers in such enclaves are extremists who with the support of the Israeli government, are determined to wipe out the indigenous Arab population in order to make room for more settlements. These Jewish extremists are legally sanctioned, given arms by the Israeli government and are often as in the case of Goldstein, directly supported by the Israeli military.

Israeli extremists are often funded by private contributions from America and a lot of substantial subsidies come from the U.S. government. And while these extremists are bent in driving out or killing Palestinians, the Israeli government is building more and more settlements which are in violation of international law and are illegal.

Even though more than 50 worshippers died in the Ibrahimi mosque four years ago, others were injured or killed when they tried to rush the injured to hospitals or bury them. They were shot at by the Israeli military. Added to this, demonstrations broke out all over the Gaza Strip and West Bank cities increasing the number of dead and injured.

To intensify hard feelings, the U.S. successfully blocked an effort by the UN Security Council to dispatch a multi-national peace keeping force to the occupied territories so that further violence would not erupt.

Though the world can little remember what was said at the Ibrahimi mosque, it can never forget what Goldstein and his cohorts did there. It is difficult to pretend to establish peace on such a shaky foundation. Dead and injured Palestinians are not likely to provide a basis for any kind of peace negotiations.

In order for peace to be concrete and real, settlements must be stopped and people like Goldstein either imprisoned or shipped out of the country. The murderer died by his own sword for he was killed by one of the faithful and did not live to face the consequences of his evil action. People like Goldstein have hearts eaten away by hate. Unfortunately, the victims of such people are the innocent.

The day the faithful martyrs were buried, a wave of rage swept over what little is left of Palestine. That rage still has to be quelled for every day, more Palestinian blood is shed on Palestinian soil, new victims fall prey to Israeli atrocities, and the confiscation of Arab land continues until there will be nothing left of Palestine at all.

Four years later let us solemnly remember the innocent who were slain or injured. As we lay flowers on their graves, we must pray that such atrocities will not happen again and that the so-called peace talks will not be the relinquishment of further Palestinian soil, nor the building of new settlements, but a just and lasting peace that is available to all.

Amnesty condemns trial of two Hamas activists

Amman (J.T.)- Amnesty International last month condemned the 19 January trial of two Hamas activists accused of having set up a bomb factory in Nablus north of the West Bank.

Nasser Abul Rous, and Jasser Salameh were tried within days of their arrest, in a summary trial before the State Security Court in Jericho. The trial was held before military judges, with a military prosecutor, under the PLO code of 1979- up to now only used against members of the security services. The public were barred from the half-hour trial. The court appointed a military lawyer to represent the two defendants, and lawyers offering to represent the defendants were not allowed into the court except to hear the sentences of 15 years imprisonment with hard labour, the statement said.

"No one can tell whether those arrested are guilty or innocent when they are tried in such a way," Amnesty said. "These abuses undermine the judicial system and are a denial of justice."

Amnesty also condemned the Palestinian Authority's prolonged detention without charge or trial of more than 150 suspected Islamist members of Hamas and the Islamic Jihad. Some of these detainees have been held for more than a year while orders from the attorney general or the Palestinian High Court to release specific detainees against whom no charges had been filed and no evidence produced have been ignored, the statement continued.

"After the Palestinian High Court ordered the immediate release, on grounds of unlawful detention, of Sheikh Mahmoud Musleh, a Hamas activist, in December 1997, he was taken for a short walk and then brought back to prison. He was told that he had been rearrested. That makes a mockery of the law," Amnesty said.

In the context of the peace accords, Israel has put increasing pressure on the PA to "fulfill its commitments to fight terrorism" by, among other things, "the apprehension, prosecution and punishment of terrorists." The Israeli government has used the issue of "terrorism" as an excuse for allowing the Israeli General Security Service to use practices which constitute torture during interrogation and for holding over 300 Palestinians in administrative detention without trial.

"It is now time for the PA and Israel to accept that a government's legitimate right to fight crime and violence can only be carried out with due respect for human rights, " Amnesty said. "The international community, particularly the United States, which is brokering Israeli-Palestinian peace talks, should also make it clear that they will not condone or encourage human rights abuses such as unfair trials, detention without trial and torture."

Palestinian human rights group denies persecution of Christians

Occupied Jerusalem (AP)- Christians living under Palestinian Authority (PA) rule do not face systematic persecution, a Palestinian human rights group said, dismissing claims by the Israeli government and some evangelical Christians.

The Palestinian Human Rights Monitoring Group (PHRMG), which is often sharply critical of PA's human rights record, said reports alleging persecution were a "deliberate manipulation" aimed at defaming the PA.

The group convened a news conference last month to release its new report, "For the record: The 'persecution' of Christians under the Palestinian Authority."

"We believe that the Christians of Palestine are not being persecuted at all," said Charles Lenchner, a member of the group.

The report said the allegations of persecution were being made by the International Christian Embassy, a staunchly pro Israeli group based in Jerusalem, and by the government of Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu. The Christian Embassy alleged Palestinian converts to Christianity are being systematically harassed by the PA. It cited cases involving several recent converts to Christianity, including Mohammed Baker, an engineering from the Nablus area, who was arrested by Palestinian police last summer.

The Christian Embassy has said Baker was targeted because of his faith. However, the Palestinian human rights group said last month that Baker was arrested during a sweep of land dealers suspected of having sold land to Jews.

"The charges against him may be false, but this does not mean that the real reason for his arrest was his Christianity," the report said.

Representatives of the U.S. consulate in Jerusalem visited Baker in a Nablus prison. A consulate official, who spoke on condition of anonymity, said Baker acknowledged having served as a witness during a sale of West Bank land to Jews.

However, the Christian Embassy said last month it stood by its allegations. David Parsons, a spokesman for the group, said the Palestinian human rights activists were losing their credibility.

In its report, the human rights group took a look at five cases it said had repeatedly come up in media reports, and concluded that in two of the cases, there was evidence of harassment.

In one of the cases, a 25 year-old Palestinian convert was arrested repeatedly on suspicion of collaboration with Israel and in a second, a 31 year-old was ostracised in his overwhelmingly Muslim village.

"The human rights violations are occurring at a local level, and not as a result of Palestinian Authority policies against Christians," the report said, adding that it has found no other evidence of harassment.

Palestinian leaders blame Algerian government and army for carnage

Occupied Jerusalem (13 January)

Palestinian leaders representing various political orientations have blamed the Algerian government and army for the continuing pogroms in that Arab Muslim North African country.

Hamed al Beitawi, a prominent Islamic figure from Nablus, said in a telephone interview he was convinced that "special units of the Algerian army and paramilitary groups, armed and backed by the government," were responsible for the bulk of the massacres of innocent civilians in the Algerian countryside.

"All indications suggest that the Algerian authorities and army are actively involved in these abominable acts of savagery," Beitawi said.

He cited persistent refusal by the Algerian government to accept any neutral international inquiry into the massacres, which he said proves that the authorities "have something to conceal from the world and the Algerian people."

"Why hasn't the Algerian government arrested and tried any of the murderers so that the true identity of the savages is established...and why hasn't the Algerian army moved to save wailing women and children in the vicinity of the army camps and garrisons," Beitawi wondered.

Rafik al Natshe, former PLO ambassador to Saudi Arabia, also criticized the Algerian government for "giving the army free rein to butcher civilians."

Natshe pointed out that the Algerian army was behaving as a militia, not as an "army of the people" as its official title suggests.

Natshe also called on the Algerian government to "immediately initiate a dialogue with the opposition, including the outlawed Islamic Salvation Front.

Hamas recently renewed its condemnation of the ghastly massacres, holding the Algerian government responsible for them.

Ismael Abu Shanab, a Movement leader in Gaza, said he was sure the pro-government militias and some army units were responsible for most of the killings.

During the second week of January, Hamas issued a leaflet blaming "French agents in Algeria" for seeking to "kill Islam in Algeria."

Occupied Jerusalem- PT

The Jewish terrorist organization, known as Kach, has threatened to kill members of an American Christian Peace Team (CPT) based in the West Bank of Hebron if they fail to leave the West Bank within 96 hours.

According to the CPT director, Gene Stoltzfus, the team received e-mail messages signed by Kach in the United States and the West Bank with the subject heading "Swift Punishment".

One message read "it has been learned that three of your members help and assist Arab terrorists in smuggling explosives. For their safety, the above-mentioned terrorists (the Christians) are advised to depart from the Land of Israel to their own countries in the next 96 hours."

Earlier, Stoltzfus retrieved a similar message from the answering machine at the CPT Chicago office that added "as a humanitarian effort to save their lives, we ask you to evacuate them from Israel before 21 January. After that date their lives will be in jeopardy. This is a warning. This is a last warning. Bye."

The CPT reacted by alerting the FBI and several American congressmen and senators to the Kach threats.

Moreover, the team issued a statement exposing the real motives behind Kach threats against them.

The statement read: "This threat came from an outlawed Jewish terrorist organization that has in the past brutally killed people. So, we take this threat very seriously. However, we will continue to support Palestinians and Israelis who are non-violently resisting the military occupation of the West Bank. Yesterday (19 January), we received just a taste of what the Palestinians face daily. They must face the terror that the occupation brings at checkpoints, settlers with M-16s, or bulldozers coming to destroy the family house."

CPT lawyer, Jonathan Kuttab, said the threats ought to be taken seriously given the terroristic and murderous history of Kach, including the murder of Alex Udeh, director of the Arab-American Anti-defamation League in California.

Kuttab said the murderers, Robert and Miryam Manning, were still fugitives hiding in the Jewish settlement of Kiryat Arba'a near Hebron.

Israel lauds "security coordination" with PA

Occupied Jerusalem-PT

In a rare recognition of the depth of security coordination between Israel and the Palestinian Authority, Israel lauded "genuine Palestinian cooperation with the PA security apparatus in foiling planned attacks on Israeli targets."

The Israeli deputy chief of staff, Major General Uzi Dayan, said joint and concerted Israeli-PA efforts enabled both sides to apprehend Islamist activists bent on carrying out "terrorist operations" against Israel.

The Israeli government secretary Danny Naveh announced the three demands in a press conference in West Jerusalem.

Naveh said the three demands would be treated as "pre-conditions" for the implementation of the second phase of redeployment in the West Bank.

Palestinian leaders reacted angrily to the new Israeli demands.

Ahmed Qrey, speaker of the Legislative Council, described them as "additional means to enable Netanyahu to evade the agreements."

Sheikh Yasin calls on the PA to release detainees

Gaza (QP)- Sheikh Ahmad Yasin, the spiritual leader of Hamas, called on the Palestinian Authority to release all political prisoners in its jails and to re-open the charity establishments it closed three months ago. Sheikh Yasin said Chairman Arafat had promised in their last meeting to release the detainees who did not commit any violation against the law and to re-open the closed charity establishments. The Palestinian Human Rights Centre also called on the PA to abolish its decisions concerning the closing of 18 social and charity bodies in Gaza and the West Bank following last September's suicide bombing. It said these measures taken by the PA had threatened the Palestinian civil society. The Centre called on the International Community to break its silence and stop the U.S. and Israeli pressure on the PA to close these establishments.

Catholic school prevents entrance of Palestinian girl with hijab

Acre (QP)- The head teacher of an Arab Catholic school in Acre of the 1948-Occupied Palestine prevented a Palestinian Muslim girl from entering her school because she wears hijab. The 15-year-old girl Hiba Al Sheikh, who was studying at the school, decided recently to wear hijab, but Reverend Corico Calila did not allow the girl to enter her classroom provided that she take her hijab off. He claimed that the head covering was a violation of the school regulations. The girl said she has been studying in the school for the past three years, adding that all attempts to convince the head teacher to allow her to enter the school were in vain.

Israel still occupies 30% of Gaza Strip

Gaza (QP)- The annual report of the Gaza Law & Rights Centre for 1997 reported that Israel is still occupying 30% of the Gaza Strip's area where there are about 3,000 Israeli soldiers in military positions near Jewish settlements. It added that there are 18 Jewish settlements in the Gaza Strip in addition to the "Erez" industrial area. The report pointed out that the Jewish settlers continued harassing Palestinians in the area and expanding their settlements under protection by the Occupation soldiers. It accused the Occupation authorities of going ahead with the demolition of Palestinian houses and preventing Palestinians from improving their living conditions through the consequent closures, resulting in about 50% of the Palestinian work force becoming unemployed.
