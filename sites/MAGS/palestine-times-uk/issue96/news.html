Misha’al: Differences between Likud and Labour merely tactical

Amman - PT

The head of the Political Bureau of the Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas), Khalid Misha’al said the Israeli political parties are united regarding the rights of the Palestinian People. He added that the Zionist parties are united in their objectives but differ in the tactics only.

Speaking to the Palestine Times, Mr. Misha’al said the Likud and Labour agree on the issues on the agenda of the final status negotiations with the self-rule authority, namely, the future of Jerusalem, the Jewish settlements, the right of return for Palestine refugees, the borders and cross-points, water and the Palestinian State. Mr. Misha’al stressed that a bet on the Labour Party pushing the negotiations forward in a way that serves the Palestinian People is a losing bet and running after a mirage.

But Mr. Misha’al pointed out that when the Oslo process began, it tackled the easy issues which made the people notice that the peace process was quickly advancing. But when the negotiations reached the hot and important issues, the Peres Labour government took a hard-line position which was evident in deactivating an agreement on the redeployment of the Zionist troops in Hebron.

Commenting on the anxiety shown by the PA and some Arab officials over the possibility of re-electing Netanyahu, the Hamas leader said the PA and many Arab governments dropped the war option and adopted the “peace settlement” as a strategic option. He added that they do not want to deal with Netanyahu, who pulled out his gun. Instead, they find it easier to deal with the Labour Party which introduces a flexible programme to the media although it does not differ from the Likud regarding the actual position on the peace process.

Mr. Misha’al pointed out that Labour is deluding the Palestinian Authority when it talks about the possibility of establishing a Palestinian State. But, Misha’al argues, the State allowed by Labour is the one which has no real content while the Likud rejects the “State” and insists on the self-rule entity. The real difference between the Likud and the Labour is that the first agrees to give back 40% of the West Bank to the Palestinians in the final settlement, while Labour is talking about 50%.

Mr. Misha’al said Hamas does not prefer any Zionist party to another. “We are not interested in who wins in the elections. We do not expect anything good from the Zionist Enemy, whether led by the Likud or by the Labour,” he said.

In reply to a question, Mr. Misha’al said, “We did not try and will not try to influence the results of the Israeli elections. This is not of any interest for us. We are not a part of the Zionist political structure. We are a part of the Palestinian People who are victims of the Zionist Occupation and who are fighting to restore and extract their rights from their enemy, regardless of who is in power in the Jewish State.”

Former Israeli Prime Minister Shimon Peres had accused Hamas of being the reason for his losing the 1996 elections following the suicide bombings implemented by the military wing of Hamas in retaliation of the assassination of the “Engineer”, Yahya Ayyash, in January 1996.

But Mr. Misha’al argued that what made Peres lose the election to Netanyahu was the hard-line and extreme policy adopted by the Labour government at the time, not Hamas military operations. The Israeli Street was responsible for the election of Netanyahu and they disappointed Peres in 1996. He added that Hamas resisted the Zionist Occupation whether under the Labour or the Likud. Hamas fights in all times and against all occupation governments, Misha’al said. But he attributed the absence of Hamas military activities during the last period to “field circumstances” and not to any political accounts. “The Islamic Resistance faces big challenges and therefore the forms of resistance change according to the circumstances, but the will of resistance is continuous,” Misha’al said.

A communiquï¿½ issued by Hamas following the Israeli elections had described the newly elected prime minister Barak as “a new professional terrorist leader, who has exerted all efforts over many decades to chase, assassinate, or arrest our people and their leaders in and out of Palestine.”

“A view on Barak’s electoral campaign which concentrated on his role in the wars against the Arab states, the Palestinian Resistance, and against our people proves that the Zionist General has assumed power in the Zionist State based on his military history and his platform which in turn is based on extremism and arrogance. This proves more that the Zionist Society is inclining more and more towards extremism,” Hamas said.

Hamas said Barak and Netanyahu have identical perspectives regarding the Palestinian issue. It called on the Arab and Palestinian officials not to be optimistic with the coming of Barak to power.

“ It is true that there are some differences between him and Netanyahu in the manner and tactics, but they have identical objectives and perspectives. Moreover, Barak stands on the far right of the Labour Party and he is an extremist hawk. His hands are stained with the Palestinian blood. The platform he adopted concerning the Palestinian track was very clear,” Hamas said

Hamas reminded Arabs and Palestinians of the first speech delivered by Barak after his victory. “ In his first speech he delivered after his winning he asserted that the united Jerusalem is the eternal capital of the Zionist state. He also declared that the Occupation troops will never go back to the 1967 borders and that he will never allow the presence of any Arab army west of the Jordan River. He said that he would not dismantle the Jewish settlements, which will be kept under the Zionist control.”

It also reminded of the bloody history of the Labour. “The Labour Party has come back to power carrying a long history of aggression, oppression and terrorism not to mention Rabin’s policy of breaking our people’s bones or Qana massacre perpetrated by infamous terrorist Peres.”

Settlers race to grab hilltops throughout West Bank

Occupied Jerusalem- Jewish settlers, heeding an earlier call by Foreign Minister Ariel Sharon to grab as many hilltops as possible lest they fall into Palestinian hands, have been on the move in much of the West Bank.

According to Palestinian and Israeli sources alike, the settlers, who are supported and protected by the regular Israeli army, have been executing “daily missions” to seize “as much territory as possible”, before any prospective “territorial settlement” is reached in the context of the Oslo process, particularly in light of Ehud Barak’s becoming Israel’s new Prime Minister.

The land-grab blitz was particularly stepped up following the signing of the hapless Wye River memorandum, when Sharon made his infamous call to seize the hilltops. Since then, the settlers, often at gunpoint, grabbed thousands of hectares in the Gaza Strip and the West Bank districts of Hebron, Bethlehem, Ramallah and Nablus.

According to data provided by the Israeli “Peace Now Movement”, over 23 hilltop settlements were erected during the period from 23 October 1998 to 10 April 1999. The vast bulk of the new settlements and outposts are 1-5 kilometres from existing settlements.

In Gaza, Jewish settlers seized over 80 hectares of privately owned Palestinian land at the Gush Katiff settlements between 25 March and 25 April. Sixty more hectares near the town of Rafah were declared slated for confiscation, apparently for the expansion of the settlement of Moraj. All in all, over 35% of the entire area of the Gaza Strip has now come under settlers’ control. Needless to say, the confiscated areas include the best farm land in the Gaza Strip, and contains most of the increasingly scarce water sources in the Strip.

In the Hebron area, efforts by the settlers to seize “as much land as possible” have been intensive and widespread. The National and Islamic Committee against Land Grab reported in early May that over 500 hectares of land were seized in the Hebron district since the signing of the Wye agreement.

A considerable land grab has been taking place around the town of Yatta, 15 miles south-west of Hebron, where over 25 hectares were confiscated in the month of April alone. Twenty additional hectares were seized a few miles south-west of the nearby town of Sammu, where a new settlement is being built. In addition, hundreds of hectares of farmland belonging to Palestinian farmers along the “front line areas” were confiscated. This land is located mainly adjacent to the former armistice line between the West Bank and pre-1967 Palestine, which suggests that Israelis are seeking to push the borders eastward by a few kilometres.

In the town of Hebron itself, over 100 hectares of privately owned land belonging to villagers from Shuyokh (10 miles north-east of Hebron) and Sair (14 miles north-west of Hebron) were seized. The land grab around Kiryat Arba’a is aimed at expanding the settlement and creating an industrial zone there. It is also aimed at creating a “Jewish metropolitan centre”, linking Kiryat Arba’a and other Jewish settlements around Hebron.

Normally, the settlers employ sinister means to arrogate the land, such as spraying lethal pesticides to prevent Palestinian livestock from grazing. Moreover, they usually root out mature olive trees, ostensibly to obliterate any signs reminding the beholder that the land belongs to Palestinian farmers and was actually stolen from them by force.

In the Bethlehem area, over 220 hectares were declared slated for confiscation for the opening of a new bypass road circumventing Arab villages in the area. That road, which the Israelis claimed was agreed upon in the Wye agreement, will link the settlement of Efrat with the planned settlement of Har Homa at Jabal Abu Ghneim.

The settlement of Efrat itself has been steadily “creeping” at the expense of the neighbouring Palestinian village of Artas (pop. 10,000), just like the rest of the settlements, known as the Gush Etzion, extending between the northern outskirts of Hebron and Bethlehem. These settlements have expanded by 50% since the signing of the Oslo Accords five years ago, and continue to expand at a fast pace, with new blocks of flats and red-tiled apartments being built almost round the clock.

In Ramallah, an exceptionally aggressive campaign of land grab has been going on for the last few weeks, particularly since March, when the outgoing Israeli premier Benyamin Netanyahu decided to go for early elections.

At least 200 hectares of Palestinian land was seized from the two villages of Deir Qeddis and Shabin, 20 miles west of Ramallah, along the armistice line. Again, as in the Hebron area, the goal of the Zionists seems to be pushing the borders eastward as much as possible.

In Nablus, prefabricated houses and mobile homes were erected on land belonging to the villages of Yitma and al Sawiya, apparently for the purpose of expanding the settlement of Rahalim.

Moreover, another settlement—Brakha—was being expanded at the expense of the adjacent Palestinian village of Deir al-Hatab. A few miles away, Israeli bulldozers last month pulverized tens of hectares of farmland and rooted out thousands of olive trees, belonging to villagers from Asala and Nabi Elyas.

Settler population on the rise
The frenzied onslaught of settlement expansion, particularly in the West Bank, is aimed at increasing the settler population from 160,000 to a potential 200,000, which would create a “solid and irreversible fait accompli” in the area. Moreover, a key goal of the current spate of land grab is to enclose Palestinian autonomous areas to prevent their expansion beyond the 10% of extra territory granted them in the second phase of redeployment stipulated in the Wye agreement.

The settler onslaught is in total harmony with Jewish settlement ambitions in the West Bank, mainly “repopulating Judea and Samaria with Jews,” as one of the settlers’ leaders put it.

According to Peace Now, approximately 1,680 new settlement units were built in 1996: 1,900 in 1997 and 3,900 in 1998. The figure for this year is expected to top 5,000.

Needless to say, these “facts on the ground” are likely to make any prospective “final status settlement” between Israel and the Palestinians “fragile and volatile”, if not impossible altogether.

Sheikh Yasin says more must be done to liberate prisoners

Occupied Jerusalem- Hamas founder and spiritual leader Sheikh Ahmed Yasin urged "all active Palestinian forces" to put up a unified and concerted effort to bring about freedom for the estimated 3000 Palestinian prisoners in Zionist detention.

The vast majority of the prisoners are members of the Islamic resistance movements such as Hamas.
Speaking on the 10th annual anniversary of his imprisonment, Yasin said the prisoners cause needed an active campaign on the part of all Palestinians, including action on the popular and street levels.
Yasin praised the role of human rights organizations in caring for the prisoners and following up Zionist repression and maltreatment of them.
Moreover, Yasin called on the United Nations to treat Palestinian prisoners in Zionist jails as prisoners of war (POWs).
Yasin pointed out that the average Palestinian prisoner in Zionist jails lives in constant anxiety as a result of the mistreatment he and his family are subject to.
Meanwhile, a report published last month by the PA Office of Prisoners Affairs revealed that as many as 150 Palestinian prisoners have died since the Zionist seizure of the West Bank and Gaza Strip in June, 1967.
The report pointed out that the 150 prisoners died as a result of inadequate or no medical care and malnutrition.
In addition to malnutrition and absence of a meaningful medical care, the prisoners who died had also undergone physical and psychological torture, including hand-cuffing or prolonged periods, reaching many months, leg-fettering, daily beating, and solitary confinement in squalid condition.
The report said the prisoners didn't die directly of torture but of illnesses caused in part by torture, which would have been treated if medical care had been available.

Meanwhile, Human rights groups in the West Bank and Gaza Strip accused the
Israeli occupation authorities of "criminal negligence" in the death last month of a Palestinian political detainee at a Zionist prison in Bearsheba, south of the 1948-Occupied Palestine.

According to the Palestinian Center for Human Rights (PCHR), Shadi Saqer abu Dahruj, 21, died on 18 May inside his cell at the desert prison in Bearsheba, apparently due to the lack of medical care.
The PCHR pointed out that Dahruj had been suffering from a cardiac problem, adding that the solitary confinement he was subjected to at the Zionist jail "undoubtedly exacerbated his condition."
The PCHR quoted Dahruj's family as saying that Israeli prison officials refused an urgent request from his cellmate for urgent medical care.
The Bearsheba prison is notorious for its medical negligence of Palestinian detainees.
In 1997, another Palestinian detainee, Riad Odwan, died of an acute illness after prison authorities refused to transfer to hospital.
Since 1967, scores of Palestinian detainees lost their lives at Israeli jails due to lack of medical care.
According to Jewish religious laws, it is not permissible to offer a non-Jew medical help, even if he or she faces the danger of death.

“He didn’t pay the bribe, so they tortured him to a clinical death”

Gaza-PT

Ayman Abdul Qader al Amsi is the latest victim of the enduring state of lawlessness and corruption permeating throughout Yasser Arafat’s Palestinian Authority. His story, say many Palestinian lawyers and jurists, is only symptomatic of a deeper and much wider modus operandi in which the administration of justice throughout the autonomous enclaves is entrusted to mostly ignorant junior police officers, with little or no formal training in jurisprudence.

On 11 May, Amsi, 32, was rushed to the Shifa hospital in downtown Gaza with a massive brain hemorrhage, apparently resulting from direct hits with hard objects on the skull while in the custody of the Criminal Investigation Squad (CIS), one of the numerous security agencies responsible to Yasser Arafat.

According to Amsi’s relatives, he was subjected to intensive episodes of torture at the hands of CIS interrogators, which eventually caused the hemorrhage and made him lose consciousness, actually rendering him clinically dead.

For its part, the police claims Amsi attempted to commit suicide after he was interrogated on charges of theft and attempted rape at the village of Tira across the Green line.

However, human rights organizations, including the Palestinian Human Rights Monitoring Group (PHRMG), disputed police accounts, and pointed out that the arrest per se was illegal since the defendant was never brought before a court of law nor arraigned before a judge or formally charged of any wrongdoing.

The Israeli court exonerated him
The story began on 11 February when Amsi was arrested by the Israeli police in Kfar Sava after a woman at the nearby Arab village of Tira claimed he had stolen her money and raped her two years earlier.

Amsi was subjected to a thorough investigation and interrogation, which found that there was no evidence linking him to the crime. Indeed, this conclusion was later corroborated by forensic and genetic tests.

Moreover, after examining his employment records at the village at the time of the incident, it was established that he had worked in a place farther away.

In light of the above, Amsi was given back his identity card and asked to return to his work.

Two weeks later, Amsi returned to Gaza to attend his grandfather’s funeral, only to be arrested by a handful of CIS agents who whisked him away to their headquarters in Gaza to answer questions on the Tira crime.

Amsi insisted from the outset that he was innocent.

He produced an affidavit issued by the Israeli police attesting to his innocence, but to no avail.

His relatives, including the Mukhtar (clan notable), contacted several top PA security officials such as Rashid Abu Shibbak, deputy-chief of the Gaza branch of the Preventive Security Force, who reportedly declined to intervene because of the “delicate and sensitive relationship between the various security agencies.”

Several legislative council members were also recruited to help, but without result.

They asked for money
After a month and a half in CIS cells, the General Investigation Department (GID) intimated to Amsi that if he agreed to pay the sum of $7,000, he could be set free.

According to Amsi’s cousin, Muhammed, the GID later contacted the family and asked rather explicitly for the money, warning that he wouldn’t be freed from jail unless the sum was paid in full. The cousin Muhammed (not the real name) said that the GID later reduced the sum to $2,000.

He added that after it became clear they were after money, “We conveyed the message to them.”

However, that (disclosing police demand for money to PA officials and the media) proved to be risky for the family and for Amsi himself. Infuriated by his audacious step to expose their corruption, CIS agents reportedly beat him mercilessly on the head until he lost consciousness. Worried about having a dead body before their eyes, the CIS summoned a local physician, called Khalid Rustum, who, realizing the seriousness of the case, recommended that he be transferred immediately to hospital.

Signs of torture all over the body
According to Amsi’s medical report, issued by the Shifa hospital, he was brought to hospital on 11 May with bruises all over his body, enlarged pupils of the eyes, and reddish skin around the neck. Moreover, the report pointed out that Amsi was completely unconscious and unresponsive to external effects.

The Palestinian human rights group, known as al-Qanun, issued a statement saying, “Amsi was transferred to hospital after he was subjected to severe torture at the hands of CIS interrogators.” The statement further pointed out that there was no legal base for Amsi’s arrest and continued detention since he was cleared of any wrong-doing by the Israeli police and was attested to his good conduct by the municipal council of Tira, where he had been working for the last 13 years as a sanitation worker.

Finally, al-Qanun expressed concern for Amsi’s life, and voiced its condemnation of all practices of torture by PA security operatives.

The statement demanded that a thorough investigation be carried out to determine the circumstances surrounding the arrest, detention and torture of Ayman al-Amsi, including trying all persons involved.

The statement also demanded that unmistakable instructions be issued to all interrogators whereby they must stop the employment of torture against detainees.

Another human rights organization, the Independent Commission on Human Rights, also condemned the torture of Amsi at the hands of the CIS, saying that all the documents it was able to obtain showed beyond doubt that he was subjected to various forms of torture while in police custody.

The Commission also condemned all forms of flagrant incarceration and brutal maltreatment of detainees, and demanded the PA to seriously look into the case.

Jabali: Amsi tried to kill himself
Gaza Police Chief, Ghazi al-Jabali, dismissed that Amsi was not tortured and that he attempted to commit suicide.

He claimed that Jabali was “in good health”, adding that Amsi had committed a theft and another honour-related crime at the village of Tira.

In response to Jabali’s remarks, which were made during a press conference on the matter, the Amsi family issued a statement accusing Jabali of falsifying the facts and of trying to whitewash the crimes of the interrogators.

The statement said that everything Jabali said was an “utter lie from beginning to end.”

PA police arrests journalist for exposing police brutality

Occupied Jerusalem- Gazi Hamad, editor-in-chief of the Gaza-based Islamist weekly al Risala was released from police custody, thirty six hours after he had been arrested for publishing an article on alleged torture of a Gazan citizen at the hands of police interrogators.
Two other members of the Risala editorial board were still detained for the same reason.
Hamad said he was arrested without an arrest warrant from the attorney-general's office, adding that the arrest was aimed at "silencing us and forcing us to exercise self-censorship."
"They (the Palestinian Authority) are trying to bully us into submission to their undemocratic reign," said Hamad.
Last month, al Risala published a detailed report on the alleged torture Ayman al Amsi by agents of the Criminal Investigation Squad (CIS) in connection with a theft and rape crime at the village of Tira across the green line in Palestine earlier this year.
However, blood and genetic tests showed there was no connection between Amsi and the crime of which he was accused.
Despite that, PA police chief Ghazi Jabali insisted that Amsi was guilty.
Last month, Amsi was transferred to hospital in Gaza with brain hemorrhage, which human rights organizations said was caused by torture in police custody.
For his part, Jabali claimed Amsi tried to commit suicide, a charge strongly denied by his family.

The Palestinian Authority police had also arrested two Islamist activists in Gaza on suspicion of carrying out acts of resistance against Zionist occupation targets.
Palestinian sources in Gaza said Khalil al Sakani and Sa'ad al Arabid were arrested on 22nd of May at the hands of the Mukhabarat General Intelligence.
The sources said Arabid was believed to be the right-hand man of Hamas fighter Muhammad Deif.
The PA often acts at Israel's beck and call in matters pertaining to Hamas and other Palestinian resistance movements.
The PA detains hundreds of Islamist activists, mainly to receive a certificate of good conduct from the Zionist regime and the American Central Intelligence.
On the other hand, the Zionist Chief of Staff, Shaul Mofaz, reportedly met with Muhammad Dahlan, Chief of the Gaza branch of the PA Preventive Security to discuss reviving cooperation against Palestinian resistance movements, particularly Hamas.
According to Israeli sources, the previously-unannounced meeting took place took at the Erez Check point, at the northern edge of the Gaza Strip, just inside the Palestinian territory seized by the Zionists in 1948.
The Israeli Hebrew paper, Yedeot Ahranot, reported last month that the new Israeli leadership was keen at reviving and enhancing "security coordination" with the PA against "terrorism."
In fact, security coordination between the PA and the Zionist regime has never completely ceased, even as the outgoing Israeli premier Benyamin Netanyahu unilaterally suspended the Wye River memorandum, signed under US President Bill Clinton's auspices on 23 October, and launched a widespread campaign of Jewish settlement expansion in the West Bank.

Zionist police repress Palestinian protest in Jerusalem

Occupied Jerusalem- PT

Zionist police on Thursday 27 May brutally repressed a peaceful Palestinian protest in East Jerusalem, injuring several Palestinians including Palestinian lawmakers and newly-elected Arab Knesset members.
The police, some of whom were mounting horses, dragged protesters to the ground and beat them mercilessly, preventing them from reaching the site of a planned new Jewish settlement at Ras el Amud in the heart of East Jerusalem.
Among the people beaten and injured was Faisal Husseini, a member of the PLO executive committee.
Abdul Malik Dahamshe, leader of the United Arab list which won five seats in the recent Israeli elections, described police behavior as "provocative, brutal and extremely violent."
"They wouldn't use this kind of violence if the protesters were Jews," said Dahamshe.
Husseini vowed to continue the protests until settlement activities at Ras el Amud were terminated.
Israeli Prime Minister-elected Ehud Barak, however, seems to be very soft on settlement expansion.
Last month, he refused to promise the leftist Meretz party, a potential coalition partner in the coming Israeli government, to put a freeze on Jewish settlement expansion in the West Bank.
Irving Moscovic, the Jewish millionaire who bankrolls Jewish settlement
activities in Jerusalem and Hebron, said that it was unlikely that Israeli Prime Minister-elect Ehud Barak will bar him from building a Jewish settlement at Ras el Amud in the heart of East Jerusalem.
Moscovic pointed out that Barak was the "godfather of Ras el Amud settlement," adding that the elected Israeli premier had personally endorsed the blue-prints for that settlement and that he was eager to see the old town of Jerusalem being repopulated by Jews.
Moscovic discounted settlers’ fears that Barak might seek to place certain restrictions on the construction of settlements in the West Bank and East Jerusalem.
He argued that Barak himself promised during his election campaign that he wouldn't terminate settlement activities anywhere in the West Bank and especially in the Jerusalem area.

Islamists defeat PLO in Jerusalem College election

Occupied Jerusalem - Hamas and Islamic Jihad supporters easily defeated supporters of the secular PLO in the student election which took place at Jerusalem’s Al Quds University Tuesday, 18 May.

According to college sources, the Islamic bloc al-Wafa (faithfulness) received 28 seats of the 51-seat student parliament. The Fatah bloc, who support Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat, won 20 seats while the three remaining seats went to a leftist coalition representing secular Palestinian groups opposed to the Oslo process.

As many as 4,000 students took part in the election which observers believe represents a parameter of Palestinian public opinion trends.

Earlier this year, the Islamist movement defeated Arafat’s supporters in college elections at major Palestinian universities in the West Bank and Gaza. Pundits in Palestine opine Islamist gains are attributed to two main factors: mass disenchantment with the Oslo peace process and Yasser Arafat’s repressive and corruption-ridden administration; and growing Islamic awareness among the Palestinian masses.

Aides of ex-mayor: “We adopted ethnic
cleansing against Palestinians”

Occupied Jerusalem - Aides of the former mayor of Jerusalem, Teddy Kollek, have admitted that the municipality of Jerusalem under Kollek adopted a systematic policy of discrimination and ethnic cleansing against Palestinians in the Eastern part of the city.

Amire Cheshin and Avi Melamed, along with Bill Hutman, a journalist who had covered Jerusalem for many years, said in a book published today that Kollek used a ruthless quota system to curb the ability of Arabs to build legally in East Jerusalem.

According to the authors, the “housing quotas” for Arab neighbourhoods had nothing to do with urban planning considerations, but were essentially meant to encourage Arabs to leave Jerusalem and limit the Arab population at 28.8%.

The authors said the quotas were outlined in a 1993 document of the city’s planning department that the authors culled from Kollek’s personal files.

“Allowing too many new homes in Arab neighbourhoods would mean too many Arab residents in the city,” has been the essence of the municipality and government policy, wrote the authors.

“The idea was to move as many Jews as possible into East Jerusalem, and move as many Arabs as possible out of the city entirely.”

The authors described the policy toward Arabs as “a ruthless policy, if only for the fact that the needs (to say nothing of the rights) of Palestinian residents were ignored.”

Israel is still adopting a policy of ethnic cleansing against Palestinians in East Jerusalem, mainly in the form of confiscating residency rights and demolishing residential Arab homes in the city.

Israel’s high court okays torture of detainee

Occupied Jerusalem - The Zionist high court has given the Israeli internal intelligence service, the Shabak, a green light to continue using “reasonable physical and psychological pressure” against a Palestinian detainee.

In a ruling issued on 18 May, the court rejected an appeal from Yousuf Al-Bayed to force the Shabak to stop torturing him for the purpose of extracting confessions.

According to Al-Bayed’s lawyer, Shabak interrogators routinely and continually torture him while his hands are handcuffed and his legs fettered.

Moreover, a filthy-smelling garbage bag is forced on his head for weeks.

The lawyer added that other forms of torture are employed against Al-Bayed, including sleep deprivation and playing deafening music round the clock, as well as beating sensitive parts of the body.

The Zionist regime is the only country in the world that sanctions torture.

Legislative council member: Arafat is an autocrat

Occupied Jerusalem - A Palestinian legislative council member accused Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat of being “an autocrat who makes sure that all reins stay in his hands.”

Jamal Shubaki, who belongs to the Fatah bloc in the council, said it was unthinkable and unacceptable that a future Palestinian leader would arrogate as much authority.

“That would be disastrous,” he said.

Shubaki, who was speaking in a seminar in Dura, 15 kilometres south-west of Hebron, on 18 May, said the legislative council was tolerating Arafat’s despotism and autocracy because of his “symbolic status” and “the deep respect he enjoys among Palestinians.”

However, when asked if he considered Arafat to be above or under the law, Shubaki dodged the question, saying Arafat shouldn’t be above the law.

The questioner persisted, asking Shubaki why lawmakers allowed Arafat to reinstate corrupt councilors. Shubaki stumbled, saying that we should start thinking of the post-Arafat era.

PA, Zionist regime deny "secret deal on Jerusalem"

Occupied Jerusalem- The Zionist regime and the Palestinian Authority both denied a report published on 25 May in the American newspaper, " USA Today," on an alleged secret agreement on the future status of Jerusalem between Israeli Prime Minister-elect Ehud Barak and PLO leader Yaser Arafat.
The report pointed out that Barak and Arafat reached a secret understanding whereby Arafat would agree to make the village of Abu Dis the capital of a prospective Palestinian state in return for having control over 150,000 Palestinians in East Jerusalem and over Muslim holy places in the town.
Shlomo Ben Ami, the number two man in the Israeli labor party, dismissed the report as "baseless," adding that Barak didn't have time reach agreements since he won the election only a few days ago.
Ben Ami also said he believed Barak's stance on Jerusalem remained unchanged.
Barak said after he was declared winner in the Israeli elections last week that Jerusalem would remain under Zionist sovereignty.
The Palestinian Authority also denied the report, describing it as "mere speculation."
