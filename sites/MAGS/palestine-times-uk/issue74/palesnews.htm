Israel decides to Judaize education for Palestinian Jerusalemites

Nablus - By Maha Abdel Hadi

The Jewish State began accelerating its measures to Judaize the occupied Arab Jerusalem, confiscate Palestinian lands, construct bypass roads and separate it from other parts of the West Bank. The most serious aspect of struggle over Jerusalem is the demographic one; the Israeli government began its plan that aims to make the Arab residents of the Holy City a minority surrounded by the Jewish settlements although they are the real owners of the land. This was in line with the confiscation of land and real estate, changing the Arab face of the Holy City, aggression on its institutions and antiquities, and the confiscation of civil rights of the Palestinian Jerusalemites in education, construction, housing and travel.

The Israeli government has started an operation to Judaize the Palestinian economic, health and educational institutions in Jerusalem as well as the naturalization of a part of the Palestinian residents. But the Judaization of East Jerusalem is the most serious which is being achieved through the disposition of the majority of its Palestinian residents.

The Israeli Interior Minister Ele Suisa said that "anyone who wants to stay in Jerusalem should abide by the Israeli laws and regulations." He added that the Jewish population of the Holy City should be increased to 80%. More than 110,000 Palestinian Jerusalemites living outside the Holy City have been threatened to have their Jerusalem residence permits confiscated for different reasons as defined by the Israeli authorities. A decision made by the Israeli Supreme Court legalized the measures taken by the interior ministry which allow the confiscation of the residence permits of all Jerusalemites living outside East Jerusalem even if it was for work or study.

According to that decision, the interior ministry began the confiscation of residence permits of many of the Palestinian residents, suspended the reunion of Palestinian Jerusalemite families, and rejected the registration of newborns for Jerusalemite mothers with the aim of evacuating the Holy City of its original population. The most dangerous thing is that the Israeli authorities treat the Jerusalemites who live in the suburbs of East Jerusalem as if they live abroad. Moreover, the Israelis create hard conditions that force the Palestinians to live over the Jerusalem municipal borders especially by confiscating their land, preventing them from building, demolishing their houses for different reasons, depriving them of basic services, and rejecting the reunification of families. Since the last closure in February 1996, the Israeli authorities have not allowed the residents of the West Bank and Gaza who married Jerusalemites to reunite with their wives or husbands.

According to an international organization, the Israeli government obliges the Palestinian residents of Jerusalem to carry four official documents: the identity card (residence permit), a magnetic card, a work card issued by the health ministry (!) and a valid three-month travel card issued by the military authority. In addition, any Palestinian that wants to enter Jerusalem must leave his car outside the city, a form of humiliation.

The struggle against the confiscation of the residence permit has become the main issue for the Palestinian Jerusalemites. These permits were given to Jerusalemites after the June 1967 war which gives them the status of permanent residents which is less than the status of full citizenship.

Israeli sources revealed last year the interior ministry's intention to divest between 50,000 and 70,000 Palestinians of their right to citizenship and residency in Jerusalem. The plan aims to reduce the Palestinian population in the Holy City from 160,000 to only 40,000. The ministry has succeeded to divest about 12,000 Palestinians of their right of residency in East Jerusalem.

Israeli sources said the main aim of the Israeli strategy is to make the Jews a majority in Jerusalem. According to the official plans, the Jewish population of Jerusalem is expected to reach 729,000 by the year 2005 and 817,000 by 2010. Now the population of the Jews in East Jerusalem is about 160,000, which is equal to the Palestinian population although there was not a single Jew in the Holy City before June 1967.

Greater Jerusalem is now forming about 28% of the total area of the West Bank. The Israeli authorities confiscated 71% of the land of East Jerusalem up until 1996. Israel has built 15 settlements and 17 Jewish quarters in East Jerusalem.

Arafat caves in to Israeli ultimatum

Occupied Hebron- By Khalid Amayreh

After weeks of intermittent and often bloody disturbances between stone-hurling Palestinian youngsters and heavily-armed Israeli soldiers, the Palestinian Authority moved swiftly on 14 July to "restore law and order" in downtown Hebron, thus caving in to a reported Israeli ultimatum to that effect and forestalling the widely-rumoured planned Israeli "re-occupation" of PA-administered Hebron, known as (H-1).

The PA deployed as many as 200 unarmed uniformed policemen and half as many Preventive Security agents along the "buffer zone" separating H-1 and H-2. Palestinian police sources said the decision to "take action" in Hebron was made based on Yasser Arafat's instructions. According to Hebron police chief Tarek Zeid, Palestinian and Israeli police officers reached a late-night agreement on 13 July stipulating that Palestinian police be solely responsible for maintaining law and order within the H-1 area, an obvious euphemistic reference to suppressing anti-Israeli protests. In return, said Zeid, the Israeli army agreed to remove the concrete slabs and other impediments which had been placed in the middle of Shallala Street and paralyzed traffic between the Israeli-controlled Old Town and the rest of Hebron. Furthermore, Zeid said the Israeli army agreed to terminate its five-week-old siege on the Hebron district and release youngsters detained in connection with the unrest.

The Palestinian decision to put an end to the protests was apparently a clear subjugation to a "stern warning " by Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu who threatened a "severe Israeli response if the riots continued." Neither Israeli nor Palestinian officials spelled out what Netanyahu had in mind, but the press spoke explicitly of "a planned retaking of Hebron by the IDF and the ousting of the PA from the town." Indeed, PA police and political officials cited this "eventuality" as the "compelling reasons" necessitating the decision to end the riots.

At least two Palestinian boys were killed and as many as 500 Palestinian youngsters injured, some seriously, by Israeli rubber bullets and gunshots during 10 weeks of what was dubbed as "confrontations" between stone-throwing youngsters and heavily-armed Israeli soldiers. The abrupt intervention by PA police to suppress anti-Israeli protests in Hebron drew mixed reactions in this embattled city. Many in the town scolded the PA for "betraying the blood of our youngsters."

"Look, they (the PA) first encouraged the boys to throw stones at the Israelis and now they return to business-as-usual with them," said an embittered shopkeeper at the Bab el Zawwiya commercial district. "I won't resist the Israelis any longer because every time we carry out acts of resistance, we are undercut by the PA," said an indignant youngster who couldn't understand how the PA could treat Palestinian lives so lightly.

Other townspeople, however, welcomed the return of a semblance of normal life to a town that had suffered so badly as a result of the enduring unrest and tension.

"We have been placed for too long between the anvil and the hammer; we need a respite," said Hamed al Karaki, a Palestinian shopkeeper who was forced to close his store by Israeli soldiers following the explosion of a home-made device outside his shop on 9 July.

The relative calm in Hebron is unlikely to last for a long time to come. At best, it will be just a respite.

 

Hamas and the PA

Just as Hamas-PA relations were moving towards a semblance of stability and maturity whereby "tension-management" gained pre-eminence over confrontation, PA police discovered what was described as a "bomb factory" inside a Beit Sahur apartment on 21 July. According to PA Bethlehem police chief, Kamal al Sheikh, the bomb factory, which most likely belonged to Hamas, contained more than 30 kilograms of ready-made TNT explosives, timers and other accessories used in making bombs. On 14 July, a man believed to be a member of Hamas died in Beit Sahur while assembling a bomb.

The raid on the Beit Sahur flat provided the PA with a badly-needed opportunity to take the steam out of the incessant Israeli charges that PA police were not only refraining from fighting terrorism but in fact getting involved in acts of terrorism against Israeli targets. It also carried a certain message to Hamas that the PA would not allow Hamas to cross over the "red lines", particularly those pertaining to preparing or actually carrying out attacks against Israel. But this logic has only ostensible validity since Fateh elements, too, may have been involved in armed attacks against Israeli targets, which really makes the PA-Hamas divide less stark than it seems.

Hamas denied any connection with the discovered bomb factory. A spokesman for the Movement accused the PA of "concocting the whole thing in order to impress the Israelis and the Americans."

"This sly act is characteristic of the PA," said the spokesman, who opted to conceal his identity.

People without land Like a body without a spirit

By: Steve Sozby

Like the question of refugees, settlements and East Jerusalem, the ban on selling Arab land to Israelis goes to the core of the Palestine conflict -- land. At the time of the new land sale ban being initiated by the Palestinian Authority (PA), Israel had already confiscated over 60% of the West Bank and 40% of Gaza. Clearly, the behaviour of a hostile Israeli government has forced the PA to take drastic steps to stave off further loss of Palestinian land. The view shared by the vast majority of Palestinians, who saw too many of their children die and sacrifice for their struggling nation, is that selling land to Israelis is the highest form of treason and should receive the highest form of punishment.

The ban on selling Palestinian land to Israelis began on 5 May when PA Justice Minister Freih Abu Median said that Arabs who sold land to Israelis should be punished with the most severe terms, including death. This is not an evolution in Palestinian political thought. Since the first uprising in 1936, fraudulent Arab land dealers have been targeted as traitors.

The usual chorus of hypocritical U.S. and Israeli denunciation came after the killing of land dealer Farid Bashiti, whose bound and beaten body was found in Ramallah less than a week following Abu Median's statement. Bashiti was reportedly last seen in the custody of Force 17, an elite security arm of the PA. According to the Israeli police, Nadia Dabash, 33, lured Bashiti to the Ambassador Hotel in East Jerusalem where he was apprehended and taken to PA-controlled Ramallah.

Not only was PA security personnel working independently in "unified Jerusalem", embarrassing to Netanyahu, but their actions go against the Palestinian promise not to punish collaborators. The Israeli insistence to protect traitors living in Palestinian areas has seemingly left the PA with only extra-judicial measures to deal with fraudulent land dealers.

The chief Muslim cleric of Jerusalem, Mufti Ikrema Sabri, called Bashiti an "infidel" for selling Arab land to Jews and declared that he could not be buried in a Muslim cemetery. The cleric later told a Friday crowd that these people should be removed from Palestinian society. "You should not speak to them, allow them to marry into your family or mix with them in any way." He also said anyone who sympathizes with the land dealers should be ostracized, including their families. This is the view shared by nearly every Palestinian, Christian or Moslem, leftist or rightist.

"A people without land is like a body without a spirit," said an emotional young man following Friday prayers outside Al Aqsa. "These dealers are killing our country so we have to defend ourselves."

The body of Harbi Abu Sara, 60, from Ein Yabroud village was found near Ramallah with a bullet in the head two weeks later. "In his village he is known as a dealer of land," said Mustafa Liftawi, the mayor of Ramallah. "He sold land to Israelis and he had a bad reputation ethically." Liftawi denied PA involvement in the death, saying it was a family dispute, but the Israelis were not buying it. "There is no doubt that it was people from the PA who stood behind the person who pulled the trigger -- even people in official positions," said Israeli Police Minister Avigdor Kahalani.

A few days later another suspected land dealer, Ali Jambour, 34, from Shoufat refugee camp was found shot dead in Ramallah after reportedly being taken by PA security forces.

While it appears that the PA security forces are enforcing Abu Median's hard-line ban on land sales, Palestinian President Yasser Arafat denied official responsibility for the killings. Palestinians who sold land to Israelis were "isolated traitors and we will act against them according to the law. It is our right and obligation to protect our land." Arafat explained that this was not a new or even Palestinian law. It was first imposed by the Jordanian government after losing the West Bank in 1967, he said. He also claimed the ban had dealt a blow to Israel's settlement policy, which was boosted recently by the impotent American (un)response to the breaking of ground at the Jebal Abu Ghneim settlement in East Jerusalem.

Embarrassed yet opportunistic, Netanyahu contemplated "breaking off" contacts with the PA. "We must consider carefully if we should continue talks with people who represent such fascist theories," said his advisor David Bar-Illan. Other right-wing ministers called the ban "anti- semitic."

Bar-Illan's claiming the PA's land policy as "fascist" is the most hypocritical comment out of the Likud since Ariel Sharon called Arafat a "terrorist." The fact is, since 1948 Israel has banned the sale of Jewish National Fund (JNF) land to Arabs, even if they are citizens. "The Palestinians are mimicking us," says Israeli lawyer Danny Seideimann. "An Israeli (Arab) citizen born and bred in Haifa who wants to buy an apartment on JNF land is not entitled to, while a Jewish resident in the U.S. is."

Losing international support over the Jebal Abu Ghneim debacle, Israeli and American officials have been waiting for an opportunity to put pressure back on the PA. It is no coincidence that Abu Median's statements came after U.S. special envoy Dennis Ross wasted two weeks trying to force Arafat into accepting the fait accompli of Jebal Abu Ghneim and return to "negotiations".

"The U.S. condemns any law or decree that would threaten death against any Palestinian for selling land to Israelis or Jews," said State Department spokesman Nicholas Burns. "That's wrong. It is contrary to what must prevail in the Middle East, which is peace and the spirit of peace." As the main power and so-called peace broker in the Middle East, one would expect that the U.S. would at least try to present a balanced position on the issue of land rights in the West Bank.

Not quite as incredulous as Bar-Illan, Burns articulates a position that is so far from being balanced that even the most docile Arab leaders face increasing pressure at home for supporting the so-called peace process. "This is now the most crucial stage of Oslo," says Ahmed Abu Hassan in Gaza. "If the effort to attain our homeland through negotiation fails, then we have no other option but to return to the armed struggle. We at least know what the rules are of that game."

Any Palestinian teenager could tell both Burns and Ross that the ban on selling land and the killing of land dealers would not have happened had the U.S. stood up to Netanyahu's blatant violation of Oslo and international law at Jebal Abu Ghneim. Clinton's failure to put a stop to the further expropriation of Palestine forced the PA to deal with this matter in their own way.

At the time of this writing, dozens of suspected land dealers are in PA jails. Haaretz newspaper reports that a "hit list" of 16 fraudulent Arab land dealers is being circulated through the PA security establishment. Far from backing down, Abu Median has since threatened Israeli Arab land dealers. "We advise those who carry the Israeli identity card that if they believe they have become Israeli citizens, and because of that they are protected, they are mistaken," he said. "They will be brought to justice one way or another." A few days later Israeli forces were able to protect known land dealer Assad Rajabi from being taken from Jerusalem to Ramallah by the PA. Netanyahu blamed Colonel Tawfiq Tirawi as the PA official behind at least two of the killings and the effort to take Rajabi into custody.

Despite the feeble efforts by Dennis Ross, the crisis is deepening and it seems that the only way to avoid even a bloodier confrontation is a U.S.-imposed Middle East land conference to determine who rightly owns what in the West Bank under the framework of the Fourth Geneva Convention. If Israel is so sure of the legality in which it has come to "own" 60% of the West Bank, then let them open the Land Registration Office for international documentation. Unlike the other offices of the West Bank "Civil Administration," which are located at Beit El near the Jalazone refugee camp, the Land Registration Office is at a secret location inside of Israel and its records are "top secret." This is for an obvious reason -- the fraudulent nature of most of the land transactions that have taken place in the West Bank.

Nearly four years ago, optimists at the White House envisioned that in 1997 the final-status peace talks would begin. Palestinians feel that it is instead the beginning of the "final-solution" for their dream of statehood in historic Palestine. Until it is clear what type of entity will emerge in the West Bank and who will control how much land, those who sell sacred and limited West Bank land to encroaching Israelis will be the first victims in what is sure to be a bloody confrontation.

Hamas Retaliates

Scores killed, injured in a Jerusalem suicide bombing

Occupied Jerusalem - Special for PT

In what is largely viewed as a retaliatory response to the defamation by Jewish settlers of the Prophet Muhammed on 28 June, two Palestinian youths blew themselves up in a crowded West Jerusalem market on 30 July, killing 14 Israelis and injuring as many as 170 others.

The incident occurred around 1:15 local time (10:15 GMT) at the Mehane Yehuda marketplace in West Jerusalem.

Israeli police chief Assaf Hefetz said two suicidal bombers apparently simultaneously detonated two large bombs they were carrying, resulting in the high toll of casualties.

The bodies of the two suspected Palestinians were found in the rubble, but as of now they have not been identified.

Israeli hospital sources said as many as 20 people sustained serious injuries while at least five others were in "very critical conditions."

So, far no Palestinian organization has claimed responsibility for the incident. However, Israeli sources suspect that radical Islamic elements may have carried out the bloody attack.

Israeli intelligence sources described the bombing as a "big disaster."

Knesset member and former Shabak head, Gedeon Ezra, said in a radio interview after the bombing that Israel would need a long time to "absorb" the bombing.

He called on the Palestinian Authority to enter immediately into a full and comprehensive intelligence and security cooperation with Israel for the purpose of "eliminating the terrorists."

Following the incident, the Israeli army imposed a strict closure on the Gaza Strip and the West Bank, preventing Palestinians from entering Israel.

Moreover, Palestinian workers in Israel were ordered to return to their places of residence immediately.

Palestinian and Israeli sources said more collective punishment measures were expected, including massive arrests among people with Islamist orientations and home demolition.

 

REACTIONS

Palestinian Authority Yasser Arafat telephoned Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu to express his condemnation of the incident and condolences over the loss of life in the bombings.

Netanyahu's media advisor, David Bar Illan, described as "very difficult" the telephone conversation.

Bar Illan said Netanyahu scolded Arafat, telling him "to stop talking and start doing something to fight terrorism."

Arafat reportedly assured Netanyahu and latter Israeli president Ezer Weisman that he would do every thing he could to fight "terrorism."

Palestinian observers, however, don't believe that the bombing was aimed specifically at sabotaging the peace process which was in a precarious state long before the incident that a bombing could only contribute to its revival rather than its collapse.

The bombings, while can not be isolated from the overall context of the Israeli of narrowing Palestinian horizons, are likely to have been precipitated by the pasting last month of anti-Islamic posters in Hebron which caricatured the Prophet Muhammed as a pig writing the Quran.

So far, most ordinary Palestinians reacted with conspicuous apathy toward the bombings.

Indeed, virtually all Palestinians this reporter has been talking to pinned "central responsibility" for the incident on the Netanyahu government for pushing the Palestinians to the borders of catastrophe.

"Netanyahu has pushed us to a situation where we would have nothing to lose and nothing to be sorry for or about," said a Hebron policeman who refused to identify himself.

"If he wants suicide bombing, then he will get them," added the man, rather laconically.

MPs: PA acts as an Israeli puppet

 

Occupied Jerusalem- PT

Several Palestinian Legislative Council members have castigated the Palestinian Authority leadership for "acting as an Israeli puppet." The councillors' anger stemmed from PA Chairman Yasser Arafat's decision to order the arrest and trial of several PA police officers accused by Israel of "implication in planning anti-Israeli activities."

Abdul Fattah Hamayel, a councillor from Tulkarm, voiced bitterness at the PA for being "so responsive to Israeli charges and pressure."

"When the PA acts on Israeli charges by arresting and trying Palestinian police officers, it conveys a very bad message which says the Palestinian Authority is just an Israeli puppet," said Hamayel during a Legislative Council session in Ramallah on 22 July. A similar feeling was expressed by Nablus councillor Muawiyya al Masri who accused the PA of showing "appalling submissiveness to Israeli blackmail." Al Masri questioned bitterly how the Israeli occupation authorities could stop the car of Colonel Munir Abbushi, arrest, handcuff and blindfold him before being taken away to an Israeli detention camp amid official Palestinian Authority silence.

"What the Palestinian Authority has been doing in this regard shows that it is but a tool in the hands of the Israelis, nothing but," said al Masri. Hamayel, al Masri and another councillor, Qaddura Faris of Ramallah, unanimously agreed that Israeli occupation authorities were pressing charges pertaining to Palestinian police involvement in anti-Israeli activities solely to extract more political concessions from an increasingly vulnerable and weak Palestinian Authority.

Last month, Arafat ordered the formation of an inquiry commission to look into Israeli charges that high-ranking Palestinian police officers were recently involved in planning and carrying out hostile acts against Jewish settlers in the northern West Bank. Among PA officers detained by the autonomous authorities is Colonel Jihad al Massimi whom Israel had accused of masterminding a planned attack on Jewish settlers near Nablus. Several other lower-ranking officers were reportedly detained or dismissed from duty.

PA accuses professor of harming national reputation

 

Occupied Jerusalem -PT

Palestinian Authority police continued to detain a Gazan professor for the second consecutive week for undermining the national reputation and distorting the PA image."

Fathi Ahmed Subuh, a professor of education at Al Azhar University in Gaza reportedly went on a hunger strike last month to protest his continued detention at a former Israeli prison in Gaza. Subuh, 44 years old and father of five children, was arrested by Preventive Security agents on charges pertaining to submitting questions on a final exam which reportedly were critical of the PA administration. Subuh's wife, Fatima, told the Palestine Times that her husband had not had any problems with the PA and that he was arrested 23 times during the Israeli occupation for being a member in Fatah, the PLO mainstream faction, and for involvement in anti-Israeli activities.

"They (the Palestinian Authority) want to make sure that people have eyes that see not, ears that hear not and mouths that speak not, for this is the perfect environment in which a corrupt and despotic regime can survive and thrive," said a spokesman for the PHRMG who opted not to mention his name.

Amnesty International has declared Subuh a prisoner of conscience and urged human rights organizations to send telegrams and letters of protest to PA Chairman Yasser Arafat.

The detention of Dr. Subuh has been completely ignored by the PA-controlled media.

Report: Israel uses Palestinian prisoners as "guinea pigs"

Occupied Jerusalem-PT

The Israeli Defense Force (IDF) has systematically carried out "scientific experiments" on Palestinian detainees in Israel, Palestinian and Israeli sources said.

A report published Thursday, 17 July in major Arabic newspapers in the occupied territories revealed that "as many as 1,000 drug-related experiments are carried out every year on Arab prisoners in Israeli prisons and detention camps." According to the report, which quoted reliable Israeli sources including Knesset members, the Israeli Health Ministry granted major drug-manufacturing companies in Israel more than 1,000 permits to use Palestinian prisoners as "lab animals" to test the effect of new drugs.

The scandalous practice was initially disclosed by Labour Knesset member Dalya Itsek who reportedly demanded that an independent investigation to that effect be carried out. Itsek revealed that "at least 1,000 experiments involving dangerous drugs are carried out each year on Palestinian prisoners without their knowledge."

The disclosure has not been completely denied by Israeli Health Ministry officials. Ami Livnat, Head of the Drug Department at the Ministry of Health, reportedly admitted that "there has been a 15% increase in the number of experiments on inmates."

The shocking revelation drew sharp reactions from human rights groups, the detainees themselves and their families. Saleh al Haj Yahya of the organization Physicians for Human Rights, described the revelation as "appalling."

"If the report is proven true, then I won't find the words to describe our reactions towards it," said Yahya. Yahya himself revealed that news of the scandal was initially leaked by a Likud activist whom he names as "Moti Areil." However, Yahya wondered why a "Likud stalwart would uncover a scandal as such."

Israeli officials: no comment

Israeli officials, including health officials, apparently sought to move swiftly to limit the damage and "not to make a big issue out of it." Yahya told the Gaza-based weekly, al Risala, that he had tried in vain to contact Israeli health officials on the matter.

"I even tried to contact Dalya Itsek of the Labour Party who spoke about the subject in the Knesset, but to no avail," said Yahya. Raji al Surani, head of the Palestinian Centre for Human Rights, also sought to contact Israeli officials on the matter but without success.

"Nobody can ignore a subject as such...It is a stigma of indescribable proportions," said Surani.

Evidence

Human rights organizations in the occupied territories were not surprised. Representatives of these organizations readily point out that many of the released Palestinian prisoners suffer from chronic symptoms that did not exist prior to their detention. Indeed, the death of at least 45 Palestinians either in detention or soon after their release constitutes prima facie evidence incriminating the Israelis. These deaths have not been investigated as they are routinely dismissed by the Israeli Prison Authority as those arising "of natural causes."

Hamas vows to respond to blasphemous posters

Occupied Jerusalem-PT

The Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) has vowed to respond to last week's anti-Islamic posters in Hebron which depicted the Prophet Muhammed as a pig writing the Qur'an.

A leaflet circulated by the Islamic Movement throughout the West Bank on 6 July attacked Jewish settlers for embarking on "an abominable act as such," warning that the "nefarious sin won't pass with impunity."

"Holding the Holy Qur'an and our Prophet (may God's peace and blessing be upon him) up to mockery is a grand crime that won't pass with impunity," said the two-page leaflet. The leaflet described the blasphemous posters as "a diabolical feat," adding that it was but "a natural extension of the racist practices perpetrated by Zionist occupiers and successive Israeli governments."

Moreover, the leaflet called on the Muslim Umma (nation) to make genuine efforts to challenge Zionist insolence and arrogance. "All doors must be opened for Muslims to defend their faith and religious shrines...and let the shackles of Oslo go to hell." Hamas pointed out in its leaflet that the Zionist crime was merely a reflection of the underlying thinking throughout the Jewish settler population and a significant segment of the Israeli Jewish society.

The Movement also urged Arab and Muslim nations to "immediately stop and sever any normalization with the enemies of our Prophet...for this is the least we can do to protest Jewish profanity against Islam." On 4 and 5 July, Hamas and the Islamic Movement in Israel proper held two large demonstrations in Nablus and Nazareth respectively during which speakers demanded that Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu apologize to the entire Muslim world because degrading the Prophet was not an exclusive Palestinian or Arab affair.

Israeli officials did apologize to the Palestinians soon after the posters were publicized by the media. However, the Israeli government refused, as always, to take any action against Jewish settlers amongst whom anti-Islamic (and anti-gentile) racism is abundantly rife. In fact, children of Jewish settlers in Hebron shouted anti-Islamic slogans in Hebron on 5 July, demonstrating their (and presumably their parents') admiration and support for the perpetrators of the anti-Islam crime.

Thousands swear allegiance to the Prophet

Occupied Hebron- PT

As many as 20 thousand Palestinians swore allegiance to the Prophet Muhammed (PBOH) in Hebron on 19 July amid renewed tension between Israel and the Palestinian Authority over alleged involvement of Palestinian police officers in acts of resistance against Israeli occupation.

The large rally, bearing the title "loyalty to the Prophet" was organized by the Islamic movement in the town in response to blasphemous anti-Islamic posters pasted by a Jewish female settler on Arab storefronts in Hebron three weeks ago.

Several Islamist speakers addressed the huge multitude, reasserting the traditional Islamic stance against the Israeli occupation and the political process and calling on Yasser Arafat's Palestinian Authority and Arab-Muslim states to terminate their subservience to American hegemony.

Sheikh Nayef Rajub, a charismatic speaker from the town of Dura 12 miles south west of Hebron, described the Jewish attack on the Prophet Muhammed and the Quran as "absolutely compatible with inherent Jewish disrespect of other peoples' religions."

"Their latest profanity is no surprise to anybody who reads the Talmud," said Rajub.

The imam of the Grand Mosque in Dura said there was inherent Jewish enmity toward the person of Prophet Muhammed since "he put an end to their settlements in the Arabian Peninsula and exposed them naked before the whole world."

Rajub also blamed Muslim leaders, without naming any of them, for "having the audacity to insult the prophet of Islam."

"We bitterly notice today that throughout the Muslim world the only people who can stand up to Jewish insolence and arrogance are behind bars."

Azzam Salhab, who was introduced as "official representative of the Islamic Movement," urged the PA to release all political prisoners and "not to close the door in the face of those who desire to fight those who usurped our homeland, arrogated our rights, and destroyed our homes."

Salhab, a professor of Islamic Studies at the Hebron University, also called on the PA "to respect political pluralism, freedom of speech and the press, and to put an end it nepotistic appointments in the public sectors."

Moreover, Salhab castigated the PA policy of appointing "municipal committees" to run local councils throughout the occupied territories, describing it as amounting to "raping citizens' rights and confiscating our people's freedoms."

The Islamist organizers of the rally pasted huge posters and posted signs warning Jews against "perpetrating this crime against" and vowing "to burn the hands of those who insult the greatest man in the past, present and future."

Hebron is considered a traditional stronghold of Hamas.

Hassan Salameh sentenced to 1,170 years in prison!

Occupied Jerusalem - Special for PT

"I do not feel sorry for what I did and my wish is to meet God as a martyr." This is what the Hamas military leader Hassan Salameh (26) said after hearing the sentence passed against him by an Israeli military court. Hassan Salameh is a Hamas military leader who planned for a number of attacks against the Israeli occupation soldiers and settlers including three suicide bombings in Tel Aviv and Occupied Jerusalem in February and March 1996, following the January 1996 assassination by the Israeli Mossad of the Engineer Yahya Ayyash, killing 46 Israelis and injuring more than 200.

Israelis start a battle to control Al Aqsa

Occupied Jerusalem - A report prepared by the Jerusalem Institute for Israeli Studies pointed out that the Jewish extremist groups increased their attempts to change the present status of the Holy Shrine (Al Aqsa Mosque and the Dome of the Rock). The Israeli report added that these attempts included simple requests by a number of Jewish official and semi-official establishments to partition Al Aqsa Mosque and allow the Jews to perform their prayers inside it. It stressed that these requests are supported by increasing numbers of Israelis who want to diminish the control of the Islamic Waqf Authority on the Jerusalem Holy Shrine by allowing Jewish establishments to be a partner in its administration. The report published the results of a public opinion poll which showed that 93% of the Jewish Israelis consider the Holy Shrine (known among the Zionists as the "Temple Mount") an important place for them. Some 87% of the respondents wished they could perform their prayers freely there and 70% called for the cessation of the Islamic control over Al Aqsa. The report concluded that the demands of the Jewish extremist groups to allow them to perform their prayers inside Al Aqsa would be enhanced in the final status negotiations with the PLO due to start next year.

Terrorist leader appointed advisor to Israeli prime minister

Ramallah - A Palestinian human rights organization expressed deep concern over the appointment of a former chief of the Israeli Internal Security Service (Shin Bet) as an advisor in Prime Minister Netanyahu's office because of his role in killing Palestinian prisoners. Al Haqq (Right) for Defence of Palestinian Human Rights said General Ehud Yatom (49) was accused of exterminating two Palestinians arrested in 1984 after hijacking an Israeli bus in the Gaza Strip. "The appointment of General Yatom is an

official reward for a man who should be tried for committing an intentional double murder with his own hands. This would encourage other Israelis to perpetrate similar crimes," a statement by Al Haqq said. Subhi and Majdi Abu Jamea were killed on 13 April 1984 after hijacking an Israeli bus with the aim of swapping the Israeli hostages with Palestinian prisoners in the Israeli jails. They were arrested alive but later killed by the Shin Bet officers on their way to detention. General Yatom had admitted last year to an Israeli newspaper that he killed the two Palestinians according to an order from the Shin Bet chief at the time, General Abraham Shalom. Yatom, who was later appointed as chief of the Shin Bet, was appointed as advisor to the Israeli Prime Minister for protection and guardianship affairs. He is a brother of the present chief of the Israeli Mossad, Dani Yatom.

$23 million plan to expand Wailing Wall

Occupied Jerusalem - The Israeli government is preparing for the implementation of a plan to expand the Buraq Wall's yard in the Holy Shrine of Jerusalem. An Israeli paper revealed that the plan aims to expand the western yard of Buraq Wall (known by Israelis as the Wailing Wall). The Wall is alongside the western wall of Al Aqsa Mosque. The paper added that the yard will be expanded to accommodate more than 40,000 Jews. The project, which will cost about $23 million and is expected to be completed in one year, will totally change the face of the ancient yard.

Israeli plans to separate Arab East Jerusalem from West Bank

Occupied Jerusalem - A study conducted by the Palestinian Applied Research Institute revealed an Israeli plan organized to suffocate the Palestinian residential quarters in East Jerusalem and separate them from the other Palestinian occupied territories. The study pointed out that the Occupation authorities confiscated 1,140 dunams (1 dunam= 1,000 square metres) in order to expand Jewish settlements in East Jerusalem last year. They have confiscated an additional 18,300 dunams to construct roads for settlements in these areas. The study added that the Israeli government continued to confiscate Palestinian land in the Holy City in order to create new facts. The Israeli authorities aim to increase the number of Jewish settlers there in order to outnumber the Arab residents in East Jerusalem before the final status talks which were scheduled to start officially in May 1996. In addition, the Israeli municipality of the Holy City took a number of measures against the Palestinian residents such as the rejection of applications for building licences, and the confiscation of their land and their residence permits.

Detained Hamas leader's health deteriorating

Nablus - The family of Sheikh Hassan Yousef, a spokesman of the Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) in the West Bank, said the health of Sheikh Yousef is deteriorating while still in an Israeli jail. They added that the Israelis transferred Sheikh Yousef from Ashkelon to Talmund jail which is known for its bad conditions since the Palestinian prisoners are detained in underground cells. Sheikh Yousef was arrested last March on his way to perform pilgrimage. He had been suffering from rheumatism after having served a lengthy detention at Mascoubiya jail where he was savagely tortured. The family complained that they were prevented from visiting him without any reason. Meanwhile, a responsible source of Hamas held the Occupation authorities responsible for the continuation of the detention of Sheikh Yousef and thousands of Palestinians which violates all international human rights conventions and laws. Hamas considered the detainees as prisoners of war whom the Occupation authorities turned into hostages in order to make deals. Hamas stressed that the releasing of the Palestinian prisoners from the Israeli jails will remain on the top of its priorities.
