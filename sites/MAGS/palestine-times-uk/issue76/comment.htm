Madeleine's Attempt at Mideast Diplomacy Fails

It is difficult to go on a mission of peace when the Middle East is in such a state of turmoil. It is impossible to achieve peace when a people have lost most of their land and continue to lose what is left on a daily basis. When U.S. Secretary of State Madeleine Albright visited the Middle East determined to breathe life into the nearly dead Oslo accords, she failed to come to terms with what the basic conflict between Palestinian and Israeli is all about. It is not just the suicide bombers. They are merely an expression of frustration at the hopelessness and despair that face the Palestinians each day of their lives. In order to cure any disease, a careful diagnosis is first made of the ailment and then proper treatment prescribed.

When settlements stop, when demolition of Palestinian houses halts, when collective punishment becomes a thing of the past, when Palestinians are allowed to live as free citizens on their own land with justice and dignity, when there is no fear of arrest or harassment, then one can talk about reviving the peace talks. Suicide bombings are a result of all these frustrations and so much more. When any people continue to be deprived of their rights, their land and their dignity, they will fight back, for they have nothing left to lose.

When Ms. Albright spoke out against Palestinian resistance at a meeting with Israeli President Ezer Weizman, she backed Israeli demands that the PA crack down on Islamists and stressed that, "We are with you in the battle against terror and the struggle for security."

But the creation of the state of Israel and its continuing infringement on Palestinian rights is the greatest form of terrorism and has been waged by wars, by the slaughter of innocent people, by mass arrests, by collective punishment, by the use of torture, by assassinations, by the confiscation of more Palestinian property, by the takeover of Palestinian homes by Zionist intruders, by the demolition of Palestinian homes and by separating the West Bank and Gaza into two enormous concentration camps. How can anyone speak of peace, of justice, of putting an end to resistance when this ongoing tyranny occurs on a daily basis? And who is this security for? Is this just an elation to be enjoyed only by Israelis? Since 1948, Palestinians have not known the meaning of what it is to feel secure.

And just how can the United States be a sincere mediator in the Middle East when every American statesman and president pays allegiance to not only the very powerful Jewish lobby within the U.S. but to the Israelis themselves? Indeed, lest it not be forgotten, it is American money in the first place that has made Israel the force that it is today and it is American money that has not only supported Israeli settlements on what is left of Palestinian land, but paid for them as well.

Whenever peace occurs, it does so because two conflicting sides want it to. Peace for Israel is only a means to accomplish what it was not able to in war and to continue to push the Palestinians off their land and into a state of desperation that is like a time bomb just waiting to explode. Suicide bombers blow themselves and others up in an attempt to secure freedom for the living and the right for the Palestinians to live on their own land without fear and with dignity as a people who are entitled to their inalienable rights of life, liberty and the pursuit of happiness.

Though Ms. Albright has been pronounced a brilliant stateswoman, she accomplished nothing on her Mideast tour and returned home empty-handed. She may have impressed a few Arab dignitaries, but the cold and hard fact remains that there is no peace and that the Palestinians are more oppressed and more tyrannized than ever before. If war begins in the minds of men, so must peace as well. The real art of diplomacy would be to bring Israel to terms with its own intransigence and violation of not only Palestinian rights, but also human rights.

Ms. Albright stated, "I am not a magician." But magic is not really needed to bring about peace. What is needed is the cessation of Israeli unilateral acts that create more tension and the beginning of Israeli sincerity to bring about a peace that includes all.
