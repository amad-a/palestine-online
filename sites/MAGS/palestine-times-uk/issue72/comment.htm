MIDDLE EAST PEACE DESPAIR

The so-called peace deal signed on the White House lawn in 1993 between Israel and the Palestine Liberation Organization has finally broken down. The latest proof of this failure can be seen by Palestinian Chairman Yasser Arafat's refusal to meet with U.S. Peace Envoy Dennis Ross on the eve of his return to Washington.

There are several reasons for the failure of the Peace Accords. Naturally, if America did not provide Israel with a constant supply of arms and money as well as political support, things might be quite different. Also, Arab disunity and the personal selfish greed of many Arab leaders makes them ineffective in playing any promising role of counteracting Israeli intransigence.

Clinton's Administration is the most pro-Israeli one so far in U.S. history. It seems many of the positions of power in the U.S. government are encumbered by Jews and/or Zionists who endorse further Israeli transgressions. There is also blatant consistency in the shameless yet inevitable double standards that the American government adopts in regard to affairs in the Middle Eastern region especially where Israel is concerned.

It is the American backing that gives the monster it created, Israel, the gall to insist on the building of new Jewish settlements on the ever-decreasing Palestinian land. Indeed, Israelis vow that their nation will one day envelop the whole of "biblical" Israel. And with Netanyahu's insistence to continue building Jewish settlements on 60% of what is left of Palestinian land, even though many of the new buildings are to be unoccupied, it is little wonder that the peace in the region is more distant than ever.

It is no secret either that one of the main reasons the U.S. backs whatever Israel does without question is because of the strong Jewish lobby within the U.S. government. It is no wonder that no U.S. President has ever been anti-Israel! To be so would mean certain death or at the very least kissing the American presidency good-bye.

Even though the latest Israeli spy scandal, involving a senior American official who had passed on sensitive information to the Israeli government, was of great embarrassment to the Clinton Administration, it is unlikely that the scandal will be used to pressure Tel Aviv into freezing the construction in the Abu Ghneim settlement near Arab East Jerusalem.

In order for the Peace Process to thrive, the PA demands that Israel order a halt to the building of the Abu Ghneim settlement. In spite of the fact that Israeli President Ezer Weizman cut off ties with Prime Minister Netanyahu because of his attitude towards the peace-making stalemate, Netanyahu said that he was proud of his decision to build the 6,500-unit settlement.

The Palestinians are victims of a cruel and ruthless oppression that denies them the basic rights entitled to all human beings. They are worse off than the black population during the apartheid in South Africa since not only are they being segregated and discriminated against, they are also being systematically forced off what is left of their lawful land by inhuman methods. The saddest part of the whole Peace Process is that Palestinians are no better off than they were before the peace initiatives and that they continue to lose what is rightfully theirs in the first place.
