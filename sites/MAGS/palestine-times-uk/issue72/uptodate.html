More reactions to anti-Islam posters in Hebron

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (30 June)

The posting by Jewish settlers of anti-Islam leaflets in Hebron on 28 June continued to draw angry reaction among Muslim leaders in Palestine and the Arab community at large, with large demonstrations being planned for the weekend.

Hassan Thbub, who assumes the Wakf portfolio in the Palestinian Authority, accused the Israeli government of "preparing the fertile ground in which such people can grow and thrive."

"They can not just claim that it is an individual act. They know well that this perception of Islam is quite rife among the settlers and a significant segment of the Israeli populace," said Tahbub.

In Hebron itself, political, religious and civic leaders reiterated their condemnation of the sacrilegious act at a meeting which they held at the University Graduate Union building Sunday afternoon.

Ahmed Tamimi, deputy interior minister of the Palestinian authority, pointed out that the poster, which depicted the Prophet of Islam as a pig writing the Quran, was symptomatic of Jewish thinking.

"It reflects their way of thinking and their perception of Islam, the Quran and our Prophet, May God's Peace and Blessing be upon him," said Tamimi before more than 500 local leaders.

Tamimi opined that the profane poster was the moral equivalent of a massacre.

"The person, or persons, who can do this, won't hesitate to perpetrate a massacre against Muslims, " said Tamimi

Tamimi called on Muslims to treat "this blasphemy in the same way that Imam Khumeini dealt with Salman Rushdi's Satanic Verses...because there can be no greater crime than profaning our Prophet and Holy Book."

Sheikh Muhammed Jamal al Natshe, a representative of the Islamic Resistance Movement, said the proper response to this "not unexpected profanity" should be to encourage further Islamic revival throughout the world.

"When those lowly settlers see Muslim states persecute and incriminate Islamist activists, they are encouraged to attack Islam and even profane the Messenger of God, may peace be on him, and the Book of God," Said Natshe.

Arab Knesset members Abdul Wahhab Darawshe and Talab AL Sane'i of the Arabic Democratic List paid a solidarity visit to Hebron on Monday.

Speaking at Hebron City Hall following a meeting with mayor Mustapha Natshe, Talab al Sane'i rejected Israeli claims that distribution of the anti-Islam poster was an individual act.

"The Israeli government stands behind the settlers among whom these morbid thoughts thrive; the government therefore is responsible for the actions of the perpetrators," said al Sane'i

Shiekh Abdullah Nimre Darwish, a leader of the Islamic Movement across the Green Line (Israel) dismissed official Israeli denunciation of the poster as insufficient.

"Condemnation of this despicable act is not enough; the Israeli government should vacate the settlers from Hebron instead of just waiting for the powder-kerg to explode said Darwish.

Sheikh Hamed al Bitawi, a senior Islamist leader in Nablus, said Muslims the world over are invited to defend and protect their religion and their Quran.

"This diabolical act is a flagrant aggression against our Aqida (faith) that calls all Muslims to duty," said al Bitawi.

It is noted though that Muslims in Palestine have refrained from placing the blame on Jews collectively, especially after Prime Minister Netanyahu, and Israel's Chief Rabbi Pakhshi Doron, expressed outrage at the act.

However, this reserved reaction doesn't cover the settlers and Israeli soldiers stationing at the spots where the posters were distributed.

According to Palestinian sources, the soldiers refused to stop or arrest the two perpetrators as they were pasting down the posters on the doors of Arab shops, a charge that has not been denied by the Israeli government.

Meanwhile, the Israeli authorities extended the remand of 26-year-old Tatiana Sosskind who reportedly confessed to having pasted the blasphemous poster in Hebron.

Sosskind, a former immigrant from the former Soviet Union, reportedly said during interrogation that "this is my response to the Arabs in Hebron."

In line with its brutal maltreatment of Palestinians, the Israeli army raided a farm in Hebron on 22 June, beating and arresting Nabil Natshe, 39, a local businessman and former deportee to Southern Lebanon.

Hebron may set pace for a new Intifada

Al Khalil (Hebron)- PT (22 June)

Intermittent clashes between Palestinian demonstrators and Israeli occupation soldiers in down-town Hebron continued unabatedly for the second consecutive week. The clashes, which started on 13 June, generally assumed a monotonous pattern whereby Palestinian youngsters would hurl stones toward rather than at or on Israeli soldiers taking positions along the "partitioning line," with the latter responding rather harshly with "rubber bullets" containing lethal iron nucleus, tear gas, stun grenades, and eventually live ammunition. According to hospital sources in Hebron, around 150 mostly teenaged protesters sustained gun-shot wounds. At least 10 people were reportedly in critical condition, including a 12 years old, Murad Jamjum, whose head was hit by an Israeli sniper bullet, causing him brain hemorrhage, and a 13 years old, Lina Rashid Misk, who sustained a serious wound in the belly, forcing doctors to sever 40 cm. of here impaired intestines.

Palestinian medical sources said many of the inflicted injuries were meant to kill or cause irreversible damage, e.g. paralysis or permanent disability.

"We have seen many of the injured were hit by bullets in the upper part of their bodies, particularly the head, the chest, and the spinal cord," said Yousef al Sharawi, Chief Physician at the Amira Alya hospital in Hebron.

Sharawi dismissed Israeli allegation that rubber bullets don't kill as "rubbish talk."

"These bullets are quite lethal as they could pierce into the body several inches often causing irreparable damage." Al Sharawi cited a case of 75 years old man from Halhul who was seriously wounded in the latest clashes and remained in critical condition at al Ahli hospital. he said a rubber bullet penetrated the elderly man's head, destroying some of the vital blood vessels.

 

Deep Frustration

Palestinian protests in Hebron were ostensibly sparked off by U.S. House of Representatives' decision to recognize Jerusalem as "Israel's united and undivided capital." Indeed, the American decision drew indignant reactions from most Palestinians and was viewed as another proof, if proof was needed, of flagrant American hostility to Arab and Muslim causes.

Never the less, the underlying reason behind the current wave of unrest sees to have more to do with the dying or already-dead peace process than with the American decision.

Observers note that most Palestinians have come to feel cheated and betrayed by a peace process that further narrowed their horizons and made them ever more vulnerable to Israeli insolence and hegemony. Manifestations of the Palestinians' collective ordeal are everywhere. Settlements are creeping in all directions, closing in on Palestinian towns, villages and hamlets turning them into "shutt-in ghettoes" deprived of any prospects of future growth or demographic expansion. The Judaization of Jerusalem, meanwhile, is continued on a frenzied pace, and concerted efforts are being made to achieve demographic "Jewish" continuity between the holy city and Jericho, thus cutting off the West Bank into two halves.

For its part, Yaser Arafat's Palestinian Authority seems to be completely naked, powerless, and demoralized. To illustrate its dismal state of affair, some PA officials are beginning to speak of reverting to the status-quo ante, a return to the direct Israeli occupation of Palestinian towns now under exclusive PA control.

Last week, a leading Fateh leader in the West Bank, Abbas Zaki, recognized that "the boat could be rocked altogether in the occupied territories."

Speaking at the PA Ministry of Information office in Hebron on 19 June, Zaki pointed out that the Palestinian people are increasingly fed up with the peace process and with what he called PA's "futile treatment of the current crisis."

"This situation can't be sustained for much longer. Either we advance forward or return to confrontation, and should we be forced to revert to violence, it wouldn't be confined to us; there would be a regional conflagration with unpredictable ramifications."

Anger in Nablus

Zaki's predictions don't seem to be far fetched. On 21 June, as many as 10.000 townspeople took to the streets to protest the House of Representatives decision on Jerusalem and America's sinister alliance with the Zionist entity. The huge mass of humanity shouted in unison "with soul and blood, we shall protect Jerusalem" and "we are willing to die so that Jerusalem may live." The rally, the first joint Islamist-nationalist endeavor of its kind, was addressed by several speakers, representing Hamas, the PA, Fateh and leftist groups.

Palestinians: conditions at concentration

camp reached explosion point

By: Khalid M. Amayreh

Occupied Jerusalem (18 June):

Conditions at the Meggido detention camp near Jenin, where nearly 600 Palestinians are detained by Israel as "hostages," have reached the explosion point, a report issued by the Jerusalem-based Prisoner Club revealed on Wednesday.

The report disclosed that Israeli "Special Forces" stormed the detention camp several times in the last few weeks, severely beating up detainees who were protesting draconian Israeli measures at the camp.

According to the report, which was published in leading Arabic newspapers in the territories, scores of prisoners and the so-called "administrative detainees" were either injured or suffered from gas suffocation during the storming of the camp by an IDF crack unit.

Israeli troops reportedly raided the camp at least three times in the last three months, with the most violent and savage attack taking place on 19 March, resulting in as many as 20 injuries among the detainees.

According to information smuggled from the maximum-security camp, Israeli soldiers in full combat gear stormed detainees tents, beating them "in bestial manner," and putting their clothes and belongings on fire.

Moreover, Israeli soldiers equipped with gas masks threw as many as 50 gas bombs throughout the camp, causing asphyxiation to many detainees.

Five detainees reportedly fainted, and one, who apparently had cardiac problems, collapsed unconscious consequently..

The Prisoner Club report blamed the Palestinian Authority for not paying sufficient attention to the detainees cause.

"We, once again, appeal to President Yasser Arafat to take immediate action to save our brothers from the claws of Israeli savagery and barbarism," said the report.

The report pointed out that Israeli army sought to conceal its "genocidal and disgraceful practices against the defenseless detainees by cutting off the camp from the outside world."

On Tuesday, 17 June, the Israeli Prison Authority, issued a statement, admitting that conditions at the Meggido were not "idealistic."

The statement said that hundreds of administrative detainees (held without charge or trial) were being transferred to other prisons.

Meggido Prison Chief Nera'am Gold-Brom told the Hebrew daily "Ha'aretz" on 17 June that "sub-standard conditions contributed to disturbances and the death of several inmates at the camp."

Meningitis Outbreak hits Gaza Strip

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (11 June)

Palestinian Authority health officials declared a state of emergency throughout the autonomous enclaves Wednesday in an effort to combat an acute outbreak of meningitis in the Gaza Strip and parts of the West Bank.

Riyad al Zanoun, who holds the health portfolio in the PA, revealed in a press conference on 11 June that as many as 350 cases of "meningitis" were hospitalized in the Gaza Strip during the last few days.

The Palestinian official exhorted the public not to panic, stressing that the disease was mostly of the relatively benign viral kind, not the more serious bacterial, although a few cases of the latter category were diagnosed in the strip.

He called on the public to thoroughly wash and rinse fruits and vegetables and not to drink contaminated water.

Many Palestinian physicians voiced increasing pessimism at the deteriorating state of public health within the some of the autonomous enclaves.

Dr. Ghazi Ikteit, a general practitioner in Dura (about 12 miles south-West of Hebron) disclosed that he had been receiving scores of children of late suffering from various kinds of infections in their digestive systems.

Ikteit opined that the infections were in the most part the result of drinking contaminated water.

Dura, a town of 25,000, reportedly has been having an acute water shortage this summer, forcing townspeople to fetch water from exposed and antiquated wells dating back to the Ottoman era. Dura mayor, Mahmud al Nammura, admitted that the municipal council was completely powerless to solve the problem.

"The Israelis are stealing our water, and the Palestinian Authority can't help us, hence we are going thirsty, this is the problem," muttered al Nammura with a tone of frustration and hopelessness.

Earlier, Palestinian officials intimated that the Egyptian leadership, particularly al Baz, cajoled Arafat into dropping his demand that "an absolute and complete halt to settlement building" be reached before the resumption of talks.

The Egyptian pressure on the Palestinians was lauded by Israeli officials who described it as "very positive."

The usually hawkish minister of Justice, Tzahi Hanegbi, uncharacteristically praised "Egyptian mediation efforts," opining that the Egyptians "were becoming more equitable and more moderate."

Hanegbi, who is avowedly anti-Arab since his college years at the Hebrew University, said during a radio interview with Cal Yisrael on 9 June that Egyptian efforts were making a constructive contribution.

PALESTINIANS HEAVE SIGH OF RELIEF FOLLOWING THE

SACKING OF ATTORNEY-GENERAL

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (7 June)

Lawyers, Judges, and human rights activists as well as many ordinary Palestinians heaved a sigh of relief following the sacking by Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat on Friday 6 June of the widely despised Attorney-General Khalid al Qedreh.

Arafat fired al Qedreh reportedly after the 70-years old had "outlived his usefulness" and become an "intolerable liability," in the words of a PA ministry of justice official who asked for anonymity.

"The man became like an inseparable stigma attached to the judicial system of the Palestinian Authority. He had to be removed," intimated the same official.

On 6 June, the Palestinian Independent Commission for Citizens' Rights issued a statement expressing profound satisfaction at the sacking of al Qedreh.

The statement, captioned "Victory for Justice," said the "presence of this man in the crucial post of the Attorney-General did an incalculable disservice to the Palestinian Authority and the Palestinian people at large.

"He always twisted the arm of the law, violated the letter and spirit of the law, made undue interference in court proceedings, concocted charges against innocent people, and, not the least, ignored and even defended human rights violations by PA security agencies.

"All this," the statement went on, "made him a premier obstacle impeding the administration of justice."

Al Qedreh, a veteran Fatah activist who came to be hated by the movement's younger members for his "excessive hypocrisy and sycophancy" is particularly blamed for the deterioration of Press freedom and expression within the PA enclaves.

In 1995, he ordered the closure of the pro-Hamas weekly "al Watan" and its sister, al Istiklal, which was published by the Islamic Jihad organization.

In both cases, al Qedreh accused the two papers of "incitement" and "endangering paramount Palestinian national interests."

Such vague charges were often evoked by al Qedreh to justify and repress the detention without charge or trial of journalists, writers and human rights activists.

"I never felt that he ever tried to uphold the law," said Ghazi Hamed, Editor of the Gaza-based Islamist weekly, al Risala.

Al Qedreh was also unpopular within Fatah.

During the Intifada, he was shot in the leg by a disgruntled Fatah activist following wide-spread rumors that he embezzled large sums of money destined to young intifada activists. He has not fully recuperated from that injury.

Other critics opine that al Qedreh did only what his ultimate boss, Yasser Arafat, told him to do.

"His main duty was to appease and please Arafat, and, paradoxically, he ended up hurting and harming Arafat," said a clerk at Gaza municipal council who expressed satisfaction at al Qedreh's dismissal.

For his part, al Qedreh denied that he was sacked by Arafat, arguing that he submitted his resignation to Chairman Arafat for reasons having to do with his deteriorating health.

However, whether al Qedreh resigned or made to resign, his disappearance from the Attorney-General Office is a cause for relief among most Palestinians.

ISRAELI LABOUR PARTY ELECTS EHUD BARAK AS NEW LEADER

Occupied Jerusalem- (5 June) by Khalid Amayreh:

As had been widely expected, the former Chief of Staff of the Israeli army, Maj. Gen. Ehud Barak, was elected on 3 June as the new leader of the opposition labour party.

Barak achieved a resounding victory, receiving 51% of the total labour vote compared to 28 per cent of for his closest rival, Yossi Beilin, a relatively dovish figure supporting the Oslo peace process.

The other two contenders for Chairman of the Party, Prof. Shlomo Ben Amy and Knesset member Iphraim Sneh, received 14% and 6% of the vote respectively.

Following his victory, Barak promised to "rearrange our house" in order to be able to bring down the government of Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu which he called "a failure.."

Moreover, Israel's former top soldier also promised to regain lost confidence with Arab leaders, and to continue the peace process with the Palestinians.

According to observers, Barak's resounding victory was largely possible due to his military credentials and the fact that he is viewed as "a spiritual son of Yitzhak Rabin."

Indeed, many Labour Party leaders, including dovish figures such as Uzi Bara'am, adopted the idea that only "a Mr. Security" like Barak could beat Benyamin Netanyahu in the next Israeli general election.

Hence, Bara'am and camp, stood behind Barak, not so much because they are infatuated with his ideas but rather because he offered the best possible chances for a Labour return to power in Israel. Bara'am headed Barak's election team.

 

TWEEDLEDUM AND TWEEDLEDEE

Barak will likely present himself as a warrior-turned peacemaker following the example of assassinated Rabin. However, many people in Israel, including some influential labor party members who know him closely, contend he is a mere replica of Netanyahu.

Critics point out that, like Netanyahu, Barak opposed the Hebron redeployment agreement because he believed it didn't provide sufficient security guarantees for the small Jewish enclave in the city.

Moreover, he opposed expanding Palestinian Authority to the rural areas of the West Bank because he believed it would undermine Israel's vital security interests.

More to the point, Barak holds more or less similar, if not identical, ideas to those held by premier Netanyahu on such final status issues as Jerusalem, Jewish settlements, and the refugees.

On Jerusalem, Barak believes Islam's third holiest site must remain "Israel's united and undivided capital," and advocates the intensification of Jewish settlement-building in East Jerusalem.

The new Labor Party chairman also believes Israel should annex all major settlements in the West Bank and keep the Jordan valley under effective Israeli control.

Concerning the refugees, Barak, and all other labor Party leaders for that matter, adopt the "alternative homeland" concept, which says Palestinian refugees ought to be resettled in the Arab world, preferably in Iraq, Lebanon and Jordan.

Barak, never the less, remains novice politician. He actually alluded to this fact when he asked his supporters to give him a chance, arguing that he was only 4 years old when his predecessor Shimon Peres began his political career more than fifty years ago.

Barak is also reputed as often indulging in "slips of the tongue." In 1995, when Yitzhak Rabin appointed him as Foreign Minister, he described Gulf Arab leaders as "a handful of white robes." He later retracted the remark, saying it was a slip of the tongue.

ISRAEL TO TAKE MEASURES AGAINST PA FOLLOWING

KILLING OF THREE "LAND TRAITORS."

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (2 /6 / 1997)

The killing in the West Bank recently of three land dealers who were involved in selling Arab land to Jewish settlers has apparently overshadowed and could undermine Egyptian efforts to resume the stalled talks between Israel and the Palestinian Authority.

On Monday, 2 June, the Israeli government reiterated earlier charges that the PA planned and executed the killing of the three Israeli agents.

Following a discussion of the issue by Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu and political and security aides, an Israeli officials said "certain punitive measures (against the PA) have been decided."

The official, Internal Security Minister Avigdor Kahlani, refused to spell out the nature of the measures taken, but said that Israel "could not ignore the murder of innocent people."

However, pundits here believe Israeli sanctions against the PA might include restricting the movement of PA political and especially security officials outside the autonomous enclaves or area (A) which is under exclusive PA control.

Moreover, Israel may well embark on imposing an intermittent or open-ended closure on Ramallah to which the three land dealers were abducted and where they were subsequently killed.

Israel actually took the draconian measure last year following the Palestinian mini-intifada which erupted after the Netanyahu government decided to open a subterranean tunnel beneath the Al Aksa Mosque in Jerusalem.

Israel may also demand the extradition of Col. Tawfik al Tirawi who is widely accused by Israeli intelligence officials of masterminding the killings.

In the meanwhile, the Israeli General Security Services (the Shabak) contacted all "vulnerable land dealers" Sunday night, urging them "to be careful."

Israel's police chief, Assaf Efets, said he received a confidential list containing all the names of land dealers sought after by the PA.

Efets didn't say how he obtained the list. However, observers opine that Efets likely got the list from the Shabak, given the fact that most land dealers are actually Shabak agents.

For its part, the PA, which doesn't seek to conceal its satisfaction with the liquidation of "land traitors," moved to, though belatedly, to counter the Israeli propaganda blitz.

Freih Abu Maddin, who holds the Justice portfolio in the PA administration, Israeli charges that the PA was involved in the killing of the collaborators as "utterly mendacious, silly, and politically motivated."

Speaking during a radio interview Monday morning, Abu Maddin said the Palestinian authority had nothing to do with the killings.

He accused the Israeli External Intelligence, the Mossad, or the Shabak, of liquidating the collaborators.

"This is often done by the Israelis, when collaborators finish their job and become a burden, they destroy them, " he said.

The liquidation of land dealers, as it is dubbed throughout the occupied territories, is received with profound satisfaction by most Palestinians.

"We are actually faced with a dilemma in relation to those land dealers. Either we get rid of them, or allow them to kill our present and future in return for a few dollars from Jewish settlers," said Sheikh Ahmed Amre, and Islamic activist from the Hebron area.

"You know, if we allowed land dealers to have their way, God forbid, there would be no place left for us in Palestine in a few decades," he added with marked determination .

Furthermore, Palestinians scoffed at concerns voiced by the Israeli government over human rights.

"It is like Hitler lecturing us on the sanctity of human life," one Palestinian teacher said in apparent reference to Israeli hypocrisy.

ISRAEL BLAMES PA FOR LAND DEALER'S DEATH

Occupied Jerusalem: by Khalid Amayreh (1 / 6 / 1997)

Israel today accused the Palestinian authority of responsibility for the killing on Saturday of a suspected land dealer on the southern outskirts of Ramallah.

Avigdor Kahlani, the Israeli Internal Security Minister, said today at a press conference that he had no doubts that Palestinian Authority agents carried out "the murder" of Muhammed Ali Jamhour.

Jamhour's body was found on a side road south of Ramallah, with his body riddled with bullets.

Kahlani said all clues and circumstantial evidence point a finger of accusation toward the PA which he accused of openly encouraging the liquidation of land dealers suspected of selling Palestinian land to settler organizations on the West Bank.

Obviously worried that the killing of land dealers has assumed phenomenal proportions, Kahalani warned "vulnerable persons" against going to areas under PA control.

"If I were a land dealer, I would be more careful," he said

On Saturday night, Shai Bazak, the prime minister's spokesman, was quoted on Israel Radio as saying that "land dealers are being murdered with the open encouragement of senior figures in the Palestinian Authority," adding that this "raises a question mark over the authority's readiness to maintain peaceful relations with Israel."

In addition to Jamhour, two other land dealers were killed of late, Farid Bashiti and Rebhi Abu Sara.

On Sunday morning, a previously-unknown group calling itself "Holy Land Guardians" claimed responsibility for "the liquidation of land collaborators."

An anonymous caller told the Ramallah-based daily, al Hayatul Jadida, the Holy Land Guardians would see to it that all land dealers working for the Israeli intelligence are killed.

Recently, the Mufti of Palestine, Sheikh Ikrema Sabri, issue a Fatwa or Islamic theological ruling, considering land dealers apostates outside the bounds of the Muslim Umma.

Apostates are treated as disbelieves, hence they are boycotted by all Muslims for their treachery, and upon death, they are not given Muslim burial nor are they allowed to be buried in Muslim cemeteries.

Sabri's edict was supported by the Sheikh of Al Azhar in Egypt, who Muslims who sell their land to Zionists interests in Palestine committed a grand perfidy against, Allah, His messenger and the community of Muslims.
