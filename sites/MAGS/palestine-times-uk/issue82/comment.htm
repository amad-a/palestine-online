The Sins of Oslo

It is amply clear by now that very few dignified Palestinians are still harbouring any illusions about the "merits" of sticking to the deformed agreement called the "Oslo Accords."

Even Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat, whose PA is the main brat of Oslo, now recognizes, though somewhat belatedly, the "scandalous flaws" inherent in that agreement.

On many occasions, Arafat pointed out that "if the opposition can cite one flaw in the agreement, I can cite a thousand flaws."

Arafat, a man who accords little regard to honesty, said on the eve of the agreement that it would culminate in an independent Palestinian State with Jerusalem as its capital.

However, the former Israeli Prime Minister, Shimon Peres, who co-engineered the agreement, was quick to rebuke Arafat for his excessive wishful thinking and distorted interpretation of Oslo by saying "I can't prevent Arafat from dreaming."

So, from the very inception, the agreement was vague to the extent that both parties were able to hold substantially different and even diametrically opposed understandings and interpretations of it--otherwise it would have been reached.

Needless to say, the vagueness always worked in Israel's favour, since the Labor government, and even more so the current Likud government, sought to translate its peculiar understanding of the agreement into tangible facts in Jerusalem as well as the rest of the Occupied Territories, which thanks to the agreement ceased to be "occupied territories" and became instead "disputed territories."

To this the Palestinian Authority reacted by making loud noises that made little impression, if any, on "the one and only sponsor of the peace process," the United States.

Indeed, that has exactly been the pattern since the conclusion of Oslo and the establishment of the Palestinian Authority, whereby the Israelis would build more Jewish settlements, confiscate more Arab land, destroy more Palestinian homes, and narrow Palestinian horizons on a daily basis while the PA would make vociferous but innocuous protestations, imploring a hypocritical world to "save the peace process" and warning of "dire consequences if Israeli intransigence is not checked."

The Americans, let alone the Israelis, understood all along Arafat's dilemma. They knew from the outset that he had no choice but to stick to Oslo because PA survival depended on Oslo, and the undoing of Oslo would necessarily mean the termination of the PA.

More to the point, the Americans and the Israelis sought to make use of that dilemma to its fullest potential, e.g. by forcing designs on Arafat that would effectively liquidate the Palestinian question.

Arafat himself made strident steps in budging to American-Israeli dictates. He agreed (according to a recent security memorandum presented to Israel and the United States) to allow Israeli and American agents to decide who of the Palestinians would be arrested and how long he or she would stay in jail. He also agreed to report to his two "partners" regularly on the activities of the "opposition."

Nonetheless, the Chairman's disgraceful submission has had no effect on the Israeli government's frenzied settlement expansion in the West Bank.

The Israeli premier Benyamin Netanyahu said plainly that he would not "give away" the "land of Israel" to the Palestinians. He added that in any prospective settlement with the Palestinians, Israel would still keep more than 60% of the West Bank. Jerusalem is, of course, non-negotiable.

In other words, the Israeli stance vis-?-vis the Palestinians is "what you see is what you get, and don't ask for more because you won't get anything."

Certainly, the Israelis and the Americans would be more than happy to bring about cosmetic improvements here and there and would also be especially generous in offering economic aide and financial incentives to Arafat's notoriously corrupt Authority in return for agreeing to "settle for a bird in the hand is better than ten in the bush."

Unfortunately, there are no genuine indications that Arafat and his money-grabbing sycophants won't settle for that. They most probably will.

But that won't be the end of it. In fact, it would mean that the conflict would take its full historical course, which in turn would end in Israel's ultimate demise and disappearance, like the Crusades.
