Moreover, the years of sanctions have failed to dislodge Saddam's regime. They have only succeeded in punishing the people who are guilty of no crime in the first place. Since the Gulf War, more than one million people have died because of sanctions, many of who are children. The deaths have been due to disease, malnutrition and lack of medicine. Water treatment plants presently operate at only 50 percent capacity and most sewage processing plants are not implementing chemical treatment. The two great and historic rivers, the Tigris and the Euphrates, have become reservoirs for untreated sewage and industrial waste, thus exposing the people to further water and air borne illnesses. Too, there is serious lack of basic medical supplies such as syringes, oxygen, anesthesia, and other medical equipment has broken down such as incubators.

Instead of protecting human rights, indeed, the sanctity of life, the UN Security Council continues to contribute to the collective punishment of the civilian population and ultimately to the countless deaths incurred by the prolongation of the sanctions.

For the Iraqi people who have already suffered more than is bearable, these carriers are weapons to wage death against the helpless civilian population.

The genocide of the innocent ensues and we, the world, are their executioners.
