The Whole Western World Against Sudan! Why?

By: Randa Awadh

When one reviews twentieth-century Sudan, one sees a poor, backwards, weak and shattered state, always run by others. Despite its rich natural resources, it still has not managed over the past 100 years to establish a solid economy or to build an economically independent state which would not be in need of the mercy of the World Bank and could avoid the interference of industrialized countries.

Since al Basheerís government came to power, things started to change and reform policies emerged. It started by choosing free, strong-willed, independently spirited men and women, proud of their heritage and religion and unaffected by foreign influences to run the country and to make use of its resources in order to establish an independent economy and to lead the nation into the twenty-first century, strong and capable of facing the world.

It was unfortunate for Sudan that it was in the way of the Zionistís plans for the African continent contained in a master plan with multiple targets to achieve. Some of these targets are:

1. To control the Nileís resources and mid-Africaís natural resources;

2. To control the countries surrounding Egypt. Egypt is a major Muslim country, considered to be a leader in the Arab world. Despite the fact that it was the first country to sign a peace agreement with Israel, it is evident to all what the real Egyptians feel in their hearts; they are from a Muslim nation that rejects Israelís occupation of Palestine, including Muslim and Christian holy places in the West Bank. Deep down they disagree with the peace agreement signed with Israel. It is inevitable that one day Egypt will be led by its true representatives who will not be pawns for Israel. To prepare for that day, Israel is planning to create a ìvice gripî around Egypt by controlling the Nileís resources. And having isolated Egypt, the planting of pro-Israeli governments in the neighbouring countries will take place, if it hasnít begun already.

3. To undermine Muslim power in mid-Africa. Their plan is to weaken the Muslim presence in mid-Africa by creating civil wars, such as that in Rwanda, and stirring up trouble among tribes, such as in Zaire. Amid the resulting chaos, Israel aims to establish a solid foothold in the area and to gain control of the natural resources, including the Nile, thus threatening the Churchís interests in the area. Controlling these areas will separate Muslims in the south from those in the north, thereby preventing any future coalition among Muslims.

It seems that this plan has been under way for a long period, but Sudan, with its present regime, proved to be aware of that plan. All Zionist efforts through political pressures and economic sanctions did not succeed in controlling Sudan. On the contrary, it seemed to strengthen the government and helped in uniting the Sudanese people. As a result, stronger measures were taken through neighbouring countries. Initially the southern war was mounted by providing Garang and all the factions which followed him with supplies of weapons, ammunition, money and the expertise of the Western propaganda to give a legitimate right to the war, but that did not prove effective in deterring or breaking the Sudanese government, so other neighbouring countries were drawn into attacking Sudan with military support from the West on the one hand and direct guidance, involvement in planning and even executing attacks by Israeli army officers and their intelligence service on the other.

By Godís guidance and the wisdom of Sudanís leaders, Sudan managed to revoke all major attacks and to make peace agreements with many opposing factions, which was considered to be a huge blow to Garang and his followers.

 

Albania and the undermining of Islamic presence in Balkan

By Nabeel Shabib

The recent incidents in Albania were seen as a mere eruption of spontaneous rebellion by people who were frustrated because they lost their life savings after the collapse of fraudulent financial companies in which their savings were invested. This is exactly what happened but observers did not explain the consequences of these incidents nor did they put them in their historic framework, especially in the short period after the collapse of Communism.

Developments in Albania should be seen as another chapter of the Balkan scene which aims to limit and undermine the Islamic presence there. The Communist regimes in the area failed to efface the dogmatic and historic essence of more than 12 million Muslims of different European races living in Balkan.

The Muslim Albanians constitute more than half of the Balkan Muslims. But for political reasons and to be contained, they were distributed over a vast area and became minorities in their original homelands, as in Maqadonia where they constitute more than 40 per cent of the population (although the authorities say there are only 22-30 per cent) and in Kusovo of former Yugoslavia where they form more than 95 per cent of the population.

The Muslim Albanians in Albania itself constitute more than 90 per cent of the population. The Western estimations put them at only 50-70 per cent. This reminds us of the Western reports during the Communist era which estimated the Muslims in the former Yugoslavia at less than 1.5 million but in fact there were about 6 million Muslims after the collapse of that country.

Albania has been a target for the ambitions of the regional powers. The Serbs who control Kusovo have ambitions in the northern part of Albania. Greeks also have ambitions to annex the southern part of that country by instigating an Orthodox minority living there. Athens did not raise the 'rights' of this minority during the Communist era, but it began doing so after the collapse of the Communists; it has also been enhancing the missionary activities among Muslims in the area since that time.

The armed insurrection that broke out in Albania last February was a spontaneous result of the Western policies and local events. Albania was the only European Communist country of which the Western governments repatriated its immigrants instead of using the Albanians to pressure the Communist regime at the time. The West did not show any sign of cooperation or financial support to Albania although the Communists sought such cooperation to reduce pressure on them. The Western governments wanted the Communist regime in Albania to continue until a pro-West political party was created in that country. They feared the emergence of an Islamic power in a country that was described as the only Muslim country in Europe.

The West searched for a power to adopt the Western capitalist perspectives and finally they had the Democratic Party, led by the present President of Albania Sali (Saleh) Berisha. Mr. Berisha was a Communist until the collapse of Communism. The Westerners also activated the missionaries which were the only organizations that provided the poor people of Albania with financial aid. They also entered into that country through the traditional means of corruption: narcotics and pornography.

The missionary and corruption campaigns were well-planned and preceded attempts to establish 'democratic' and economic structures in Albania. The West wanted a base in that country which eventually connected with the Western morals that contradicted Islam, the religion of the overwhelming majority of Albanians.

The direct contacts between Mr. Berisha and his party on the one hand, and the support he received from the West, particularly the United States of America and Germany, on the other, transformed him from an unknown Communist activist to leader of a party that carried the title of the Western 'democracy' and adopts the extremist capitalistic ideologies. He achieved a marginal success in the first presidential elections and did not hesitate to suppress his rivals in the May 1996 elections, which prevented them from running for the elections. He forged the results in order to remain in power. His party governs the country in the same autocratic manner of the Communist regimes.

President Berisha supported the fraudulent investment companies which mainly depended on arms trading in Bosnia and were involved in drug trading and laundering money. The government continued its support of these usurious companies despite warnings of being close to bankruptcy. They began declaring themselves bankrupt and people who invested their life savings in these companies lost them and were left out in the streets. Tirana's policies did not change. The Western countries did not pressure the Albanian government to interfere nor did they link loans they granted to Albania with making economic reforms as they do with certain other countries.

This Western attitude towards Albania had no reasonable meaning except a reality that they wanted Albania to collapse as a state in order to divide it into smaller parts to be annexed to its neighbouring countries.

What is going on in Albania with a shameful absence of the Islamic governments in that part of the world drives the Western governments to show themselves as the friends who came to rescue that country. Their intervention in Albania will never serve the religious, historic or cultural interests of its Muslim people, nor will it maintain the Islamic presence in the Balkan region in general.

The recent developments in Albania complete what the Western powers achieved in Bosnia but with some formal differences. In Bosnia, there was a need to encourage wars between the Muslim Bosnians, Croats and Serbs in order to destroy a country that began to have a strong growing economy led by Muslims. When the economic structure of Bosnia was completely destroyed and its land was divided into several small entities and cantons, the Western governments showed themselves as friends who came to the rescue while in reality they came to dominate the internal construction of these entities.

In Albania there was no infrastructure to be destroyed externally as was the case with Bosnia. Instead, the destruction came from inside by the autocratic regime of Berisha, the collapse of the fraudulent investment companies and by spreading corruption and sending missionaries. This created a political vacuum in Albania and, again, the Westerners came to fill it by playing the role of the 'rescuer'.

 

Israel's Interests in Turkey

By: Randa Awadh

The Turkish Prime Minister Erbakan is striving to improve the economic situation in Turkey by turning it into an economically and politically independent state. The policies he is implementing are: establishing long-term trade deals with neighbouring countries, such as Iran and Iraq; opening trade channels with the Islamic republics (the former Soviet Union); as well as trying to form an Islamic economic bloc comprising East Asian, Middle Eastern and North African countries. The Prime Minister is also concentrating on the home-front by increasing the peopleís national and religious awareness in order to reduce the impact of Western culture on Turkish thinking.

These policies and measures are not approved of by the army generals because such a trend will minimize their role in the government and limit their interests. This trend will also not be approved of by Western governments, especially the United States, because it will affect its influence and authority over the second-most largest army in NATO. It will also harm and drastically affect Israelís interests and plans in the area for the future. Thus Israel, backed by America, is fighting Erbakanís policies directly through the Turkish army generals and indirectly through its Jewish and Turkish agents inside Turkey.

Israel and America stand to lose much if Turkey achieves political and economic independence because:

1. Both Turkey and Israel have vital and important relations with Syria. Usually Israel makes its deals with Arab countries only when they are stuck in a corner. Thus Israel wants to weaken Syria, which is still strong, by applying pressure. Turkey also has its own interests in Syria which include controlling the Euphrates and finishing off the Kurdish rebels. Losing Turkeyís support would therefore reduce the pressure on Syria.

2. Northern Iraq is vital for both Israel and Turkey. Israel is interested in helping the Iraqi Kurds because a large group of Jewish Kurds in Northern Iraq, who were forced to move to Israel in 1948, thus causing them to hate the ruling government of Iraq, would like to be reunited with their kin. At a higher level, both Israel and the U.S. are worried about the possibility of Iraq forming a powerful force because of its abundant natural resources, which could tip the balance of power in the area. On the other hand, Turkey is still interested in reclaiming al Mousel and Karkouk in Northern Iraq (both were previously Turkish provinces). Such a step would relieve Israel because Northern Iraq is rich in oil and water resources. This explains Americaís insistence on not lifting the sanctions placed on Iraq; it is to ensure Iraqís breakdown while decreasing the danger it poses to Israel.

3. Iran also poses a threat to both Turkey and Israel. The American-Israeli support to the Turkish army helps Turkey to have the upper hand always in any political strife. And the freedom given to the Israeli air force to use Turkish airports and air bases gives Israel the opportunity of reaching targets deep in Iran which would have been otherwise inaccessible from Palestine. Such privileges will be denied to Israel in the future.

4. Israel has realized the potential of the Islamic republics (the former Soviet Union). They are rich in oil minerals and agriculture. However, they are weak because of their newly won independence and their political quarreling. In the hope of influencing them, Israel has made huge efforts to build relationships with these states to the extent that the number of Jewish missions and institutions exceeds those of all the Arab countries put together. Turkey as well is trying to spread its national and cultural identity in the area. In addition the area is a dump-site for all the excess arms produced during the cold war. So both Israel and Turkey would have strong allies, which to America is important, in case Russia resumes its previous power. Also by creating this block between the Middle East and Europe, America has the upper hand and can stop any cooperation between united Europe and the Arab/Islamic world. These advantages will all be lost to America if Turkey follows the path it has chosen.
