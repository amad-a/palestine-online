In a far-reaching strategic development with implications beyond the Middle East, Iran, with the help of Russian experts, two weeks ago tested components of a long-range missile capable of hitting Israel, the Israeli Air Force's commander has said. According to Israel, the missile will have a range of 950 miles, more than twice that of any now in Tehran's arsenal. Earlier, Binyamin Netanyahu, the Israeli Prime Minister, gave a warning that Iran was trying to develop ballistic missiles to strike not only at Israel but also at Western nations. Last night senior diplomatic sources said that the rapid advance in Iran's missile capability, combined with its efforts-again with Russian help-to acquire a nuclear capability, had increased the chances that Israel may launch a pre-emptive strike similar to that mounted against Iraq's Osirak nuclear reactor in June 1981.The Times, April 15, 1997.

General Mordechai (Israeli defence minister) declined to follow Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu's lead in blaming the current impasse on what he considers to be poor agreements that Mr. Rabin signed with the Palestinian leader, Yasser Arafat. "I am not happy with the Oslo agreement, but it's the agreement we have," said General Mordechai, who is retired from the army. "Do you think it's going to help me to say, 'It's bad, it's weak,' and so on? We have to improve it and make it work." The International Herald Tribune, April 15, 1997.

Regardless of whether the Israeli attorney-general, Elyakim Rubinstein, decides to press charges against Binyamin Netanyahu, it seems clear that the prime minister's days in office are numbered. Ten months after his election triumph, Mr. Netanyahu appears to be near the end of his political career. Even if Mr. Rubinstein rejects police recommendations to indict the prime minister on criminal charges of fraud and breach of trust, there is serious doubt whether he can cling on. Many Israelis wonder whether the prime minister can continue to operate as if nothing has changed. Some argue that the past 10 months of Mr. Netanyahu's government have been the worst in the history of the Jewish state.The Guardian, April 18, 1997.

Israeli prime minister Binyamin Netanyahu has credited the Palestinians with co-operation in locating the body of Sergeant Sharon Edri..He had apparently been kidnapped and killed by a Hamas cell based in Kafr Tsurif (Surif), near Hebron..Mr. Netanyahu, speaking during a visit to Holland, said the Palestinian Authority's arrest of two members of the Hamas cell had led to the locating of the soldier's body. But Israeli media reports this week suggested that the Palestinians' role in locating the body of Sergeant Edri-and in uncovering the Hamas cell in Tsurif allegedly responsible for the deaths of Edri and 10 other Israelis-may have gone far deeper than Mr. Netanyahu acknowledged. The Jewish Chronicle, April 18,1997.

But even she is building a villa to rival Abu Mazen's; and even she has been spotted shopping in Tel Aviv's Dizengoff Street. David Hirst writes from Gaza, the Guardian April 21, 1997.
