Doing Israel’s bidding

The deportation by the Jordanian government on 21 November of four Hamas leaders (Khalid Misha’al, Ibrahim Ghoshe, Izzat Resheq and Sami Khater), who are all Jordanian citizens, is undoubtedly an indelible stigma that will continue to stigmatize the Jordanian government for many years to come. Thanks to the unlawful and draconian measure, Jordan will be remembered as probably the first country whose “government” expelled its own citizens to a second country in order to appease and please a third country. Indeed, no other country, no other government that is worth the name, ever resorted to deporting its own citizens in order to please another country.

The infinitely disgraceful act is another infamy, added to the many infamies already dotting the collective political Arab polity. It is no secret that Arab regimes treat their own citizens as mere “subjects” rather than “citizens” with constitutional, political and human rights. The government of Abdel Rauf al-Rawabdeh, by expelling Jordanian citizens to a distant, though nominally “brotherly” land, left no doubt as to the contempt with which it views and treats its citizenry.

The deportation, an act often equated with death in Islamic traditions, is a naked, brash and scandalous act that is inherently immoral and intrinsically illegal by all measures and standards.

Expelling citizens from their own country, irrespective of any conceivable circumstances, can thus be considered a criminal act of grave proportions, and the fact that it was perpetrated by a “national government” makes it even more appalling.

Governments may prosecute offending citizens, imprison them, even hang them if they commit a given crime; but no respectable government on the face of earth, probably except the Zionist regime, resorts to expelling its own citizens (blindfolded and handcuffed) in the middle of the night.

And, in any case, the deportees are actually not offenders of any kind. More so, they are not just ordinary citizens. The four leaders deported represent the cr�me de la cr�me of contemporary Arab societies. They are the last-remaining (and hopefully enduring) symbols of Arab-Muslim pride by virtually insisting on standing aloft for Arab rights in Palestine while the rest hastened to raise the white flag, in utter disgrace, in the face of American-Zionist hegemony and insolence.

The deportation to Qatar was Abel Rauf al-Rawabdeh’s way of rewarding Khalid Misha’al for defying and defeating the Mossad in the streets of Amman, the only Arab capital where the Mossad felt (and still feels) it has carte blanche to move and act against patriotic Palestinians and Jordanians who still dare say “no” to the Zionists.

In other words, the Jordanian government did Israel’s bidding. That should explain it all.

Of course, there is always no shortage of the kind of “rubbish talk” that is parroted by irate officials of Rawabdeh’s government who seek nervously to turn the big lie into a “truth”, all in a desperate effort to cover their shameful act against Hamas leaders.

Thus, in their frivolous quest to explain their shameful act to a no-longer beguiled audience, Rawabdeh and his government have sought to concoct “justifications” for deporting the four Islamists.

However, it is amply clear that any respectable observer can’t help seeing and feeling the baselessness of these justifications, so much so that seeking to refute them would only lend them the dignity and seriousness they so conspicuously lack.

So, in addition to practicing arbitrary measures against its own people and violating the constitution which so obviously prohibits the deportation of citizens under any circumstances, the Rawabdeh government resorts to misleading the public opinion in order to justify its folly against its own citizens.

All this should serve to bring an end to this government, having adopted a modus operandi of deception vis-�-vis its own citizens.

As for Hamas, it is indispensable that the Movement must pay the price for refusing adamantly to raise the white flag before Israel and the United States.

However, in doing this, Hamas will command the respect, admiration and support of Jordanians, Palestinians, Arabs and Muslims everywhere.

Indeed, this is, after all, Hamas’s bidding and with that, victory will some day come, God willing.
