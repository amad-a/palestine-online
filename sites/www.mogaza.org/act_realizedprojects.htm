Realized Projects Funded by The Donors 

    The Israeli occupation left the city with a totally destroyed infrastructure. Therefore, since the current Municipal Council took office the Municipality executed several vital, important and quality projects; and works are in progress to complete the other projects and prepare for future projects for the sake of rehabilitation, development and building of the city.

    As such, the Municipality appeals to citizens to measure up to their responsibilities. We are one people with the same problems and same goals that we collectively endeavor to realize. Let us be loyal to God and the Nation. Let every one of us perform his duty in order to enjoy his rights.

    We remind our citizens that development and rehabilitation cannot take place except after digging and temporary closing of some street. Soon digging will end and development will remain.

 

Brief Description of Some Projects:   
    Consistent with the policy laid out by the current Municipal Council, the Municipality executed during the past five years several important infrastructure projects such as water, sewage, storm water, electricity and telephone projects, in addition to the pavement of streets and sidewalks in the different parts of the city, parks and cultural centers. These projects are either self-financed or by donor countries financing sources, i.e.:

- Grant from USAID.
- Grants from the European Union, either directly or in cooperation with the Palestinian Economic Council for Development and Reconstruction (PECDAR).
- World Bank loans in cooperation with PECDAR and the Ministry of Local Government.
- Grants from Save the Children Foundation (SCF).
- Grants from Japan and other states through the United Nations Development Program (UNDP).
- Direct Norwegian and French grants.
- Grants from Twinning agreements.
- Other sources.   

    These projects, which met the satisfaction of citizens, were executed on a good level through cooperation with local and international agencies such as PECDAR, UNDP, Save the Children and the Norwegian Foreign Ministry.
