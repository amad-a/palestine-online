Tourism

 

      Tourism in Gaza depends mainly on the sea whose shores are famous for their golden bright sand; several precautionary measures have been taken to protect the shores from pollution. Tourism also depends on the archeological and historical sites, which are wide spread in Gaza; more archeological sites are under exploration. Gaza is famous for its moderate weather in the summer and winter, which encourages the people, to go to the beaches, parks and gardens. People also spend their leisure time on farms, orchards, and at touristic facilities.

The Sea

The most beautiful sights in Gaza are the sea, the beaches, the coast, the sky, the golden bright sand, the sun and warmth, and the soft breeze. Gaza is the beautiful mermaid, which attracts tourists to its sea and beaches. Gaza depends on tourism. It is expected that the number of tourists and tourist trade would increase after the declaration of the independent Palestinian State.

Several fantastic tourist projects were implemented on the sea beach; they provide the visitors of the city and the sea with the services they need. Gaza municipality has given the beaches special concern because of their important role in attracting tourists and residents. The municipality cleans the beaches and works on developing them. It also provides beach observers and lifeguards in the summer, the busiest tourist season. The municipality has taken several preventive measures to protect the waters and beaches from pollution. It implemented several projects for sewage and treatment.

Archeological Sites

Gaza and its surroundings are considered a historical museum and a painting on which history carved a valuable and glorious heritage. It is one of the cities in the world, which has a large historical and archeological heritage; many events took place in the city. A large number of ruins were discovered after excavations started in the nineteenth century.

Gaza City, which is now full of houses, streets and fields, hides magnificent historical treasures under its sand. They should be excavated in order to manifest our deep-rooted Arab identity at the time when we face a foreign enemy, which has no history. The enclosed areas, from the northern to the southern end are like an integrated historical painting. Thus, it is not surprising that Herodos, "Father of History", called it 'the great city' or "Beilini", the Roman historian, praised its greatness. Unfortunately, many of the wonderful pieces of ruins were stolen, destroyed, smuggled, or buried by time or because of negligence.

The ruins in Gaza did not get the attention they deserve during the past decades; they remained neglected without any research, documentation or maintenance. The Israeli occupation period had witnessed what we might call "the ruins massacre" because important pieces were stolen in a planned and systematic method. High Israeli officials including Moshe Dayan have done this. When the National Palestinian Authority took over, there were serious efforts paid by the concerned parties to restore the ruins; Gaza municipality has an important role in that. As a result, these places have regained their beauty and their historical and Islamic importance so as to be a window through which the world can look at our culture and which encourage tourists to visit our great ruins.

The most important historical sites in Gaza:

  • Sayyed Hashem Mosque

The mosque contains the tomb of Hashem Ben Abd Manaf, the Grandfather of the Prophet Mohammed. It was built by the Memlukes and renovated by the Sultan Abdul Hamid in the year 1850 AD (1268 Hijra). Gaza was called Hashem's Gaza, after him.

  • Ibn Marwan Mosque

It contains the tomb of the holy man, Sheikh Ali Ben Marwan.

  • Omari Great Mosque

It was built during the first Islamic era, the Omari conquest.

  • Kateb Al-Wilayeh (clerk of the district) Mosque

This mosque is adjacent to the Roman Orthodox church known as "Saint Perforius Church". It was built in 735 Hijra during the Memluke period. After the mosque was destroyed, Ahmad Beik, the clerk of the district, during the reign of Salim II, the Ottoman Sultan, rebuilt it in the year 995 Hijra. There are 23 historical mosques in Gaza City.

  • Al-Radwan  Fortress

Dabboya is the only fortress whose ruins still exist. It was located in the place where the Zahra' School is situated. It was a Crusaders fortress built on the remains of an Arab fortress. The fortress was renovated in the your 1149 AD during the reign of Baldwin III and during the Crusade Wars. There are other ruins from the times of the Pharaohs and the following historical periods.

  • The Roman Orthodox (Saint Perforius) Church

Saint Perforius was born in Salonika, Greece. He came to Gaza on 21 March 395 AD accompanied by "Deacon Murqus" who wrote the biography of this saint. The building of the church continued for a period of 5 years and it was opened on 14 April 407 AD.
