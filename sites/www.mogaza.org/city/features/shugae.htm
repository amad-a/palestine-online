El-Shuja’eya Neighborhood

Many of Gaza districts were built outside the overhill city walls during the Ayoubi period and flourished during the Mameluk era. One of those districts is El-Shuja’eya which is situated to the east of the ancient city. The name of this part of the city refers to the martyr, Shuja' El-Kurdy, who died in a battle between Saladin and the Crusaders in 1239. The northern part of this district is called Judaydah while the southern part is known as Turkman.

One of the most important sites of El-Suja’eya is the Mosque of Ahmed Iben Othman which is called the big mosque. It lies in the center of the district amidst the commercial center. Yalkhja, one of the followers of Mameluk Sultan Zaher Barkouk, and who was appointed as chief commander of Gaza in 849 AD is buried in this mosque. The various foundation stones demonstrate that the mosque was restored in the ninth century BC. The inscriptions and the gorgeous decorations on its minaret add some beauty to the mosque. Another beautiful sight of El-Shuja’eya is El-Mahkama Mosque the inscriptions of which show that there was a school of great value inside the mosque. There are other ancient mosques such as El-Tawashi, Sheikh Mosafer and El-Takzadory. The latter used to have El-Shuja’eya Public Bath.

 

The graveyard which is situated to the east of El-Shuja’eya and is called either El-Tunisia or El-Tefleesy is believed to, have had the tombs of many martyrs and religious men. An annual festival called El-Khomsan, Thursdays, would be held in allegiance to those men.
