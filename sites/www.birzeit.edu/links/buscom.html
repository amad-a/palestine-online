  • Al-Bank Al-'Aqari Al-'Arabi: http://www.planet.edu/~aqari/ - This site provides literally no information about the Arab Land Bank, basically because none of the hotlinks off the homepage work. Reviewed by Michael O'Neill / Added to Complete Guide: 19 May 1997.

  • Bandak International Trading Co. Ltd: http://www.bandak.com/ - "The Palestinian Coffee Company", Bandak International Trading Co. Ltd., "was established many years ago for the purpose of doing international commerce. Ever since, we have been successful in maintaining our markets and developing new ones." A simple homepage appealing for distributers of green coffee beans to sell to Bandak. Reviewed by Nigel Parry / Added to Complete Guide: 6 February 1997.

  • Holy Land mosaics-Bethlehem: http://www.stone-mosaics.com/ - This site gives some basic background information about Holy Land Mosaics, and features a few examples of their work. It doesn't list prices, purchase information, etc. In essence, when shopping for that perfect wedding gift for my cousin, this site didn't help me. Reviewed by Michael O'Neill / Added to Complete Guide: 19 May 1997.

  • Palestine - The Promising Land: http://www.palnet.com/inv/net-3.htm - Palnet's introduction to investment and business for the West Bank, Gaza and East Jerusalem. Reviewed by Nigel Parry / Added to Complete Guide: 19 May 1997.

  • Palestine International Bank: http://www.pibank.com/ - Excellently designed website with lots of information about the activities of the bank, and opportunities for investors. Reviewed by Nigel Parry / Added to Complete Guide: 19 May 1997.

  • Palestinian Trade Development Center (PalTrade): http://www.paltrade.org/ - PalTrade is "your guide to doing business with Palestine". The website has a profile of the organisation and information about chambers of commerce, privite sector organisations, an export directory, trade agreements, doing business in Palestine, the Cairo Economic Summit in November 1996, PNA press releases, business news and links. PalTrade correctly recognises that "rebuilding Palestine's economy is fundemental to the successful continuation of the Middle East peace process." Visit now and do some business! Reviewed by Nigel Parry / Added to Complete Guide: 6 February 1997.

  • Telepal: http://www.tele-pal.com/ - Telepal describes itself as "the premier Palestinian publishing company." If this is the case, the websites for other Palestinian publishing companies must truly be frightening. Only one hotlink (out of five) on the homepage worked, and on that second page, none of the graphic or hotlinks worked. This has been the case for months. Reviewed by Michael O'Neill / Added to Complete Guide: 19 May 1997.
