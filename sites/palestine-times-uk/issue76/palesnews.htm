Hamas: Israeli intelligence is behind assassination

attempt in Amman

Occupied Jerusalem- PT

The Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) has accused Israeli intelligence of master-minding an assassination attempt on Khalid Misha'al, the head of the Hamas Political Bureau, that took place in Amman the morning of 25 September.

Hamas leader and spokesman in Gaza, Dr. Abdul Aziz al Rantisi said he had no doubt whatsoever about the identity of the perpetrators, saying "Khalid Misha'al has one and only one enemy in this world and that is the Zionist criminals."

Rantisi said in a telephone interview from his Gaza home on the afternoon following the incident that he would not like to be hasty in drawing conclusions, adding that if indeed it turned out that Israeli agents were behind the assassination attempt, it would mean that Israel has "chosen to open wide a new door of confrontation with Muslims everywhere."

Another Islamist spokesman, who asked for anonymity, said "Israel is playing with fire, and fire doesn't burn only Islamist leaders."

The assassination attempt on Khalid Misha'al's life reportedly took place as he was on his way to work in Amman. According to Islamist sources in the Jordanian capital, the incident was preceded by Mr. Misha'al sensing that he was being followed by an unidentified car carrying five men. Upon reaching his destination and alighting from his vehicle, two of the men from the unfamiliar car were close behind him as he reached the entrance of his building. As they got closer to him, he noticed that one of the men was carrying a small, unidentified device which the assailant attempted to deploy close to Mr. Misha'al's ear. However, one of the two security guards looking after Mr. Misha'al was able to strike the assailant's arm just before detonation. There was a loud noise and a sort of electric shock that made Mr. Misha'al lose his sense of balance. It is still unknown what the effect of this device would have been had the detonation been complete and without interference. The two men attempted to flee the scene but were captured by the security guards who subdued them until the arrival of the police and their eventual arrest. The other three accomplices escaped in their vehicle. According to the security guards, all five are believed to be non-Arab nationals. In the interim, Mr. Misha'al was rushed to hospital and is still under observation.

Earlier this month, the internal Israeli ministerial council on security adopted a number of secret recommendations against Hamas.

According to Israeli sources, the recommendations included "reactivating Mossad death squads in Jordan," an allusion to carrying out assassinations of Islamist leaders in Jordan.

Two years ago, the murder of the Palestinian Islamist guerrilla Yahya Ayyash by the Israeli intelligence sparked off a series of deadly urban bomb attacks in Israel in which over 100 Israeli soldiers and civilians were killed.

Israel is launching a war on Islam

Occupied Jerusalem-PT

Israeli occupation troops raided last month the Grand Mosque in Dura in the latest attack on Islamic holy places in the occupied territories.

According to local Palestinian sources, scores of Israeli soldiers forcibly entered the Mosque shortly after midnight Sunday, 21 September presumably to carry out searches for "inciting materials."

The Mosque Muezzin, Ja'afar Dasseh, said the soldiers behaved with obvious disrespect inside the Mosque and stepped on copies of the Qur'an.

He added that the commanding officer then produced a poorly written closure order saying that the Mosque, the largest in the Hebron area, be closed off for one month.

Sheikh Salah al Natshe, Head of the Hebron Wakf Department, strongly condemned the Israeli army transgression against Islamic holy places. "When somebody writes graffiti at a Jewish grave in a remote part of Europe or North America, they make a big fuss about it; however, when their murderous soldiers and settlers desecrate Muslim mosques and Christian churches, nobody says anything."

Natshe pointed out that Israeli army attacks on Islamic holy places in the West Bank have assumed phenomenal proportions of late. He said Israeli raids on mosques and their imams have become "almost a daily occurrence."

"The matter is simply a dirty war by Jews on Islam, the Qur'an and the holy prophet." Muhammed Ayyash, a clerk at the Bethlehem Wakf Department, concurred. He pointed out that the Israeli army has arrested many local imams in the villages of Tku, al Khader, Beit Fajjar and the Dheisheh refugee camp in the last two weeks.

Ayyash admitted that the vindictive arrests were undermining the holding of prayers at the respective mosques.

On 21 September, as many as 600 Israeli soldiers raided the village of Asira al Shamaliyya near Nablus. Assa'ad Sawalmeh, head of the local council, said the heavily-armed soldiers raided homes, beat up defenseless villagers and arrested scores of youth for "harbouring Islamic tendencies." Sawalmeh pointed out that the Israeli troops, commanded by Uzi Dayyan (Israeli army commander in the West Bank) forced as many as 600 local citizens into the local school where they subjected them to various forms of humiliation and occasional beatings.

In the meantime, the Israeli troops confined Palestinian Authority policemen to their local centre, preventing them from leaving the premises "until further notice."

The Israeli operation at the village lasted for more than 12 hours, and as many as 25 local citizens were arrested.

Violent Attack on Muslim Charity in France

British Muslim groups fear for safety

London- PT

British Muslims condemned the 9 September attack which targeted the Paris offices of the Muslim charity Comité de Bienfaisance pour la Solidarité avec la Palestine (CBSP).

A press release issued by British Muslim organizations said that the CBSP is one of the four charities currently being targeted by the Israeli government.

"One worker was in the office when six men of European appearance and speaking French approached the offices. Two stayed outside whilst four entered the premises and sprayed the worker with CS Gas just before two of them brutally assaulted him. The other two assailants sprayed graffiti on the walls, including several Stars of David with the initials GSC written below. GSC are known in the Middle East as the 'Zionist Action Group,' and have claimed responsibility for the attack. They are equated with the Jewish Defence League, who are active in the UK and USA. The GSC have been becoming more active in Europe," said the press release.

The Muslim groups in Britain expressed concern over possible attacks on Muslim charities in the UK by Jewish extremists. "British Muslim organizations are concerned that they are even more vulnerable to attack. A few weeks ago, some Muslim organizations were warned by New Scotland Yard to expect such attacks. When one of the charities contacted wrote to the Home Secretary Jack Straw requesting to discuss security, they were refused a meeting," they said. They wondered if the British government, in the light of this latest incident, would provide security for Muslim organizations and charities, "as it already does to its Jewish counterparts."

The UN Partition Plan Remembered

By Ray Hanania

Much is made of how Israel "accepted" the United Nations Partition Plan, and the Arabs rejected it, thus launching a wave of anti-Arab propaganda that has haunted the debate over Palestinian rights for half a century.

The argument, offered by the Israelis, goes something like this: Israel compromised and accepted half of British Mandated Palestine. The Palestinians and Arabs rejected the compromise causing the events of their loss that followed.

It's not quite like that, though. And, it didn't start on Nov. 29, 1947 when the UN General Assembly barely approved the Partition Resolution.

In fact, the real vote occurred on September 8, 1947 when the United Nations Special Committee on Palestine (UNSCOP) released a controversial report calling for the Partition of Palestine. Dissenting members of UNSCOP endorsed a "Federal Solution" in which Jews and Arabs would manage Palestine together.

But why was Partition recommended?

Haganah forces had already laid out a detailed plan to occupy the majority of Mandated Palestine and had been given advance information on British Plans to depart. In fact, they had assurances from the British, the United States that the State of Israel would receive international recognition and the backing of the United Nations.

The Haganah was receiving arms and men from Europe and with the help of the British and also the Americans, while the British maintained a nearly airtight blockade of Arab volunteers trying to stream into Palestine to fight with Palestinian irregulars.

In contrast, armies from neighboring Arab States were prohibited from entering Palestine by the British, while armed Jewish volunteers streamed in under the watchful eye of the British. (On March 24, 1947, the Arab League accused Britain and the US of interfering and blamed them for the deteriorating conditions.)

But Israel needed the partition plan and knew in advance that it was unacceptable to the Arabs because Arab allies on UNSCOP had led the opposition during committee debates.

It was very simple. If Israel accepted the Partition and the Arabs did not, Israel would be free to continue its aggressive military land grab. If the Israelis accepted "Federation," they would be forced to halt their military advances and give up hope of an Independent State.

Meeting in Sofar, Lebanon Sept. 16th through the 19th, the Arab League did what Jewish leaders expected, they denounced the UNSCOP Partition Plan. The Arab League made a strong argument, pointing out the so-called "Jewish State" consisted of a majority of Christian and Muslim Palestinians. The unworkable plan proposed dividing Palestine into six separate regions. The "Jewish" and "Arab" states would each consist of three intersecting segments, looking like a six square chess board with one side having black squares and the other with red squares.

On Sept. 26, British Colonial Secretary Arthur Creech Jones announced that the British Mandate would terminate. The deadline of May 14 was not publicly announced until one month later, although the Jewish leadership already knew the date.

On Sept. 29, 1947, the Arab Higher Committee for Palestine, which had only been recently allowed to return to Palestine after a 10 year exile, announced it would reject the UNSCOP Partition Plan. They endorsed the Federation plan that recognized Palestine's independence.

The Jewish Agency had privately rejected Federation, but waited until the Arab Side announced their position first, before telling the world they would "accept" Partition, announcing it on Oct. 2, 1947. American endorsement came on Oct. 11.

Arab critics accused the Jewish Agency of insincerity, charging Haganah forces were already advancing on dozens of Palestinian Villages in and out of the proposed Partition Plan areas assigned to the "Jewish State."

Politically at the United Nations, the Jewish Agency had won its battle for perception, falsely claiming at the United Nations podium during debates that followed that they, the Jews in Palestine, had endorsed compromise and the Arabs had rejected it.

That public posture allowed them to continue to attack Arab villages without condemnation from their American or British supporters, who kept the United Nations deadlocked on resolution after resolution introduced to denounce the Haganah and Irgun attacks.

On November 29, 1947, with some minor modifications, the UN voted to recommend the Partition Plan, approving it by a vote of slim margin of 33 votes, with 13 voting against, 10 abstaining in protest, and the entire Arab delegation walking out in protest.

This concept of having rejected "compromise" has haunted the public posture of the Arabs and Palestinians, and undermined all future Arab efforts to justify its case against an unfair Partition Plan.

With no one to complain, Jewish forces stepped up their military occupation of Arab villages and land outside of the proposed Jewish State area. They had priorities: rid the proposed Jewish areas of Arabs to make the Jewish population a the Jewish population a "MAJORITY by force," and to capture additional land to make the state defensible.

Haganah forces launched battles in Jaffa and Safad, targeting Arab residents there, and stepped up their attacks and terrorist bombings in Jerusalem, which was designated as an "International Zone" outside of both proposed states.

Examples of pre-Independence assaults on Arab villages by the Haganah included: Abu Kebir, (Jaffa), Dec. 6; Tireh (Haifa), Dec. 13; Khisas (Safad), Dec 19; Qazaza (Ramleh), Dec. 20; Jerusalem, Dec. 29; Balad al-Sheikh (Haifa), Dec. 30; Abu Shusha, Jan. 3, 1948; Shafa Amr (Haifa) and Tamra (Nazareth), Jan. 19; Sukreir (Gaza), Jan, 26; Sa'Sa (Safed), Feb 14; Biyar and Ads (Jaffa), March 5.

Meanwhile, US President Harry Truman played a deceptive game of politics that favored the Jewish forces: On March 18, 1948, Truman met secretly with Chaim Weizmann at the White House and pledged US support for Jewish independence.

In a deceptive move the next day, Truman's UN representative called for a suspension of the Partition Plan and endorsed an UN Supervised truce to work out a "Trusteeship" to replace Partition. On March 20, The Arab League endorsed the trusteeship idea, proposing a cessation of hostilities. But that day, Weizmann and the Jewish Agency rejected Trusteeship and any hope of a real peace.

In that light, the Jewish attack against Deir Yassin on April 9, 1948 where 254 civilians were murdered, was intentional. Irgun forces, in a plan supported at first by the Haganah, distributed Arabic leaflets in Jerusalem warning other Arabs of a similar fate. Dozens of captured villagers were paraded through Jerusalem's Jewish Sector on an open truck.

Following in Hitler's footsteps:

Victims of Jewish ethnic cleansing languish in homelessness

Occupied Jerusalem -PT

Hundreds of Palestinian victims of the Israeli policy of ethnic cleansing in East Jerusalem have entered their second month at a makeshift refugee camp at the Suwwana neighbourhood, across from the Mount of Olives.

The camp, say Palestinians, only epitomizes the increasingly acute ordeal of Jerusalemite Arabs whom the Israelis seek to drive out "by hook or by crook" in order to check Arab population growth in Jerusalem.

Today, the "Jerusalem Camp of Steadfastness" is "home" to as many as 70 Palestinian families, including at least 35 children below the age of eight.

So far, the Israeli municipal authorities have behaved totally callously towards the refugees, refusing to "recognize the camp" or provide refugees with electricity and water supplies.

However, Israeli callousness does not seem to deter the refugees or weaken their resolve. "We are here to demonstrate to the whole world the racist, Nazi nature of the Israeli authorities who refuse to treat us as human beings," said Um Ahmed, a woman refugee at the camp. "Look! They just don't want us to be in Jerusalem because we are not Jews...It is like the Nazis who wanted to have a pure Arian Germany."

Another woman, Maha Jayyousi, cited a different reason for becoming a refugee in her own town. "For 15 years my husband and I have been trying to obtain a building license from the Israeli municipal authorities, but to no avail...they told us 'if you don't like it, you can leave the town...and we will help you leave if and when you decide to do so'," she said. Jayyousi recognized that life in Jerusalem is difficult and that things are particularly difficult for the children. "Life here is difficult, but being contiguous to Jerusalem makes us forget all hardship."

Virtually all refugees vow to stay at the camp until "our just demands are met."

"We are not asking for the impossible...we just want to be treated on equal footing with other human beings," said Waheed al Resheq, pointing out to his married son Muhammed, also a refugee whose residency rights had been withdrawn by the Israeli interior ministry. "See, they took away his identity card because he lived for six months at the Ram outskirts after he couldn't find housing in East Jerusalem."

Abdullah Burqan, another refugee at the camp, voices essentially the same grievances. "The Israelis have one goal, that it is to force us out of Jerusalem; and since they can't just collectively expel us outside the town for public relations considerations, they create a situation in which our life in the town becomes unbearable, so that we would leave voluntarily."

The refugees hope that media coverage of their plight would prompt Israel to reconsider its stance on their cause. However, this does not seem to be forthcoming.

Palestinians reject the Ras-al-Amud "joke"

Occupied Jerusalem -PT

A general state of tension continued to prevail in Israeli-occupied East Jerusalem despite the conclusion on 18 September of a "compromise agreement" between the Israeli government and the Jewish settlers who earlier had seized two Arab houses at the Ras al Amud neighbourhood.

The agreement, which Palestinians branded as a "silly joke", stipulated that the three Jewish families who had entered the premise be evacuated and replaced by 10 students belonging to the ultra-fanatic Gush Emunim movement.

Israeli sources described the new settlers as "potential murderers" and "indoctrinated in hate of everything Arab."

Palestinians at all levels flatly rejected the "silly joke," labelling it as "a deal between thieves."

Faisal Husseini, who holds the Jerusalem portfolio in the Palestinian Authority, described the "deal" as "a naked trick" and "a crime greater than its predecessor." PA minister of Information, Yasser Abed Rabbu, said the "Jewish-Jewish deal" was like an understanding between "field gangsters and Mafia heads." Abed Rabbu denounced the American stance on the matter, saying it provided a political cover for colonialist Jewish activities in Arab Jerusalem.

Earlier, PA Chairman Yasser Arafat rejected the deal between the Netanyahu government and settler leaders. "This is a big trick, a public relations ploy to perpetuate Jewish colonialism at Ras al Amud," Arafat reportedly told a meeting of the Arab League foreign ministers in Cairo on Saturday, 20 September.

Meanwhile, Jewish settler leaders lauded the "deal" as "a victory for our cause."

"The families have left; the people who remained are here to guard the property and make renovations because at some point the families will come back here...this is only temporary," said settler leader Hagit Harel, a spokesman for the group of settlers who seized the building last week.

Another settler, Shimon Gaznov, said he and five fellow settlers planned to get to work soon on putting up partitions and laying ceramic tiles. More Palestinian protests are planned outside the premise.

On 20 September, Palestinian schoolchildren in East Jerusalem staged a demonstration demanding "the removal of settlers from our midst." Moreover, Palestinian leaders vowed to continue their protests until the Israeli government removes the settlers for good.

In another development, Palestinian Islamist sources discounted Israeli claims concerning the apprehension of two armed Islamic cells in East Jerusalem. An Islamist source in the West Bank town of Hebron said the Israeli claims to that effect "were fraught with exaggeration" and "aimed at boosting the morale of the Israeli public following recent bombings in West Jerusalem and Israeli army losses in South Lebanon."

On 19 September, the Israeli intelligence service (Shabak) disclosed that two Hamas and Islamic Jihad cells had been captured and that members of the first cell had planned to kidnap West Jerusalem mayor Ehud Olmert.

Palestinians lambaste Albright; PA ambivalent

Occupied Jerusalem- Special for PT

While most Palestinians reacted negatively to U.S. Secretary of State Madeleine Albright's visit to the region, PA officials chose to be more circumspect, awaiting the outcome of further Israeli-Palestinian talks slated to take place in Washington later this month.

Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat said Albright's maiden visit to the region was a positive development in itself.

However, the Palestinian leader seems to be postponing judgment on the merits of Albright's visit "until we know how things will unfold in a few weeks to come."

Chief Palestinian negotiator, Sa'eb Ereikat, told the Palestinian Legislative Council on 16 September that "there was still a wide gap between the Palestinian and American perception of the current crisis, its causes, and ways and means to revive the peace process."

Ereikat said there was a conspicuous dichotomy between "our views and those of Mrs. Albright on the concept of security and terrorism," adding that it was clear that Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu "uses the security issue as an excuse to renege on the peace process."

Ereikat disclosed that PA Chairman Yasser Arafat handed Albright a list of 150 Palestinians murdered cold-bloodedly by Israeli soldiers and settlers since Oslo without the perpetrators being charged or tried.

He disclosed that Albright and Arafat indulged in "a long and acrimonious argument" over the role of Palestinian police in preventing and thwarting violence against Israeli targets.

"The president told her 'I don't receive orders from anybody'," said Ereikat, alluding to Albright's incessant demands that the Palestinian Authority "clamp down on the infrastructure of Hamas and the Islamic Jihad."

Earlier, Arafat's media adviser Marwan Kanafani intimated that the PA Chairman rejected Albright's demands that the "PA mount an all-out campaign" against the Islamist infrastructure within the autonomous enclaves.

According to Kanafani, Arafat asked Albright if she would guarantee that the U.S. Congress would not place the PA on the list of states violating human rights should he heed her demands to that effect. Kanafani said Albright was reticent when Arafat told her he couldn't put people behind bars just because Mr. Netanyahu felt like it.

Other Palestinian officials lashed out at Albright for the stark obliquity inherent in her "overemphasis on Israeli security requirements, while only making a passing and half-hearted allusion to Jewish settlement expansion in Jerusalem and the West Bank."

"Albright's imbalance is stark, blunt and scandalous. She amply and unashamedly demonstrated her emotional identification and political solidarity with Israel. She simply can't be the honest, impartial and even-handed broker that she should be," said PA Minister of Information Yasser Abed Rabbu.

Many Palestinians were particularly incensed by Albright's statements on the "Voice of Palestine" and later at the "American Friends School in Ramallah" on Friday, 12 September, in which she claimed that "Hamas and the Islamic Jihad were the main enemy of the average Palestinian." Albright opined that "Hamas actions against Israel caused Israeli reactions against the Palestinians."

In the first case, questions were not allowed, and at the Ramallah school, only 20 pupils were carefully selected to be the audience and the questions submitted to Albright were meticulously chosen by officials of the American Consulate in East Jerusalem, ostensibly to rule out "provocative questions."

One Palestinian student, who listened to Albright's speech at the Friends School, marvelled at "the American lady's incredible perception of things in this part of the world."

"So, Mrs. Albright would like us to believe that Hamas, not Israel, is destroying Palestinian homes, stealing Palestinian land, conspiring against the Al-Aqsa Mosque and narrowing Palestinians' horizons," the student said.

Palestinian opposition figures criticized the PA for not allowing a response to her "shocking charade."

"Both Albright and the PA insulted the Palestinian people. First, Albright had the audacity to tell us that Hamas, not Netanyahu, was our enemy; and second, the PA allowed the wicked lady to get away with her incredible lies without allowing the victim to challenge the oppressor," said Sheikh Yousef Ibrahim, an Islamist figure in al Beireh near Ramallah.

Israeli interrogators torture Palestinian detainee to death

Occupied Jerusalem-PT

Israeli intelligence agents have tortured a 31-year-old Palestinian political detainee to death, Israeli and Palestinian sources said on Wednesday, 17 September.

The detainee, Marwan Hasan Ibrahim Ma'ali of the village of A'jja near Jenin, was strangled to death at the hands of three Israeli Shabak interrogators at the Megiddo detention camp.

An Israeli army spokesman said Ma'ali died in "vague circumstances" and "may have committed suicide."

However, a number of his friends reportedly denied the Israeli claim of a possible suicide saying that "it was a routine ruse to conceal the crime."

"Marwan didn't commit suicide; he was murdered by the criminal thugs" said one inmate from Hebron, referring to Shabak interrogators.

According to his family, Ma'ali had no mental or psychological problems.

His friend, Ragheb, from A'jja flatly denied the Israeli insinuation that Ma'ali committed suicide.

"Why would he commit suicide? He was in good health; he was married and a father of three children, and had no social or psychological problems," said Ragheb.

Isa Karaqe'i, head of the Palestinian Prisoner's Club said "I'm 100% sure that Shabak agents murdered Marwan...He was not the kind of person who would commit suicide."

"This (the suicide claim) is not the first, nor will it be the last claim of its kind by those evil murderers...They always perpetrate their crimes and then seek to conceal them in a variety of sinister ways."

According to the Palestinian Human Rights Monitoring Group, Ma'ali was arrested on 4 August following the 31 July bombing in West Jerusalem. An administrative detention order for two months was issued for him. His family was not allowed to visit him despite an illness that Ma'ali suffered from. The family tried to pass medical documents related to the illness through the Red Cross without success.

Ma'ali's body was taken to the Abu Kabir forensic institute for an autopsy. He was due to be buried Wednesday night at his village.

Ma'ali was detained without charge or trial like so many other Palestinians currently held hostage by the Israeli government; they know not why they are being detained or if and when they would be let go.

Ma'ali leaves behind him a pregnant wife and three children.

Israel is the only country in the world that legalizes the use of torture against detainees. So far, as many as 15 Palestinians died of torture in Israeli custody since the signing of the Oslo agreement four years ago.

The Roots of terror

By Steve Sozby

Off a main road north of Jerusalem is the Palestinian village of Bani Samuel. In August, Israeli troops stormed into the tiny village and demolished four "unlicensed" homes belonging to residents who trace their family roots to the area back hundreds of years. This place sits on the highest mountain around Jerusalem and is the site of the Tomb of Samuel the Prophet, who is sacred to Moslems and Jews. Only 200 Palestinians are left in the village, and you have to pass a gated military base surrounding the Mosque, which has been forcibly turned into a Synagogue, and then drive past the home of a local Arab collaborator, who does much of the dirty deeds for the Israeli intelligence and occupation authorities against his own people.

It is only getting past these obstacles can you enter this ancient town to see where four homes now lie now in a heap of rubble. A lawyer, Mohammed Barakat, who is now a quadriplegic from an auto accident in Jordan a few years back, describes the story of his poor village:

"In 1971, the Israelis destroyed all of our homes surrounding the mosque, which was the old part of the village, and forced us to move in to these homes about 200 meters away. These are homes that were abandoned by people who fled to Jordan in June of 1967. Since then, the Israelis have not given us a single permit to build on our own land. In 1967 there where maybe five persons to a house. Now we are nearly 20 people trying to live in a modest home. And still, we have not been permitted to build a single room here. This is a policy designed to force us to leave our village. We are living on a high mountain and a holy site, so they do not want any Arabs here and are using many methods to get us to leave. The soldiers from the base always come and harass us, checking I.D.s and humiliating the youths in front of the people. We have no services either. There is no school, so we have to send our children to nearby villages. We applied for a permit for a driver to take the children to school in a bus, but this was denied. They have to take a local bus, and then walk in the heat or rain for a kilometer from where it leaves them to the school. We do not have a clinic or public facility here for anyone to use and there are no phones. We tried to fix the roads, but this too was denied. In the four homes that were destroyed last week, 23 people are now homeless. Many more are affected, but some are away in Jordan and others are back living in the cramped conditions of their previous situation. 23 people, however, are living in tents supplied by the Red Cross. The army came at 9:00 am, told the people they have five minutes to remove their things and bulldozed the homes. Many people lost valuables. You will see now when you tour the area clothes, food and other items in the rubble."

I did not interrupt the man as he spoke. Too few journalists are willing to come and hear his story, and he was eager to express himself. Even among the Palestinians a few miles away in Ramallah, there is little knowledge of Bani Samuel and its plight.

"The objective of this government is to put pressure on the people. They want us to leave, to go from this spot, our land, so they can further build their own settlements. Just down the mountain, go, look there, you will see the construction of Ramot, a settlement that was build on our land 10 years ago. They have all of the services we lack. Roads, schools, clinics, phones, and space. No permits are denied there. Their children play in parks that are my father's olive trees. Netanyahu tells the Americans that this building is only to address the issue of 'natural growth', but where is our natural growth? We have families here living five persons in a kitchen. They sleep, eat and live in a single room. People became fed up and built a new room without permission, which would never come, and the army came and destroyed that room. This is the situation we are facing. We have our land, our rights, but this government will not release us. We want peace. We are against the bombs and terror, but what is the cause of this terror. Look at those children who are seeking their toys in the rubble of their home. What do you think they are going to believe when they grow older about peace? This is the real terrorism. Demolishing some one's home on their own land because they want space to raise a family. Netanyahu speaks of democracy and peace, but these are just slogans. It means nothing. And the Americans, your country, is also to blame. We ask any sponsor of peace to be fair, to not take the sides of someone and to recognize oppression, but from the Untied States we just get more warnings about terrorism. This is the cause of violence, especially at a time when we are asking to live in peace."

I went down to visit the four families living in the Red Cross tents.

"You journalists just come and write things, but never write about a solution. What is the point talking to you?" Asks one man, whose family of four now share a tent the size of a small room. When I tell him that it is not the objective of a journalist to offer solutions, he snorts and goes on smoking quietly.

"We want peace," says another man. "But you cannot have it under these conditions. Tomorrow, Peace Now will come from Israel and hold a demonstration with us against this policy of destroying our homes, but what will it do? They are powerless to stop their own leaders from taking us closer to war."

"We are sick of fighting," Mohammed Barakat, the crippled lawyer, tells me before we leave. "This peace agreement was to say enough. Enough killing and suffering and pain for everyone. But this government of Netanyahu is pushing us everyday to the point of returning to the armed struggle. When you see your land and rights being taken and you have tried the path of peace and it failed, what other options remain for you to find a way to survive on your own land?"

As I drove out of Bani Samuel, on to the main road to Jerusalem, dozens of new Israeli cars roared past, on to expensive homes in the Ramot settlement. They are completely oblivious to the situation or conditions in a place just over the hill in Bani Samuel. Until it is clear that the key to peace is the fulfillment of basic rights for everyone, there will never be lasting peace here. Unfortunately, the drums of war are beating louder. With each new home demolition in the West Bank and every new settlement, another youth will see the path of suicide bombs as the only language that Netanyahu and his cronies understand.

A new law to protect Shabak interrogators from legal accountability

Occupied Jerusalem - An Israeli legal committee has completed a draft law regulating the work of the Israeli Shabak. The new law gives more authority to Shabak interrogators and protects them from any legal accountability if a Palestinian detainee dies while being interrogated. Israeli government isolates Jerusalem from West Bank

Bethlehem (West Bank) -

Reliable Palestinian sources said Israeli occupation troops began digging military trenches south of Occupied Jerusalem with the aim of isolating the holy city from the south of the West Bank. The sources pointed out that the occupation soldiers closed the work sites in order to protect the Israeli bulldozers working there. The trenches, the first of their kind, are being dug east of Jebal Abu Ghneim where a Jewish settlement has been under construction since last March.

Tulkarm (West Bank) -

Palestinian sources said Israeli soldiers last month kidnapped a Palestinian Islamic leader near Tulkarm, a city north of the occupied West Bank. CARE: Advances in Palestinian Education

By Lena Winfrey Sayyed

Most of us in the free world consider education to be extremely vital to live, and we are always trying to improve its quality. However, we take it for granted. We forget sometimes that not everyone in the world can get a good education or even an education at all.

In Palestine, the occupation has been very hard on popular education. During the intifadah, as well as during closures in other periods of time, schools and universities were closed. Those students had no place to study. They used to gather in basements to study together. One time the United Nations sent school books to the Palestinian children, but the Israelis refused to give them the books. It is sad when a people are prevented from getting the education that they desire.

In the midst of the intifidah and its troubled, chaotic time, an organization sprang up in Jerusalem. This organization, the Center of Applied Research in Education (CARE), was established in 1988 to take charge of popular education during the intifidah. It still exists and is currently continuing with new projects.

The director of CARE, Ghassan Abdullah, spoke to me at the Popular Jerusalem Conference to tell me more about CARE and its work. CARE is not only for Jerusalem, but for all of Palestine. However, lately there has been more of a concentration in Jerusalem.

Mr. Ghassan told me that CARE began in 1994 as a "Jerusalem and Education" campaign. He stated that "This program takes several approaches: round-table, seminars and training sessions that deal with how to present in education the history and geography of Jerusalem."

He went on to tell me about two of their big achievements. The first is the fact that CARE has produced visual aides for teaching about Jerusalem through fun, which was sponsored by the Islamic Organization for Cultural and Educational Affairs.

Their second achievement is a collection of short stories that is directed towards raising awareness about the issue of Jerusalem. These stories were written by prominent Palestinian scholars, and the target audience is Palestinian and other Arab children. Mr. Ghassan stated that one of their goals is "to focus on an Islamic and an Arab identity."

The General Assembly of the Islamic Organization, which is located in Jeddah, Saudi Arabia, held the 6th Annual Conference about Jerusalem in education on 5 and 6 July of this year. 174 educationalists from the 1948 Palestinian areas, the West Bank and Gaza attended. Mr. Ghassan said that "For two days we discussed the strategy and methodology of getting Jerusalem into education."

CARE focuses on helping teachers and students as well as helping to teach parent councils how to operate. "We are trying to take practical steps to improve the educational situation," says Mr. Ghassan. "We hold training sessions for the teachers and try to advise them on how to face the violence phenomena in the classroom."

"We focus on the issue of democracy (not the political meaning)-the democratization of education," he says. "We try to help the teachers create a democratic atmosphere between themselves and the students."

He told me that in 1993 the parents worked to establish parent councils, which had been prevented in the past by the Israelis. He said, "We threatened to go to the High Court, so they finally gave them permission." CARE trains these parent councils how to work and how to cooperate.

CARE is actively achieving where others only talk and dream. "Actions speak louder than words," as the old saying goes. CARE needs our support and prayers to continue their work. We need to arm the Palestinians with a good education because "knowledge is power," and this kind of power can help in bringing about freedom.
