The malady and the remedy

Khalid Amayreh

Despite Israel’s ongoing genocide against the Palestinian people, which has so far resulted in the killing of some 310 Palestinians–one fourth of them children–and the wounding and maiming of as many as 12,000 people, the Arab world has yet to take even one effective measure to make the Nazi-like Israel feel that Palestinian lives are not expendable and Palestinian blood is not water.

Arab leaders did hold a summit in Cairo in October but, as usual, the outcome was virtually a big, fat zero. The Islamic conference in Doha was not any better.

And in both cases, the member States, whether individually or collectively, demonstrated a startling degree of impotence, incompetence and powerlessness.

In practical terms, the message the bleeding Palestinians received from both summits is, “We feel sorry for you, but we can’t do anything to help you because peace is our sole and only strategic option!”

The Palestinians, to be sure, had never expected Arab and Islamic summit participants to take revolutionary measures against Israel and its ultimate protector and sustainer–the United States. Needless to say, this would be extremely unrealistic and even far-fetched, given the fact that some of the OIC member States are bound to the Zionist regime by a strategic alliance.

Even the modest demand of severing ties with the criminal Jewish State was rejected as some Arab and “Muslim” States argued, explicitly rather than implicitly, that their relations with Israel, and therefore with the Zionist-controlled U.S., were more important than Palestine, its people and even Jerusalem and al-Aqsa Mosque!

Subservience to America
The manifestly shameful outcome of both the Arab and Islamic summits demonstrated unequivocally the extent to which most Arab and Islamic countries lack the political independence and, therefore, political will required to put up any meaningful opposition to American hegemony in the region.

In other words, subservience to the White House proved much stronger than any pretence of pan-Arab or even Islamic solidarity.

That is the bad news. The worst news is that this depressive reality is going to remain intact for as long as the Arab-Muslim masses choose to allow the usurpers of their will to continue treating them as herds of animals that need only fodder, but deserve no freedoms or human and civil rights.

The fact of the matter is that our calamity with Israel–an outpost of American and Western imperialism that will disappear sooner or later–is much smaller than our calamity with the existing Arab and pseudo-Muslim regimes that rule hundreds of millions of Arabs and Muslims with an iron fist all in the service of Zionism and Zionist-controlled America.

Hence, all other matters, including the bleeding Palestinian wound, are merely residual symptoms of the Arab-Muslim malady, which impedes Arab-Muslim unity and perpetuates the present state of paralysis throughout the Muslim world.

Hence, it would be futile and unrealistic to count on the member States of the Arab League to take a truly dignified stand in support of the Palestinian uprising because, as it is said in Arabic, “one can’t give what one doesn’t have.”

In short, the present Arab reality can be diagnosed as follows:

1. Most Arab regimes do not really represent their peoples, and public opinion in most Arab countries has virtually no bearing or effect on government policies or actions.

2. Most Arab regimes, are not really answerable to their peoples, but rather are ultimately responsible to the United States.

3. Many influential Arab regimes are not really independent since American armies are stationed on their national soil.

4. The United States and Israel are two sides of the same coin, since American policy, not only toward Palestine but toward the entire region, is determined by Zionist Jews holding American citizenship but whose hearts, minds and loyalty lie with their Jewish-Zionist brethren in Tel Aviv.

5. An inescapable inference from this is that Arab regimes are indirectly and invariably controlled, or at least heavily influenced, by Israel.

6. This means that most Arab States cannot take effective measures against the Zionist regime, irrespective of the amount of young Palestinian blood the Zionist Nazis spill in the streets of the West Bank and the Gaza Strip.

7. The vast bulk of Arab regimes are more concerned with keeping themselves in power, by whatever means necessary, than in supporting the Palestinian cause.

Now, this should explain the state of individual and collective Arab powerlessness and paralysis.

The remedy
The ultimate remedy or solution to the morbidity facing the Arab-Muslim world is to reinstitute the political authority of Islam.

However, the first step of expediting this ultimate goal is to dislodge Arab and Muslim countries from subservience to America.

The first step in doing this is the strengthening of the defenses of the collective Arab-Islamic body and boosting the morale of the Umma through the following means:

  1. Fostering the awareness among the masses that the existing regimes are not legitimate in any way, not only because they are not elected by the people, but also because they connive with the United States against the vital interests of the Umma.
  2. Fostering the awareness among the masses that they have an absolute religious, human, legal, moral and political right to disobey and disregard the authority of these regimes.
  3. Mobilizing and recruiting free elements (the vanguard ) throughout the Arab-Muslim world, who would be willing, even eager, to sacrifice their own lives, for the purpose of rooting out American colonization in and of Arab countries.
  4. Launching a widespread Jihad from Casablanca to Jakarta against the enemies of Islam.
  5. This Jihad must be guided by true and sublime Islamic principles, and innocent civilians must not be targeted under any circumstances, except when Muslim civilians are attacked.
  6. This Jihad should be very decentralized in order to guarantee continuity and thwart enemy repression.
  7. The forces of Jihad must be willing to offer martyrs and prisoners in the hundreds of thousands because the price for freedom will be as precious as freedom itself.
  8. If Arabs and Muslims cower, then they will only have themselves to blame, and Israel and the Zionists would be perfectly free to destroy al-Masjidul Aqsa and even more!

So, the choice is ours: either dignified Jihad or enslavement by the Zionists and America!

It’s Disgusting!

Dr. Musil Shehada

Our children are being killed on a massive scale. Our cities are being bombarded by heavy artillery by the Israeli choppers. Our women and children are becoming the hunting targets for Israeli snipers. Our lands are being savagely ravaged by the Israeli ever-expanding cruel occupation and the death toll keeps climbing. The Palestinians are indeed suffering greatly. They are being collectively tortured. Their economy is in shambles, their leadership twisted, their breaking point within reach. Israeli killing is not a tactic but is just part of its strategy which has been in practice since the emergence of Zionism into our midst. The Jewish State has to remain predominantly Jewish and the presence of non-Jews among them reminds them of the danger of diluting their majority with non-Jews.

What are we doing about it? Not much, I am afraid. Three hundred million Arabs from the Arab Gulf to the Atlantic Ocean and over 1 billion Muslims are simply going on with their normal lives while all kinds of political charlatans are calling for Arab unity and Arab support for the suffering of the Palestinian people. Let’s not forget the summit meetings of Arabs and Muslims who in shameful repetition are “condemning” Israeli atrocities but that’s as far as it will go. “Allah Akbar”, “Honor”, “Dignity”, “Freedom”, “Glory to the Arabs” are nothing but old clichés that have been repeated by our leaders to put the Arab masses to sleep. They are calling others to do something about this tragedy, but they should be kept out of any involvement. What’s wrong with the Arabs, for God’s sake? Do they lack the human and financial resources? Do they lack weapons? Where are all these sophisticated weapons that were purchased with hundreds of billions of dollars? Every purchase is explained as weapons to be used against the enemies of the Arabs and Islam.

Desecrating the Aqsa Mosque, killing Arab women and children and the occupation of Arab lands has not given them a clue as to who the enemy is? Were these weapons bought to defend the Arabs or to defend the leaders against their own peoples? Are the people the enemy? Have they ever heard of Zionism and Israel? Have they ever heard of Israel’s daily cruel atrocities against the Arabs in Palestine? Do they watch TV to see the daily killing of Arab women and children, or do they use the remote to change the channel so they don’t have to be reminded of their leadership obligations toward the oppressed masses? They cannot look at these grotesque scenes that reminds them of these obligations.

Not that they are blind, but it’s easier to pretend that they are, so as to cater to their “friendship” to America and to their “secret” commitments toward the Zionist enemy. Relations with the U.S. and the Zionist entity should remain intact and strong and nothing the enemy can do could weaken such relation, not our bloodshed, not the killing of our women and children, not the caravan of dead martyrs! Our pride is dead and our low self-esteem has become the goal of such leadership. Protests by the disgruntled masses are prohibited and our glorious armies are being used against the masses they were supposed to protect.

Worse yet, the PA leadership is singing the same mantra along with others. Our destiny, our freedom, our longing for independence and our rights in Palestine have been subjugated to cater to the personal goals of Mr. Arafat, who is desperately seeking to become a president of our shambled and dissected patrimony regardless of the heavy price we have to pay.

The lands of our ancestors have to be sacrificed so that Mr. Arafat would gain the title of a president on 10% of our lands. Never mind the rights of our children and their legacy to the lands, never mind the suffering of the Palestinians in the sub-human conditions of the refugee camps and forget about their rights to return as long as our “glorious” president has returned with his gang to augment the Israeli’s control of our destiny. The man who used to fight the enemy is now fighting those who are presently fighting the enemy.

The siege and occupation of our towns and cities by the cruel Israelis would not change his resolve to join the fight; on the contrary, he is simply offering his apologies to the enemy while putting in jail all those who are fighting for freedom. How can a man who used to fight Israel become its protector against his own people? Whatever happened to the old slogan “liberation from the Jordan river to the sea”? Now Mr. Arafat has changed the slogan to read “From the Jordan River to the Jordan River” but with a dent along the river called the West Bank.

Of course, never mind the humiliating conditions attached to such a pathetic “State”. No army, no continuity, no freedom of movement and Israeli checkpoints will remain a reminder that we are not free. Oslo, which was supposed to become a venue for freedom, has become the gate to permanent enslavement, and the Palestinian masses have gained a new oppressor who has joined the U.S. and Israel, namely the PA.

Those who claim that we have no other alternatives have not studied history and they have not heard of liberation movements whose weapon has been nothing more than determination. They have never heard of Vietnam, a country with inferior capabilities that dragged the U.S. into the mud. They have never heard of Nelson Mandela who from his jail was able to lead his people to total freedom against overwhelming odds. They have never heard of Afghanistan, a country with modest numbers and inferior weapons and strong determination that fought the mighty Russian army to gain independence.

Why is it that most of those who fought for freedom have reached their goals, while we are still suffering under the cruel yoke of our oppressor? Well! We have a unique situation where our leaders, instead of fighting alongside the masses, are fighting alongside the enemy and against our masses. The whole thing is simply disgusting, to say the least!

Palestinians die for their land and olive trees

Ramzy Baroud

On 17 October a Palestinian farmer was shot dead by Israeli settlers near the village of Beit Fourik.

On 26 October, a 75-year-old farmer was tortured to death by a group of settlers near the same location. In between, many farmers have been wounded by armed settler attacks.

The death of those Palestinians was by no means an accident, as many Palestinian deaths are justified. In both cases, the killing was deliberate and, like earlier killings, they will go unnoticed and unpunished.

And in both cases, the men were harvesting their olive trees, a steadfast symbol of a confiscated land, stolen at gunpoint. The olive branch has come to be known as a symbol of peace. For Palestinians, the olive tree is also a symbol of peace, courage, defiance and life. For the Israeli army and Jewish settlers, the symbol of the olive is simply death. When many Palestinians innocently believed that the Oslo peace accord was bringing them a just peace, they rushed to the streets waving olive branches, celebrating the long awaited justice and peace.

Seven years later, Palestinians realized that Oslo was nothing but a wicked experience that brought little peace and much poverty and humiliation. And as they began to pass by their martyrs one by one as they headed to crowded graveyards, they attached olive branches to the caskets and around the newly watered graves, a symbol of continuation and resistance.

Olive harvests have always been a festive occasion for most Palestinians, even for the refugees who own no land or olive trees. Complete strangers help each other harvest the land. Young children help to clean the olives from small branches that fall as men and women stand on high ladders, collecting the olives with enthusiasm and a great sense of joy.

Beit Fourik, however, like other Palestinian villages in the West Bank, has long lost that festive spirit that used to come with the olive harvest. The once small Israeli Jewish settlement of Eitamar near Nablus has stripped them of their harvest joy, as armed gangs of settlers are dedicated to stealing Beit Fourik’s land, to burning and destroying its olive trees and to endlessly expanding at the expense of helpless villagers. Two years ago, 75-year-old Al-Haj Mohammed al-Zalmout was killed at dawn by a gang of settlers. The settlers may have feared that the sound of their bullets would gather nearby farmers, so they surrounded the man and savagely crushed his skull with rocks. His own son, Udi, then failed to recognize his father’s face. The old man’s donkey was still there, as were the harvested olives of the remaining four trees which sat on a nearby blanket. Nothing was done since then to protect Beit Fourik from barbaric individuals who thought that killing the old man would bring terror to other villagers, provoking them to abandon their land.

Despite the daily assaults, harassment, beatings of young and old farmers, destruction and seizure of the land by Eitamar settlers, supervised and aided by the Israeli army, Beit Fourik has never given up its claims to its land or to the remaining olive trees. As the Israeli army went on the rampage a few weeks ago, shooting, killing and shelling Palestinian towns and villages with missiles and apache helicopters, Jewish settlers also went on a rampage. The recent violence and iron fist of the Israeli army was a golden opportunity for West Bank and Gaza settlers to abuse more Palestinians, including the villagers of Beit Fourik. It was time to steal more land, to destroy more trees, and to expand the borders of Eitamar.

On 17 October, Farid Manasra, joined by three other family members, met at their land to resume their harvest. Despite the expected threats of Eitamar settlers, the young men insisted on harvesting their olives on time–after all, the land was theirs as it had been in their family for many generations. Little did the men know, Eitamar settlers were hiding nearby, waiting for the “zero hour.” Minutes later, Farid was laying dead after being hit by a bullet, and all his relatives were wounded. There is little doubt that the young man’s casket was showered with olive branches, as his fellow villagers held his dead body high with pride, delivering him to the graveyard where Al-Haj Mohammed al-Zalmout was buried two years ago. And like Al-Haj al-Zalmout’s olives, Farid’s harvest must have been carried to the grieving family, a gesture of pride and a vow of resistance. With every drop of blood and every new martyr, Palestinians grow more attached to their land, to their olive trees and to their harvest. No wonder Palestinian painters equate the olive trees to ever-standing martyrs.

Perhaps once we understand the deepening intimacy between Palestinians and olive trees, which for them is an icon of lasting resistance, we may be able to understand what a Palestinian poet meant when he said, “If the olive trees knew the hands that planted them, their oil would have become tears...”

When the President lies

Khalid Amayreh

During his visit to the Jewish settlement of K’far Dorom in the southern Gaza Strip on 21 November, Israeli President Moshe Katsav proved again that his tongue functions much more swiftly than his brain does.

Reacting to Egypt’s decision to recall its ambassador to Israel in protest against continued Israeli atrocities against the mostly unarmed and unprotected Palestinian population, Katsav said, referring to Ambassador Muhammed Basiouni, “I hope he will tell the truth when he returns to Cairo, that Israelis don’t kill innocent people, don’t shoot passenger cars, and don’t set mosques on fire.”

Without the slightest doubt, Katsav’s remarks are brash and malicious lies reminiscent of other infamous lies produced by Zionists throughout their relatively brief history. It was the Zionists who told the world that Palestine was “a land without a people for a people without a land,” and it was they who claimed that the Nazi-like Zionist gangs didn’t drive the bulk of the Palestinians out of their ancestral homeland when Israel was misbegotten in 1948. And now, Mr. Katsav has the chutzpah to claim that Israel is not killing innocents.

The remarks are so brazenly blatantly false that one is required to belie his own eyes in order to believe Mr. Katsav.

Katsav’s stark lies would have passed undetected had he made them a few years ago, when Zionist killers could carry out their dirty missions “quietly”, away from the eyes of the world.

But now, as the crimes are reported and transmitted live, as they happen, to every bedroom around the world, the Zionist task of selling their lies to the world becomes ever more difficult because there are limits to the Zionists’ ability to twist the facts and because one can’t deceive all the people all of the time.

But, let us go back to Katsav’s remarks. For the sake of argument, let us ask, is it true that Israeli occupation soldiers and settlers don’t shoot innocent Palestinians? Is it true they don’t shoot at passenger cars? Is it true they don’t set mosques on fire?

The answer to all these sophistic queries, as every Palestinian man, woman and child, and as every reporter, cameraman and human rights activist operating in Palestine knows quite well, is an absolute and clarion “YES”.

Indeed, and without indulging in argumentation and debate, we would like to ask Mr. Katsav, if he is truly interested in knowing the truth (I firmly believe he isn’t), if Jamal Yazbek, who was killed during the attempted Jewish pogrom against Nazareth on 8 October, was innocent or not, and if not, please tell us what his crime was?

We would like to ask Mr. Katsav if Isam Judeh Saleh, who was abducted by Jewish settlers on 9 October while returning home from work at the village of Um Saffa near Ramallah, and then electrocuted, axed, bludgeoned, his eyes plucked out, and then shot twice in the head, was innocent or not, and if not, please tell us what his crime was?

We would like to ask Mr. Katsav if Sayel Sweidan, of the village of Azzun near Kalkilya, who was abducted and subsequently murdered by settlers also on 9 October, was innocent or not, and if not, please tell us what his crime was?

We would like to ask you, Mr. President, to tell us if Farid Nassasreh, the Palestinian farmer who was murdered in front of his children on 17 October while harvesting his olive crops at the village of Beit Furik near Nablus, was innocent or not, and if not, please tell us what his crime was. We would also like you to tell us, Mr. President, what happened to his murderers, Yaron Degani and Gad Tena of the settlement of Itamar, and why they were not arrested and tried?

We would like to ask you, Mr. President, if 2-year-old Sara Abdul Haq, who was killed by a Jewish settler’s bullet while returning home from hospital in her father’s car on 14 October, was innocent or not, and if not, please tell us what her crime was?

We are not going to ask you what you think about the death of Muhammed Durra, and the other 60 Durras that your murderous and trigger-happy soldiers killed since 28 September, for these are, as your lie-fabricators unashamedly claim, guilty of intercepting IDF bullets and their mothers sent them to die because sacrificing children is an old Palestinian custom! Why did the Jews of Ghetto Warsaw send their children beyond the Nazi lines? Didn’t they worry that the kids might get killed? Were their mothers so callous?

And you said Israeli soldiers and settlers don’t fire on civilian cars? Are you sure, Mr. Katsav, that what you are saying is true? Then who killed the four university students near Rafah on 22 November? And who fired at the school bus in Gaza two weeks earlier? And who killed 55-year-old Mustafa Mahmoud Ilayan on 14 October while he was returning home to Nablus from Ramallah, where he worked at a grocery store?

Tell us, Mr. President, why you kill those innocent people. Their souls and innocent blood will continue to chase you and haunt you unceasingly, day and night, until you go to hell where you belong.

And you said Jews don’t burn mosques? Who then burned and destroyed all these mosques in Palestine? Do Muslims burn their own mosques? Who set the Mosque of Hiwwara on fire on 20 October? Who burned the al-Baher Mosque? Who burned the Tabariyya Mosque? Who demolished the Sarafand Mosque, Mr. President? Who converted as many as 50 mosques in Palestine into night-clubs and dens where vice is practised? Why do you carry out such ugly deeds, Mr. President? Don’t you claim that you are a heavenly light upon mankind? Are you really a light or an abomination upon mankind?

Yes, Mr. President, you may think that “goyem” cannot be innocent since, as the Talmud teaches, the life of a non-Jew is nonessential, or you may think, as the Talmud teaches, that “the best of the goyem, kill ‘em.”

But this kind of thinking, Mr. President, is what had made you incur all the persecutions, the inquisitions, the pogroms, and eventually the holocaust.

Mr. President, so much has been written and said about the strife in Palestine, but very few put their hands on the gist of the problem.

The gist of the problem, Mr. President, is your twisted and deformed perception of and toward non-Jews.

The problem, Mr. President, is that you think of yourselves as “the chosen people of God,” and others (e.g. us, the Palestinians) as animals in human form. Doesn’t it all come down to this in the final analysis?

You may believe as you wish, Mr. President, but everything has a price, that, sooner or later, will have to be paid.

If the Western democracies are waiting for an exodus of Palestinian refugees before they rein-in Israel, they may have to wait indefinitely, as this is…

 The last stand

 Dr. Daud Abdullah*

 Faced with cries of ‘death or expulsion’ and having no sovereignty over their territories, this is the last stand by the Palestinian people to preserve their national identity and existence in occupied Palestine. They are determined, despite the heavy losses, not to increase the ranks of the world’s 5 million Palestinian refugees. Their task is undoubtedly a difficult one. But even so, they are not prepared to wait for the world community to act. Bitter experience has taught them never to be bitten from the same hole twice.

 Like every other people who were subjected to foreign rule, the Palestinians cannot afford the luxury of reading history simply for entertainment. For them, its primary purpose must be to identify the pitfalls of the past and extract guidelines for the future. They had with other Arabs risen up against their Ottoman rulers in support of the European allied war effort with the promise of gaining independence. Their condition today is, arguably, worse than it was then. They have no control over their economy or territorial borders. They cannot move freely within their own land without the permission of the settler. And they cannot trade with fellow Arabs, because the latter now recognize the borders imposed by the occupier.

 This is the background to the current fighting; a people yearning to be free in their land, to shape their own destiny, and to conduct relations without the let or hindrance of external forces. Thus, the measure by which these rights are denied is truly inconceivable. One misconception must be eradicated before any sense can be made of this gory affair; that is, the Palestinians are not up against three or five million Jewish settlers only. They are for all practical purposes the sacrificial victims of Western political wrongdoing.

 Whereas 100 hundreds years ago the sponsors of this project endorsed it on the basis of the white man’s burden to civilize the dark peoples of the world, Westerners today do so in the name of democracy. But what kind of democracy? If Israel is a model and true representation of the only democracy in the Middle East, then surely the Palestinians and Arabs have the right to reject it.

 Here are a people [the Jews] who because of their friendship with Western colonial powers managed to obtain 57% of Palestine at a time when they only constituted 33% of the population. Today they are fighting the Palestinians tooth and nail for what remains of their historic homeland–less than 20%. When confronted with this injustice, they claim immunity by divine right, dismissing every suggestion that they are essentially invaders, settlers and occupiers.

 Creating a monster
In order to advance this cause, Zionist Israel concluded convenient peace treaties with the neighbouring Arab States to secure their borders. After agreeing to return the stolen land for peace in 1993, the Jewish State went ahead and seized another 600,000 dunums of land in the West Bank and Gaza Strip. Since the entire Oslo process turned out to be a scam to increase their subjugation, the Palestinians have decided to throw all caution and restraint to the wind. They have nothing to lose now except the repressive laws that seek to dehumanize them.

 Under the present situation they have few rights but unlimited duties. There is no hope. What hope can there be when 6,000 settlers control 42% of the Gaza Strip and 1.2 million Palestinians are crammed into the remaining 58%? Throughout the 1980s, the Israeli Occupiers debated the options of ‘apartheid or expulsion’ for the inhabitants of the West Bank and Gaza Strip. Their choice of apartheid was eventually packaged and endorsed in the Oslo agreements. Palestinian rejection of the various agreements has forced Israel to resort to military measures to ensure compliance if not acceptance. The democratic nations are, meanwhile, sitting on the fence waiting for the job to be completed as fast as possible and done with. But, this is neither 1948 nor 1967. A new generation has come of age since the occupation; a generation that is not only committed to resistance but also to the liberation of all that was illegally occupied.

 For all its worth, Western politicians can issue calls for self-restraint and return to the negotiating table. Yet, the truth is such pleas would never realize Palestinian independence, repatriation or sovereignty over national borders, Jerusalem and the natural resources, or the dismantling of Jewish settlements. These calls, moreover, would never be respected or trusted when the same powers continue to arm Israel to the tune of billions of dollars each year.

 In reality, this is a process in which some have more rights than others. While the Jewish State has the right to use helicopter gunships and missiles against civilian targets, it is the Palestinian duty to die peacefully. Israel can prosecute a strategic war against the Palestinians and their neighbours while the Arabs must scrupulously pursue the option of peace. Whereas the Israelis have the right to form governments of national unity with extremist fundamentalists and war criminals all lumped together; the Palestinians must dutifully perform their obligation of imprisoning their opposition. In the same way, while heavily armed Jewish settlers from Poland, Russia, Ethiopia and America must be afforded the right to roam, pillage and plunder, the Palestinian sons and daughters of this land must be denied the right to build an army to defend themselves. They must rely on stones and Molotov cocktails.

 This is the reason why the Oslo process ended in disgrace and bankruptcy. It invested all its effort in providing security for one party while neglecting the rights of the other. It enabled Jews from everywhere to immigrate to Israel while it asserted the requirement that Palestinians should live in permanent exile. If the West were waiting for a flood of refugees across the borders before they rein-in Israel, they may have to wait indefinitely. They have created a dangerous monster without realizing it. A monster that does not hesitate to undermine Arab interests but even the Western democracies that support it. The issues of imprisoned spy Jonathon Pollard, the sale of military technology to China, and the recent improprieties of U.S. Ambassador to Israel, Martin Indyk, are only the tip of the iceberg. The deluge is nearer than it seems.

Why Palestine?
Nato’s success in ensuring the return of 810,000 Kosovar refugees from a total of 850,000 was certainly a commendable achievement that required enormous political will backed by military force. That it neither tolerated Serb “brutality and butchery” nor allowed them to go unpunished was also laudable. So why are they condoned in Palestine? Is it a lack of information? Or, is it a lack of moral courage to rein-in the Jewish State that defies the will of the international community by refusing to cooperate with a UN human rights inquiry? In the case of Kosovo, British Foreign Secretary Cook told the House of Commons on 25 March 1999, “We have seen villages shelled, crops burned and farm animals slaughtered not for any legitimate military purpose, but as acts of ethnic hatred.”

 Palestine may not be in Europe but thanks to satellite technology the evidence of civilian homes and factories being shelled cannot be denied. Yet, here in Palestine it is not only farm animals that are being slaughtered. Is the Honourable Foreign Secretary not aware that children are being killed in their beds and in orchards picking olives with their parents?

 There is today no lack of corroborating evidence that the Israeli occupying forces and settlers continue to destroy Palestinian agricultural lands throughout the West Bank and Gaza. Olive orchards have been bulldozed in Hossan village, in Bethlehem, Salfit and Nablus. Banana plantations were similarly bulldozed in Jericho. The destruction of these olive orchards and agricultural lands comes at a time when Palestinian farmers have begun to harvest crops tended over the past year. This constitutes a major form of income, which sustains the majority of the Palestinian population. Is there really any difference between this systematic killing of the Palestinian people and destruction of their economy with that obtained in the Balkans?

 Surely the same international humanitarian laws and human rights treaties that were invoked in Kosovo and East Timor should be applied here. Of course, this is not only the failure of the British government but also the entire European Union. They still view this as a war against Palestinian terrorism, even though the Palestinians have suffered the most casualties throughout the past month. This really is the consequence of policies that ensured the appointment of security advisers to the region without ever contemplating the deployment of human rights officers to protect the victims of Israeli terror.

 Elsewhere, in East Timor, Britain gave an undertaking that it would be one of the six nations leading the implementation of the UN process. The democratic world welcomed and launched the plan to establish the wishes and legitimate aspirations of the people of East Timor on whether they wanted autonomy or full independence. The Palestinians who have been struggling for independence since 1915 are still denied independence even though their right to self-determination has been endorsed and upheld by Article 22 of the Covenant of the League of Nations and numerous UN resolutions thereafter.

 If the Palestinians were seeking a status or rights not recognized by international law, one would probably understand and excuse the attitude of Western governments. But since they are a people whose land was invaded, conquered and occupied by foreigners and they are only exercising their legitimate right to end that occupation, the policies toward them appear both inexplicable and inexcusable.

Everything that transpires in Palestine today smells of the dogma of a master race and a subhuman people. One in which Jews have divine rights to rule, dominate and abuse while Palestinians must serve as hewers of wood and drawers of water. This is a dangerous development. In the past it landed Europe into two destructive wars that extended far beyond its shores. Whereas in the 1980s Israel toyed with the idea of ‘apartheid or expulsion’ for the Palestinians, they adopted the worst-case scenario of ‘death or expulsion’ after the Palestinians rejected the apartheid option packaged in Oslo. In this al-Aqsa Intifada the Palestinians have chosen the first instead of the latter. This is the last stand.

* The author is a researcher at the Palestinian Return Centre (London) and editor of its Return Review. See (www.prc.org.uk)

Crossfire

E. Yaghi

When 12-year-old Palestinian Mohammed spoke of how he wanted to be a soccer champion, his black eyes flashed and he would kick an imaginary ball off to a successful goal. His mother, busy with her six other children, would nod her head and say, “Yes, perhaps one day when you are big and stronger, you will go off to the Olympics and win a gold medal.”

“Before that, Mom,” he would say enthusiastically, “I just want to be the star of my school soccer team. Then I will begin to think about growing up and the Olympics.”

On 30 September 2000, Mohammed’s father said to him, “Come on, son. Let’s go and try to sell that old Fiat. Since I’ve been out of work, we need the money to buy food and supplies.”

It would be good, thought Mohammed. Good to get out and away from the demonstrations. Good to forget that the Israelis had closed the schools. Good to forget the curfews at night. Good to forget the Israeli bullets aimed at kids like himself during confrontations with them. Good to have the chance to be alone with his father.

So, he put on his new striped blue shirt and faded blue jeans, and ran a comb hurriedly through the soft curls of his thick dark hair. He dashed out of his three-room cinder-block home to join his father whose fingers fidgeted as he took long deep puffs on the butt of his cigarette. They got in the used Fiat and drove off in a cloud of red dust to look for a used car salesmen. As his father drove, Mohammed looked at the sturdy hands that gripped the steering. Hands that will protect me, he thought. Slim hands but strong. That his mother would have a nice lunch ready upon their return let a smile creep across his small, troubled face. He tried to shut scenes of the demonstrators, many of whom were children his own age or younger, out of his mind. Instead, he tried to picture himself and his soccer team heading to victory on the field.

The car screeched to a halt. Father and son jumped out and the boy heard his father say to a salesman, after some bartering, “OK. It’s a deal then.”

He helped his father count the money before boarding a taxi that would take them back home to the cheerful face of his mother and the shouts and laughter of his younger brothers and sisters. Being with them almost made him forget the fact that they were poor and that his father lost his job and now had no means to support his family. Looking up, he noticed his father studying him. Then the parent reached over to tousle the boy’s dark hair and with his strong protective hand, patted him on his shoulder. “It will be ok, son. Don’t worry.” Then, “Let’s go home and get something to eat.”

Mohammed’s mother never let him join in any demonstrations. It was because as she said, “I don’t want them carrying pieces of your body home dead to me. What will I do without you?”

He would look into her gentle black eyes, so similar to his own, and find peace and security there. She was the essence of love. She was what mothers were all about. He was tempted to join in the struggle for freedom but heard his mother’s voice saying to him every time he went out, “You are a bright boy. Everyone likes you. Stay out of trouble and your future will be promising. You can help your people by getting an education and by winning the gold medal in the Olympics you so admire.”

So he would bury himself in a book, trying not to hear the screams, the shouts, the bullets that flew right past his window. He would listen to his mother and use his energy for something other than throwing stones at armoured tanks.

And now, here he was with his father, waiting for the yellow taxi to take them home. He caught sight of one not too far from where they stood and stepped out to flag it down. It stopped and his father told the driver, “to the camp.”

He sat between his father and the driver. Had it not been for the clashes, the day would have been beautiful. The relentless heat had given way to an Indian summer. The smell of the sea was in the air and if the boy listened, he could hear the cry of seagulls in their search for food.

Unexpectedly, his body shuddered and his father put his arm around him seeming to sense his son’s apprehension of something foreboding and evil. Then it came as sure as the day. The sound of bullets flying everywhere and the shouts of the demonstrators.

“We are surrounded. Come on, son, let’s get out of the taxi.”

Mohammed almost fell out. His whole body shook with fear. He felt paralyzed, not able to move. His father half dragged him, half carried him next to a small wall. There, crouching, he threw his son behind him, shielding him with his own thin body. At first the boy encouragingly told his father, “Don’t worry, Daddy. The ambulance will come and save us.”

But then bullets flew faster and more ferociously. He tried to be brave, but he did not want to die. Not now. Not when he was only 12. Not when he had hardly begun to live. He screamed in fear and huddled closer to his father who he knew was desperately trying to protect him. A hail of bullets rained down all around them. One Israeli soldier took aim at the small frame of the boy. An ambulance rushed to the scene. The same Israeli soldier, who shot Mohammed in the stomach, shot the ambulance driver in the head and riddled the boy’s father with bullets.

Mohammed did not live to go on to play in the Olympics or even to be a soccer star for his own elementary team at school. But his mother, left to care for his pet finches said, “He is now a star in heaven.”

His death has become a symbol of freedom and the Palestinian struggle against the Israeli occupation.

American Zionism

Edward Said

The recent events in Palestine have been a near-total triumph for Zionism in the United States for the first time since the modern re-emergence of the Palestinian national movement in the late 1960s. Political as well as public discourse has so definitively transformed Israel into the victim during the recent clashes that even though more than 200 Palestinian lives were lost and more than 5,000 casualties have been reported, it is still something called “Palestinian violence” that has disrupted the smooth and orderly flow of the “peace process.”

There is now a small litany of phrases that every editorial commentator either repeats verbatim or relies on as an unspoken assumption: these have been engraved in ears, minds and memories as a guide for the perplexed, a manual or machine for turning out phrases that have clogged the air for at least a month. I can recite most of them by heart: Barak offered more concessions at Camp David than any Israeli Prime Minister before him (90% of the territories and partial sovereignty over East Jerusalem); Arafat was cowardly and lacked the necessary courage to accept Israeli offers to end the conflict; Palestinian violence, directed by Arafat, has threatened Israel (all sorts of variations on this, including the wish to eliminate Israel, anti-Semitism, suicidal rage in order to get on television, putting children in the front lines so that they would become martyrs) and proved that an ancient “hatred” of the Jews motivates Palestinians; Arafat is a weak leader who allows his people to attack Jews and incite against them by releasing terrorists and producing schoolbooks that deny Israel’s existence.

There are probably one or two more formulae that I have not cited, but the general picture is that Israel is so surrounded by rock-throwing barbarians that even the missiles, tanks and helicopter gunships that have been used to “defend” Israelis from the violence are simply warding off a terrible force. Bill Clinton’s injunctions (dutifully parroted by his Secretary of State) for Palestinians to “pull back” goes a long way to suggest that it is Palestinians who are encroaching on Israeli territory, not the other way round.

It is also worth mentioning that so successful has this Zionization of the media been that not a single map has been published or shown on television to remind American viewers and readers–notoriously ignorant of both geography and history–that Israeli encampments, settlements, roads and barricades crisscross Palestinian land in Gaza and the West Bank. Moreover, as happened in Beirut in 1982, there is a veritable Israeli siege of Palestinians, including of Arafat and his men. Completely forgotten, if it was ever at all understood, is the system of Areas A, B and C by which the military occupation of 40% of Gaza and 60% of the West Bank continues, and which the Oslo peace process was never really designed to end, much less totally modify.

As suggested by the absence of geography in this most geographical of conflicts, the resulting void is a vitally important point since the pictures that are either shown or described are without context at all. I think the omission by the Zionized media was a deliberate one at the outset and has now become automatic. It has allowed phony commentators such as Thomas Friedman to peddle his wares shamelessly, droning on about American even-handedness, Israeli flexibility and generosity and his own perspicacious pragmatism with which he berates Arab leaders and stuns his bored readers. It has the result not only of permitting the completely preposterous notion of a Palestinian attack on Israel to prevail, but it also further dehumanizes Palestinians as being beasts without sentience or motive. Thus little wonder that when the figures of the dead and wounded are recited, no nationalities are given: this lets Americans assume that the suffering is equally divided between the “warring parties,” and in fact elevates Jewish suffering and reduces or eliminates Arab feelings entirely, except of course for rage. Rage and its cognates remain as the only and certainly the defining Palestinian emotion. It explains the violence, and indeed, it reifies it so that Israel has come to represent a decency and democracy that is forever surrounded by rage and violence. No other process can logically explain the stone-throwers and the stalwart Israeli “defense.”

Nothing is said of house demolitions, land expropriations, illegal arrests, torture and the like. Nothing is cited about what is (except for the Japanese occupation of Korea) the longest military occupation in modern times; nothing about UN resolutions; nothing about Israeli contraventions of all the Geneva Conventions; nothing about the sufferings of one entire people and the obduracy of another. Forgotten are the catastrophe of 1948, ethnic cleansing and massacres, the devastation of Qibya, Kafr Qassem, Sabra and Shatila, the long years of military government for non-Jewish Israeli citizens to say nothing of their continued oppression as a persecuted 20% minority within the Jewish State. Ariel Sharon at best is a provocation, never a war criminal; Ehud Barak a Statesman, never the assassin of Beirut. Terrorism is always on the Palestinian side of the ledger, defense on the Israeli.

What Friedman and pro-Israeli “peaceniks” fail to mention when they extol Barak’s unprecedented generosity is the real substance of it. We are not reminded that his commitment to a one-third withdrawal (of about 12%) made at Wye 18 months ago has never occurred. Of what value then are more such “concessions?” We are told that he was willing to give back 90% of the territory. What gets left out is that the 90% consists of what Israel has no intention of giving back. Greater Jerusalem is well over 30% of the West Bank; large settlements to be annexed are another 15%; military roads of areas have yet to be determined. So after all this is deducted, 90% of the balance isn’t so much after all.

As for Jerusalem: the Israel concession was principally in being willing to discuss and maybe, just maybe, to offer shared authority over the Haram al-Sharif. The breathtaking dishonesty of the matter is that all of West Jerusalem (principally Arab in 1948) was already conceded by Arafat, plus most of a vastly expanded East Jerusalem. One detail further: Palestinians’ firing by small arms on Gilo is routinely made to seem like gratuitous violence, whereas no one mentions that Gilo itself sits on land confiscated from Beit Jala, the place from which the firing emanates. Besides, Beit Jala was disproportionately shelled by Israeli helicopters using missiles to destroy civilian houses.

The total blanketing of the mainstream has taken place on the assumption that no Palestinian or Arab or Islamic position on such matters as Israeli terror tactics against civilians, settler-colonialism, or military occupation exists at all, or is worth hearing from. This is simply without precedent in the annals of U.S. journalism, and is a direct reflection of a Zionist mind-set that makes Israel the norm in human behaviour, thereby excluding from equal consideration the existence of 300 million Arabs and 1.2 billion Muslims. In the long run, this is of course a suicidal position for Zionists to be in, but such is the arrogance of power that the thought seems not to have occurred to anyone.

The mind-set I have described is truly staggering in its recklessness and, were it not very much a practical as well as actual distortion of reality, one could quite easily be talking about a form of private mental derangement. But it corresponds very closely to the official Israeli policy of dealing with Palestinians not as a people with a history of dispossession for which in large measure Israel is directly responsible, but as a periodic nuisance for whom force, and neither understanding nor full accommodation, is the only possible response. Everything else is literally unthinkable. This astonishing blindness is compounded in the United States since Arabs and Muslims are scarcely paid attention to except as (I have said in an earlier article) the butt of every aspiring politician. Recently, Hillary Clinton announced in a gesture of the most revolting hypocrisy that she was returning a $50,000 donation from an American-Muslim group because, she said, they supported terrorism; this in fact was an outright lie, since the group in question had only said that it supported Palestinian resistance against Israel during the current crisis, not in itself an untoward position but criminalized in the American system only because a totalitarian Zionism requires that any–and I mean literally any–criticism of what Israel does is simply intolerable and the rankest anti-Semitism. And this despite the fact that (again literally) the entire world has criticized Israel’s policies of military occupation, disproportionate violence, and the siege of the Palestinians. In America you must refrain from any criticism, otherwise you are hounded as an anti-Semite requiring the severest opprobrium.

In fine, American Zionism has made any serious public discussion of Israel, by far the largest-ever recipient of U.S. foreign aid, its past and its future, a taboo not to be broken in any circumstance. To call this literally the last taboo in American discourse is by no means an exaggeration. Abortion, homosexuality, the death penalty, even the sacrosanct military budget have been talked about with some freedom (although always within limits). The American flag can be burned in public, whereas the systematic continuity of Israel’s 52-year-old treatment of the Palestinians is virtually unimaginable, a narrative with no permission to appear.

This consensus might be somehow tolerable were it not for the fact that it makes the continuing punishment and dehumanization of the Palestinian people an actual virtue. There is simply no people in the world today whose killing on television screens seems to be considered by most American viewers to be acceptable as well-deserved punishment. This is the case with Palestinians whose daily loss of life in the past month is herded under the rubric “the violence on both sides,” as if the stones and slings of young men thoroughly tired of injustice and repression were a major offence rather than the courageous resistance to a demeaning fate meted out to them not just by Israeli soldiers armed by America, but by a peace process designed to coop them up in Bantustans and reservations fit for animals.

That the U.S. supporters of Israel could have plotted for seven years to produce a document designed essentially to cage people like inmates in an asylum or prison–that is the real crime. And that this could be passed off as peace instead of the desolation that it really has been all along, that surpasses my powers to understand or adequately describe as anything less than untrammeled immorality. The worst thing of all is that so iron-like is the wall protecting American discourse about Israel that no questions can be put to the minds that produced Oslo and that for seven years have been passing off their scheme to the world as peace. One scarcely knows which is more pernicious, the mentality that thinks of Palestinians as not entitled even to express a sense of injustice (they are too low a form for that) or the one that continues to plot their further enslavement.

Were this the whole it would be bad enough. But our miserable status as far as U.S. Zionism is concerned is compounded by the absence of any institution here or in the Arab world ready and able to produce an alternative. I fear that the coverage of those stone-throwing protesters in Bethlehem, Gaza, Ramallah, Nablus and Hebron may not be adequately reflected in the dithering Palestinian leadership, unable either to retire or to go forward. That is the ultimate pity of it.

When will the world wake up and break out of their conditioning? When will they learn to think for themselves? When will they learn to understand the language of politics, or rather its lies?

My wife, Suha, looking extremely shaken by the explosions, ushered Amir to me to hold and hug him. She was carrying at the same time his shocked but silent twin sister, Sama, who always prefers to keep her emotions inside whenever she is extremly frightened. I hugged Amir, kissed him, and whispered in his ear, “Don’t be afraid; it’s only the sound of thunder.”

But my words went unheard in little Amir’s ears. As he strongly clung his arms around my neck, he buried his head in my chest and his loud cry turned slowly, slowly into a frightened weeping voice. Again and again, I kept telling him the same words and explaining to him, “It is the winter season, and you know, in winter and before it rains, we see lightening in the sky, and then we hear loud thunder, but it really doesn’t hurt us; in fact, it is good for us, because after the lightening and the thunder comes the rain, and it provides us with drinking and bathing water, and it makes trees blossom.:

My little Amir suddenely stopped weeping, looked at me with tearful eyes and said, “I don’t like winter.”

I answered, “Why? It’s good for us.”

He looked at me again and said, “If it’s good for us, why does it make all those horrible sounds and frighten us?”

For a moment I didn’t know how to answer. I was searching for some logical argument, but he didn’t even allow me to continue my search as his soft voice mixed with his weeping cut through my thinking as he said, “Daddy, you tell me that a liar goes to hell. I don’t want you to go to hell. That is not thunder, Daddy, that is a saroukh (rocket). Al-Yahudi (the Jews) shell us. I know, my friend Mahdi at school says that the Jews shell us with rockets every night. Yesterday, my other friend Anwar said at school that his brother Hammoudeh was shot by the Jews. He said that his face was full of blood, and then they carried him on a bed [meaning a coffin] and took him away.”

I found myself asking him, “Where did they take him to?”

My little Amir answered as he cried louder, “They took him to where he dies.”

He buried his face again in my chest and shouted as he continued crying and weeping, “I don’t like to die, Daddy, I don’t like to die...”

Later in the night, as the shells and heavy machine gun fire kept us all very sober, I tried to put Amir to bed so he could get some rest. He strongly kept resisting my efforts to put him in his bed. When I asked why he didn’t want to sleep in his bed as he usually does, his answer was, “I am afraid; they will come and carry me and take me to where I die, like Hammoudeh.”

I took my little Amir to my bed and let him sleep next to me. As he closed his eyes and started drifting into a deep sleep, I found myself telling him, “Sleep, my little one, sleep, have sweet dreams; it’s bed time here, I just hope that death time goes away, very far away, before you grow up into your youthful years!”

The National Religious Party and the Religious Settlers

The final innovation of Rabbi Kook the elder contributed most decisively to the popularity and political influence of his early followers and subsequently of Gush Emunim. During the period of redemption this innovation affected the conduct of the elect in relation to worldly concerns and contacts with other Jews and non-Jews. Rabbi Kook taught that the elect should not stand aloof from the rest of the world, as Jews had often done in the past. Realizing that other people were sinful and even satanic in nature, the elect had to attempt to bridge the gap between themselves and the others by actively involving themselves in society. Only by so doing would the elect have any chance to sanctify others. The elect should provide an example, exert influence politically and increasingly make contact with other people. Since the 1920s this doctrine has greatly influenced the behavior of those affiliated with the NRP. After being established in 1974, Gush Emunim vigorously reasserted this doctrine in spite of great resentment of the public. Unlike Orthodox Jews previously, Rabbi Kook's followers began to dress like secular Jews and only distinguished themselves outwardly by wearing skullcaps. To date they have followed the Israeli secular clothing fashions of the 1950s. In their schools they introduced portions of secular teaching into their curricula. They permitted their people to enroll in Israeli secular universities. They additionally established the religiously oriented Bar-Ilan University. Although restricting the Bar-Ilan teaching staff to religious Jews, Gush Emunim sought to expand the university's scope of instruction to include all the usual academic disciplines. The Haredim have consistently resented and viewed with abhorrence these pursuits of what they regard as secularization. Rabbi Kook insisted that each Jew had a religious duty to fight and to train to fight. NRP members have faithfully followed this teaching. Many Gush Emunim members have been and still are officers of the Israeli army's select units; their proportion in such units has continually increased. Gush Emunim religious school students have gained renown for their excellent combat qualities, their high motivation to fight, their relatively high casualty rate during the Lebanon war and their willingness to beat up Palestinians during the Intifada.

Gush Emunim has won broad public sympathy in Israeli Jewish society because of its attitude towards army service. This contrasts sharply with the societal antagonism directed against the Haredim for their dodging of military service. The doctrine of sanctity, attributed by the two Rabbi Kooks to almost every Zionist enterprise, contributed even more to the widespread public sympathy for and support of Gush Emunim. Tal contrasted the religious Zionist outlook of Rabbi Kook the younger and Gush Emunim with that of the secular left. Tal defined the secular left's Zionist outlook as a "poetic, lyrical notion, according to which the return to the soil, life within nature, the agricultural achievements, the secular creativity [are essential pans]." The two Rabbi Kooks, while acknowledging that the secular left's notion unwillingly served the coming of messianic redemption, emphasized "the military victories upon holy soil and the Jewish blood spilled on this soil." Rabbi Kook the younger, together with other Gush Emunim leaders, went further, according to Tal, by defining "the State of Israel as the kingdom of Israel and the kingdom of Israel as the kingdom of heaven on earth." Followers of Rabbi Kook still refer to Israel as the "earthly support of the Lord's throne." Israel Harel, one of the most important Gush Emunim leaders, used this expression to make a political point in his weekly column in Haaretz on September 12, 1996. Quoting an early essay by Rabbi Kook the elder, Harel wrote that the State of Israel was "the base of the Lord's throne in this world" and thus is and should be completely different from states "considered by Locke, Rosseau and others." For such people as Harel, total holiness envelops and justifies everything Israel does within the context of divinely inspired guidance. Tal wrote that from this vantage point "every action, every phenomenon, including secularism, will one day be engulfed by sacredness, by redemption." It is not inconceivable that this type of sacredness could lead to the exploding of nuclear bombs in order to end the power of Satan and to establish "the base of the Lord's throne in this world."

In many respects Gush Emunim members and the majority of NRP supporters have continued to resemble the early Zionist pioneers. This fact has boosted their public image. They have helped to promote this image by presenting themselves to the uninitiated as successors of the pioneers of the 1920s and 1930s who are still cherished in the Jewish national memory and lauded in Israeli education. As previously indicated, Gush Emunim members, except for their miniscule skullcaps, continue consciously to emulate the dress and mannerisms of the early pioneers. The almost exclusively Ashkenazi background of both the early pioneers and the Gush Emunim settlers help this emulation. All Gush Erriunim rabbis are Ashkenazi. The accepted Israeli standards of religious education, discussed in Chapter 3, are largely responsible for the absence of Oriental Jews among Gush Emunim rabbis. Although unwillingly to join, many Oriental Jews have supported and continue to support Gush Emunim. The Likud constituency has to date consistently supported Gush Emunim. By contrast, most members of the Labor Party supported Gush Emunim until the end of the 1970s but changed after Gush Emunim opposed the peace treaty with Egypt and demanded that Lebanon be annexed "as a part of the heritage of our ancestors, the tribes of Asher, Naphtali and Zebulun." Gush Emunim infuriated many Labor supporters by continuing to advocate other extreme hawkish policies and by fiercely opposing Sharon's 1982 alliance with the Lebanese Falangists, who were Christians and therefore considered to be idolaters. Gush Emunim's position in 1982 was that Jews in their battles and conquests should only rely upon God's help. Any alliances with non-Jews could incur God's wrath and lead to His withholding help. Such ideas were, even for extreme Labor Party hawks, unacceptable.

Gush Emunim and NRP politics must be understood within the context of ideology. The ideology makes clear what members of these groups wish to accomplish. Books written in English have unfortunately failed to discuss adequately this ideology. Lustick's book, For the Land and the Lord, which discusses Gush Emunim’s outward political behavior, is the prime example. Lustick relied to a great extent upon the writings of Harold Fisch for his analysis of Gush Emunim's political ideology. Fisch, a professor of English literature who seemingly has only limited competence in the Talmud and Cabbala, has mostly written for English-speaking readers and has primarily concentrated upon Christian fundamentalists in the United States. Lustick also relied somewhat upon the writings of Rabbi Menachem Kasher. Kasher was a highly respected talmudic scholar who wrote in Hebrew and influenced potential Gush Emunim initiates. His messianic tracts are well-known to many Gush Emunim and Yeshiva students. Lustick only briefly quoted Kasher twice and then obfuscated what he did quote. In our book we have relied more upon what Kasher wrote and have additionally utilized other Gush Emunim literature.

Gush Emunim activists live in a homogeneous West Bank society that they control. This society is mostly protected against "contamination" by rival detested ideologies, especially those that stem from Western culture and have been to some extent influenced the secular part of Israeli Jewish society. The possibility clearly exists that the Gush Emunim homogeneous society and its NRP supporters can increase their political power and influence within Israeli society. The ideology of the two Rabbis Kook is the determining force of NRP and Gush Emunim political action. The fundamental political tenet of Gush Emunim is that the Jewish people are unique. Gush Emunim members share this tenet with all Orthodox Jews, but they interpret it somewhat differently. Lustick discussed this tenet by focusing upon the Gush Emunim denial of one classical secular Zionist theme. Lustick correctly pinpointed the two assumptions of this theme, the first being that "Jewish life had been distorted on both the individual and the collective levels by the abnormality of diaspora existence." Second, only by undergoing a "process of normalization," by emigrating to Palestine and by forming a Jewish state can Jews become a normal nation. Quoting Fisch, Lustick stated that for Gush Emunim this classical idea "is the original delusion of the secular Zionists." The Gush Emunim argument is that secular Zionists measured that "normality" by applying non-Jewish standards that are satanic. The secular Zionists focused upon certain nations that they considered "normal" and asserted that the non-Jews in these normal nations were more advanced than were most diaspora Jews. Because of this, so argued the secular Zionists, Jews should try to emulate those non-Jews by becoming a "normal" people in a "normal" nation state. The Gush Emunim counter argument is: "Jews are not and cannot be a normal people. Their eternal uniqueness ... [is] the result of the covenant God made with them at Mount Sinai." Lustick further explained this Gush Emunim position by quoting one of the group's leaders, Rabbi Aviner: "'While God requires other normal nations to abide by abstract codes of justice and righteousness, such laws do not apply to Jews."' Haredi rabbis often cited this idea in their writings, but they strictly reserved its glaring consequences for the yet-to-come messianic age. The Halacha supports this reservation by carefully distinguishing between two situations in discussing codes of justice and righteousness. The Halacha permits Jews to rob non-Jews in those locales wherein Jews are stronger than non-Jews. The Halacha prohibits Jews from robbing non-Jews in those locales wherein the non-Jews are stronger. Gush Emunim dispenses with such traditional precautions by claiming that Jews, at least those in Israel and the Occupied Territories, are already living in the beginning of the messianic age.

Lustick failed to explain adequately the messianic age considerations and the distinctions between Jews and non-Jews. Harkabi's treatment was better. In discussing the halachic teaching and the Gush Emunim position regarding murders, Harkabi explained that the murder of a Jew, particularly when committed by a non-Jews, is in Jewish law the worst possible crime. He then quoted the Gush Emunim leader, Rabbi Israel Ariel. Relying upon the Code of Maimonides and the Halacha, Rabbi Ariel stated: "A Jew who killed a non-Jew is exempt from human judgment and has not violated the [religious] prohibition of murder." Harkabi noted further that this should be remembered when "the demand is voiced that all non-Jewish residents of the Jewish state be dealt with according to halachic regulations." Gush Emunim rabbis have continually reiterated that Jews who killed Arabs should not be punished. Gush Emunim members not only help such Jews who are punished by Israel's secular courts but also refuse to call those Jews "murderers." It logically follows that the religious settlers and their followers emphasize the "shedding of Jewish blood" but show little concern about the "shedding of non-Jewish blood." The Gush Emunim influence on Israeli policies can be measured by the fact that the Israeli government's policy on this matter has clearly reflected the Gush Emunim position. The Israeli government under both Labor and Likud leadership has refused to free Palestinian prisoners "with Jewish blood on their hands" but has not hesitated to free prisoners "with non-Jewish blood on their hands."

Another practical consequence of such attitudes is Gush Emunim's impact upon the conduct of the Israeli government in all matters concerning the territories. Gush Emunim continues to encourage Israeli authorities to deal cruelly with Palestinians in the West Bank and the Gaza Strip. The refusals of Prime Ministers Rabin, Peres and Netanyahu to advocate the evacuation of even a single Jewish settlement is attributable primarily to the influence of Gush Emunim. Gush Emunim's influence upon all Israeli governments and political leaders of varying political persuasions has been significant.
