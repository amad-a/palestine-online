Corruption is Modus Operandi

of PA Financial Administration- Report

Occupied Jerusalem- PT

Rampant corruption and financial mismanagement have become the "norm" rather than the "exception" in the financial administration of the Palestinian Authority; this is the conclusion contained in a report prepared by the Palestinian Monitoring Board and published in leading Palestinian dailies as of 25 May.

The Board, a semi-official body that functions as a comptroller, pointed out in its unprecedented report that an estimated $326 million (roughly 40% of the total PA budget) were "dispersed, embezzled or spent in dubious ways."

According to the Board's head, Jarar al Kudwa, much of the sum in question has been accounted for or has disappeared altogether.

Speaking during a radio interview on 24 May, al Kudwa said that about $50 million were lost to "certain elements" affiliated with the PA.

Moreover, a high-ranking official in the PA Ministry of Finance, who agreed to speak on condition of anonymity, said that most of the financial irregularities, e.g. embezzlement, were perpetrated by Ministers, Director-Generals, as well as third-degree and fourth-degree officials.

The same official intimated that persons holding key portfolios in the PA administration purchased luxurious cars, built fancy villas and were leading an obscenely-rich lifestyle thanks to money embezzled from the public.

The unprecedented disclosures were not surprising for the majority of Palestinians in the occupied territories. News of widespread corruption within various PA ministries and institutions became common knowledge within the autonomous enclaves following the 23 March publication in the Guardian of David Hirst's investigative report entitled "Shameless in Gaza".

Nevertheless, the report, however low in its profile and euphemistic in its wording, drew sharp reactions from ordinary Palestinians. A cab driver from Dura, 10 miles south-west of Hebron, protested that "we didn't know that these folks (the PA) were a gang of thieves." Another Palestinian who makes a living selling the traditional falafel sandwiches in downtown Hebron remarked, "Now we know where the donor countries' money goes."

For its part, the Palestinian leadership sought to distance itself from the scandal. Chairman Yasser Arafat said in a laconic statement that he was studying the report and promised to take measures to combat corruption.

According to Tayyeb Abdu Rahim, Secretary of Arafat's office, the Chairman will keenly follow up all the findings of the report and will see to it that "problematic matters" and other "excesses" be rectified.

Pundits here do not believe that the Palestinian Authority, or Arafat himself, would take any serious action against rampant corruption for the simple reason that any step as such would eventually reach the "red lines" bordering Arafat, his close net of officials, advisors and, most important of all, the profiteers.

Israel Using the Aids Virus as a Weapon of Mass Destruction

They have used live ammunition to kill and maim Palestinian children. They have buried Palestinian youths alive. The Israeli occupation authorities have even resorted to the closure of the West Bank and Gaza as a weapon to starve the entire Palestinian population into submission, yet the Palestinian intifadah is flourishing and is as defiant as ever in the face of their tormentor.

Palestinian children face Israeli guns and tanks alone. Their only weapons are stones, which have little impact on the Israeli bulldozers that demolish their homes and clear their lands for Jewish settlements. So-called moderate Arab regimes make promises, but no meaningful support ever reaches the truly needy. Gulf rulers, who in the last few years have spent over $80 billion on useless weapons that rust in the desert, give Palestinian children nickels and dimes in donations.

The Israeli government is now experimenting with a new type of weapon of mass destruction to deal with the unyielding Palestinian children. The Israelis are using the Aids virus. The method of delivery is not through sex, which is currently being used to spread the disease amongst Egyptians by Israeli tourists mobilized by the Israeli intelligence (Mossad). The Israelis are using the needle. The deadly virus is being transferred by injection.

The slow and painful murdering of Palestinian children is being carried out in Israeli hospitals. The injection of death into their muscles is being performed by Israeli nurses and doctors. The green light is given by members in the Israeli government at the highest level.

Palestinian children are alone. They have no direction in which to turn for support. No one with actual power or influence is defending their cause. None are raising their voices in international forums to protest this ugly and unforgivable crime. Hillary Clinton, the so-called champion of children's causes around the world, has not uttered one word on their behalf. Arab presidents, kings and emirs, including Arafat, prefer not to say anything about this newest development. Even the media in the region controlled by the Arab Gulf states is silent. Yet Palestinian kids are suffering! Palestinian children are dying!

The Israeli daily acknowledged that 305 AIDs cases among Palestinian children were recorded. The children were being treated in hospitals run by the Israeli occupation authorities. However, Palestinian sources believe that the unreported cases are actually in the thousands. The paper also said that attorneys who were approached to represent the children in their suit against the Israeli government declined.

The "peace" agreements Arafat signed with the Israeli government specifically state that crimes committed by the Israeli authorities against Palestinians cannot be brought to justice. All legal suits by Palestinians against the Israeli government were dropped. Palestinians have no right to turn to courts to solve their grievances against the Jewish state.

Dr. Mufid Shehab, who heads Cairo University and National Security in the Egyptian Parliament, said that the world is confronted with a human tragedy; the rights of victims to go to a court to obtain justice cannot be relinquished by any authority according to international law, the Oslo Accords or any other agreement signed on behalf of the Palestinians; they cannot be denied their legitimate and universally recognized rights; the Israeli authorities cannot be absolved from their legal and moral responsibilities.

Demolishing Arab Homes: An Established

Israeli Policy in the Occupied Territories

Occupied Jerusalem- PT

The Israeli "Civil Administration" in the West Bank has reportedly decided to destroy as many as 500 Arab homes located within area "C" which, according to the ill-fated Oslo Accords, is to remain under exclusive Israeli control at least until the final settlement. The Israeli occupation authorities claim the houses were built without "proper permits" and are located on land needed for bypass roads, or in the vicinity of army camps. However, it seems amply clear that the real motivation behind the planned "massacre of Palestinian homes" is first and foremost to curtail as much as possible natural, demographic Arab population growth particularly in the West Bank. If true, and all indications suggest that it is, the Israeli government would be reverting to the notorious policy adopted by the late Israeli Prime Minister Yitzhak Shamir, who sought to "narrow Palestinian horizons so much so that they would eventually be forced to leave their homeland for its 'rightful ownersí, the Jews."

In fact, far from upholding the "law", the established Israeli policy of systematically destroying Palestinians' homes is actually meant, regardless of any concocted excuses, to uproot the Palestinian, Arab and Muslim presence in Palestine, a goal that has always been the essence of Zionist policy toward the Palestinian people. Tactically, however, home demolition is primarily aimed at inflicting maximum emotional distress and pain on Arab families. The extremely callous practice is routinely resorted to when a member of a given Palestinian family is involved, factually or preemptively, in resistance acts against the Zionist occupation, or when, as previously mentioned, the house is built without a license.

Second only to death, home demolition is the worst calamity that could befall a Palestinian family. The experience is particularly overwhelming because of the symbolic status that a Palestinian attaches to his or her home. When a Palestinian wants to express extreme condemnation to someone in Arabic, he says "Yikhrib Beitak", meaning "May your home be destroyed."

Clearly, the Israeli occupation authorities have understood this "weak point" in Palestinian social psychology and spared no effort in utilizing it to its fullest potential. Hence, the policy of destroying residential homes of Arab families was adopted immediately following the end of the 1967 war. Since then, thousands of Arab homes have been demolished, leaving indelible scars in the hearts and minds of the innocent victims. In fact, the effects of such barbaric acts are incalculable, especially for children of the affected families who are most vulnerable and least immune to mental distress. They are likely to suffer permanent psychological and emotional disorders that will stay with them for the rest of their lives. Phobias, disorientation, depression and various forms of psychosis and neurosis are some of the mental and emotional disorders afflicting them. (It is not difficult to imagine what goes through a child's mind as he sees his home uprooted and destroyed by a bulldozer driven by soldiers.)

Unmoved by the emotional and physical suffering inflicted to the respective families, the Israeli army actually introduced stringent restrictions on the rebuilding of destroyed homes. The utter savagery and insensitivity of this policy became even more evident as the granting of building permits was made ever more difficult for Palestinians, particularly for those living outside the boundaries of area "A," the few towns nominally controlled by Yasser Arafat's Palestinian Authority, which doesn't exceed 2.5% of the total area of the West Bank.

Faced with the all too real prospects of becoming homeless, many Palestinians, especially young couples who have nowhere to live, often venture to build a home without obtaining the precious building permit beforehand. However, in many cases, Israeli army bulldozers move swiftly to destroy the newly-constructed house and with it a family's dream of living in a home of its own. Indeed, many people risk building without a license only because it is extremely difficult to obtain one, owing to the Israeli policy of preventing demographic Palestinian expansion. Even for those who can get a building license, it usually takes years to actually get one for the same reason.

The manner in which home demolition is carried out is significant in itself. The operation often begins by declaring the town or village where the doomed house is located a closed military zone. Then, after soldiers take position around the house, an officer would approach the head of the family to inform him that he has a two-hour grace period to get all belongings and furniture out. Distressed and distraught, family members would then hasten to get some of their valuables out. Older children would comfort younger children, as they all begin to sense an impending disaster of some kind about to be inflicted upon them. A crying wife would beg an apathetic army officer to spare the home they so laboriously built, brick by brick, and spent all their life savings on it, but to no avail because the officer informs them that they are "only carrying out orders." And as the deadline approaches, and all hopes of saving the home prove unrealistic, a child, returning from school and not knowing what is going on, would make a last dash into the home before the deadline in order to get his favorite story book. Then another child would make a last minute venture to get a toy he couldn't bear to see crushed. Then the deadline would arrive and the commanding officer would order the family to move away. Soon afterwards, explosives would be attached to the house columns and when the go-ahead is given, the explosives are detonated, the home becomes rubble, and "Mission Brutality" is executed.

The ruthless savagery of the home-demolition policy underscores the incompatibility of the Zionist occupation and justice. It also vindicates the fact, which has yet to have access to Westerners' minds, that "Zionist Jews are like all underdogs; when they get on the top, they become as merciless and barbaric to their victims as their former oppressors were to them when they were underneath." These are not the words of an overly-irate Palestinian indulging in "corrupt analogies" or of an anti-Semite, incapable of overcoming his "pathological hatred for everything Jewish". These are the words of Harry Truman, who historians say was instrumental in establishing the malignant entity called Israel in Palestine in 1948. Or was he anti-Semitic?

Palestinians: Land Dealers Deserve Death

By: Khalid Amayreh

Hebron (West Bank)- The recent killing in the West Bank of two land dealers believed to be involved in illicit land sales to Jewish settlers has once again brought to the fore the sharp and bitter confrontation between Jews and Palestinians over landóthe essence of the Arab-Israeli conflict. In one respect, the killing (as seen by Palestinians) or murder (as branded by Israeli circles) has refocused attention on frenzied Israeli efforts to acquire, by hook or by crook, as much Palestinian-owned land in the West Bank as possible before a political arrangement is reached. The matter, needless to say, acquires added emphasis now as the current Likud-led government is embarking on a frantic settlement-building campaign in and around East Jerusalem as well as throughout the West Bank.

The Palestinians, truly and rightly, view the closing-in of Jewish settlements on their villages and hamlets as a mortal threat to their future. Hence the liquidation of Farid al Bashiti and Harbi Abu Sara in Ramallah in mid-May. Both men had been widely suspected of plotting and facilitating the "sale" of Arab land in the central region of the West Bank to Gush Emunim settlers in return for huge sums of money they reportedly received as "commission" for acting as "middlemen". Bashiti was bludgeoned to death near the Ramallah police station with his hands tied and his mouth taped, while Abu Sara was shot four times in the head. It is not known for sure who killed the two land dealers, but a finger of accusation is already pointed toward the Palestinian Authority. Indeed, Israeli police arrested two persons (a man and a woman) affiliated with the Preventive Security on suspicion that they killed Bashiti, who held Israeli citizenship.

For its part, the PA expressed no compunction over the killing. Indeed, the Palestinian Ministry of Justice had earlier decided to reactivate an old but extant Jordanian law imposing the death penalty on Palestinians involved in "selling land to the enemy". The step was received with profound satisfaction throughout the occupied territories and was particularly commended by Hamas, which reportedly volunteered to provide the PA with detailed lists of "land smugglers deserving retribution for their treacheryî. At al Aqsa Mosque, the Mufti of Jerusalem, Sheikh Ikrema Sabri, lashed out at "traitors, motivated by greed, who seek to kill our future in return for a sum of money." He spelled out a fatwa or "religious ruling" ascribing as apostasy to land dealers who knowingly take part in "Israeli genocidal designs against our peopleî. On 25 May, the Sheikh of Al Azhar in Egypt issued a similar fatwa on "land sales to the Jews within the Palestinian context", saying that "land dealers in this particular context are treacherous to God, His messengers and the community of believers" and therefore "deserve the death penalty."

According to Islamic law, however, it is not forbidden to sell land to "the people of the book" (e.g. Jews) under normal circumstances. However, when and if such a sale harms the collective interest of Muslims (as in Palestine), it becomes absolutely unlawful.

The invoking of the death penalty against land dealers prompted the Israeli-Zionist propaganda machine to launch a vitriolic campaign against the weak and vulnerable Palestinian Authority. Thus, comparisons were made between the measures against land dealers and the Nazi laws against Jews. Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu went as far as drawing a parallel with the Holocaust. He described the said measures as "anti-Jewish laws that remind us of events from the past that we thought had passed over." Foreign Minister David Levy also joined ranks in attacking "the rising ugly head of Palestinian racism against Jews." Even, the avowedly racist Gush Emunim leaders lambasted Palestinian measures as "inimical to the Jews" and "proving that 'they' and 'we' cannot live together."

In fact, Israeli audacity went as far as complaining to the United Nations Commission on Human Rights about Palestinian racism and anti-semitism!

The Palestinians, for their part, scoffed at such remarks, describing Israeli and American reactions as "hypocritical and ludicrous", and "aimed first and foremost at blackmailing us into budging to the Israeli arrogance of power."

"How would you like us to deal with those traitors; you yourself killed Rudolf Kasztner when you suspected that he had collaborated with the Nazis," protested Freih Abu Maddin, who holds the justice portfolio in the PA, during a radio interview with the Voice of Israel on 24 May.

"It is totally shameless that a state that practices institutionalized racism against its Arab citizens and enacted and continues to enact every conceivable law solely to dispossess Palestinians of their land has the audacity to speak about Palestinian racism against Jews," said Yasser Arafat's spokesman Ahmed Teibi during a radio interview on 18 May.

Teibi pointed out that there are established laws in Israel against Arab property, including the "Absentee Law," which treats Israeli Arabs as "absentees" and therefore enables the government to confiscate their land.

Teibi's remarks were vindicated by Dan Yakir, an Israeli authority on Land Law at the Association of Civil Rights in Israel, who said in an interview with the Jerusalem Post on 25 May that "there is a policy which limits non-Jewish purchase of land." In fact, Yakir's brief note, which utterly understates the facts, conceals a long-standing policy of systematic discrimination against Arabs in all matters pertaining to land. For example, there is an absolute ban on selling Hakeren Hakayemet (National Fund) land to non-Jews, and Arabs are prohibited to purchase land in areas such as Galilee, where Israel is concerned about increasing the Arab population.

Palestinians generally recognize that the imposition of the death penalty on land dealers will produce negative public relations, especially abroad, and particularly in the United States, where Zionist propaganda is very strong. However, they argue that the severity of the crime committed by greedy land dealers makes death justifiable and a just retribution for a monstrous crime of unparalleled proportion. The issue, Palestinians would argue, is not over the identity of the buyer but the consequences of the sale.

Palestinian prisoner dying

Gaza- A Palestinian human rights group held the Israeli government responsible for the deterioration of the health of a Palestinian prisoner in Bearsheba prison. Al Dameer (the Conscience) human rights organization said that Uwaida Sulieman, 34, who is serving a life imprisonment on the charge of belonging to a Fateh military cell, suffers from several chronic diseases, including epilepsy, and may die as his health deteriorates. A statement by the organization pointed out that Uwaida has not been able to see his lawyer or family for two years because of the ban imposed by the Israeli authorities on visits to the detainees. "Although his mother obtained permission to visit him, she could not make the visit because the Israeli authorities would not reveal his location," the statement added. The Palestinian organization accused Israel of delaying the release of Palestinian detainees as stipulated in the PLO-Israel peace agreement.

Israeli plan to build 500 housing units in Nablus and Bethlehem

Occupied Jerusalem- The Israeli Ministry of Construction and Housing announced its intention to build more than 500 new housing units for settlers in two Jewish settlements in the West Bank. Sources from the Ministry said that 278 houses will be built in Ariel, the largest settlement near Nablus, north of the West Bank and 222 houses in Alon Shebut, south-west of Bethlehem and south of the West Bank. Sources from the Israeli Peace Now group said that the new housing units in Ariel would increase its population to 16,000 settlers while the population of Alon Shebut would increase to 3,600. Peace Now criticized the Israeli government for funding settlements in Occupied Palestinian Territories while demolishing Palestinian homes.

Israeli soldiers attack Palestinian detainees

Gaza- On 15 May, Israeli prison guards used tear-gas to suppress demonstrations staged by about 300 Palestinian detainees protesting against the lengthening of their imprisonment period, according to a human rights group.

The incident was reported in Megiddo Prison north of Tel Aviv, where some 280 Palestinians are held without trial, said Khalida Jarrar, executive director of Al Dameer, a Palestinian group monitoring rights of Palestinian prisoners.

Jarrar pointed out that prison guards ordered the detainees to evacuate the prison's yard and go back to their tents in the evening, then attacked the tents with large amounts of tear-gas. The raid went on for several hours leaving 90 per cent of the detainees overcome by the gas. She added that at least three of them had to be taken to a hospital outside the prison.

Jarrar said that the prisoners, earlier in the morning of that day, had held a protest against the renewing of administrative detention for 26 detainees who had been up for release. The prisoners refused to cooperate during the morning count and then marched into the prison bearing a coffin, she said. "The protest was over long before the tear-gas started," she added.

Megiddo Prison witnessed a similar riot last March after protests were staged against administrative detention policies and poor prison conditions.

Some of the detainees have been in Megiddo for over a year without trial. The prison also holds over 600 other Palestinians who have been charged or tried.

Meanwhile, Palestinian sources said that Israeli occupation soldiers attacked 17 Palestinian prisoners in the desert Nafha Prison south of Occupied Palestine. They added that the detainees were beaten with canes by about 50 soldiers of a special force of the Prison Guards. Some detainees were badly injured, the sources said.

The Palestinian Human Rights Centre condemned the Israeli atrocities against Palestinian detainees saying that the attack on Palestinian prisoners in Nafha Prison was a violation of International conventions pertaining to human rights protection. The Centre also called on the guardians of the peace agreements between the PLO and Israel to oblige the latter to honour its commitments and release the Palestinian detainees as stipulated in these agreements.

Nafha Prison was opened by the Israeli authorities in March 1996. About 360 prisoners were transferred to the new prison from Majdal Prison north of Gaza.

There are some 4,000 Palestinian detainees in Israeli prisons who were supposed to be released last year according to the Oslo Accords. Israel refuses to release them although it had promised to do so as part of the Accords.
