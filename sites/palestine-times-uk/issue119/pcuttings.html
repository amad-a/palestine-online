Press Cuttings

  • Ha'aretz, 27 April 2001

    Following two days of fiery rhetoric at a pro-Intifada conference Iran hosted this week, the final statement called for the creation of an international court to try Israeli for "war crimes."

    The document, signed by more than 30 states, also condemned the United States for its political, military and economic support of Israel and called for an Arab and Muslim boycott of American products .The statement, hammered out after intense haggling between Iranian officials and visiting delegates, called on Islamic nations to "break all ties, especially political and economic relations" with Israel.

    It denounced "organized crimes by Israel against the Palestinian people, the attacks against leaders of Palestinian movements, the destruction or the seizure of houses and land, as well as the development of Jewish sites."

  • The Daily Telegraph, 26 April 2001

    THE European Union is preparing to strip Israel of privileged trade access as a punishment for the use of "excessive force" against the Palestinians.

    EU diplomats confirmed yesterday that it was now almost certain that the 15 EU foreign ministers would agree to punitive action at a key meeting on May 21, clamping down on Israel's long-standing abuse of Europe's "Rules of Origin" regime.

    This means Israel will no longer be able to get away with exporting duty-free goods made at Jewish settlements in the West Bank and Gaza as if they were Israeli products.

  • The Chicago Tribune, 25 April 2001

    As Israel honored its war dead with solemn words and graveside remembrances, the radical Islamic group Hamas held a memorial Tuesday for its latest suicide bomber and warned that more attacks would soon follow.

    ..Meanwhile, militant Islamic groups said they are poised to carry out more bombing attacks during Israel's back-to-back observances of memorial day and independence day.

    Israeli police commanders said they would mobilize large numbers of officers in a bid to prevent attacks on memorial day..

  • The Los Angeles Times, 19 April 2001

    Politicians and soldiers in Israel turned on each other Wednesday in a war of words over who was to blame for the army's hasty withdrawal from newly reoccupied Palestinian land--an about-face that many here said made the country appear confused and too easily swayed by U.S. opinion.

        Israeli troops on Tuesday seized a sliver of the Gaza Strip ceded to the Palestinians seven years ago. But scant hours after military commanders determined to halt Palestinian mortar fire announced that they would remain in the area indefinitely, they withdrew after a stern rebuke from Washington.

        Embarrassed Israeli officials Wednesday insisted that U.S. pressure had nothing to do with the decision to abandon the Gaza area, saying they had meant all along to withdraw Tuesday night. But that seemed to be news to the field commanders, not to mention Palestinian residents of the Gazan town of Beit Hanoun who watched the Israeli tanks and bulldozers digging in well past dusk Tuesday.

  • The Independent, 19 April 2001

    America's condemnation of the Israeli action in Gaza reflects a growing tension in the relationship, although not a breakdown or even a fundamental shift in US policy.

    US-Israeli relations have been under strain for a while from the American perspective - and not only because of the election of Ariel Sharon and the tougher Israeli stance towards the Palestinians that his arrival presaged. Among the irritants have been Israeli sales of military technology without Washington's authorisation or even knowledge. Depending on the class of weapons, neither is necessarily required. But the US clearly feels that as Israel's major ally and arms supplier, it should be kept informed.

    An illustration was supplied just this week. The Pentagon confirmed that a Chinese jet fighter shown in a US Defense Department video was armed with an Israeli-made Python air-to-air missile. The video was used by the Pentagon to show how close Chinese planes had come to US reconnaissance aircraft in the months before the collision on 1 April in which a Chinese pilot died.
