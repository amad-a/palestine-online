Israel demolishes more Arab Homes in Jerusalem

 

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (27 August)

Israeli occupation bulldozers destroyed three additional Palestinian homes at the Ras al Amud neighborhood in East Jerusalem.

Palestinian sources in Jerusalem said two Israeli bulldozers destroyed the three houses shortly before noon Wednesday, rendering three families homeless.

A spokesman for the Israeli mayor of Jerusalem, Ehud Olmert, justified the brutal act on the ground that the three premises were built without license.

However, Palestinians point out that licensing is only a pretext, arguing that demolition of Arab houses in East Jerusalem, as it is in the West Bank, is an established Israeli policy aimed at checking Arab demographic growth.

"House demolition is consistent with the undeclared Israeli policy of preventing the Arab population in the city from exceeding the 27% -line," said Faisal Husseini, member of the PLO executive committee.

Husseini pointed out that Israeli municipal authorities in East Jerusalem refuse to issue building licenses for Palestinians inside the city for racist reasons pertaining to the ethnic cleansing policy.

"They don't want to see more Palestinians here," said Husseini

On Tuesday, a prominent Israeli journalist and writer, Uri Avneri, disclosed that the successive Israeli governments adopted an undeclared policy against Arab demographic expansion and growth in East Jerusalem soon after the Israeli occupation in June 1967.

Writing in the Hebrew daily Ma'ariv, Avneiri pointed out that Labour and Likud governments alike committed themselves to maintain the proportion of non-Jews (the Arabs) in Jerusalem at the 27 % point, the same percentage in 1967.

Faced with a crushing housing shortage stemming from natural growth in their population and the scarcity of building licenses, many East Jerusalemite Palestinians are forced to seek shelter in surrounding areas such as Bethlehem and Ram, thus risking withdrawal of their residency rights by the Israeli authorities.

"They are launching a demographic genocide against us in the full view of the world...they don't allow us to build shelters for our children, and when we go elsewhere to seek one, they tell us "you no longer have the right to live in Jerusalem," said Abdul Hamid Nasser Addin, a Jerusalemite Arab who lost his residency right in Jerusalem because he moved to a new house in the Ram neighborhood.

In other development, Israeli soldiers prevented a group of Christian clergymen from reaching their church in Jerusalem.

The clergymen, along with civic leaders and Legislative Council members from Bethlehem, walked to the Israeli army roadblock near the settlement of Gilo north of Bethlehem, hoping to be allowed to proceed to Jerusalem.

However, the soldiers turned them back, threatening to shoot them.

Bethlehem has been under a tight siege for a month, sparking off violent protests by townspeople.

On Tuesday, 26 August, as many as 40 school girls fainted after their school was bombarded with tear gas shells by Israeli soldiers.

The Israeli government has so far refused to lift the siege on Bethlehem allegedly for security reasons.

On Wednesday, the Israeli War minister, Yitzhak Mordachai, said the siege on the Palestinian town would continue for the foreseeable future.

The Israeli government has been demanding that the Palestinian Authority destroy the social, economic and educational infrastructure of Hamas, an allusion to hospitals, schools, universities and charitable institutions run by Islamists.

So far, the Palestinian Authority has refused to the Zionist dictates.

Jewish murderer freed for "psychological reasons"

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (25 August)

An Israeli military court decided on Sunday 24 August to set a Jewish murderer free "because he is unfit to stand trial."

According to Israeli sources, the military court in Jaffa ruled that Noam Freidman, 19, was not responsible for his crimes against Palestinians since he suffered psychiatric disorders rendering him unable to realize what he was doing.

Freidman, an officer in the Israeli army and member of the racist Gush Emunim movement, opened fire from his automatic rifle at Arab civilians in Hebron on 1 January this year, injuring six Palestinians, one seriously.

Freidman reportedly confessed to having planned to "slaughter as many Arabs as possible in order to clean this land of non-Jews."

The murderer was overpowered by an Israeli soldier before he could carry out his evil design.

Following the incident, Israeli army sources claimed that Freidman was mentally unbalanced and trigger-happy.

A concocted psychological evaluation by army psychiatrist was hastily produced, effectively absolving the murderer of responsibility for his crime.

Palestinian sources rightly argue that Israeli authorities usually seek to justify the murder of innocent Palestinians by serving Israeli soldiers and settlers on the ground that the perpetrators suffer from psychological disorders.

Very often, the "demented perpetrators" find their way back to Israeli army units in the West Bank, preparing for new crimes against Palestinians.

Israeli Army 'Major' Indicted for Murdering English Tourist

 

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (23 August)

After initially seeking to pin the blame on Palestinians for the murder of an English tourist in Elat last week, the Israeli authorities have now admitted that Jeffrey Hunter was shot dead by a "demented" Israeli holding the rank of Major in the Israeli Army.

On Friday 22 August, an Israeli police spokesman said Daniel Okev, 45, confessed to having killed Hunter and moderately wounding his girl friend Charlotte Geib.

The spokesperson said Okev, a Major in the Israeli Army, didn't cite any motive for the crime, adding that "I just did it on the spur of the moment."

"I don't know why I did it. I just pulled my gun and shot them...may be I need to see a psychiatrist," the murderer was quoted as saying as he was being remanded at a Be'ir al Sab'ba court Friday.

Okev reportedly agreed to give the British couple a ride from the southern Israeli town of Elat to Beir Sheba, but as the two Britons stopped over for five minutes to smoke and have refreshment, the driver, Okev, pulled his pistol and fired three bullets into Hunter's head, killing him instantly.

Okev, also fired at Geib, who was injured, before fleeing back to Elat.

Earlier, all Israeli police officials claimed that "an Arab driver from northern Israel was the chief suspect" and that "the murder had nationalistic motivation."

Even Israeli Minister of Tourism, Moshe Katsav, made similar remarks while visiting Geib who was recuperating from her wounds at an Israeli hospital.

Katsav was quoted as telling Gieb that "Arab terrorism was not only an Israeli problem and that the whole world ought to close ranks against it."

Israeli psychiatrists and psychologists often contend that the Israeli army brutal repression of Palestinians during the Intifada left deep psychological scars on many soldiers and field officers.

"Many of those soldiers have developed a trigger-happy personality that is often coupled with a schizophrenic character whereby they are given to easy shooting as they used to do in the streets of the West Bank and Gaza," said an Israeli psychiatrist from the small town of Omer near Beir al Sab'a.

Interestingly, the Israeli Defense Ministry repeatedly deployed army psychiatrists and psychologists to aide soldiers and officers who developed psychosis as a result of killing Arab children.

Palestinians Hail "National Unity Talks"

 

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (20 August)

Palestinians from various political orientations have hailed the "National Unity Conference," held in Gaza on Wednesday, describing it as "our response to Israeli insolence and morbid efforts to fan the flames of war amongst our people."

"National unity should be a fixed Palestinian strategy in the face of (Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin) Netanyahu''s sinister efforts to strangle our people and narrow our horizons," said Muhammed Khalid, a prominent Hamas leader in the Jenin area.

"It is clear that Netanyahu wants to see Palestinians shed their own blood in order to appease his own cannibalistic instincts. We must not allow him to realize his diabolic dream," added Khalid.

Jamal Shubaki, a Legislative Council member and refugee from pre-1967 Palestine, concurred, saying "that under any circumstances should the Palestinian Authority give deference to Israeli demands to repress a significant sector of our citizens."

"Our strategy should be to cooperate with , rather than fight, the Islamist camp. This policy shouldn't be a seasonal reaction to Israeli insolence and oppression, but a principled strategy independent of all other considerations," said Shubaki in a radio interview on Wednesday.

In Gaza, the National Unity Conference was opened at the Rashad al Shawwa Centre, under the chairmanship of Palestine Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat.

Hamas representative, Abdul Aziz al Rantisi, called on Arafat "to abandon the Oslo Agreement because it utterly failed to restore our rights and dealt a painful blow to national unity."

"This agreement has forced us into a dark tunnel at the end of which there is no glimmer of hope of flicker of light," said Rantisi before a speechless Yasser Arafat.

Furthermore, Rantisi called on the PA to see to it that human rights are respected and the authority of law and order be made to prevail.

For his part, Sheikh Nafez Azzam of the Islamic Jihad organization, reiterated his movement's implacable rejection of the Oslo Accords.

"This agreement has given Israel all the assets and us all the liabilities," said Azzam.

The Islamist leader also called on the PA to boycott regional economic conference scheduled to be held in Qatar in November.

Am labeled the conference "the economic normalization conference," exhorting the PA not to play the role of "a normalization middle-man between Israeli and the Arab world."

Low Incentives

while Arafat is not expected to heed Hamas and Islamic Jihad calls for "terminating Oslo," he does seem to be trying to reach a certain common ground with the Islamist camp.

Indeed, Arafat's costly refusal to succumb completely to Israeli dictates is likely to be vindicated and could pay off in terms of enhancing his position in the occupied territories.

Indeed, the fact that Hamas decided, somewhat hastily and without any reservations, to participate in the Gaza talks suggests that the movement see a certain gratitude in Arafat's reluctance to clamp down on its activists and infrastructure as incessantly demanded by the Israelis and the Americans.

But Arafat is not being altruistic toward Hamas. The chairman seems to realize that Netanyahu is after him as much as he is after Hamas, and that he is interested in weakening him by making him fight Hamas on behalf of Israel in return for nothing.

"In the past Arafat fought Hamas with a vigor because he thought the peace process would pay off eventually. Now, the chairman nearly lost this glimmer of hope, hence he has no incentives to fight Hamas," said an "insider" close to the chairman.

Israeli policemen indicted for assaulting Palestinians

Occupied Jerusalem- (19 August)

The Israeli justice ministry charged on Monday three Israeli para-military border policemen with kidnapping and assaulting two Palestinians earlier this month.

An indictment released by the ministry said the policemen took the two men to an isolated wood where they continually beat them, hitting them on the head, chest and limbs.

They were rounding up Palestinians who had entered Israel without permits in the face of a closure imposed on the West Bank after the July 30 suicide bombing in West Jerusalem.

The indictment said the Israeli policemen tied the legs of one of the men with a rope attached to their jeep and dragged him several meters.

The policemen continued to brutalise the other Arab "even after he began to beg to show him mercy because he suffered from heart troubles," the charge sheet said.

The Israeli television broadcast last week an interview with the two Palestinians, Ahmad Mousa and Mahmoud Ghneim who lay side by side in hospital.

During the interview, Mr. Mousa, breathing heavily through a hospital mask, said "each one of them carried a club and administered beatings. One of them asked me if my chest was all right and hit me in the chest. There were three who hit me."

"One of them pointed an M-16 rifle at my head- he pointed the rifle next to my ear and told me, "now you'll get out of here. If I see you again, I will kill you. You must die."

Israeli Soldiers Tread on the Quran in Dura

 

Occupied Jerusalem -PT(13 August)

Israeli soldiers reportedly raided several mosques in the Hebron area on Monday night , desecrating the holy places and, in a clear provocation, treading on copies of the Holy Quran.

Palestinian sources in Dura, 10 miles south west of Hebron, said scores of Israeli soldiers raided the Grand Mosque in Dura at 2:30 a.m. Monday allegedly to "look for inciteful materials"

The mosque muezzin, Subhi al Masalemh, said soldiers smashed glass inside the mosque.

One soldier, he said, trod on copies of the Holy Quran in his full view probably to test his reaction.

Al Masalmeh said the soldiers desisted from the sacrilegious act only when al Masalmeh complained to his superior.

Meanwhile, Israeli troops raided Masjid al Mujahed three blocks away, smashing furniture and glass.

Afterwards, the soldiers raided the Al Anwar Athletic and Cultural Club to carry out "security searches."

Ahmed Qazzaz, who supervise cultural activities at the club, said "the soldiers turned every thing upside down, but found nothing that would incriminate us."

"They came looking for inciteful material and perhaps more than that, but found bags and pencils for school children," Qazzaz said, referring to a school paraphernalia sale at the club for low-income families in the area.

According to Palestinian sources, Israeli occupation troops have raided as many as fifty Mosques throughout the West Bank since the 30 July double suicide bombing in West Jerusalem.

At least twenty mosques were closed down for varying periods of time and as many as fifty Imams were rounded up as an act of reprisal against the Islamic movement which Israel blames for responsibility for the bombing.

Israeli sources said that the number of Palestinians rounded up since the bombing reached 300 half of were reportedly sent to detention and concentration camps inside Israel.

Israel treats Palestinian detainees as hostages and makes their release conditional on receiving political concessions from Yasser Arafat's political authority.

So far, Israel and the PA have not established the identity of the perpetrators of the bombing.

Arafat Resists Israeli Dictates

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (12 August)

Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat is reportedly resisting Israeli (and to a lesser degree) American dictates to carry out massive arrests against Palestinian citizens associated with the Islamic Movement, official Palestinian sources said on Tuesday.

The sources said Arafat told the American peace envoy Dennis Ross whom he met three times in the last two days that "it is difficult for me to start arresting people just because their names happen to be on an Israeli list."

Acting at Israel's beck and call, Ross repeatedly urged Arafat to "fight terrorism and hit hard Hamas' social and educational infrastructure."

However, Chairman Arafat, according to the sources, retorted, saying that "indiscriminate arrests proved counter-productive in the past, and we are not going to do it this time just to appease the whims of Mr. Netanyahu."

On Monday night, Ross met with Arafat in Ramallah for more than two hours during which he briefed him on his talks with Israeli leaders.

Following the meeting, Ross told journalists that he had achieved some progress "mainly in the form of resuming security coordination" between Israel and the Palestinian Authority.

Three "security meetings" did indeed take place since Ross's arrival in Palestine on 9 August, during which Israeli and Palestinian security and intelligence officials exchanged information on "terrorist elements," a reference to Islamic activists.

PA officials only spoke laconically about the meetings in which officials from the CIA took part.

Nabil Sha'ath, who assumes the planing portfolio in the PA, recognized that it was "disquieting that the security coordination is resumed without addressing any of the political issues that brought about the current crisis."

Sha'ath apparently sought to give Ross the benefit of the doubt, saying that his present mission was but a preparation for the upcoming visit to the region by U.S. Secretary of State Madline Albright.

Other Palestinian officials, however, dismissed Ross's mission as a failure.

Palestinian negotiator Hasan Asfour said the visit didn't achieve anything since it didn't deal with the causes of the current crisis.

Meanwhile, Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu vowed not to enter into the final status talks "until Arafat fights terrorism with soul and heart."

The Israeli premier also said that security coordination with the Palestinians was meaningless without the PA arresting Hamas supporters.

Ross is due to return to the United States on Wednesday morning.

Jews Attack Jews at Wailing Wall

Occupied Jerusalem -PT(12 August)

Thousands of Haredi ultra orthodox Jews shouted obscenities and threw stones and garbage at an estimated 200 conservative Jews who had come to pray at al Burak Wall Monday night, eventually succeeding in evicting them from the place.

Jews from both sects had gathered for "a prayer of lamentation," marking the destruction of the Jewish Temple by the Romans around 70 A.D.

According to Israeli sources, the ultra-orthodox leadership had warned the police that they would take the law into their own hands if "the heretics are not evicted immediately."

Earlier, the police reportedly had given the conservatives a permission to pray at the site, but as the ultra-orthodox threatened to turn violent, the police reneged on its promise to the conservatives and evicted them.

The commotion turned nasty when the police tried to force the Conservative crowd out of the wailing wall plaza.

Many people reportedly were thrown to the ground and trampled on, as a group of Haredi youths shouted at the conservatives "get out, get out, you are desecrating the site."

The Israeli state-run radio "call Yisrael" reported that the ultra-orthodox religious leadership was furious because the conservatives held prayer sessions without men and women being segregated.

However, the real reason seems to go much deeper, given the Orthodox camp's enduring refusal to attach any Jewish religious legitimacy to Judaism's two other main sects, the Conservative and Reform movements.

The Israeli English daily, the Jerusalem Post, reported an American Jew from Kansas City in the United States as saying that "it is repulsive that it should come down to attacks, Jew to Jew, especially tonight."

Israelis, save the Haredim, are generally embarrassed by the recurrent friction between the predominant Orthodox leadership and the followers of the other two sects which master a numerical majority especially among American Jewry.

Last month, an American Rabbi from the Reform movement told this journalist that "Israeli apartheid is not only directed against the Palestinians and non-Jews but also against Jews affiliated with Conservative and Reform sects."

Faced with the recurrent dilemma of having to reconcile the seemingly impossible task of appeasing the powerful Orthodox leadership and at the same time not alienating the bulk of American Jewry, the Israeli government repeatedly sought to evade the issue of "who is a Jew" by sticking to the religious status quo in Israel whereby the Orthodox maintains their hegemony over the non-Orthodox.

In June, Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu decided to postpone until November making a decision on whether the Israeli government should or shouldn't recognize the Jewishness of Jews converted at the hands of non-Orthodox rabbis.

The issue seems to be very important to many in Israel and abroad that some within the secular Israeli camp described it as "a time bomb."

Israel Holds More Palestinian Hostages

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (7 August)

Like gangsters, terrorists and common criminals do, the Israeli occupation authorities have rounded up more than 500 Palestinians in an apparent retaliation for the 30 July double suicidal bombing in West Jerusalem.

Palestinian sources pointed out that since the bombing, Israeli soldiers, border police and Special undercover units have been carrying out nightly raids on Mosques and homes throughout the West Bank, often desecrating holy books, including the Quran, and arresting people with Islamist orientation.

Virtually all the detainees are effectively treated as "hostages" as their detention and continued incarceration for long periods of time are done completely outside the confines of the law.

Human rights organizations, including B'tselem and al Haq, pointed out that the hostages are incarcerated in large detention camps for as long as it expedient for the Israeli government's policies and interests.

"They (the Israelis) detain those people for many months, even years, without telling them why they are being detained...Their detention may well be compared with the detention of Japanese in the United States during WWII," said Mazen Dandis, a B'tselem activist working in Hebron.

Dandis added that the rounding up of people was the "occupier's tool" of "punishing and tormenting the occupied."

"Virtually all the detainees are innocent since the Israelis themselves don't charge them with any wrong-doing nor, of course, try them before court."

According to B'tselem, as many as 700 Palestinians are held as "hostages" (many in solitary confinement) in three detention camps in Israel.

Human rights organizations, local and foreign, repeatedly wrote to the Israeli Defence Minister to "either try those people or set them free," however, to no avail.

Last month, an Israeli Labour Party Knesset member, Dalya Itsek, revealed that Israeli prison authorities "routinely carry out scientific and medical experiments on Palestinian detainees without their knowledge."

Itsek also disclosed that "several Israeli drug-manufacturing firms are involved in testing the effectiveness and safety of their products on humans."

Humiliation

The manner in which the detention of Palestinians is interesting.

Palestinians point out that Israeli soldiers often raid Palestinian homes at the quiet hours before dawn, pounding doors, and terrorizing the entire family.

Furthermore, to further terrorize a given family, the heavily armed soldiers would often beat up the family's adult male members, particularly the father, in the full view of his wife and children.

Needless to say, observers have always believed that Israeli mistreatment of Palestinians was instrumental in the suicidal bomber phenomenon among Palestinians.

TOTAL CLOSURE

Occupied Jerusalem- PT ( 5 August)

In retaliation for the 30 July suicide bombing in West Jerusalem, the Israeli government imposed a "total closure" on the West Bank and the Gaza Strip, cutting off Arab towns and villages individually from each other and collectively from the outside world. Consequently, many vital supplies and services, including those usually taken for granted, were stopped due to IDF blockades of Palestinian population centers. In several instances, patients died and women had to give birth at military roadblocks, having been denied access to nearby hospitals. Moreover, the collective "town-arrest" seems to have had inflicted incalculable damage to the originally fragile Palestinian economy.

Palestinian Authority sources described the draconian Israeli measures as "unprecedented in harshness" and "aimed at subjugating and humiliating our people." PA Chairman Yasser Arafat termed the Israeli reprisals as a "criminal act of genocidal proportions" and amounting to a "declaration of war," a description repeatedly echoed by his aides and ministers.

Speaking during a press conference in Gaza on 4 August, the Palestinian leader accused Israel of "tightening the noose on the Palestinian Authority and the Palestinian people instead of fighting terrorism." Arafat's desperate words did carry an air of veracity. The Israeli measures, probably the harshest ever, virtually paralyzed the PA as it forced Palestinian officials "to hibernate, each in his immediate place of residence," as one official put it. For some officials, the "claustrophobia" was so acute, that media celebrities such as Nabil Sha'ath publicly complained on the air to a Voice of Israel reporter "to help me if you can because I'm stranded in Gaza due to the closure."

Hafez al Barghouthi, Editor-in-Chief of the pro-PA daily, al Hayatul Jadida, marveled at the effects of the Israeli measures in preventing terrorism." "I don't understand anymore, He sarcastically wrote in his daily column on 5 August, "how the dumping of a truck-load of earth at village entrances would prevent a would be terrorist from proceeding to his ultimate destination." "Nor how barring newspapers from reaching Gaza would contribute to fighting terror...unless one takes into account the plausible possibility that a terrorist could very well fold himself in paper form or be resurrected from one of the obituaries."

Ostensibly, the collective punishment is meant to induce the PA to crack down on Hamas on behalf of Israel, and also to "further narrow Palestinians' horizons" in the hope that they would come to terms with the Israeli "fait accompli." However, it is seemingly unlikely that the Israeli policy would achieve the desired goal on both levels. The PA, which has never been weaker among Palestinians, is not in a position to openly and unashamedly act at Benjamin Netanyahu's beck and call as this would quicken its demise in the eyes of the Palestinians and decisively vindicate the often-voiced charges that it is merely an Israeli puppet militia aimed at tormenting the Palestinians and ravaging their collective psychological immunity vis-¦-vis Israel.

Hence, a wholesale crackdown on Hamas and the Islamic Jihad under Israel's incessant dictates would conceivably strengthen the Islamists in Palestinian opinion. It could also corrode the already frail political constituencies on which Arafat has based his leadership and orchestrated his support for the Oslo Accords.

Indeed, this seems to explain why the PA has not yet embarked on "an all-out campaign of mass arrests" of Islamist elements as was done following the spate of suicide bomb attacks in Israel in the spring of 1996. But there are other important reasons militating against a decision to that effect. The PA has come to feel that Hamas is no longer the sole sought-after enemy by Israel and that Fatah, the PA itself and, indeed, the "collective Palestinian interest" would eventually be , more-or-less, ultimate targets for Israeli insolence.

This view was recently echoed by Abbas Zaki, a prominent Fatah leader and member of the Palestinian Legislative Council. Speaking in Hebron last week (before the suicidal bombing), Zaki said Hamas was no longer the only bad guys in the sight of Israel, adding that "every patriotic Palestinian acting on his patriotism is now viewed as terrorist by Israel."

Zaki's remarks were vindicated a day later when the """Israeli army issued a warrant to arrest Arafat's top police officer Brig. Gen. Ghazi al Jabali on terrorism-related charges. Needless to say, the arrest warrant against al Jabali, who had led the crackdown on Hamas and Islamic Jihad last year, served as an "eye-opener" for many PA officials and ordinary Palestinians, who had previously been told that "Hamas was the enemy).

Zaki's views, however, don't seem to reflect "Yaser Arafat's thinking and that of his deputy, Mahmud Abbas. Arafat reportedly expressed willingness to "resume full security and intelligence coordination" with Israel. For his part, Abbas, whose increasingly focal obsequiousness vis-¦-vis Israel has come to be viewed with growing repugnance and simmering discontent, said the West Jerusalem suicidal bombing necessitated the immediate resumption of the security coordination with Israel. However, it seems that Arafat's and Abbas's reconciliatory approach toward Israel won't do the Palestinians any good in the long run. The reason largely stems form the reasonably sound premise that "Palestinian terrorism is not the main factor behind Israeli intransigence, and that Israel accentuates this factor purely for political and propagandistic reason," a premise the Palestinian leadership has not really understood, much less acted on.

IS IT TOO LATE FOR PEACE?

Most ordinary Palestinians reacted to the latest suicidal bombing with stark ambivalence. However, this time it seems that their "indignation at Netanyahu" and "disenchantment with Arafat" markedly overweighed their innate reservations and objections to wanton killing. Indeed, a sizable majority of Palestinians seemed to squarely blame the Israeli government for "pushing Palestinians to the brink of despair."

"This man (Netanyahu) is offering us either of two choices, surrender or terrorism," said one PA minister from the Hebron area, adding that "many more people would ultimately become potential and actual suicidal bombers if the Israeli government continued its current policies in the West Bank."

The minister's pessimism will likely gain added significance in light of continued mutual violence. On 4 August, and Israeli settler murdered an elderly Palestinian shepherd outside the village of Yatta, 15 miles south of Hebron, apparently in retaliation for the West Jerusalem bombing. Interestingly, members of the bereaved family reportedly shouted "death to the Jews" and "Ayyash, Ayyash," a reference to Hamas master bomber who Israel says stood behind the wave of suicidal bomb attacks in Tel Aviv and Jerusalem last year.

More significantly, however, is the fact that an increasing number of Palestinians, Arabs and Muslims, have come to believe that it is already late to reach an equitable, lasting settlement between Israel and the Palestinians in light of the facts in Jerusalem and the West Bank. Unfortunately, this view, however gloomy it may be, is being vindicated.

Israel Reconsiders Decision to Jam PA Radio Station

 

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (3 August)

The Israeli government has apparently reconsidered an earlier decision to jam the "Voice of Palestine, " a Ramallah-based radio station representing the official views of the Palestinian Authority.

It is not clear what prompted the Israeli government to decide to keep the "Voice of Palestine" on air, but informed Israeli sources in West Jerusalem dismissed official Israeli claims that "technical difficulties" was the reason as "nonsense."

"It is relatively easy to jam the Ramalla radio station (the Voice of Palestine); Israel has been jamming for sometime another Palestinian radio station, the Damascus-based 'this is Jerusalem' run by Ahmed Jebril," said the sources.

The sources opined that the real reason had to do with intelligence assessment that jamming the Voice of Palestine would weaken PA chairman Yasser Arafat, which eventually would not serve Israeli interests.

On Friday, Knesset member and former deputy-head of Shabak,, Gedeon Ezra, reportedly advised the Israeli government not to go ahead with plans to jam the Palestinian radio station.

The Israeli decision was lauded by Palestinian officials.

Radwan Abu Ayyash, Head of the Palestinian Broadcasting Authority, described it as " a step in the right direction."

Abu Ayyash, however, sounded defiant, saying during a radio interview with the Voice of Palestine that the Palestinian Authority would have sought alternative channels of information had the Israeli government put its original decision to jam the VOP into effect.

In other development, the Israeli army continued to round up suspected Islamist activists in the West Bank.

Palestinian sources close to the Islamists estimated that as many as 150 activists had been arrested since 31 July, the day after the suicidal bombing in Jerusalem took place.

Mahmud Sha'alan, an Islamist leader from the Nablus area described the latest spate of arrest as "random, politically-motivated, and aimed at appeasing the Israeli public."

"When they arrest hundreds of people and keep them behind bars as hostages, without charge or trial, it means that they have scored an intelligence fiasco," said Sha'alan.

On Sunday, Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu gave Israeli troops the green light to operate freely within area (B) which is nominally under civilian PA control.

The measure is not likely to make a big difference since the Israeli army never ceased operating within area (B) and, especially recently, area (A) which is supposed to be under exclusive PA control.

For its part, PA police, too, have been making arrests among Palestinian Islamists, mainly under virtual Israeli duress.

However, it is noted that the PA hesitates to carry out an all-out effort to heed incessant Israeli calls to that effect for fear of losing face among ordinary Palestinians.

A responsible source of the Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) has castigated what it called American and Zionist attempt to disfigure Hamas' image and to cripple its resistance.
