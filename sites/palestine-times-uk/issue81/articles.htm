Questions on the 50th Anniversary of Israel's Creation

By Azzam Tamimi*

How much different are the Zionists from the Nazis?

One would expect the horrible persecution of Jews at the hands of the Nazis to create among world Jewry a culture of opposition to and abhorrence toward all forms of oppression and persecution. Regrettably, very few Jews nowadays­in fact only those who by virtue of their humanity, sincerity and conscience are shunned and ridiculed by their Jewish communities around the world­attempt to compare the painful experience of the Holocaust suffered by their ancestors to the no-less horrible and equally sinister policies pursued by the State of Israel. The eyes of few Jews, indeed very few, are not blurred by the Zionist propaganda that has been successful in achieving two main objectives: the first is to blackmail the entire humanity in the name of Holocaust victims, and the second is to justify, as a consequence of the first, the crimes against humanity perpetrated in Palestine and Lebanon by the State of Israel. Just as Hitler gave a bad reputation to Germany and its people, history and culture, Zionism is more anti-Semitic than the most avowedly anti-Semitic fascists. Hitler brought shame on Germany, and Israel brings shame on the Jews of the world. Zionism and Israel have managed to create and reflect the image of a dehumanized Jew, one who enjoys the killing of Palestinian and Lebanese children for no reason other than for the determination of their fathers and mothers to struggle for the liberation of their homelands from foreign occupation. Zionism and Israel have also created, and they continuously reflect, the image of a hypocritical Jew, who claims to be a victim of anti-Semitism while at the same time supporting and funding the racist Zionist regime in Occupied Palestine.

Nowhere in the world (at least not after the dismantling of Apartheid), apart from the State of Israel, is racism, theft, torture and murder justified in the name of a divine right and sanctioned by law. Based on a promise believed to have been given exclusively to the Jews by God, the confiscation of the land of another people, the killing of many of them and the banishment of many more is legitimate in the eyes of the Jews. In the name of saving the lives of the sacred super-human Jews, and by law, Palestinians suspected of terrorism may be tortured by their Israeli interrogators until they confess or die.

Amnesty International has recently criticized the Israeli government for proposing a new draft of the General Security Service (GSS) Law that will continue to legitimize Israel's torture of so-called security detainees during interrogation and to allow the perpetrators of such abuses to get away with it. One article of the new law grants GSS employees immunity from criminal liability if they are acting 'in good faith and in a reasonable manner in the course of carrying out their duties.' Amnesty International warned that if interrogation rules sanction and defend the use of torture and ill-treatment as they do now, even GSS agents who cause death, permanent disability or psychological damage will continue to benefit with impunity for their activities.

To prevent any exposure of the atrocities perpetrated by Israeli interrogation officers, the draft law would make it a criminal offence punishable by imprisonment for any member of the GSS or any member of the Knesset committee entrusted with oversight of the GSS to disclose information without authorization. In other words, as Amnesty International puts it, the new draft law provides no defense for whistleblowers, who, for reasons of conscience, reveal information to the public about human rights violations by the GSS.

Is it possible that the Israelis mistake the Palestinians for the Nazis? Throughout the history of Palestine, since its conquest by Muslims in the seventh century, Jews and Muslims coexisted peacefully. Even when Jews were persecuted in Europe and banished from many of its countries, they found no safe haven to seek refuge in other than the Muslim world, including Palestine itself. However, the attitude of twentieth century world Jewry toward the Muslims, and the Palestinians in particular, is not one of gratitude and appreciation, but one of revenge, hatred, malicious aggression and shameful thanklessness. It is as if the Muslims, and the Palestinians in particular, not the Europeans, were responsible for the miseries and sufferings of Jews. On the fiftieth anniversary of the occupation of Palestine, one must ask: why should the Palestinians be made to pay for the crimes of Europe, and especially those of the Nazis, against the Jews? Why should comforting the survivors of the Holocaust and compensating them take the form of the creation of a homeland for them in somebody else's homeland? If the West is so drowned in guilt because of its attitude toward the Jews, would it not have been more just for it to act collectively to create a homeland for European Jews somewhere within Germany or perhaps somewhere in the United States of America, their current foster parent?

Is it time for the Jews to wake up and dissociate themselves from Zionism? On the fiftieth anniversary of the 1948 Zionist crime against humanity in Palestine, one notices significant and very interesting changes in the world. Some of these changes indicate that the life of the State of Israel is approaching an end. On the one hand, the survival of this islet of alien intruders, who clearly belong more to Europe than to the Islamic Middle East, in an increasingly furious and boiling ocean of Muslims, is impossible. On the other hand, the creators of the State of Israel will not be able to maintain it for long, and the post-colonial Arab territorial States that provide it with security and protection from the anger of more than 1 billion Muslims are themselves desperately struggling for survival. The peace process, which is aimed primarily at reducing the dependency of the State of Israel on the West, has proven to be a non-starter, a deformed and stillborn foetus, whose conception had, in some ironic way, convinced so many Palestinians and Arabs that peace with Israel is impossible. The promised land of milk and honey, which was alleged to have been without a people, has shown itself to be a harsh and thorny terrain belonging to a people who generation after generation grow more determined neither to forget nor forgive. It would indeed be better for the Jews and their future generations to reconsider their position toward the State of Israel. It will do them no good to be involved in a campaign of hostility against the Muslims, who are instructed by their own faith to treat them well so long as they do not initiate aggression. The association of the Jews with the State of Israel should not be thought of as a historical inevitability. At the turn of the century very few Jews supported Zionism, few of them nowadays oppose it. It is hoped that very soon many more of them will realize that Zionism and the State of Israel, not the Holocaust, are the worst things that have ever happened to the Jews in their entire history.

 

*The writer is the director of the London-based Liberty for the Muslim World.

Palestinians exasperated by America's "scandalous double standards"

By Khalid Amayreh

While most Palestinians are not harrowing to secure gas masks (they haven't any), sealed rooms and other protective means against a conceivable Iraqi attack on Israel-Palestine in which weapons of mass destruction could be used, most Palestinians' hearts are never the less boiling with anger at "the odious duplicity" characterizing American policy toward Iraq and Israel.

Indeed, the mere allusion of the current Iraqi crisis invites ready and embittered Palestinian protestations at the bluntly-conspicuous American double standards in dealing with Saddam Hussein's partial non-compliance with UN resolutions on the one hand, and with Israel's enduring and contemptuous disregard for international legitimacy, on the other.

The asymmetry is amply stark in every Palestinian's mind, if only because Netanyahu is nonchalantly turning his back to the entire peace process, leaving an exhausted Yasser Arafat imploring a scandal-ridden Bill Clinton to do something to save the moribund peace process from Netanyahu's equivocations.

So, the words are on everybody's tongue: "Why don't they (the United States) force Israel to comply with UN resolutions just as they are determined to do so in the Gulf?" Why, why...?

For most Palestinians, the answer is simple: The United States is only the monkey and Israel is the organ grinder that determines American steps toward Iraq and Palestine.

For many ordinary Palestinians, the current American crusade against Iraq has nothing to do in reality with the alleged hiding by Iraq of forbidden weapons of mass destruction. This is only a pretext. In fact, some Palestinians (their number is increasing) go as far as arguing that the American build-up is not even aimed against Saddam Hussein. Their rationale is that the Iraqi dictator and the continuous crises he instigates serve the strategic and geopolitical interests of the United States. Last week, a professor of Political Science at Bethlehem University was even more audacious, claiming that the current showdown "is merely concocted by Saddam Hussein and the Americans."

"Look," the professor said, with a mystifying grin, "they are using him to blackmail the Gulf sheikdoms for more oil money and also to perpetuate their military presence in that part of the world."

The professors' words, however, had no echo of note among the few thousand, mostly young, demonstrators who during the last two weeks demonstrated in support of "the Iraqi people" and against American bellicosity and arrogance of power.

The fact that Saddam Hussein is a repugnant despot didn't deter hundreds of disenchanted Palestinians from taking to the streets, shouting anti-American and anti-Israeli slogans and even holding Saddam Hussein's portrait aloft alongside that of Yasser Arafat.

"We can't remain silent when the cowboys are about to rain death on Baghdad....men, women and children will be the victims," protested one demonstrator outside the Dheishe refugee camp on 16 February.

The sporadic demonstrations in support of Iraq proved to be a sensational item for much of the Israeli media and a definite propagandistic food for thought for Benyamin Netanyahu, as well as an embarrassment for the Palestinian Authority, which found itself pressed between the American-Israeli hammer and the anvil of the Palestinian public opinion.

The PA, however, proved once again that it valued the "legitimacy" that comes from American and Israeli acceptance more than that which comes from the Palestinian public.

Hence on 18 and 19 February, the PA Minister of Culture and Information, Yasser Abed Rabu, ordered the closure of a score of private TV stations for "inciting the public to show solidarity with Iraq."

Earlier, Ghazi al Jabali, the General Commander of the PA police in Gaza, issued a decree banning all pro-Iraqi demonstrations throughout the autonomous enclaves "due to their detrimental impact on Palestinian national interests."

Clearly, Jabali and Abed Rabu were only carrying out instructions from Yasser Arafat who viewed American consternation with the demonstrations as harmful to Palestinian interests.

Meanwhile, Palestinians, along with the peoples of the region, are holding their breath, awaiting the unfolding of the crisis and hoping for a peaceful end.

Arafat and the Holocaust Museum

By Ray Hanania*

Much was made of the on-again/off-again invitation extended by the Holocaust Museum in Washington, D.C. to Palestine Authority President Yasser Arafat.

Many Jewish leaders denounced the invitation saying it would send out the "wrong message," alleging that Arafat is responsible for the murder of many Jews.

Well, let's get one thing straight from the beginning.

President Arafat is genuine when he says that he does not discriminate against Christians, Muslims or Jews. Further, Arafat does not brand every member of the Jewish faith as being responsible for the atrocities that Israel and Zionism committed against the Palestinians.

At issue is not whether Arafat should or should not visit the Holocaust Museum. The real issue is whether or not Arafat should make such a visit before Israeli officials make similar gestures toward memorials marking the killings of Palestinians by Israelis and Jews.

Neither Arafat nor the Palestinian people are responsible for the murder of 6 million Jews by the Nazis during World War II. In fact, some argue that the non-Arab West is more responsible. Jewish refugees that America, Britain, France and other European countries routinely refused immigration entry, ended up in Nazi concentration camps.

Typically, Israel and many Jewish leaders who complain about a visit by Arafat to the Holocaust Museum are guilty of a double standard. To them, the issue is simply whether an enemy of Israel should be allowed to show compassion for the suffering of Jews.

But the real issue is much broader and more startling.

You see, I don't think Arafat should visit the Holocaust Museum, at least not until after Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu shows similar compassion. He should first express an interest in visiting a memorial marking the murder of Palestinians by Jews and Israelis.

The one that comes immediately to mind is the memorial that Palestinians and some Americans are striving to establish to mark the now-destroyed village of Deir Yassin. On 9 April 1948, Etzel and LEHI units attacked the village and murdered 254 Palestinian civilians in cold blood, a massacre the Israelis continue to treat with arrogant denials and unbelievable "excuses." (Visit the Internet at www.deiryassin.org to learn more about this event.)

Netanyahu should embrace this effort and recognize the massacre as a crime against the Palestinian people, in much the same way that Arafat intended to visit the Holocaust Museum and demonstrate compassion for Jews murdered by the Nazis during World War II.

Even more so, Netanyahu has a special need to show compassion to the Palestinians for this particular event. The massacre was perpetrated by the very men who established the Likud Party that he now heads, the late Prime Minister Menachem Begin and former Prime Minister Yitzhak Shamir. Begin headed Etzel and Shamir headed LEHI.

Both Begin and Shamir are Netanyahu's mentors. Netanyahu wears their legacy.

Have Jews died as the result of Arafat's leadership?

Absolutely. Arafat is the leader of a revolution that has targeted Zionist Jews, Israelis and others who have supported the continued persecution and obliteration of Palestinian civilian life in Palestine since the founding of Israel 50 years ago this May.

But, to single out Arafat without acknowledging the killings of Palestinians by Israelis and Jews is morally wrong.

Palestinians have been murdered and continue to be brutalized by Jews in Israel. Israel deserves the same measures of guilt and condemnation as have been heaped upon Arafat, who has dedicated his life to restoring the rights of Palestinians. Rights denied to them by Jews in Israel!

Before Arafat even considers visiting the Holocaust Museum, Netanyahu might consider visiting the remains of Deir Yassin, which symbolizes for all Palestinians one of the most heinous of crimes by the Jewish people against the Palestinians.

If we are talking about compassion, that is a good place for most Israelis to start.

How the Day was Won?

By Muneer Shafiq*

It was inevitable for the agreement between the Iraqi government and Kofi Annan, the Secretary General of the U.N., to include a clause binding Iraq to the implementation of Security Council resolutions against it. Also inevitable was opening up for inspection all sites including Presidential Palaces.

After all that was requested of Iraq by almost all Arab and Islamic states, and by the superpowers leaning Iraq-wise in the crisis like Russia, China, and France.

Neither was Iraq opposed in principle to inspection even as it prevented UNSCOM's team into the Palaces! Rather it was clear from the start that inspection was negotiable if Kofi Annan and the majority of the Security Council would go along with proposed Iraqi changes to inspection teams, and with the setting of some kind of conception as to ending the state of siege once the inspections are through.

Inspections then would be conducted within a definite time frame even under any maximum limit..

That's why the U.S. government's battle revolved diplomatically speaking around depriving Iraq from that element.

Hence the essence of the American position has been to dominate inspection teams and to totally forfeit decision-making especially with regards to whence inspections are to be declared finished. With the ill-intention of leaving the sanctions indefinitely in place, this malicious policy has thus far driven UNSCOM's inspection efforts for the last seven years.

Therefore 1)the participation in inspection teams of other members of the Security Council giving them a supervising and chaperoning role, 2) adjusting the structure of those teams, and 3) the implicit promise thereof of lifting sanctions when the work of the born-again teams is over, all in all represents a political defeat for U.S. foreign policy.

The U.S. Administration might try to sweep that defeat under the rug by arguing that its demand of unrestricted access to all sites has been achieved. Yet nothing can be more indicative of the truth of that defeat more than U.S. Administration insistence that it reserves the right to reject the Iraq-UN deal and to strike Iraq unilaterally even before Secretary General Annan has had the opportunity to take the agreement to the Security Council; doing otherwise be tantamount to conceding diplomatic defeat before Arab and Islamic states, and before Russia, China, and France.

True the scale of the defeat was relatively small! But with far-reaching and meaningful implications for the international balance-of-power nevertheless.

But if the U.S. Administration didn't accept its diplomatic defeat, it would then make a more grievous mistake by entering a war alone without counting even on British support, or with British reservations.

Striking Iraq or entering a war in the midst of such international isolation, and in the shadow of the confirmed deal between the UN and Iraq would however make political defeat more certain and more macabre than simply backing off by agreeing to the UN-brokered deal.

That's why Iraq did good by reaching that agreement with Kofi Annan...

Sex, Lies And The U.S. Initiative

By Ghada Khouri*

The latest U.S. initiative to revive the stalled Middle East peace process was overshadowed by the allegations against President Bill Clinton of an affair with a former White House intern. As Palestinian leader Yasser Arafat sat across Clinton following their meeting last month, members of the press were eager to ask the American President about the alleged affair. Television news that night carried his defensive response as a lead story, while the substance of the meeting with Arafat took the back burner. The sex scandal engulfing the Clinton White House is paralleled with a Mideast initiative, which seems to be bearing little fruit.

During his visit to Washington, Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu remained firm on his pre-conditions for any further Israeli moves to put the peace process back on track, while Arafat painstakingly tried to impart upon the American public the Israeli government's continued violations of signed agreements. The American Administration found itself stuck in the middle, still refusing to publicly admonish Israeli intransigence while making its dissatisfaction known in its treatment of Netanyahu in private. Clinton first snubbed the Israeli Prime Minister in November when he refused to meet with him as he visited American Jewish leaders. Again this week, the White House was unusually cold in its reception, with no special public appearances or shared meals between the two leaders.

These unusual personal snubs come as a result of Netanyahu's lack of cooperation with a U.S. effort to revive peace talks that ground to a halt since the Israeli government approved the construction of a Jewish settlement on Jabal Abu Ghneim in Arab East Jerusalem. An Israeli redeployment from the West Bank scheduled to take place last March is nowhere in sight.

Rather than present a plan for a "credible" withdrawal, as requested by the United States, Netanyahu did not offer any percentages. Prior to his arrival in Washington, the Israeli Cabinet imposed 50 specific measures to be taken by the Palestinian leadership before any further redeployment is carried out, including cracking down on extremists, eliminating most of the Palestinian National Covenant, and reducing the size of the Palestinian police force. The Israeli Cabinet's decision could in fact stall any further pullbacks indefinitely by citing Palestinian non-compliance to its stringent demands.

Netanyahu justified his government's position by underscoring Israeli security concerns. "I cannot think of peace without security," he said. "As long as we have undemocratic regimes around us, the only peace that will hold is a peace that can be defended, and we insist on those defenses." Among his principal demands is the annulment of sections of the Palestinian Charter calling for Israel's destruction. The Palestine National Council voted in 1996 to repeal several sections of the Charter, which were spelled out in a letter Arafat brought with him to Washington. The White House clearly accepted the letter as a formal recognition of the Palestinians' acceptance of Israel's right to exist. But Netanyahu remained adamant about his demand that further action be taken to formalize the annulment.

Netanyahu was equally insistent on Palestinian steps to "fight terrorism." He criticized the Palestinian Authority for adopting what he calls a "revolving door" policy, whereby Palestinians accused of involvement in "terrorist attacks" against Israel are jailed, then released before carrying out their sentence. Arafat denied the existence of such a policy and accused Netanyahu of using the security issue as an excuse to renege on Israeli obligations. "It's obvious that Mr. Netanyahu's interest is not in reaching and concluding an agreement on security, because he wants to use security as a pretext and as an excuse for his non-compliance with what was agreed upon," Arafat said.

The U.S. strategy is now focused on addressing concerns on both sides by encouraging security cooperation and an Israeli troop withdrawal from unspecified West Bank areas. Under the Oslo agreements, Israel is mandated to carry out three further redeployments from the West Bank, to be completed by mid-1998. Netanyahu's tactic is to get away with one modest withdrawal and jump into final status talks, thus avoiding a substantial pullback acceptable to the Palestinians. The Clinton Administration has reportedly requested a two-digit withdrawal plan of at least 10%, but Netanyahu has refused to make any promises. Arafat, on the other hand, stated that the Palestinians expect to gain control over more than 90% of the West Bank before proceeding with final status negotiations.

Netanyahu left little doubt as to his government's intention to hold on to as much of the West Bank as possible. He argued that the conquest of the West Bank during the 1967 Six-Day War created a secure buffer between Israel and its Arab neighbours by establishing a physical wall between Israel and "any potential invader" from the Arab world. "If you ask us to withdraw from that wall, we know the whole peace process would collapse, because Israel would revert again to a narrow band along the Mediterranean that will invite aggression and a future conflict," he said.

Netanyahu has not only resisted U.S. calls to produce an acceptable plan for troop withdrawal, but he has also rebuffed a U.S. request for a "time-out" on further Israeli settlements. "In Oslo, there is no limitation on settlement activity, even the creation of new settlements," he said.

Netanyahu's unyielding stand is partly shaped by his government's slim hold on power since the resignation of Foreign Minister David Levy earlier this month and the withdrawal of his Gesher faction from the governing coalition. With a remaining 61-vote majority in a 120-seat Parliament, the Israeli government is now controlled by a powerful right that refuses to cede land to the Palestinians.

Netanyahu used these domestic constraints as a card to justify his resistance to U.S. pressure. The support he is receiving from the evangelical right and members of Congress has helped strengthen his hand in dealing with the Clinton administration. Several pro-Israel members of Congress, including House Speaker Newt Gingrich, House International Relations Committee Chairman Benjamin Gilman, and Senate Foreign Relations Committee Chairman Jesse Helms, have strongly criticized President Clinton for giving Netanyahu the cold shoulder.

On the eve of his meeting with Clinton, Netanyahu addressed representatives of the Christian right, including evangelical leader Reverend Jerry Falwell, who pledged to actively encourage his constituents to oppose the ceding of land to the Palestinians. Falwell is one of President Clinton's most zealous critics. However, the television preacher's credibility runs low among the mainstream American public, which sees him as a right-wing leader bent on sensationalism. Through his television programme, Rev. Falwell is currently selling a far-fetched videotaped account accusing President Clinton of involvement in drug- dealing and the assassination of political opponents. Netanyahu's association with Falwell, which angered the White House and some American Jewish groups, is indicative of the close ideological affinity between Zionist Christians and conservative Jews. Many evangelical Christians believe that Israel is a nation promised to the Jews by God and that the gathering of Jews in Israel will usher in the second coming of Christ.

Arafat dealt with a controversy of his own upon his arrival in Washington. He was invited, uninvited, then re-invited to visit the Holocaust Museum, a federal institution honouring the victims of the Nazi Holocaust. The invitation generated a polemic among Jewish groups, with some opposing his visit while others welcomed it as a positive step toward reconciliation. Arab critics, on the other hand, felt Arafat's visit was inappropriate at a time when he was expected to focus on Israeli violations of Palestinian rights. A visit to the Museum would have signalled an overture to Israel at a time wconciliatory moves on Israel's part are not forthcoming. In addition, it would have implied acceptance of the moral justification for the State of Israel, namely to create a safe refuge for Jews around the world. Arafat managed to avoid the controversy by stating that he accepted the invitation, but that his schedule did not allow him to carry out the visit.

Besides the various controversies surrounding the three main players in the Mideast peace process, the Washington initiative underscored the intransigence of the Israeli government and mounting U.S. dissatisfaction with Netanyahu's tactics. Clinton attempted to exhibit a greater degree of even-handedness after being repeatedly criticized by Arab leaders for a hitherto timid response to Netanyahu's provocative policies. He stated that U.S. efforts are centred on bridging the gap between Israel's security concerns and Palestinian aspirations. In a significant recognition of Palestinian national rights and a reiteration of U.S. commitment to the land-for-peace formula, Clinton stressed the need to address both peoples' concerns "so that Israelis can live in security, recognized by all their neighbours, and the Palestinians can realize their aspirations to live as a free people."

The U.S. government's next step is to attempt to set up a meeting between Netanyahu and Arafat in the next few weeks in order to revive direct negotiations between the two sides. As of now, the unravelling sex scandal, which has led some media analysts to prematurely doubt the very survival of the Clinton presidency, is playing in Netanyahu's favour. Whether Clinton will manage to keep up the heat on Netanyahu while keeping the lid on the Lewinski affair could affect his credibility and effectiveness in reviving a peace process on the brink of total collapse.

 

*The author is a freelance writer on Middle East issues.

Israel: shielding abusers with immunity

By Ruba Farkh

A well-known Israeli columnist Mosheh Negbi once wrote during the first anniversary of the Intifadah (uprising) that "the Intifadah has destroyed completely the Israeli claims that it is one of the few countries in the world that respects the International Declaration for human rights, not just theoretically but practically as wellalmost every procedure taken by Defense Minister Rabin to break up the Intifadah contradicts with the principles of the International Declaration." This stigma on the Israeli government's record seems to have become permanent over the years.

The latest draft law submitted to the government organizes the work of the Interior Security Force, Shin Bet. The law also outlines the basic rules for the government and Knesset supervision over the performance of Shin Bet, as well as the mechanisms for appointing the senior staff and directors. This draft law, which was approved by the Supreme Court, would give complete immunity to Shin Bet officials and would openly allow the use of "moderate physical pressure" while interrogating a prisoner. This loosely worded law, by virtue of its non-specificity, allows interrogators to use methods such as hooding and sleep deprivation combined with painful posture positions. One can understand why the draft law passed, especially when one also knows that Shin Bet officials, who are directly connected to the Prime Minister, can go beyond the use of "moderate physical pressure" when a suspect under interrogation is a so-called "time bomb", or in other words has information on an operation under preparation. The law, which was widely condemned by human rights organizations all over the world and described as a "green light" for the use of torture by Amnesty International, makes Israel "the only country in the world known to permit explicitly the use of torture for purposes of interrogation," said Amnesty in a report criticizing the Israeli government.

Israel has more than gone beyond the limits of its discriminatory policies. Its acts are becoming worse by the day and its lack of response to outside criticism have allowed its long-standing bias attitudes to leak into its judicial system and even to some human rights groups. For instance, during its annual meeting last month, the Federation for Human Rights decided to discontinue the membership of the Israeli Committee for Human Rights, accusing it of being bias and of being influenced by the government, thus raising doubts about its credibility and the reliability of its reports.

Ironically, at the same time that the draft law was submitted to the government, the Supreme Court was considering the case of two Palestinian prisoners subjected to torture during interrogation. As recently as a few months ago, the Israeli Ministry of Justice drafted a law that would exempt the State of Israel and its security forces from tort liability for bodily injury and wrongful death of Palestinians during the period of the Intifadah. In other words, the Ministry of Justice was seeking to receive full exemption from the responsibility of killing some 1,000 Palestinians and injuring around 18,000 others. If this takes place, the Palestinians in the West Bank and Gaza would no longer be able to seek compensation in Israeli courts from Israeli security forces or from the State for any bodily injury or wrongful death having occurred between 8 December 1987, when the Intifadah broke out, and 13 September 1993, the date of the signing of the Israeli-PLO Declaration of Principles. Human Rights Watch said in its report regarding this matter, "the proposed bill should be abandoned because it violates international human rights standards in several ways. First, it undermines the ability of victims of human rights abuses to seek fair compensation, a right that is enshrined in various instruments. Second, the proposed law is blatantly discriminatory in that it applies to Palestinians but not to Israeli citizens or permanent residents or tourists. Third, the proposed law would also narrow the range of acts for which compensation may be sought so as to disqualify many victims of wrongful injury from seeking damages."

In spite of the record of multiple gross human rights violations, one can't help but wonder how come this shameful record is always able to slip out of the spotlight of Western politicians? By remaining in the shadows, it has managed to secure not only full support of the West, but also generous loans, donations and funds.

Netanyahu unveils his security map: Toward a greater Israel

By Dr. Daoud A. Abdullah*

After weeks of playing hide and seek, Benyamin Netanyahu has finally decided to unveil his secret security map. It contains few surprises and considerably even fewer assurances. As expected, he has offered a minimal 6%-8% withdrawal from the occupied West Bank, leaving the Palestinians with approximately 40% of the total area, including the Gaza Strip. A cursory glance at the mysterious map illustrates that Netanyahu has no intention of ending Israel's military occupation of the territories. On the contrary, it demonstrates his unwavering determination to prevent, and create enough obstacles where necessary, the emergence of an independent Palestinian State in the occupied territories with Jerusalem as its capital.

Driven by a fanatical ideology, which stigmatizes the Palestinians as an alien people, Prime Minister Netanyahu, like all his predecessors, is at heart a devout Zionist committed to the realization of a pure and exclusive Jewish State in occupied Palestine. In certain ways, though, he may be considered more honest than many other Israeli politicians if only because he has never concealed his suspicion and disdain for the Oslo process. Although the Declaration of Principles (DOP) makes no clear mention of a Palestinian State, Netanyahu nevertheless fears that the notoriously flawed agreement may ultimately be used as a vehicle to deliver one. It is for this reason, therefore, he decided to wreck the Oslo process by deliberately violating Palestinian rights and sensibilities with provocative policies such as the undermining of al Aqsa Mosque and the building of settlements in Abu Ghneim and Ras al Amoud.

During his visit to Washington in February 1997, Netanyahu presented a similar map to President Clinton proposing the annexation of 50% of the West Bank. Although the map contained no specific borders, Clinton neither approved nor opposed it. One week after the meeting, Netanyahu's foreign policy adviser at the time, Dore Gold, disclosed that the United States no longer insists on the withdrawal to the pre-June 1967 borders. Encouraged by the unqualified support of the United States, Netanyahu has returned one year later to announce Israel's claim to a further 10% of the West Bank and plans to build an additional 30,000 homes for Jewish settlers in the territories.

 

Old map on new paper

In terms of its originality and creativity, the latest Israeli security map reflects a total lack of imagination. For although its style and presentation may have changed, its intrinsic character conforms to the prototype drawn up by the former Deputy Prime Minister, Yigal Allon, in July 1967. In brief, Allon recommended the annexation of 40% of the West Bank and half of the Gaza Strip. He also called for an Israeli security belt 10-15 kilometres wide extending the length of the Jordan Rift and the establishment of an autonomous framework for the Palestinians.

Though never officially adopted by any Israeli cabinet since it was first proposed, the Allon Plan has provided the basic guidelines of negotiation for those Israelis who favoured the land-for-peace approach. Consequently, all the subsequent Israeli plans from Dayan, Sharon and Netanyahu have displayed one common feature. That is to create demographic and military obstacles to prevent the emergence of a Palestinian State. They only differ in the degree and quantity of obstacles whose varying proportion may lead to a greater or lesser measure of autonomy but definitely not independence.

International support for their claim to Statehood is growing in almost every major capital of the world except Washington. Though by all means timely and welcome, Palestinians are yet awaiting further diplomatic and political measures that would lead to the establishment of a new, just and legal order in their troubled land.

Amer the Imbecile

By E. Yaghi

Shadows of the deserted night cautiously tiptoed around the dimensions over the large mountain square. Rough winds swept gnarled trees and bent them over further in an easterly direction while small bushes, still rooted firmly in the hard ground, held their positions. The gray cement and stone houses guarded the square with shuttered windows and owners who had at last retreated to their beds, surrendering themselves to slumber.

Sidewalks (kept immaculately clean from continuous efforts by the city's orange-uniformed workers) resounded the lone footsteps of a man known as Amer the Imbecile. The silence of the night was broken by his voice booming in a solo conversation. "You'll never learn. How many times have you had the chance to improve yourselves, yet remain the same," he complained. His round body ambled along against the wind, flapping his loose jacket and trousers.

Whenever Amer begged for bread, he was fed a meal. Whenever he asked for water, he was given yogurt. At intervals he was given odd jobs to do by various merchants whose shops sat on or near the square. He stomped through the neighbourhoods, now a beggar, now a captain, usually holding complete conversations with invisible associates.

Curious folks often inquired, "Where do you come from?"

"I come from nowhere," or, "I come from everywhere," he would reply.

So he became known as feeble-minded and given odd jobs sweeping out stores and carrying sacks of goods here and there. His demands were simple and no one refused him. Feared by some, laughed at by others, he dwelt in a realm somewhere between reality and insanity. Voices from his heart or mind would speak to him and he would answer, striking up a conversation with himself wherever he was at the time.

On this particular night, some policemen unfamiliar with the square and the surrounding neighbourhood, stopped Amer as he shouted to himself. "Who are you?" they asked him.

"My name is Amer and I'm from the Akl family. I live over there," he pointed, "under the stairway of my family's house."

The police questioned the Akl family about Amer and were told, "No, he isn't a member of our family, but we feel sorry for him, so we let him sleep under our stairs at night. He's an idiot, you know, and harms no one."

Amer's incoherence and remoteness further convinced them that he was indeed an imbecile and better left alone. The policemen jumped back in their car and sped away. Continuing his journey, Amer plodded on. The night dew was fresh on his tongue and its moisture wet his face. He stooped down to catch a flying paper and stuffed it into his black trash bag where he hoarded his cache of treasured rubbish, all the while bickering with himself.

He didn't know why he was out in the middle of the night talking to himself. They had told him to come and their voices had guided him. They spoke to him from the gnarled trees and from the shrubs. They spoke to him from distant winds. It was they who ordered him to gather precious flying papers, and it was their invisible voices whispering from his heart to "put

more fuel on the fire. There are discarded clothes and wood to burn lodged under some rocks.

So he dug for them with his hands and dirt from the soil crept in under his fingernails to add to the dirt already stored there. He stumbled over to the fire and threw his find on the flames, nearly smothering them. Black wisps of smoke rose in curled clouds and filled Amer's nostrils and filtered into his nose and throat.

Satisfied, he sat on some rocks nearby as the fire gained strength. He answered the voice in his heart and said, "I keep cleaning after the cattle that throw their rubbish in the streets and homes of ghosts. But they never did listen. My work never finishes."

When the fire finally died down, the imbecile sat confused. He scratched his head and looked at his toes peeping out at him from ragged shoes. He would wait for the voices to tell him what to do next. The chill of the night gnawed at his bones but he was unheeding. He looked up to the sky and found night was giving way to light. He inhaled deeply and let the air of the new dawn enter his lungs. He swallowed. The taste of the new day was fresh in his mouth and he smiled again.

But the voices returned and spoke to him, fighting, teasing and bullying him. Once more his voice rose in protest, "I swear by God that no one, no one ever listens to me. I'm an important person. My work is endless, yet you keep bothering me. Go and disturb someone else for a change."

And he continued to speak to himself in a loud voice as the winds pushed and pulled at him and blew his hair in different directions. At length, after many hassles, he returned to his cold shelter under the stairs of the merchant Akl, who kindly let him sleep there. He flung himself down on a worn blanket and slept like a baby until he was awakened again by rustling voices living in his heart, his mind, or grew out of the windblown trees and shrubs. Soon he would awake with pangs of hunger grinding in his stomach. Then he would scrounge once more for food.

No one knows why Amer degenerated into a mentally handicapped person. Some say that he wasn't always that way. At one time he had a good job, wore a suit and was respected by all. But somehow, some kind of misfortune struck him that warped and destroyed his mind and now he lives at the mercy of others while his mind pulls him in different directions and he is confused, frustrated, without a home and tragically alone.

Perhaps, if you pass Amer's square in the deep of the night when shadows tiptoe among the trees and the winds whisper strange secrets, you will hear Amer the Imbecile fighting with his invisible voices locked in his own world, completely out of touch with everyone and everything.

Poem: My Poor Arab!

By S. Norashkharian

My poor Arab! The abused child of the world!

The darkest clouds blown by the winds of the West

Have cast shadows upon your land once again...

Who knows this time which of your sons will they claim?

Which of your towns will vanish with keyboard strokes

And how many friends will you search for in vain?

And how much blood, how much blood will they vomit

Flooding your streets and your bedrooms once again?

O my brother, where will you now hide from them?

 

They call themselves civilized...!

 

Yet their warplanes will very soon reappear

With blue and red and white colors marked on them

And fifty stars boasting their crimes without shame...!

Soon they will creep into your space once again

Invisible, like the fingers guiding them

Raping! Raping! The virgin face of your skies

Chasing your nights away with their blinding fires

And bomb by bomb and shell by shell tearing down

All that you built stone by stone and breath by breath...!

 

They call themselves civilized...!

 

O, my brothers, close your eyes and plug your ears!

No! Do not wait for what your dawns will unveil!

For their terror will make you hope for the nights

Which are trapped in the black holes of collapsed stars...!

No! Do not wait for the yellow dust of death

To clear away from the limbs spread everywhere

Like the remnants of your defiled dignity...!

And do not wait for your mothers to stumble

Upon the charred shoes their children left behind...!

 

They call themselves civilized...!

 

Yet these bullies will trade your blood for your oil

And the blood of their sons for their bank accounts...!

So let us pump your black liquid down their throats

Until they gasp like ducks caught in their oil spills

And while it flows in the veins and tentacles

Of their global conglomerates and World Banks

Let us burn it! Let us burn it with revenge!

For they built them to rule our fate with blood

And they bred them to squeeze our lungs like pythons...!

 

They call themselves civilized...!

 

Where is your voice, where is your voice my brother?

Where is your voice and why is it lost again

In the uproar of lynching mobs which exclaim:

"Entertain us, entertain us now or else!

If you have no more scandals give us wars!"

Where is your voice and how is it missed again

By armies of reporters with satellites?

And which court ruled that you deserved punishment

For suffering the curse of your own tyrant?

 

They call themselves civilized...!

 

"Allahu Akbar!" You cry from your minarets

"Bism Illahi El Rahman ou El Rahim!"

And you bow down like a zebra caught by the throat

And accept your Allah's will with frozen eyes

While the High Priests debate your fate in Councils

Which they have made to legalize their vile deeds...

O, the High Priests and the nations they butchered!

Five times a day you accept your Allah's will

My dear brother, how will you now accept theirs?

 

They call themselves civilized...!

 

Walk with me now beyond borders drawn for us

And beyond the deserts where we lived like ghosts

Without footprints and without a signature

And beyond all of the fountains where our lips

Blistered with thirst which could never become quenched

And let us leave forever all the cities

Where our conscience became a clown wearing rags

Come with me now with a shovel and a rose

Let us bury a century of coffins...!

Only my soul hears your scream...!

 

Ontario, California

February 1998
