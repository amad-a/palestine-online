Helmet shield lifted and arms spread wide, a broad-chested Israeli sergeant, the biggest man in his squad, stood exposed on Shalala Street (Hebron of the West Bank) this afternoon and called to the young Palestinians heaving stones and flaming bottles of gasoline. "Give it to me in the eye!" he roared in Hebrew, pantomiming his challenge. "Give it to me in the ear!" Two sharpshooters of his military police squad, one high and one low, crouched under cover nearby. Whenever a torso or limb poked out from behind makeshift Palestinian battlements, the soldiers opened fire with steel-cored rubber bullets. "We did good shooting today," said the sergeant's platoon leader, who identified himself only as Lt. Ron. "Yes," affirmed a corporal, laughing nearby. "You can check Alia Hospital." Barton Gellman writes from Hebron- The Washington Post, June 21, 1997

Mustafa Diab, born and raised in East Jerusalem, went to the Israeli Interior Ministry seeking residency papers for his Jordanian wife and nine children. But instead of granting permits, an official there seized the identity card that allows Diab to live in Jerusalem and gave the Palestinian truck driver 15 days to leave the country. Two months later, Diab's 11-year-old daughter died, and his request for a death certificate to bury her in the family plot in Jerusalem was denied. No longer a legal resident, Diab had no rights in Jerusalem, the Israeli authorities told him. "I am destroyed," Diab said. "How can I describe it? They kicked me out of my homeland, and I cannot do anything about it." Marjorie Miller writes from the Occupied Jerusalem, The Los Angeles Times, June 21, 1997

With Palestinians increasingly losing faith in PLO Chairman Yasser Arafat and in American mediation, Gen. Uzi Dayan, operations commander of Israel's central army command, charged that up to a third of the Arab demonstrators clashing with Israelis were brought in from villages outside Hebron. Asked if the rioters were being directed or encouraged by Arafat's self-rule Palestinian Authority, Dayan replied, "I can make an educated guess." Israel Radio, quoting other security sources, said Friday that Israel believes the Palestinian Authority is, in fact, instigating the violence, a charge PLO officials deny. Storer H. Rowley writes from Hebron- The Chicago Tribune, June 21, 1997

ISRAEL'S prime minister Binyamin Netanyahu, survived a no-confidence vote last night, but the price of his political survival is likely to be a further downward lurch in the sinking Middle East peace process. Mr. Netanyahu's embattled right-wing coalition won the vote by 55 votes to 50, after ensuring the support of new Russian immigrants by offering them financial assistance in an eleventh-hour deal on Monday night. But the vote exposed significant erosion in the prime minister's authority. Eleven coalition members either abstained or stayed away, including the foreign minister, David Levy, and his supporters, and the former finance minister, Dan Meridor. "Another front has opened against Netanyahu," said Ari Shavit, a political commentator. "There is a sense this thing is disintegrating. This is non-confidence from the heart of the party." Julian Borger writes from Jerusalem- The Guardian, June 25, 1997

An investigation by The Independent has revealed that most of the American bombs and air-to-ground missiles fired by Israel in Lebanon last year were sold to the United States armed forces - not to Israel. Israel's "Operation Grapes of Wrath" - the bombardment of southern Lebanon that killed almost 200 civilians and 14 guerrillas from the pro-Iranian Hezbollah - used at least 1,700 bombs and missiles that were "transferred" from US military stocks with no prohibition on their use against civilians... The Israeli pilot who targeted the ambulance on 18 April last year - the Israelis claimed it was being driven by a member of the Hizballah guerrilla movement which was untrue - had in fact fired two missiles at the vehicle. One of them failed to detonate and the unexploded rocket revealed a complete set of code numbers which - according to our defence sources - proved that the missile was contracted for use by the US armed forces in 1986 and manufactured in 1988. Robert Fisk writes from Washington- The Independent, June 24, 1997
