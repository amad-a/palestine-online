Mazin B. Qumsiyeh (PhD)

- Born in 1957 in Beit Sahour, a suburb of Bethlehem in the area now occupied by Israel since 1967.  He is married with one son (now enrolling at MIT).
- Received Bachelor degree in Biology at Jordan University (1978), Master in Zoology at UConn (1982), Ph.D. from Texas Tech University (1986). 
- Did Postdoctoral work in St. Jude Children Research Hospital (1989) and a Clinical Fellowship at the University of Tennessee in Memphis (1991).
- Taught at schools in Bethlehem, Jericho, and Jerusalem before coming to the US.
- Currently serves as Associate Professor of Genetics and Director of Clinical Cytogenetic services at Yale University School of Medicine since 1999. Previously held similar position at Duke University.
- Authored over 110 scientific papers in areas of mammalogy, biology, and medicine including mammalian biology and evolution, clinical genetics, and cancer research.


- Author of three books
1) Bats of Egypt (1985, 102 pages)
2) Mammals of the Holy Land (1996, 387 pages)
3) Sharing The Land of Canaan (In press, 2004)

- Published over 100 letters to the editor and 30 op-ed pieces in International, national, regional and local papers on issues ranging from politics to environmental issues.  
- Interviewed in TV and radio extensively (local, national and international). Appearances in national media included the Washington Post, New York Times, Boston Globe, CNBC, C-Span, and ABC, among others. 
- Founder and president of the Holy Land Conservation Foundation and ex-President of the Middle East Genetics Association. 
- Won the Jallow activism award from the Arab American Anti-Discrimination Committee in 1998.
- Cofounder and currently national treasurer and media coordinator of Al-Awda, the Palestine Right to Return Coalition (see http://al-awda.org). 
- Cofounder of the AcademicsFor Justice.org and BoycottIsraeliGoods.org campaigns.
- Vice President of the Middle East Crisis Committee (http://TheStruggle.org)
- Member of a number of human rights groups (Amnesty, Peace action, Human Rights Watch, ACLU etc.).
