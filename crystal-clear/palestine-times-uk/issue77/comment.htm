Mossad's fiasco and the release of Sheikh Yassin

It was no coincidence nor change of heart that led to the Israeli release on 1 October 1997 of the founder of Hamas, Sheikh Ahmed Yassin, from a life imprisonment sentence passed down by an Israeli court in 1989. And it is not a surprise that tens of thousands of Palestinians turned out to welcome Sheikh Yassin's heroic return to Gaza after he received treatment in a Jordanian hospital.

What actually led to Sheikh Yassin's release can be traced to two main factors: (1) the Israelis fear that the quadriplegic, who is not only the founder of Hamas but a charismatic symbol to the Palestinians as well, would die in prison (since his health had been slowing deteriorating) and thus become not only a greater exemplification of the Palestinian struggle but also a martyr, and martyrdom could ignite a situation that might not be possible to keep under control and may even set the stage for another intifadah; and (2) Yassin's release was in fact in exchange for two Mossad agents captured in Amman, Jordan, on 25 September for their botched assassination attempt on Khaled Misha'al, the head of the Hamas Political Bureau.

In a show of goodwill, Yassin has offered to call a halt to suicide bombings if the Israelis withdraw completely from the West Bank and Gaza Strip, remove all Jewish settlements, and release Palestinian prisoners.

It is essential to point out that the Palestinian people are not terrorists as suggested in a recently published list by the Clinton Administration. The list contains about 30 names of 'terrorist' organizations and labels around 10 of them as Palestinian in origin. The Sein Fein is not listed at all, yet for years it's military wing, the Irish Republic Army, has engaged in an armed struggle against its own oppression and was involved in multiple bombings in the United Kingdom.

Palestine was Palestinian for more than 3,000 years until an influx into Palestinian territory of mostly European Jews was allowed by British colonists prior to the end of the Second World War. War broke out in 1948 when the heavily armed Jews (supplied with weapons by the West) made Palestinian homes and orchards theirs and either killed or expelled the indigenous inhabitants of Palestine to make way for a gathering of Jews from all over the world who had and still have no claim to the land either historically or legally.

It is only natural that through the years of ruthless and cruel oppression, the surviving Palestinians would resist the occupation of their land. It is a fact that no Palestinian man, woman or child is safe from Israeli tyranny that in turn produces counter-actions and -resistance in a multitude of forms and shapes. As long as this oppression goes on, the Palestinian people will continue to resist.

Added to Israeli intransigence, the Palestinians now must endure a double occupation against their new oppressor and tyrant, the Palestine Authority who, often seen to be an agent for the Israelis, arrests those who allegedly belong to the opposition or are Hamas activists. Thus, the Palestinians are surrounded on all sides and from every angle, increasing their despair and hopelessness and often making them desperate. The Oslo accords have brought no significant changes and day-to-day living is never secure and seldom fruitful. When a man is starving to death, he may be tempted to steal in order to live.

Under such circumstances, it is no wonder that such heroes as Sheikh Yassin are symbols to the masses. Peace cannot be real unless and until the Israelis withdraw from the West Bank and Gaza Strip, Jewish settlements are removed, refugees return to their homeland, Palestinian prisoners are released and the Palestinian people are given the freedom they need to conduct their own affairs within a statehood they are entitled to. As long as these conditions do not exist, then there will always be resistance in one form or another and it is more likely to become more desperate, perhaps even more violent, than ever before. It is a dangerous thing when people are poor, are hungry, are cold, have no proper means to earn a living, are without freedom and dignity, and have nowhere to turn. The only way to stop suicide bombings is to create an atmosphere where they are no longer deemed necessary. There will always be war until the Israelis decide they really want peace and are willing to listen to and act upon the demands of a people who have already lost more than they can afford.

The real terrorists are not those Palestinians who concoct molotov cocktails from nothing or who blows himself up with a homemade bomb in order to set his people free, but the State of Israel, an armed state that wages a war of terror and oppression against the native inhabitants of Palestine. The next U.S. list published about terrorist organizations should have Israel at the top.
