SCORES KILLED, INJURED IN A JERUSALEM SUICIDE BOMBING

Occupied Jerusalem-PT(30 July)

In what is largely viewed as a retaliatory response to the defamation by Jewish settlers of the Prophet Muhammed on 28 June, two Palestinian youths blew themselves up in a crowded West Jerusalem market Wednesday afternoon, killing 16 Israelis and injuring as many as 140 others.

The incident occurred around 1:15 local time (10:15 GMT) at the Mehane Yehuda marketplace in West Jerusalem.

Israeli police chief Assaf Hefetz said two suicidal bombers apparently simultaneously detonated two large bombs they were carrying, resulting in the high toll of casualties.

The bodies of the two suspected Palestinians were found in the rubble, but as of now they have not been identified.

Israeli hospital sources said as many as 20 people sustained serious injuries while at least five others were in "very critical conditions."

So, far no Palestinian organization has claimed responsibility for the incident. However, Israeli sources suspect that radical Islamic elements may have carried out the bloody attack.

Israeli intelligence sources described the bombing as a "big disaster."

Knesset member and former Shabak head, Gedeon Ezra, said in a radio interview after the bombing that Israel would need a long time to "absorb" the bombing.

He called on the Palestinian Authority to enter immediately into a full and comprehensive intelligence and security cooperation with Israel for the purpose of "eliminating the terrorists."

Following the incident, the Israeli army imposed a strict closure on the Gaza Strip and the West Bank, preventing Palestinians from entering Israel.

Moreover, Palestinian workers in Israel were ordered to return to their places of residence immediately.

Palestinian and Israeli sources said more collective punishment measures were expected, including massive arrests among people with Islamist orientations and home demolition.

 

REACTIONS

Palestinian Authority Yasser Arafat telephoned Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu to express his condemnation of the incident and condolences over the loss of life in the bombings.

Netanyahu's media advisor, David Bar Illan, described as "very difficult" the telephone conversation.

Bar Illan said Netanyahu scolded Arafat, telling him "to stop talking and start doing something to fight terrorism."

Arafat reportedly assured Netanyahu and latter Israeli president Ezer Weisman that he would do every thing he could to fight "terrorism."

Palestinian observers, however, don't believe that the bombing was aimed specifically at sabotaging the peace process which was in a precarious state long before the incident that a bombing could only contribute to its revival rather than its collapse.

The bombings, while can not be isolated from the overall context of the Israeli of narrowing Palestinian horizons, are likely to have been precipitated by the pasting last month of anti-Islamic posters in Hebron which caricatured the Prophet Muhammed as a pig writing the Quran.

So far, most ordinary Palestinians reacted with conspicuous apathy toward the bombings.

Indeed, virtually all Palestinians this reporter has been talking to pinned "central responsibility" for the incident on the Netanyahu government for pushing the Palestinians to the borders of catastrophe.

"Netanyahu has pushed us to a situation where we would have nothing to lose and nothing to be sorry for or about," said a Hebron policeman who refused to identify himself.

"If he wants suicide bombing, then he will get them," added the man, rather laconically.

Arafat Seeks to Regain Popularity in Hebron

(News analysis by Khalid Amayreh)

Hebron -PT (29 July)

Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat arrived in al-Khalil (Hebron) Monday night in what was described as a "routine" visit which observers believe is aimed at regaining popularity among the mostly pro-Islamist population of this conservative town.

Upon arrival in Hebron, Arafat met with local political and civic leaders, including Hebron mayor Mustafa Natshe, himself appointed by Arafat.

Arafat also met with several Islamist leaders, including the recently elected administrative board members of the local Islamic Youth Association. Later on, Arafat is expected to meet with the Islamist head of the local chamber of commerce, Hashem Natshe, who will reportedly communicate to the chairman "our people's disenchantment with the political process."

In fact, Arafat's and PA popularity has been steadily plummeting in Hebron, a traditional stronghold of Hamas and the Muslim Brotherhood, as well as in other Palestinian regions.

In May, a PA police officer manning a roadblock in Hebron shot and killed a local youth "because he didn't heed orders to stop."

The incident, only the latest in a series of similar incidents blamed on "inexperience," sparked off widespread anger and indignation among the local population that the victims funeral turned into an opportunity for disgruntled Hebronites to vent their frustration at the PA.

However, there seems to be more fundamental and underlying reasons contributing to Arafat's receding popularity in Hebron.

The town, which Arafat declared less than a year ago "liberated" three times, is actually still an occupied town.

Any visitor to Hebron today will see for himself that the town is surrounded by the Israeli army on all directions and that one can not possibly leave the town, or return to it in case he succeeded in leaving it, unless allowed to do so by Israeli soldiers manning the ubiquitous roadblocks erected on roads to and from Hebron.

More to the point, undercover Israeli soldiers disguising themselves as Arabs reportedly have been roaming the streets of Hebron, chasing "fugitives" and "wanted activists" and dragging them in the full view of PA police to the Old town where the Israeli army has full security responsibility.

Last month, an IDF undercover unit abducted several Palestinian stone-throwers from the Bab el Zawiyya commercial district where PA police maintain a strong and conspicuous presence.

The incident drew angry reactions from the citizens who began making analogies between the role played by the PA police vis-¦-vis Israel and that performed by the Israeli-backed and financed South Lebanon Army.

Furthermore, disillusionment with Arafat rose markedly recently when the PA decided to deploy hundreds of policemen along the "partitioning line" separating H-1 and H-2 (the Old town and the area of Hebron run by the PA).

The PA step abruptly ended three months of "confrontations," believed to have initiated by the PA itself, in which at least two Palestinian youngsters were killed and as many as 500 were injured.

A local Islamist leader, Muhammed Jamal Natshe, described PA management of the unrest as "totally stupid."

"It is lamentable indeed that after we lost two martyrs and sustained 500 injuries, the PA returns to a business-as-usual policy toward the Israelis," said Natshe.

The enduring unrest in Hebron also hit hard the local economy thanks to the hermetic closure of the town by the Israeli army, which lasted more than six weeks, rendering Hebron a virtually "shut-in ghetto."

With the political process stranded in its more or less precarious state, and with the PA manifestly unable to develop its autonomous authority toward a more promising arrangement that would give the Palestinians a genuine hope for a better tomorrow, the PA and Arafat will likely continue to languish between the Israeli hammer and the Palestinians' anvil.

However, Arafat is not known of giving ample deference to his people's disenchantment. The Chairman, it is well known, always values the legitimacy that comes from international acceptance, e.g. American acceptance, more than that which comes from his people's acceptance.

Hence, it is safe that the Palestinian powder-keg will continue to grow more tender with or without Arafat's approval.

JERUSALEM MUSLIM OFFICIALS VOW TO

PROTECT AL AKSA MOSQUE

 

Occupied Jerusalem -PT(27 July)

Muslim Wakf officials in Jerusalem have vowed to protect the Aksa Mosque against renewed Jewish attempts to hold prayers at the site and, therefore, gain a foothold at Islam's third holiest site.

Sheikh Hasan Tahbub, who holds the religious affair portfolio in the Palestinian Authority administration, said Sunday the Muslims of Jerusalem would not allow Jewish extremists "to transgress against our mosque."

Tahbub warned that any renewed intrusion by Jewish extremists into the Al Aksa plaza would "set the place on fire because Muslims will not bear seeing their most sacred shrine being desecrated by the extremists."

"We know quite well what these evil elements are after. They are after the mosque itself; they want to destroy the Mosque and build their alleged Temple on its site," Tahbub told Journalists on Sunday.

Tahbub and other members from the Supreme Muslim Council met briefly Sunday with Israeli police officials in an effort to avert a showdown over a planned attempt by the head of a fanatic Jewish group to pray at the Islamic shrine.

Following the meeting, Israeli police briefly detained Yehuda Etsioni, for "endangering public safety and security."

However, Etsioni apparently escaped from police custody and was able to slip into the Mosque plaza, disguising himself as a Mexican tourist, before he was detained again by the police.

Last week, an Israeli court issued a ruling allowing Etsioni, head of an ultra-religious group called "Hay Ve Kiyyam" to pray within the confines of Jerusalem's Haram al Sharif.

Interestingly, the declared goal of Hay Ve Kiyyam, indeed its reason d'?tre, is the destruction of al Masjidul Aksa (al Aksa Mosque) in preparation for the "rebuilding of the Solomon Temple" on its site.

Leaders of the group, who have many supporters in Israel, are driven by eschatological beliefs stipulating that redemption of the Jewish people would not be fulfilled unless the temple had been "re-established" on the same site now occupied by the al Aksa Mosque.

There are several fanatic Jewish groups subscribing to "the Al-Aksa-destruction-theory" including, inter alia, "the Temple's Faithfuls," "Kahana Hay," "Gush Emunim and its various splinter groups," and, of course, "Hay Ve Kiyyam."

The mentioned groups believe that the existence of the Hebrew state is meaningless without the temple, which they view as the "symbol" of Jewish redemption and self-fulfillment.

The latest attempted aggression against the al Aksa Mosque coincides with frenzied Jewish efforts to obliterate the Arab-Islamic identity of East Jerusalem which take the form of building Jewish colonies in the midst of Arab neighborhood and practicing the policy of "ethical cleansing" against the Arab population of the town.

JEWISH MAYOR DESECRATE NEGEV MOSQUE

Occupied Jerusalem -PT(26 July)

Israeli authorities prevented hundreds of Muslims from holding congregational prayer at the Be'ir al Sab'a Mosque Friday 25 July, by dumping several truckloads of garbage at the premise's entrance, forcing worshiper to hold their prayer on the street.

The worshipers, who came mostly from Be'ir al Sab'a itself and surrounding Bedouin villages, protested the desecration of the Mosque (reportedly on orders of the town's mayor Eli Boker) by holding a demonstration outside the mosque.

Demonstrators shouted slogans demanding the reopening of the mosque for religious services.

One demonstrator, 25-year-old Hasan abu Rbei'a attacked official Israeli racism and religious discrimination against non-Jews.

"The Jews who themselves were for a long time victims of racism are now indulging in the worst kind of racism and religious discrimination," said Abu Rbei'a

"Look! this is a Muslim mosque which was built in 1906, 42 years before Israel's birth, and they are preventing us from entering it and praying therein," he added angrily.

According to Knesset member Abdul Wah-hab Darawshe, who took part in the demonstration, the municipal council of Beir al Sab'a object adamantly to Muslims recovering and restoring the Mosque purely for "racist reasons."

"They simply don't want to see any Muslim presence here," Darawshe told journalists outside the Mosque.

Another Knesset member participating in the protest was Talab al Sane'i who further castigated "state-sanctioned anti-Islamic racism in Israel"

Al Sane'i vowed that Muslims would return to the mosque every Friday until they recover it from usurpation.

"The scum of racism will not succeed in making us abandon our most legitimate right in restoring our mosque and praying in it...this is our mosque, " he said.

Sheikh Juma'a al Ksasi, former mayor of the Bedouin town of Rahat, also attacked the "dogged Jewish insistence on arrogating our holy shrines."

"They must understand that the dignity of our holy places is an integral part of our human personal dignity." said Al Ksasi.

He added that Muslims in the Negev would continue their struggle until the liberation and recovery of the Mosque.

In other development, Muslim leaders in Palestine have warned against recurrent Jewish attempts to forcibly enter the AL Aksa Mosque and hold prayers with al Haram al Sharif compound in Jerusalem.

Sheikh Hayyan al Idrisi, an Imam at al Aksa Mosque said Muslims were watching keenly "systematic Israeli efforts to gain a foothold at the al Aksa Mosque."

He called on the Muslim world to "stop playing the spectator's role and do something for al Masjidul Aksa."

The Israeli High Court issued a ruling in May allowing ultra-religious Jews to hold prayers within the confines of the Al Aksa plaza.

The decision drew angry reactions from Muslim leaders in Palestine.

MPs: PA Acts As an Israeli Puppet

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (24 July)

Several Palestinian Legislative Council members have castigated the Palestinian Authority leadership for "acting as an Israeli puppet."

The councilors' anger stemmed from PA Chairman Yasser Arafat's decision to order the arrest and trial of several PA police officers accused by Israel of "implication in planning anti-Israeli activities."

Abdul Fattah Hamayel, a councilor from Tulkarm, voiced bitterness at the PA for being "so responsive to Israeli charges and pressure."

"When the PA acts on Israeli charges by arresting and trying Palestinian police officers, it conveys a very bad message which says the Palestinian Authority is just an Israeli puppet," said Hamayel during a Legislative Council session in Ramallah on 22 July.

A similar feeling was expressed by Nablus councilor Muawiyya al Masri who accused the PA of showing "appalling submissiveness to Israeli blackmail."

Al Masri questioned bitterly how the Israeli occupation authorities could stop the car of Col. Munir Abbushi, arrest, handcuff, and blindfold him before being taken away to an Israeli detention camp amid official Palestinian Authority silence.

"What the Palestinian Authority has been doing in this regard shows that it is but a tool in the hands of the Israelis, nothing but," said al Masri.

Hamayel, al Masri and another councilor, Qaddura Faris, of Ramallah, unanimously agreed that Israeli occupation authorities were pressing charges pertaining to Palestinian police involvement in anti-Israeli activities solely to extract more political concessions from an increasingly vulnerable and weak Palestinian authority.

Last week, Arafat ordered the formation of an inquiry commission to look into Israeli charges that high-ranking Palestinian police officers were recently involved in planing and carrying out hostile acts against Jewish settlers in northern West Bank.

Among PA officers detained by the autonomous authorities is Col. Jihad al Massimi whom Israel had accused of masterminding a planned attack on Jewish settlers near Nablus.

Sever other lower-ranking officers were reportedly detained or dismissed from duty.

THOUSANDS SWEAR ALLEGIANCE TO THE PROPHET

Occupied Hebron- PT (21 July)

As many as 20 thousand Palestinians swore allegiance to the Prophet Muhammed (PBOH) in Hebron on 19 July amid renewed tension between Israel and the Palestinian Authority over alleged involvement of Palestinian police officers in acts of resistance against Israeli occupation.

The large rally, bearing the title "loyalty to the Prophet" was organized by the Islamic movement in the town in response to blasphemous anti-Islamic posters pasted by a Jewish female settler on Arab storefronts in Hebron three weeks ago.

Several Islamist speakers addressed the huge multitude, reasserting the traditional Islamic stance against the Israeli occupation and the political process and calling on Yasser Arafat's Palestinian Authority and Arab-Muslim states to terminate their subservience to American hegemony.

Sheikh Nayef Rajub, a charismatic speaker from the town of Dura 12 miles south west of Hebron, described the Jewish attack on the Prophet Muhammed and the Quran as "absolutely compatible with inherent Jewish disrespect of other peoples' religions."

"Their latest profanity is no surprise to anybody who reads the Talmud," said Rajub.

The imam of the Grand Mosque in Dura said there was inherent Jewish enmity toward the person of Prophet Muhammed since "he put an end to their settlements in the Arabian Peninsula and exposed them naked before the whole world."

Rajub also blamed Muslim leaders, without naming any of them, for "having the audacity to insult the prophet of Islam."

"We bitterly notice today that throughout the Muslim world the only people who can stand up to Jewish insolence and arrogance are behind bars."

Azzam Salhab, who was introduced as "official representative of the Islamic Movement," urged the PA to release all political prisoners and "not to close the door in the face of those who desire to fight those who usurped our homeland, arrogated our rights, and destroyed our homes."

Salhab, a professor of Islamic Studies at the Hebron University, also called on the PA "to respect political pluralism, freedom of speech and the press, and to put an end it nepotistic appointments in the public sectors."

Moreover, Salhab castigated the PA policy of appointing "municipal committees" to run local councils throughout the occupied territories, describing it as amounting to "raping citizens' rights and confiscating our people's freedoms."

The Islamist organizers of the rally pasted huge posters and posted signs warning Jews against "perpetrating this crime against" and vowing "to burn the hands of those who insult the greatest man in the past, present and future."

Report: Israel uses Palestinian prisoners as guinea-pigs

Occupied Jerusalem-PT (17 July)

The Israeli Defense Force (IDF) has systematically carried out "scientific experiments" on Palestinian detainees in Israel, Palestinian and Israeli sources said.

A report published Thursday 17 July in major Arabic newspapers in the Occupied Territories revealed that "as many as 1000 drug-related experiments are carried out every year on Arab prisoners in Israeli prisons and detention camps."

According to the report, which quoted reliable Israeli sources including Knesset members, the Israeli health ministry granted major drugs-manufacturing companies in Israel more than a thousand permits to use Palestinian prisoners as "lab animals" to test the effect of new drugs.

The scandalous practice was initially disclosed by Labour Knesset member Dalya Itsek who reportedly demanded that an independent investigation to that effect be carried out.

Itsek revealed that "at least 1000 experiments involving dangerous drugs are carried out each year on Palestinian prisoners without their knowledge."

The disclosure has not been completely denied by Israeli health ministry officials.

Ami Livnat, Head of Drugs Department at the Ministry of Health, reportedly admitted that "there has been a 15% increase in the number of experiments on inmates."

The shocking revelation drew sharp reactions from human rights groups, the detainees themselves and their families.

Saleh al Haj Yahya of the organization "Physicians for human rights," described the revelation as "appalling."

"If the report is proven true, then I won't find the words to describe our reactions towards it," said Yahya.

Yahya himself revealed that news of the scandal was initially leaked by a Likud activist whom he names as "Moti Areil." However, Yahya wondered why a "Likud stalwart would uncover a scandal as such."

Israeli officials: no comment

Israeli officials, including health officials, apparently sought to move swiftly to limit the damage and "not to make a big issue out of it."

Yahya told the Gaza-based weekly, al Risala, that he had tried in vain to contact Israeli health officials on the matter.

"I even tried to contact Dalya Itsek of the Labour Party who spoke about the subject in the Knesset, but to no avail," said Yahya.

Raji al Surani, head of the Palestinian Centre for Human Rights, also sought to contact Israeli officials on the matter but without success.

"No body can ignore a subject as such...It is a stigma of indescribable proportions." said Surani.

Evidence

Human rights organizations in the occupied territories have not been surprised. Representatives of these organizations readily point out that many of the released Palestinian prisoners suffer from chronic symptoms that had not existed prior to their detention. Indeed, the death of at least 45 Palestinians either in detention or soon after their release constitutes a prima facie evidence incriminating the Israelis. These deaths have not been investigated as they are routinely dismissed by the Israeli Prison Authority as "of natural causes."

Palestinian Authority caves in to Israeli ultimatum

Occupied Jerusalem -PT(14 July)

The Palestinian Authority moved swiftly Monday to "restore law and order" in downtown Hebron, thus budging to Israeli demands to that effect.

The PA deployed as many as two hundred uniformed policemen and half as many Preventive Security agents in plain-clothes to downtown Hebron in order to create a "buffer zone" between Palestinian demonstrators and Israeli settlers and soldiers.

Palestinian police sources said the decision to deploy the police force in downtown Hebron was made on Yasser Arafat's instructions, and was reached during a late-night meeting between Palestinian officers and their Israeli counterparts.

According to PA police chief, Tareq Zeid, the agreement stipulated that Palestinian police be responsible for maintaining law and order in H-1, an obvious euphemism for suppressing the anti-occupation protests in the city.

In return, the Israeli army reportedly agreed to remove the concrete slabs which had been placed in the middle of the Shallala street and paralyzed traffic between the Old town and the rest of Hebron.

Moreover, according to Zeid, the Israeli army agreed to end its siege on Hebron and surrounding villages, and to release Palestinian youngsters who were detained in connection with the recent unrest.

It is not clear if the Palestinian step to suppress street demonstrations in Hebron was a subjugation to Sunday's stern warnings by Israeli Prime Minister Benyamin Netanyahu who threatened a sever response in case "Palestinian riots continued."

More than 500 Palestinian youngsters were injured, some seriously, by Israeli rubber bullets and gun-shots during five weeks of unqualified confrontations between stone-hurling activists and heavily-armed Israeli soldiers.

Mixed Reactions

The abrupt intervention by the PA to "restore law and order " to Hebron drew mixed reactions in this embattled city.

Yousef Takrouri, a shoe-maker at the Bab el Zawiya commercial centre, criticized the PA for "betraying the blood of our youngsters."

"Look, they (the PA) encouraged the boys to take to the streets and to throw stones at Israeli soldiers, and now they return to business-as-usual with them," sighed Takrouri.

Another Hebronite was even more embittered.

"I won't resist the Israelis any longer because every time we carry out acts of resistance, we are undercut by the PA," said a Youngster who opted to keep his identity anonymous.

Other Hebronites welcomed the return of a semblance of normal life to a town that has suffered badly as a result of the enduring unrest.

"We have been placed for too long between the anvil and the hammer, we need a respite," said Hamed al Karaki, a Palestinian shopkeeper who was forced to close off by Israeli soldiers following the explosion of a home-made explosive device outside his shop last week.

The relative calm in Hebron is likely not to last for a long time to come. At best, it will be just a respite.

Looming explosion in the occupied territories

Hebron- PT (13 July)

The Israeli army has reinforced its 13-day siege on al Khalil (Hebron), in effect isolating the town from the outside world and cutting it from surrounding villages and hamlets.

On Saturday, eyewitnesses spoke of long columns of cars being stranded at Israeli checkpoints on roads to and from Hebron.

The scenes are a reminder of the situation that preceded the installment of Palestinian Authority administration in Hebron and a fresh proof that the PA is almost totally powerless in substance and now, apparently, in form as well.

The effective siege on Hebron was imposed nearly two weeks ago, ostensibly in response to continuing clashes in city-center, and also as a warning to the PA that all the cards in the area are in Israel's hands.

Indeed, Hebron citizens readily admit that the latest Israeli measures are reminiscent of the "old days" when the Israeli occupation was "direct, and un-vicarious."

They point out that the Israeli siege on Hebron, which has effectively reduced the town to a shut-in enclave, has demonstrated the utter futility of the Oslo Accords and subsequent agreements as it shows that Hebron is still an occupied town despite the presence of a 400-member police force.

This feeling of disenchantment is often expressed by Palestinians who are stuck, occasionally for hours, at Israeli roadblocks.

"The so-called liberated towns have suddenly become isolated ghettoes," complained Walid Suleiman, 35, of the nearby village of Dura, whom Israeli soldiers manning a roadblock just on Hebron northern entry refused to allow to proceed to his town 8 miles away.

"They told me that this was not their problem," said an embittered Suleiman who had to circumvent about 50 miles to reach Dura.

"This means that the days of the occupation were much better and that we have been victims of a sinister conspiracy," shrugged Suleiman in exasperation..

Meanwhile, the Israeli commander of the West Bank, Moshe Ya'alon, threatened to take stringent actions against Hebron citizen if the current unrest continued.

Speaking to the Israeli army radio on Saturday, Ya'alon warned the Palestinians that the occupation authorities would impose draconian measures on Hebron and its surroundings in case the riots didn't stop.

Ya'alon didn't spell out the contemplated actions, but observes believe they could include further restrictions on the current closure on Hebron and barring Palestinian workers from Hebron and district from reaching their jobs in Israel.

Moreover, the Israeli authorities could make harder Palestinian movement across the partition line between H-1 and H-2.

As for the PA, it is no secret that it is finding itself in an unenviable situation not only in respect to Israel but in the Palestinian Main Street as well.

In light, and given the failure of the Egyptian efforts to resume peace talks on the Palestinian-Israeli track, the PA must have realized by now that a sort of popular explosion is inevitable and necessary, if only to get out of the present deadlock.

As to when the explosion will take place, the next few weeks will tell.

Palestinians go on strike; Israeli soldiers kill

Arab youth, injure four children

Al Khalil (Hebron)- PT (2 July)

Most Palestinians in the Occupied Territories observed a two-hour general strike today to protest the anti-Islam posters pasted on Arab shopfronts on Saturday 28 June by Jewish settlers.

The strike paralyzed commercial life and brought public transportation in most Palestinian towns, including East Jerusalem, to a standstill.

In some areas, where the strike was not thoroughly heeded, uniformed Palestinian policemen urged citizens to stay indoor in deference of the strike call.

Meanwhile, violent confrontations between Palestinian protesters and heavily-armed Israeli soldiers continued unabatedly for the second consecutive day.

Eyewitnesses described today's disturbances as "the most violent in two weeks."

Palestinian sources said that 10 demonstrators, including four children, were injured by rubber bullets and taken to local hospitals for treatment.

A spokesman for the Amira Alya hospital in Hebron described the injuries of four demonstrators as serious.

There were no reports of casualties among Israeli soldiers.

On Tuesday, two Israeli soldiers were seriously wounded when an explosive device thrown by a Palestinian went off near an Israeli position along the demarcation line separating H-2 (under exclusive Israeli control) and H-1, which is administered by the Palestinian Authority.

An Israeli army spokesman said the Israeli side was viewing gravely Palestinian attempts to "change the rules of the game."

The spokesman said the army was "beefing up our forces in Hebron in response to Arab escalation."

In another development, Israeli soldiers shot and killed a Palestinian protester in Deir al Balah in the Gaza Strip and injured four others around noon Wednesday.

Palestinian sources said Israeli soldiers opened fire at a group of Palestinian land owners trying to protect their land from seizure by Jewish settlers.

Maher al Assar , 17, received a fatal wound to which he later succumbed at the Shifa'a hospital.

Observers opine that the Palestinian Authority is finding it increasingly difficult to quell public anger over Israeli provocations and what is often described as Benyamin Netanyahu's "horizon-narrowing policy" vis-¦-vis the Palestinians.

In fact, many Palestinians seem to be further enraged not only by PA powerlessness and inaction but also by its sycophancy to Israel.

On Tuesday, Israeli officials declared that the PA chairman Yaser Arafat had agreed to fully resume security and intelligence cooperation with Israel.

Palestinian officials, embarrassed by the disclosure, refused to comment.

One Palestinian official, who asked for anonymity, said "Abu Ammar (Arafat's nom de guerre) had no choice but to budge to American pressure."
