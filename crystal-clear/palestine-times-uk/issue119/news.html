Israeli torment of Palestinians continues
despite renewed contacts with PA

Occupied Jerusalem – From Khalid Amayreh

As Israel remembered “the holocaust” and celebrated “independence day,” her army continued to torment Palestinian civilians in a sustained effort to crush their seven-month uprising against Israel’s 34-year military occupation and to force them into submission.

Thus, and despite renewed security talks between the two sides, the killing of Palestinians, the destruction and demolition of their homes and the pulverization of their farms continued unabated, as the intifada is about to enter its eighth month, without showing signs of waning.

In April, the Israeli army murdered at least 25 Palestinians, including six children, four male adolescents and a Palestinian security officer who died after one week of wounds he sustained during heavy Israeli bombardment of Khan Younis in the central Gaza Strip.

In a particularly grisly incident on Wednesday, 25 April, Israeli occupation troops detonated a land-mine planted near the Salahuddin Gate in Rafah, at the southern tip of the Gaza Strip, killing four Palestinian civilians and injuring six, two of them seriously.

Palestinian sources described the killing as a “premeditated massacre.”

“This is not death in a cross-fire; this is not criminal negligence; this is cold-blooded murder perpetrated with malice aforethought,” said a Palestinian spokesman in reference to the incident.

Among the children murdered by the Neo-Nazis were two who were gunned down while returning home from school.

One child from Rafah was killed by an Israeli army sniper while returning from a math lesson at his teacher’s home not far from his own, having been unable to attend school due to continual Israeli strafing of the area.

In addition to the blood-letting, the Israeli army kept up its incursions into PA-controlled territories, particularly in the Gaza Strip.

However, unlike the brief reoccupation of a Palestinian position in northern Gaza during mid-April, which then drew strong international (including American) criticisms, forcing a prompt Israeli withdrawal, the occupation army is now adopting a slightly different tactic, based on the formula “go, destroy, and leave.”

This way, the Israeli political leadership hopes tactical goals can be achieved without inviting a strong international reaction. Indeed, this seems to be working to some extent, especially in the southern part of the Gaza Strip where Israeli troops entered PA-controlled territory twice during the last week of April, destroyed Palestinian security installations and withdrew immediately afterwards.

Meanwhile, the occupation army continued to carry out intermittent blanket bombardments of Palestinian population centres in the West Bank and the Gaza Strip. During the last week in April, an entire Palestinian family in Hebron narrowly escaped what could have been a terrible massacre.

Israeli tanks and artillery reportedly bombarded residential homes throughout the town, home to 170,000 Palestinians, with one shell landing on the veranda of the Abedeen family, ravaging their home and injuring everybody in the house.

“It was a miracle, a real miracle, that we were not killed. This is the real terror carried out by true terrorists,” said Ibrahim Abedeen, who was injured by shrapnel along with his wife and six children.

Bethlehem, Jericho and much of the Gaza Strip also came under heavy bombardment from Israeli tanks in which numerous houses were partially damaged or completely destroyed.

The Israeli army routinely claims that the often ferocious bombardment is in reaction to Palestinian firing toward Jewish settlements.

However, an objective examination of the situation on the ground reveals the utter falsehood of the Israeli claim.

“Whenever they hear a gunshot sound, even if it is 10 kilometres away, they immediately start bombarding us with tanks, artillery and heavy machine-guns,” said Mustafa Abdul Nabi Natshe of Hebron.

He adds, with frustration clear in the tone of his voice, that “their real aim is not to protect the settlers, but rather to kill the Palestinians and drive them away.”

Frustration fuels determination
This frustration, or collective powder keg, is not driving Palestinians away from their towns and villages, but is rather pushing at least some of them to carry out more attacks against Israelis and in the heart of Israel itself.

On Sunday, 22 April, a 17-year-old Palestinian schoolboy detonated explosives strapped to his waist near an Israeli bus in the Israeli Jewish town of K’far Saba, 15 miles north of Tel Aviv, killing himself, an Israeli man, and injuring scores of other Israelis–civilians and soldiers alike.

The following day, a car-bomb blast injured four Israelis in what the Israeli police described as a “terrorist act.”

Hamas took responsibility for the first blast while the small military wing of the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine (PFLP) took credit for the second.

The car-bomb attacks inside the Jewish State are likely to continue and even increase as the Israeli army continues to narrow Palestinian horizons further and further, all for the purpose of forcing the leadership to accept the fait accompli as decided and defined by Ariel Sharon.

Meanwhile, the Sharon government, staffed with the most hawkish of the hawks in the Jewish State, seems to be hell-bent on tampering with the extremely sensitive issue of Haram al-Sharif of Jerusalem.

On Friday, 20 April, scores of Israeli policemen armed with assault rifles mounted a show of force at the esplanade of the Islamic shrine.

Israeli officials claimed the provocative measure was directed against “stone throwers.” However, Palestinian waqf officials charged, with some justification, that the Israelis were only trying to test the limits of Muslim forbearance.

Last week, the right-wing Israeli President Moshe Katsav “suggested” that Jews and Muslims “share” the Haram al-Sharif compound.

Using a characteristically sly language, Katsav asked, “Why can’t there be arrangements at the Haram al-Sharif similar to those at the Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron?”

What Katsav failed to mention is the fact that the usurpation of the “partitioning” of the Ibrahimi Mosque in Hebron was carried out unilaterally by the Israeli army and neither the Palestinians nor Muslims at large ever accepted the effective Jewish take-over of the Mosque.

The Haram al-Sharif is considered one of the most sacred holy places in Islam, and Muslim officials have repeatedly warned that any Jewish encroachment on the holy site would spark an inextinguishable fire throughout the Middle East that no government would be able to put out.

It is unlikely though that Muslim warnings will deter Israeli extremists, who enjoy almost total support and backing from the Sharon government and the official Jewish establishment, from proceeding with their ominous designs against the Islamic shrine.

This means that the chances for a showdown are increasingly building up and menacingly moving toward a climax, one that many politicians dread even to imagine.

Rantisi: Hamas will continue mortar and other operations

Gaza - Dr. Abdul Aziz Rantisi, a prominent leader of the Islamic Resistance Movement, Hamas, in the Gaza Strip, has affirmed that Hamas would continue firing mortar shells in addition to launching martyrdom operations for the sake of liberating Palestine regardless of circumstances.

Responding to news on Palestinian Authority chairman’s orders to ban firing mortar shells, he said, “We are an Islamic resistance movement and our option is resistance. We have realized since the Madrid conference that our sole option for liberation was struggle, and our option has proven successful. Everybody now knows that any other option was a failure and that negotiations bore no fruit except more suffering for our people.”

Rantisi wondered, “If the mortar shells, as some like to publicize, did not bring about any benefit, why then are the Zionists panicking and America is pressuring to stop them? It is a very serious contradiction because the enemy is screaming as a result of the use of the shells…yes, maybe the casualties are very few, but they forced the enemy to take refuge in shelters for many days.”

The Hamas official explained that the mortar was the strongest deterrent weapon now available even though it inflicted few casualties.

Asked what would happen if the PA security bodies intercepted Hamas commando units launching mortar shells, he said, “We will not be dragged into a civil war, and we will continue resistance,” but he stressed, “We will use any available means to fight occupation.”

Meanwhile, a Zionist army report yesterday revealed that the Palestinian fighters had fired more than 100 mortar shells at Zionist targets since the start of this year, according to AFP.

The report said that one shell was fired in January, nine in February, 13 in March and 80 in April.

The report noted that all mortar shells were fired from the Gaza Strip and targeted military positions, settlements and towns within the entity.

Jewish Nazism flaunts itself in Hebron

Hebron – PT

Palestinian civilians in this Arab town of 180,000 are forced to fight a daily battle for survival against bands of virulently racist, Messianic Jewish settlers protected by thousands of often trigger-happy Israeli occupation soldiers.

Palestinians and many Israelis routinely label the settlers “Jewish Nazis,” to underscore their brash racism against everybody and everything that is not Jewish.

What is striking though is that many settlers in Hebron don’t feel offended by the term which some of them at least see as connoting the “fear and terror” which Jews ought to cast in the hearts of their enemies, in this case the Palestinians.

“Some of our tactics might be described as Nazi,” said Daniel Rider, a spokesman for the estimated 250-300 Jewish settlers in Hebron, but not without adding that “unlike German Nazism, our Nazism is ordained by God.”

Rider, who immigrated from New York to Israel 20 years ago, continues, “using harsh methods against one’s enemies is something commended in Judaism.”

“The Torah teaches us that we should have no mercy with the enemy; the concept of human rights in Judaism is entirely different from the same concept in Western liberal traditions.”

In Hebron, as elsewhere in the West Bank, the racism of the settlers is translated every day into blatant provocation against Palestinians and flagrant vandalism against their property.

On 26 April, for example, a band of settlers, including children and women, attacked a Palestinian family with stones and iron bars outside the small settlement of Kharsina, on Hebron’s northern suburbs.

The settlers shouted at the Arab family, “Get out of here…go away; this is a Jewish house.”

Now, as members of the Palestinian family sought to defend themselves against the obviously rabid settlers, Israeli army soldiers watching from a fair distance started firing machine-guns at the Palestinian house.

A few minutes later, many soldiers arrived at the scene to arrest the Palestinians for “disturbing security” and for “attacking Israeli citizens.”

The settlers, gleeful and content, returned to the Jewish enclave in the old town, apparently to plan another expedition.

Similarly, on Tuesday, 25 April, the settlers asked a Jewish woman to hurl stones at a group of Palestinians who were standing peaceably outside their stores, not far from the Ibrahimi Mosque.

And as always, and according to plan, the Palestinians try to stop the woman; the woman becomes even more rabid; then the soldiers arrive, beat up the Arabs, and declare the area a “closed military zone.” Mission accomplished.

Most of the Hebron settlers belong to the fascist Jewish movement known as Gush Emunim, which combines nationalism and religion.

The movement, represented in the Israeli Parliament, the Knesset, by seven MPs, believes that “all goyem (a derogatory Hebrew word for non-Jews which literally means beasts or animals) ought to be massacred, expelled or enslaved by the Jews.”

This virulent ideology is not confined to the theoretical sphere.

On 25 February, 1994, a member of Gush Emunim, a doctor named Baruch Goldstein, murdered 29 Palestinians while they were praying at dawn at the Ibrahimi Mosque.

The settlers praised the carnage and sought to immortalize the murderer, who was subsequently killed by Palestinian survivors, by building a memorial for him in Kiryat Arba’a.

The place became a pilgrimage site for Jews from San Francisco to Canberra, who come in throngs to be blessed by the grave of the “great Jewish saint and martyr.”

But, while Goldstein’s body is no longer around, his ideas, ideals and way of thinking are still very much alive among the settlers and their supporters, not only in Hebron but throughout Israel.

In fact, one wouldn’t overstate the fact by saying that virulent racism is today more rampant and more rooted among Jewish settlers.

Last March, Israeli settlers detonated explosives attached to a large gas canister that demolished an Arab building.

The terrorist act was reportedly perpetrated in full view of Israeli occupation soldiers, who made no efforts to stop the settlers.

The soldiers’ connivance with the settlers, an established reality, only gives credence to the Palestinian assertion that the “snake wouldn’t bite its tail.”

According to Palestinian sources as well as foreign observers from the Temporary International Presence in Hebron or TIPH, the real goal of the settlers is to drive away the estimated 34,000 Palestinians living in the old town so that the settlers might be able to build an all-Jewish town.

And the Israeli army, which often acts towards the Palestinians like the Nazi police (the Gestapo) did toward Jews in Germany in the 1930s and 1940s, does all it can to expedite the silent expulsion of the Palestinians from one of the most ancient Arab towns.

To achieve the sinister goal, the army adopts a combination of psychological and economic measures to torment the Palestinians, all designed to make life (Arab life) in the old town unbearable, so much so that the Palestinians would leave.

For example, since the outbreak of the Palestinian uprising nearly seven months ago, the Palestinians (not the settlers) have been under an intermittent debilitating curfew which forces men, women and children to stay indoors, occasionally for days and weeks.

The suffocating and psychologically tormenting curfews are interrupted by a respite lasting for one or two hours a week to enable the decimated Palestinians to buy food which many of them can’t afford to buy, given the town’s destroyed economy.

The situation in Hebron has now become even worse, even much worse, with the almost daily bombardment of Palestinian residential areas by Israeli tanks and gunners and the mounting casualties sustained consequently.

Last week, an Israeli tank shell landed on the home of Muhammed Abedin, damaging it beyond repair.

Luckily, this time a massacre was averted, and the family of seven sustained only moderate to medium injuries by the flying shrapnel.

The settlers, interestingly, welcome the rising violence. They think it will accelerate the coming of the Messiah.

Palestinian scholars reject Saudi Mufti’s
view on martyrdom operations

Occupied Jerusalem - Muslim ulema or religious scholars in Palestine have disputed a view by the Mufti of Saudi Arabia expressing reservations about the “the compatibility of martyrdom operations with Islamic tenets.”

The Saudi mufti, Abdul Aziz Ibn Abdullah al-Sheikh was reported as saying that “as far as I know, these operations are not validated by the shariah (the Muslim code of Islamic law).”

“It depends on how the question was worded to him; the man is not well acquainted with the Palestinian situation,” said Mustafa Shawar, an Islamic scholar in Hebron.

He pointed out that there was a fundamental difference between “suicide” and martyrdom.

“Suicide is the result of hopelessness and despair; it is something psychological; while martyrdom is a wish to sacrifice one’s soul for a noble cause for the sake of God,” he said.

For his part, the famous Egyptian religious scholar and propagator Dr. Yousef al-Qaradawi said that the martyrdom operations were not suicide.

He emphasized that such operations in the light of the present circumstances were a religious duty on all those capable of performing them.

Qaradawi called on the youth in Palestine to launch martyrdom operations without any hesitation as long as they were the only means available to launch Jihad and to offer one’s life for the sake of raising high one’s religion and Nation.

Dr. Hamdi Murad, professor of Islamic Shariah at the Jordanian Balqa’ University said that the martyrdom operations were considered Jihad for the sake of Allah as long as they were serving right and deterring oppression.

He affirmed that such operations were the highest degree of sacrifice, adding, “we might resort to martyrdom operations if the results of which proved to be better than other methods and if the enemy was better equipped and militarily superior to us.”

“All means are legitimate for the sake of ending oppression, including the infliction of losses in the ranks of the enemy, which would include martyrdom operations, especially when other means failed to end the aggression,” he elaborated.

“We hail such operations when they serve as a deterrent to that arrogant enemy and in the absence of other weapons,” the professor pointed out.

Meanwhile, Israel’s Chief Ashkenazi Rabbi Yisrael Meir Israel Lau has condemned Muslim ulema or religious scholars for supporting martyrdom operations against Israeli targets.

Lau made his remarks at a K’far Saba hospital last month where he visited several Israeli civilians and soldiers injured in a martyrdom operation in K’far Sava on 22 April. One Jewish settler was killed and scores of others injured in the incident, along with the Palestinian martyr who carried out the attack.

“There is nothing more repugnant than telling young people that they will go to heaven if they die fighting Jews,” Lau, who holds extremely racist attitudes against non-Jews in general and Palestinians in particular, was quoted as saying.

Lau suggested that Palestinians shouldn’t resist Jewish oppressors “because the latter happen to be God’s chosen people.”

A Muslim scholar from the Hebron area retorted saying, “The moment the rabbi stops viewing non-Jews as sheep and goats in human form to be slaughtered and enslaved, Muslims will reconsider their views…we are waiting to see if he will.”

Last March, Lau tacitly approved virulently racist statements by Shas mentor Ovadia Yosef in which Yosef, a former chief rabbi, called for the extermination of all Arabs whom he referred to as “an evil race.”

Yosef’s remarks were not widely condemned by Jewish religious leaders or even by the Israeli political establishment.

Some Israeli officials described the remarks as “bad public relations,” but not racist.

Lau and the other chief rabbi representing Sephardi Jews (Jews originating in the Middle East) have refused to denounce the genocidal actions of the Israeli occupation troops against Palestinians, including the uprooting of vast tracts of Palestinian trees.

The Torah makes explicit injunctions against uprooting trees, which suggests that the rabbis place political expediency first and religious idealism second.

Jewish settlers spread terror in
Hebron in collusion with army

Hebron (West Bank) – Special to PT

The Israeli army dubs their wanton terror against innocent and unprotected Palestinians as merely “peaceful protests,” a malicious misrepresentation of reality underscoring the often criminal collusion between Israeli occupation troops and violent Jewish settlers against Palestinian civilians.

The settlers, heavily armed and indoctrinated in a virulently racist ideology, have been on the rampage in Hebron lately with the aim of terrorizing Palestinians and vandalizing their property.

On 2 April, settler terror took a turn for the worse when seemingly armed rabid settlers, in full view of Israeli soldiers, brought in and detonated a huge gas canister attached to a highly explosive substance inside a Palestinian-owned business in the Sahla neighbourhood, not far from the Ibrahimi Mosque.

The powerful blast rocked Hebron, utterly destroying the Palestinian store and irreparably damaging a three-storey building.

The settlers proved that they were thieves besides being terrorists, as they looted all the merchandise at the store and other businesses in the area.

And, as always, none of the perpetrators were arrested, a message construed by the settlers as a tacit approval from the army and the Sharon government to carry out further acts of terror and vandalism against Palestinians with impunity, of course.

For its part, the Israeli army, instead of arresting the terrorists, turned to public relations and misinformation in order to extenuate the damage done to Israel’s image.

An army spokesman claimed it was investigating “whether or not the Jews were involved in the incident,” a carefully concocted statement that is apparently designed to cloud settler crimes and deceive international public opinion.

There are close to 3,000 Israeli troops stationed in the old town of Hebron, whose total area does not exceed 1 square kilometre, and it would be highly unlikely that the settlers could do what they have been doing without being observed by the soldiers.

“It is impossible for a rat to move in the old town without being observed by the Israeli army,” said one Palestinian, scoffing at the army’s claim that it didn’t see the settlers embark on their crime.

Earlier, and after deliberate postponement lasting for five days, the parents of a Jewish settler toddler, who was accidentally killed in late March during an exchange of fire between Israeli occupation troops and Palestinian resistance fighters, agreed to bury their daughter.

The parents and fellow settlers had vowed not to bury the little child until the Israeli army “re-conquers or bombs-out” the Abu Sneineh neighbourhood, from which the settlers alleged the shot that killed the baby was fired.

Several Israeli officials, including Prime Minister Ariel Sharon and Chief Rabbi Yisrael Meir Lau, sought to convince the settlers to bury the child quickly in accordance with Jewish traditions.

According to an Israeli journalist covering the funeral, the Israeli army commander in Hebron, Arnon Shimon, sought to comfort the bereaved family by telling them, “We have killed ten Arab children for your daughter.”

The mother of the toddler reportedly said, “No! ten is not enough; I want a thousand Arab children killed for my daughter; all Arabs are not worth a Jew’s fingernail.”

The Israelis actually killed 12 rather than 10 Palestinians in five days, including 11-year-old Mahmoud Darawish, who was riddled with bullets on 27 March, a few hours after the settler child was killed, while sitting in his living room watching television in Dura, 12 kilometres south-west of Hebron.

The Israeli media, including the State-run radio, made no mention of Darawish, or Lauay Tamimi, another 11-year-old Palestinian child who died on 1 April of wounds inflicted earlier by an Israeli army sniper near Ramallah.

Predictably, the burial of the child received unusually ample coverage by the Israeli media and the Zionist-influenced American television networks which normally barely give any attention to the even more tragic Palestinian deaths wrought by Israeli soldiers and settlers.

Seeking to exploit the tragic death, the Israeli foreign ministry even instructed Israeli diplomatic representatives around the world to highlight the incident and manipulate it to the hilt in order to besmirch the Palestinians’ image.

The right-wing Israeli paper, the Jerusalem Post, went as far as describing the incident as “a little holocaust.”

Similar corrupt analogies and hyperbolic labels were also made by Israeli commentators, not only in Israel but in North America, where the Israeli government recently began a huge public relations blitz aimed at enhancing Israel’s image.

The Israeli government and media maintained that the incident was deliberate, and that a Palestinian sharpshooter deliberately targeted the baby, a claim that seems to have not an iota of credibility.

However, a report by the Jewish human rights group “Gush Shalom,” released on 1 April, stated that “there is not the slightest shred of evidence suggesting that the killing of the child was done on purpose.”

Since the death of the settler, the Israeli army has been intermittently bombarding Palestinian neighbourhoods on a daily basis, using artillery and heavy machine-guns, and inflicting widespread havoc and destruction.

One of the buildings directly hit was a clinic at the Abu Sneineh neighbourhood, an indisputable fact belying Israeli army claims that it only fires at “terrorist targets.”

According to Hebron mayor Mustafa Abdul Nabi Natshe, over 2,000 buildings in Hebron have been hit and damaged by Israeli artillery bombardment since the Palestinian intifada erupted more than six months ago.

“Their aim is to inflict as much destruction as possible; they just want to prove to us that they are the powerful party and that we should come to terms with this reality.”

Following the incident, the Zionist army brought in to Hebron three tanks and directed its large guns toward Palestinian neighbourhoods. The tanks were not for show, for as soon as the Jewish child was buried, the tanks fired salvos of artillery and machine-guns at the Abu Sneineh and Takruri neighbourhoods.

There are 250-300 Jewish settlers living in Hebron’s Old Town among the local Palestinian inhabitants totalling 170,000.

The settlers belong to the Gush Emunim movement, which believes that all non-Jews ought to be expelled from “the Biblical Land of Israel”…and if they refuse, then they ought to be massacred.

On 2 April, Uri Avnery, one of the last remaining voices of moderation and common sense in Israel, called on the Sharon government to remove the settlers from Hebron.

“Hebron is a Palestinian town,” Avnery wrote, “and the only way to maintain peace and stability in the city is by removing the settlers from it, once and for all.”

However, as expected, Avnery’s words fell on deaf ears.

“The Jewish presence in Hebron will remain forever,” retorted Sharon.

Open Letter to Crown-Prince Abdullah Ibn Abdulaziz

Your Highness: Assalamu alaikum wa rahmatullah.

After some hesitation, I’ve decided to write to you publicly about the Palestinian plight and the shocking failure of the Arab-Muslim Umma (community) to offer real tangible support for the struggling Palestinian people against Jewish-Zionist terror.

Your highness, the gruesome atrocities we are facing, the wanton destruction of property, the suffocating siege, the sinister torment are all too real to be described in words. Indeed, whatever finds its way to the screens of Arab and foreign televisions communicates only a small part of the reality.

In short, we are facing a real genocide, a holocaust in the making, which often transcends reality.

Your highness: the Palestinian people, with God’s help, are still steadfast and resisting the barbarian Jews with whatever meagre tools are at their disposal.

But that steadfastness is not without a price, and its continuity can’t be guaranteed without truly active Arab-Muslim support, which, unfortunately, hasn’t been forthcoming.

Your highness, the price we have been paying for the last seven months is, as always, in blood, the blood of our children, whom the criminal Zionist barbarians target in order to destroy us psychologically.

Yes, the barbarians deliberately and intentionally target our kids, who might be playing in their backyards, walking in the street, or returning home from school, in order to make us depressed and break us from within, all for the purpose of defeating us and forcing us to surrender.

And in addition to murdering nearly 500 innocent civilians so far–a third of whom are children below the age of 14–the barbarians effectively destroyed our fields, burned our trees and demolished many of our homes. They ravaged our economy so much so that mere survival has become our sole preoccupation.

In short, the barbarians are doing to us what the Nazis had done to them, although we are innocent of any crimes against them save our desire to be free from their terror and domination.

Your highness, this overwhelming terror is leaving a devastating effect on all of us, including our children who ask bitterly and helplessly, “Why are we alone? Where are our Arab brothers? Where are our Muslim brothers? Why don’t they come to our aid?”

As a Muslim talking to a Muslim, I believe it is imperative to remind your highness of the saying of our Prophet Mohammed (peace be upon him) that “a Muslim is brother to his fellow Muslim; he mustn’t betray him, he mustn’t deliver him into the hands of the enemy.”

Yes, we know that Arab and Muslim States have held several meetings in which they decided to offer the Palestinians some money. And we know of the many other decisions and empty rhetoric which can only enforce a feeling of helplessness and impotence in the ranks of the umma.

Your highness, what the Palestinian people and the Aqsa Mosque badly need these days is concrete political action, not money.

And we truly believe that the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, as indeed the rest of the Arab and Muslim States, is capable of delivering that kind of support if and when there is the political will on the part of officials in the Kingdom.

You know, your highness, that the terrorist and war criminal Sharon wouldn’t be doing what he is doing to the Palestinians as I write these words were it not for the unlimited and unconditional military, political and economic support Criminal Israel is receiving from the United States.

It is the American support, connivance, encouragement and protection that feed Zionist insolence, murder and aggression. America, your highness, is the ultimate tormentor of the Palestinian people; it is our executive killer.

Your highness, the Aqsa Mosque is in danger; the Jews are only waiting for the opportune time to destroy it and make it a thing of the past, and the stream of our children’s blood is flowing, even overflowing, while the Americans are brashly flying in our faces by shielding the Zionist entity with their veto power and by refusing to allow the deployment of international observers in Palestine.

Your highness, the U.S.–Israel’s ally–needs Saudi Arabia more than Saudi Arabia needs the United States. The planes, the cars and the merchandise you purchase from the U.S. can always be purchased from other countries that are less hostile to Muslims and their just causes. And your oil can be sold elsewhere if the Americans stop buying Saudi oil. In the final analysis, “the noble woman would rather starve than breast-feed for money!”

Hence, in the name of the bereaved mothers, in the name of the murdered children whose tender bodies were riddled with Zionist bullets, in the name of Muhammed Durra and Faris Oudeh, and the other 100 children murdered by the disciples of Hiter, we call on you, we appeal to you, we implore you to reconsider Saudi ties with the United States.

Your highness, the Palestinian people are appealing to you to tell the Americans that they can’t have it both ways: enable Israel to kill us and at the same time continue to enjoy the benefits of its strategic and economic relations with the Arabs.

Please, tarry not in protecting the honour of the umma.

May God give you strength to do what is right.

Ahmad Abed

Sharon to create more “facts” in the West Bank

Occupied Jerusalem- From Khalid Amayreh
Despite claims to the contrary, the Sharon government has apparently endorsed plans to create at least one large Jewish settlement south of Jerusalem and significantly expand a number of existing settlements throughout the West Bank.

According to the Hebrew press, the Israeli housing ministry has already prepared plans for building a new Jewish settlement that would include 6,000 flats in an area located between Bethlehem and Hebron.

The new settlement, to be called Giva’ot, would be inhabited by Jews from the “national-religious camp,” considered to be among the most racist and anti-Arab of Jews anywhere.

If built, the new settlement would further truncate the southern part of the West Bank, bisecting it into two main enclaves cut off from each other and lacking territorial continuity with the Ramallah region in the central West Bank.

In addition to the planned settlement, a Jerusalem municipal committee has approved new plans to build another 3,000 apartments on confiscated Arab land at Jabal Abu Ghneim (Har Homa).

The governments of Benyamin Netanyahu and Ehud Barak had already constructed hundreds of settler units at Jabal Abu Ghneim on Jerusalem’s southern suburbs, apparently in order to complete the chain of Jewish settlements around occupied East Jerusalem.

The political aim of this settlement is clear, namely to render impossible the return of land to the Palestinians.

Meanwhile, the grabbing of Palestinian land by armed Jewish settlers continued unabated throughout the West Bank.

Palestinian sources reported earlier this month that Jewish settlers were seizing land in the vicinity of their settlements throughout the West Bank, cordoning it off with barbed wire, all with the Israeli army’s tacit approval.

In Hebron, for example, the Israeli army confiscated a large expanse of Palestinian farmland adjacent to the former armistice line of 1967.

The owners of the land, peasants from the nearby village of Beit Kahel, organized a sit-in on the land on 26 March to protest the illegal seizure. However, Israeli soldiers stationed at the site threatened to shoot the villagers if they didn’t disperse.

Israeli army bulldozers were similarly busy this week pulverizing Palestinian vineyards and olive groves south of the town of Dura, 10 miles south-west of Hebron.

There the Israeli army is opening another bypass road that would link the tiny settlement of Otnael, inhabited by no more than 30 settler families, with inter-city roads in southern Israel more than 15 miles away.

The opening of the road, and the accompanying brutality of destroying Palestinian fields on which villagers depend for their livelihood, underscores the brutal ugliness of the Zionist mentality and the extent to which the Sharon regime is willing to go to torment the Palestinians for the sake of guaranteeing the welfare of a handful of settlers.

And as the expansion of settlements continues, so do the often murderous attacks of Jewish settlers against Palestinian civilians throughout the occupied territories.
