Grim Prognosis

One need not be a prophet of doom and gloom to testify to the virtual death of the "peace process" between Israel and the Palestinians. The Americans (the conspiring sponsor), the Israelis (the arrogant aggressor) and Yasser Arafat (the vanquished supplicant) have all come to recognize the demise and death of the peace process.

Indeed, Netanyahu, the megalomaniac Israeli leader who thinks he is the greatest man on earth, left no doubts as to his prognosis of that process. On 22 May, he told the diplomatic corps in Jerusalem that "as far as I'm concerned, the peace process died two years ago...even before I became Prime Minister."

For its part, the American administration, the only remaining hope for breathing life into the process, seems to be so terrified by the powerful Jewish lobby in Washington, D.C. that the President of the United States et al. will not dare to formally spell out the general outlines of the latest talked-about American initiative, which was accepted by Arafat and utterly rejected by Benyamin Netanyahu.

As to Yasser Arafat and those who gave him the benefit of the doubt, it is now amply clear that the "dream" has turned into a nightmare, a big nightmare. Needless to say, Arafat must have found by now, although somewhat belatedly, that he has been cheated, betrayed and deceived by the Americans and the Israelis alike, and also by some Arab leaders who encouraged him to make the White House his ultimate Qibla (House of Worship).

But Arafat has only himself to blame. Indeed, it was a sign of utter political naïveté to capitalize on American "evenhandedness." America, after all, is par excellence the tormentor of the Palestinian People, and Palestinians have never had "a true friend at the White House," as Arafat wantonly claimed on several occasions.

Moreover, Arafat has, probably willfully, heeded American-Israeli instructions and dictates to persecute and repress a good many of his fellow-Palestinians merely for speaking out against the Oslo sell-out. Very often, appeasing the Americans and satiating Zionist thirst for Palestinian blood seemed to be "the policy" that Arafat adopted in the vain hope that this would make the Israelis relent and hand over more territory to him in the West Bank.

Even now, as the moral and political bankruptcy of Arafat's policy becomes manifestly clear, the Palestinian Authority is still incarcerating hundreds of Islamist activists on no other account than pleasing the Americans in order to qualify for a White House certificate of good conduct.

Unfortunately, Arafat has a long history of sacrificing the vital interests of his people, thinking that this would at one point help the Palestinian cause. Thus, he accepted the Hebron Agreement, which was "a take it or leave it offer" solely structured to serve the security and economic interests of the Israeli occupier at the expense of the Palestinians.

Furthermore, he lightly agreed to hold virtually all aspects of the Palestinians' individual and collective lives hostage to Israeli security whims, an act amounting to treachery.

Notwithstanding, Arafat, along with those Arab leaders who pushed him to launch the "peace offensive" despite all considerations to the contrary, are yet to awaken to the full consequences of their disastrous miscalculations.

The Zionist leader Netanyahu has declared solemnly that the River Jordan will always be Israel's eastern border, meaning that the Zionist State would never allow the creation of a Palestinian State west of the river.

This is not rhetoric. The West Bank, the supposed home of Arafat's State-in-the-dream, is now swarming with Jewish settlements so much so that very little room is left for a viable or semi-viable Palestinian political entity. And as for Jerusalem, which Arafat wishes (rather than hopes) to make the capital of his would-be Palestinian State, it has been nearly completely Judaized and encircled by settlements in every direction. Hence, it is unrealistic to give any credence or credibility to Arafat's recent avowals that he will formally declare the establishment of "the Palestinian State" by 4 May 1999, when the interim phase is due to end.

Hence, in light of the imposing facts on the ground, it is completely safe to label Arafat's proclamations as no more than "rhetorical overindulgence, wishful thinking and without substance."

This should bring us to the crucial question of "what is next?" In order to answer this question honestly, one would have to obtain an accurate prognosis of the state of affair in Palestine, the Arab-Muslim world and the world at large. To begin with, one must come to terms with the fact that it is already too late to achieve a real peaceful settlement between Israel and the Palestinian people. The "land-for-peace formula", the essence of the peace process, has been rendered unworkable, to say the least, for the simple reason that the "land", e.g. the West Bank, is no longer "returnable" to the Palestinians, given the new demography.

Moreover, it is highly unlikely that the United States or the EU countries will go, at least in the foreseeable future, as far as pressuring Israel, e.g. to "comply with UN resolutions," for the former is virtually in the Zionists' pockets and the latter are yet to recover from their enduring "guilty conscience" vis-à-vis the Jews.

This leaves the Palestinians, the Arabs and Muslims, with one and only one choice: Resistance in its various and multifaceted forms. A strategy of resistance must rest on the conviction that Palestine in its entirety is an Arab-Muslim land that was occupied by the Zionists by sheer military force and that sooner or latter it will have to be recovered in full. No one harbours any illusion about the long duration of this historical conflict between Zionist invaders and Muslims. Likewise, no one should be deceived into thinking that resistance would force the Zionists to capitulate in a matter of a few years. The battle will be bloody, bitter and above all long, and many generations will likely pass away before seeing Palestine back where it eternally belongs.
