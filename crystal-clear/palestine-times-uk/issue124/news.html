Israel marks Intifada anniversary with fresh killings

Occupied Jerusalem � From Khalid Amayreh

As Palestinians throughout Palestine observed on 28 September the first anniversary of their intifada (uprising) against Israeli occupation and apartheid, trigger-happy Israeli troops killed at least seven more Palestinian civilians, including a ten-years-old child in a Hebron village.

Palestinian sources said Muhammed Tarayreh, of the village of Bani Naim, 10 kilometres southeast of Hebron, was killed when Israeli troops fired indiscriminately on Palestinian boys in the western part of the village. Five more boys were reported wounded, one seriously.

In Hebron, Israeli occupation soldiers stationed on rooftops strafed the city centre with machine-gun fire, shedding still more blood and terrorizing the civilian population. A 25-year-old youth, named Imam Muhammed Sharif, was killed when occupation troops opened fire on Palestinian worshippers who were just leaving the Ali Bakka Mosque following Friday's congregational prayer.

Hospital sources in Hebron said 20 people were injured, including a young man identified as Zakariya Abu Rajab, who reportedly sustained serious-to-critical gunshot wounds in the head and neck.

In Bethlehem to the north, Israeli occupation troops opened fire on a peaceful march marking the first anniversary of the intifada, killing a teenager named Muhammed Sukkar.

A fourth Palestinian from a Ramallah village also died of wounds he sustained earlier. The boy was identified as Muhsen Arar and his family said he was shot deliberately by an Israeli sniper without any provocation.

"He was killed because he was Palestinian."

In the Gaza Strip, an Israeli tank fired an artillery shell on a Palestinian civilian car near Rafah on Friday night (28 September) killing at least three people. The latest killing brought to 13 the number of Palestinian victims of Israeli-State terror in a 48-hour time span.

A hundred other civilians were also injured and maimed by merciless Israeli forces, among them a two-year-old child from Rafah who, hospital sources said, sustained a serious wound in the face.

Meanwhile, thousands of Palestinians took to the streets in many cities in the West Bank and Gaza Strip, vowing to keep up the struggle until "deliverance from the shackles of Jewish occupation and apartheid."

Thousands headed to cemeteries to pay their respects for the martyrs and lay wreaths, while thousands assembled in a Gaza stadium to listen to Hamas spiritual leader Sheikh Ahmed Yasin, who vowed that the struggle against apartheid and occupation would continue.

"We have no choice but to continue the struggle; our enemies want us to accept apartheid and come to terms with perpetual military occupation. We will not. Nobody else the world over would. The right to resist oppression is guaranteed by man and God alike, and we will uphold this right," said Yasin before thousands of his followers.

He added that, "Our struggle is not against Jews as Jews; it is against oppression, occupation and apartheid."

Yasin recognized that the Palestinian people were paying a dear price for their struggle, but added "if we don't pay the price for freedom, we will pay a hundred-fold as much in terms of humiliation, enslavement, torment and persecution."

He lashed out at "this hypocritical world that views terrorism selectively."

"When Jewish forces murder Palestinian children in cold blood, and when Palestinian towns and villages are reduced to concentration camps, the world is silent and refuses to condemn the murder and terror, but when the victims are of a different color, or citizens of a powerful State, then the whole world is mobilized to fight the great menace."

 Victims are mostly civilians

Moreover, the barbarians demolished and utterly destroyed nearly a thousand homes, and Israeli bulldozers ravaged as many as 300,000 Palestinian-owned olive and fruit trees as well as vast tracts of verdant fields.

The former British Foreign Secretary Ian Gilmore, who visited the West Bank and Gaza in June, described the havoc and destruction the Israelis inflicted on the Palestinian environment as "an affront to civilization."

Furthermore, the Israeli army used virtually all lethal weapons of death at its disposal, including, inter alia, cluster bombs, explosive bullets, pin-shaped bullets designed to cause maximum damage to demonstrators and many kinds of asphyxiating gases, euphemistically called "tear gas."
