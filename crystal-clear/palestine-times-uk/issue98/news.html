Hamas accuses PA and Israel of besmirching its image

Occupied Jerusalem – PT

The Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) accused the Palestinian Authority and Israel of launching a smear campaign against the movement.

The accusation followed a series of articles of unknown origin published by the London-based journal al-Watan al-Arabi in early July and circulated in a number of anti-Islamic papers in several Arab countries.

The articles, which were attributed to “confidential sources within Hamas” alleged that there was a serious rift within the Islamist movement between the “Misha’al faction” and the “Abu Marzuk faction” and also hinted that Hamas leaders embezzled millions of dollars.

Furthermore, the articles claimed that Hamas was seeking to dominate the leadership of the Muslim Brotherhood movement in Jordan.

The publication of the said articles coincided with a vitriolic smear campaign against Hamas in the PA newspapers in the self-rule areas.

Two journalists working for a PA Security agency, Muhammed Daraghmeh of the daily al-Ayyam, and Tawfik Abu Khusa of al-Hayatul Jadida, wrote several pieces in which they alleged that Hamas was undergoing a serious power struggle and even claimed that the very survival of the movement was at stake.

Hamas vehemently denied the accusations, describing them as “concocted, politically motivated, cowardly and completely and absolutely baseless.”

Hamas official spokesman, Ibrahim Ghoshe, challenged the writers who fabricated the articles to identify themselves and their sources, arguing that it was likely that the writers were agents of the Palestinian Authority and the Zionist regime.

“These lies are motivated by hatred for Hamas and by a desire on the part of sources of these lies to besmirch Hamas’ image and weaken the movement in the service of the Zionist regime,” Ghoshe was quoted as saying.

He argued that the “mendacious information were disseminated by suspicious agencies full of hatred for Hamas.”

Ismael Abu Shanab, an Islamist leader in the Gaza Strip, also dismissed the articles as “sheer fabrications and a propaganda war aimed at discrediting the movement for its principled opposition of the Oslo process.”

He pointed out that the fabrications and concoctions were made by the same people “who are hounding Hamas and incarcerating its supporters,” an obvious allusion to Israel and Yasser Arafat’s autonomous regime.

Abu Shanab stressed that the talk about embezzlements within Hamas was “absolutely untrue.”

“I challenged our accusers to produce a single piece of evidence supporting their claims,” he said.

Abu Shanab, nevertheless, advised that “we shouldn’t give ample attention to these fabrications, because Hamas is always targeted for defamation by its numerous enemies.”

On 17 July, a leaflet believed to be distributed by Hamas in the Hebron area renewed accusations against the PA and Israeli intelligence of “spreading disinformation about Hamas.”

“The corruption-ridden Palestinian Authority and the Zionist regime are seeking to besmirch the name of Hamas because Hamas is the last line of defense in the face of the ongoing conspiracy to liquidate the Palestinian cause,” reported the leaflet.

The leaflet said Hamas was as united as ever and even though differences of opinion appear among the movement’s leaders, they are settled through the Islamist democratic institutions.

The leaflet described the “war of camps” within Hamas, as alleged in the articles, as “malicious, sinister and absolutely and completely without essence.”

Moreover, the leaflet challenged the PA to produce any evidence of any financial irregularity of any kind within Hamas.

“Our people don’t squander the people’s money in the Tahuna bars, nor on Russian girls, nor on building red-tiled villas and purchasing smart cars,” said the leaflet.

Finally, the leaflet pointed out that this was not the first time Hamas was targeted by hostile intelligence agencies to besmirch its image in the vain hope that this would weaken the movement.

“Hamas will continue to be a thorn in the side of all conspirators and quislings whose treacherous deeds have become manifest to our people.”

The PA has refused to comment on the matter, but its two dailies (al-Hayatul Jadida and al-Ayyam) featured prominently the accusations published in al-Watan al-Arabi.

Israeli human rights group admits
Israel is racist toward non-Jews

Occupied Jerusalem- The Association of Civil Rights in Israel (ACRI), a human rights organization monitoring the human and civil rights situation in Israel proper, has charged that the Zionist regime adopts and employs "racist standards and measures against Israeli citizens who are not Jewish."

The Association pointed out during a press conference in Jerusalem on 21 July that discrimination against non-Jews was ubiquitous, adding that the rights of Palestinians, who are citizens of Israel, were grossly violated.

"There is still discrimination between Israeli Arabs and Jews and the rights of Palestinians are grossly violated."

According to ACRI director Vered Livne, Israeli policy toward the issue of human rights for non-Jews either stood still or even regressed.

Livne said the worst human-rights violators were the Interior Ministry, adding that many other ministries were also involved in institutionalized violation of the human and civil rights of non-Jews, including the labour, social and defense ministries.

ACRI director, Prof. Yael Tamir, pointed out that over the last few years, the Israeli interior ministry conducted a starkly discriminatory policy against non-Jews in many fields, including family reunification, residency rights, marriage certificates and the granting of citizenship.

Tamir cited the ministry's policy toward Israeli Arabs who marry Palestinians from the West Bank and Gaza Strip, who, she said, are forced to live apart from their spouses for many years because Israeli regulations don't encourage more non-Jews to become Israeli citizens.

However, the most flagrant form of human rights violations that are deliberately committed by the Israeli interior ministry took place in East Jerusalem.

There, the ACRI said, thousands of residency rights were confiscated from Palestinians.

This form of ethnic cleansing has so far victimized as many as 4,000 Palestinian citizens of Jerusalem, and human rights organizations and civil rights groups admit there is no end in sight for that policy.

At the political level, confiscation of Jerusalemites' residency rights is aimed at reducing the Arab population of the town to the absolute minimum.

According to Palestinian sources, Israeli interior ministry patrols routinely scour the streets of Jerusalem, hounding Arab youths in an effort to strip them of their identity cards and residency rights.

At the bottom of this sinister policy and wicked practice is the racist Jewish desire to make Jerusalem "ultimately clean of Goyem," or non-Jews. The policy of withdrawing residency rights from Palestinians is coupled with another equally-sinister policy against the Arab population, that is the routine practice of home demolition.

Israel is extremely parsimonious in granting Arabs building licenses in Jerusalem (for the above-cited reasons). This often forces Palestinians to build houses without receiving a license beforehand, prompting the occupation authorities to send bulldozers to demolish the houses.

ACRI chief legal counsel Dan Yakir emphasized the violation of human rights in the Palestinian sector, including the widespread use of physical and psychological torture and violence against Palestinian detainees by General Security Service interrogators. Needless to say, the use of torture in Israel is officially sanctioned by the law, making the Jewish State the only country all over the world to accept torture as a legitimate means during questioning of suspects.

Yakir called on the new government of Ehud Barak to pass a law banning the use of torture against detainees, saying the High Court should hand down a "clear, explicit ruling that torture is unacceptable."

Yakir's call, however, is expected to fall on deaf ears whether in the political or in the judicial establishment. The reason is simple: Israel is a particularistic, racist Jewish State where non-Jews are treated as "children of a lesser God."

Arafat refuses to free Islamist internees lest he upset Israel, CIA

Occupied Jerusalem – From Khalid Amayreh

The continued illegal detention of hundreds of Palestinian citizens without charge or trial by Yasser Arafat's Palestinian self-rule authority is still poisoning the general political atmosphere in the occupied territories. The illegal incarceration, for which there seems to be no end in sight, is not only taking a heavy toll on the detainees' immediate families and relatives (who suffer most), but is leaving indelible scars on the relations between the masses and the autonomous authority.

Indeed, the adamant refusal by Arafat (the sole decision maker in the PA regime) to release the political activists, or bring them before a court of law, allows for no other assumption save that the Chairman is not answerable to his people� whose rights and freedoms he is supposed to safeguard and protect� but rather to Israeli and American intelligence services, which insist that the Chairman violate his people’s human and civil rights as "a price" for continued American and Israeli backing for his "police State without a State." (Incidently, Arafat has always valued the legitimacy that comes from international acceptance more than that which comes from his people's acceptance.)

In fact, it often seems that even Arafat himself is unable to free those people, as the Palestinian Human Rights Monitoring Group (PHRMG) pointed out at a Jerusalem press conference earlier in July. The PHRMG revealed that according to the Oslo Accords and the Wye agreement, Arafat is demanded to act at the Israeli intelligence's beck and call in matters pertaining to security, including the "apprehension, detention and punishment of people considered security risks." In other words, Arafat must act as an additional, extraordinary or vicarious Israeli repressive arm against his people. Unfortunately, this seems to be just what he has been doing.

Total number of “hostages” unknown
The number of Islamist “hostages” (this is the term used by most Palestinians within the autonomous enclaves to describe detainees) is not exactly known since Arafat's various and often competing security agencies continue to "round up" new detainees at Israel’s instructions. (All the Israelis have to do is hand the PA a list of names of individuals that ought to be placed behind bars.) Moreover, there are full-time detainees, solitary-confinement detainees, part-time detainees, on-time/off-time detainees, administrative detainees and so on. None the less, it is generally believed that between 600-800 mostly Islamist detainees are interned in PA custody, the vast majority of whom are detained indefinitely without knowing when they will be set free.

The inmates are kept in the former Israeli Military Authority jails in Nablus (Jeneid), in Hebron (al-Imara), in Jericho (al-Mukata'a) and in three or four prisons in Gaza. Indeed, the first impression one would get on visiting these symbols of oppression is that the Israelis are still there, albeit with Palestinian faces and bearing Palestinian names. One Islamist prisoner, Jamal Mansour, said that many detainees were kept in the very same prison chamber they had served prison sentences in previous to the conclusion of the Oslo Accords and the establishment of Yasser Arafat's PA.

"The main difference between the Israeli occupation authorities and Arafat's PA is that the former is wicked and sinister but smart and cunning while the latter is almost equally brutal and harsh but stupid and brash." A wise enemy, he said, is often better than a learned friend.

But, in reality, PA security officials most often don't act as "a friend", particularly when they are put to the test. According to human rights activists, PA interrogators use physical and psychological torture "almost routinely" against Islamist detainees, usually for the purpose of extracting confessions from them, but also as a means of "reprisal for their (the detainees') opposition to the Oslo process and Yasser Arafat's regime."

Last year, Munir al-Hurub, a teacher from the town of Dura near Hebron who has been interned by the PA for the last two and a half years, testified that PA interrogators beat him mercilessly on all parts of his body, spit into his mouth, and forced him to crawl for several hours a day. And when he called on God for mercy, an interrogator would tell him that "even if God were to come, he (the interrogator) would put him behind bars."

In addition, many PA security operatives routinely and habitually indulge in "cursing the Almighty," presumably in order to provoke the mostly religious detainees. According to sources close to the detainees, this virulent practice is most common in the Nablus jails, especially in the Jeneid Prison, which on 5 July witnessed violent riots after prison officials began blaspheming God, the Prophet Muhammed, and Islam.

Brutal force used against women and children
In accordance with the above-cited adage "a learned enemy is better than an ignorant friend," PA police, including Preventive Security cadres and especially the so-called Force-17 soldiers, violently dispersed a peaceful demonstration that the wives, mothers, and children of the detainees staged to protest the continued detention of their loved ones.

According to eyewitnesses, Force-17 crack soldiers pushed, dragged, and even beat women and children outside the Jeneid prison "without provocation," prompting the protesters to call the soldiers "collaborators, collaborators." An undisclosed number of demonstrators were injured and taken to hospital. Moreover, Force-17 soldiers dragged and beat a Palestinian cameraman, Jafar Ishtaya, for "filming an illegal demonstration."

The demonstrators carried placards demanding an end to "political incarceration" and the upholding of the rule of law." Moreover, the women demonstrators released a leaflet castigating the PA for "demanding the release of our political prisoners from Israeli jails while the PA itself is interning its own people on suspicion of resisting the Zionist occupiers."

In the meantime, prison guards physically attacked Islamist inmates, blaming them for the protests outside the prison premises and calling them "dogs and terrorists," the same labels used by Israel.

On 6 July, the PA police decided to impose further restrictions on the detainees in the Jeneid prison, including depriving them of the daily furloughs (within the prison compounds) and denying them family visits.

As of the writing of this report (23 July), the ban on family visits was still continuing with no signs that it would be lifted soon.

The brutal treatment of the demonstrators and the prisoners at the hands of PA police galvanized the atmosphere in Nablus. Consequently, representatives of several political factions in the town met with Mahmoud Al-Alul, the PA-appointed governor of the town (no election has been allowed since the advent of the PA five years ago). The delegation included Jamal Salim (Hamas), Taysir Khalid ( PFLP), Majid al-Masri, who is a member of the Palestine National Council, as well as representatives from the Palestine People's Party (PPP) and other local leaders.

Alul denied that he ordered the police to use force against the protesters, and suggested that the use of brutal force by the police was a mistake.

Moreover, Alul indicated to the delegation that the issue of political detainees was "above me, and may even be above Arafat as well!"

Maltreatment of demonstrators condemned
The maltreatment of the demonstrators by the police received widespread condemnation by the Legislative Council, student and professional unions, as well as human rights organizations and civic groups.

The student council at the National Najah University in Nablus strongly condemned the "disgraceful behaviour of the police", which it said could "only be equated with Zionist repression against our people."

The council challenged the PA to "point its guns toward the malignant growth of settlement activities which are pulverizing our land and corroding our aspirations for freedom, independence and Statehood."

Muhammed Khader, the council's head, pointed out that the police were acting as "hoodlums and punks without any consideration for the fact that the demonstrators were their people."

"It is a lamentable fact that Palestinian patriots are kept behind bars while Zionist collaborators are at large," said Khader.

Finally, Khader urged the PA leadership to take a brave decision to put an end to political detention and to control the behaviour of its policemen who, he said, were acting like enemy police. He also demanded that an immediate end be put to the abusive, virulent and blasphemous language used by PA prison officials, which he said was "unethical and reflecting cheapness in the character of the users of this language."

The "Law Society" also denounced the behaviour of the police against the demonstrators and the detainees. In a statement issued on 7 July, the Society demanded that the police refrain from using force, certainly excessive force, against popular protests and demonstrations. The statement also urged the PA to bring those who attacked the protesters to court, saying "that nobody should be above the law."

Osama Burhum: We need more perseverance, solidarity and unity

Occupied Jerusalem- The Israeli occupation authorities released from “administrative detention” Osama Burhum, who was interned for more than six years without charge or trial. Burhum was accused of belonging to and leading Islamic Jihad activism in the northern part of the West Bank. However, he challenged the Zionists to bring him before a trial if they had any evidence against him, but to no avail.

Last week, the Gaza-based al-Istiklal weekly spoke to Burhum in his home in Tulkarm. The following are excerpts of the interview:

Question: After over six years of internment, how would you assess this experience?

Answer: Imprisonment is the antithesis of humanness; however, with perseverance and resilience, a Muslim should be able to overcome the ordeals of the jail. I see my internment in the context of the perpetual confrontation between the forces of truth and the forces of evil. In fact, there is a continuous showdown, a continuous clash, a continuous dichotomy between us and them. This entity (Israel) is inhuman, illegal and immoral.
Concerning the treatment I received, I could say it reflected and embodied the overall policy of the Israeli military and intelligence establishment toward the Palestinian people, especially toward the Islamist movement. That sinister and insidious policy is aimed at demoralizing the Palestinians, breaking our will, and destroying our spirit.
The internment conditions were difficult, particularly in the last weeks where I lived in solitary confinement. But, again, this is the nature of the conflict with them, and they can't be good towards us.

Q: Did they seek to bargain with you? Did they offer you anything?

A: They constantly use one form of bargain or the other. During interrogation, for example, they used to urge me to hand over “written orders from higher movement leaders” and that in return they would get me freed. Then they proposed to deport me for one year, but I absolutely rejected the principle of deportation, even for three months. Then they proposed that I accept home arrest and talk to no one, but I refused.
Finally, they set conditions, namely that I renounce Jihad and organizations involved in Jihad, declare publicly that I recognize their State, and distance myself from the Islamic Jihad organization.
I told them that I couldn't label Islamic groups that carry out Jihad as terrorist. Terrorism and Jihad, I said, are diametrically opposed and can't be reconciled.

Q: What charges did they level against you?

A: They charged me with the following: That I am a leading member of the Islamic Jihad organization in Palestine, particularly the military wing of the movement, and that I possessed weapons. They employed all forms of physical and psychological pressure to extract information from me. At one point, I lost consciousness. Indeed, I was transferred three times to hospital as a result of torture. Among the evil forms of torture they performed on me was the notorious shaking technique, beating on the neck and throat, hooding, fettering, sleep deprivation and other forms of torture.

Q: How could the prisoners stay in high spirits for a long time?

A: The prisoners are a blazing revolution, and despite all the frustration outside, the prisons remain strongholds of patriotism and heroism. There is a semi-daily confrontation between the prisoners and the Zionist authorities, which necessitates popular backing. We must prop up those heroes by securing the needs of their families.

Q: You warned you would begin an open-ended strike to protest your incarceration?

A: In truth, I had intended to do this, and my goal was not so much for myself, but rather to contribute to putting an end to this evil practice, i.e. political internment.
I have spent 13 years of my life in Zionist jails during which I took part in six hunger strikes, the longest of which lasted for 20 days.

On this occasion, I would like to say that the solidarity put up with the strikers often serves to shorten the duration of the strike, and that is good, as it forces the Zionists to meet at least some of our demands.

Q: Do you think Israel will rescind the policy of administrative internment?

A: I don't believe so. However, I believe the Zionist regime is seeking to enhance its ugly image by these propaganda cosmetics. We are essentially talking about a racist and vindictive enemy that views non-Jews as "less than full humans." This racism on their part is at the heart of their maltreatment of us.
Besides, I was arrested during the Labor Party government, which means that my release (after six years) didn't signal any change of policy on the part of the Zionists.

Q: What would you say to human rights organizations?

A: I believe the Zionist regime should be tried before the whole world. We must seek to expose this regime, especially its racist ideology and practice against us. On this, I would also like to voice my anger at the fact that the Geneva Conference was postponed indefinitely and prevented from saying the things it should have said.

Q: Finally, what would you say to Hamas and the Islamic Jihad?

A: More perseverance, more solidarity and unity, because the enemy doesn't distinguish between us.

85% of Palestinian detainees in Israeli jails undergo torture

Occupied Jerusalem- Over eighty five per cent of Palestinian detainees in Israeli jails under go various forms of physical and psychological torture, according to a report released by the Israeli Human Rights organization known as B'etselem.
The report said the vast majority of Palestinians rounded up by Israeli occupation troops are subjected to forms of torture ranging from "stiff psychological pressure to genuine physical torture."
Only 15% of the detainees, said the report, are not tortured, but even those are subject to "moderate psychological and physical pressures."
Furthermore, the report pointed out that as many 1500 Palestinians detainees are interrogated every year, the bulk of whom are members of Hamas and other Islamic organizations opposed to the Zionist occupation of Palestine.
Israel is the only country in the world where torture is officially sanctioned by the law.
Scores of Palestinians lost their lives as a result of torture.
Meanwhile, The Israeli press reported last month that Israeli Prime Minister Ehud Barak would inform PA Chairman Yaser Arafat that he was going to release hundreds of Palestinian prisoners in Israeli jails.
The sources said the Israeli government prepared a list of hundreds of names of prisoners who would be released.
The same sources said the list included Palestinian political activists who had carried out "normal security violations" not involving killing of Israeli soldiers and settlers.
However, the sources said Barak wouldn't agree to free Palestinian prisoners "who had Jewish blood at their hands."
If true, this stance would be in line with the policy of the former Israeli premier Benyamin Netanyahu.
This attitude on the part of the Israeli government is strongly rejected by the Palestinians who argue that tens of thousands of Jews' hands are stained with Arab blood and that "Jewish blood is never redder than Arab blood."

Hasan Salamah declares hunger strike

Occupied Jerusalem - A Palestinian detainee who is spending a jail sentence for about 1,200 years in an Israeli jail continued to observe an open-ended hunger strike for the fourth consecutive day to protest maltreatment and solitary confinement.

Palestinian jurist sources said Hasan Salameh, a Gazan freedom fighter whom a Zionist court found guilty of planning martyrdom operations against Zionist targets in 1996, has reportedly refused to take food until prison officials end his solitary confinement.

According to the Palestinian Prisoners Society (PPS), Salameh’s health deteriorated substantially since he began his hunger strike on Wednesday, 14 July.

The PPS called on human rights organizations to exert pressure on the Zionist regime to allow a physician to visit Salameh in his Ramleh prison cell.

Salameh was arrested in 1997 in Hebron in what seemed to be a joint Israeli-PA operation.

In 1999, Salameh was sentenced to 500 years in jail for causing the death of scores of Israeli soldiers and settlers in a series of attacks in 1999.

On the other hand, a report issued by the Palestinian Prisoner Club revealed that the Palestinian political prisoners in the Zionist Talmund jail, north of Palestine, were subjected to continued ill treatment by the prison guards. The report added that the Zionist jailers used to break into the prisoners’ cells and spray them with hot water. Several prisoners suffered burns, the report said.

A letter sent to the Club by the prisoners said they were facing a “slow murder”. They called on the human rights groups and the law organizations to visit the prison and see for themselves their miserable conditions.

Palestinian legal sources revealed that the Zionist interrogators would not allow doctors to treat several ailing prisoners although their health situation was deteriorating.

The Club held the Zionist regime responsible for the serious living conditions in the Israeli jails and for the escalation of inhuman practices against the prisoners.

Palestinian Leftists Seek to Form “Third Way”

Occupied Jerusalem - From Khalid Amayreh

Like other Leftists around the world, Palestinian Leftists suffered a serious identity crisis after (and as the result of) the collapse of the former Soviet Union, which more or less had been their ultimate role model.

That identity crisis is still enduring, and one of its conspicuous features is the steady decline in the status and stature of the Left at the elite as well as grass-root levels.

Indeed, opinion surveys in the West Bank and Gaza Strip put all Palestinian Leftist groups combined at a distant third place (4%-5%) behind Fatah (the mainstream faction of the PLO) and the Islamist group Hamas.

Even in student and other professional unions, the traditional strongholds of Leftist activism, Leftist presence receded markedly, often giving way to Islamic fundamentalist movements, primarily Hamas.

This didn’t mean that Leftists were making a dramatic turnabout from the Marxist-Leninist ideology to political Islam. But it did mean rampant low morale and an increasing sense of hopelessness and powerlessness coupled with a definite inability to recruit new supporters, particularly among the younger generations.

It also meant that many Leftist party activists, especially at the elite level, were joining the increasingly swollen ranks of “independents” and non-governmental organizations (NGOs) that are active in promoting a civil society culture and safeguarding human rights in a society that is still tormented by the Israeli occupation.

“There is a general sense of weakness permeating in the Left. This undermines its ability, even its inclination, to assert itself more forcefully on the Palestinian arena,” said Ghassan al-Khatib of the Jerusalem Center for Media and Communications, adding that “the Left is facing a crisis.”

Leftists explore “Third Way”

Worried that their voice might “get lost” in the clamorous rivalry between Hamas and Fatah, scores of Palestinian Leftist leaders and intellectuals began lately exploring the possibility of forming what they described as the “Democratic Pole,” or a Palestinian “Third Way” that would provide an alternative to Fatah and Hamas.

Fatah is viewed as too corrupt and authoritarian to be entrusted with the crucial task of building a civil society where the rule of law is paramount, while Hamas’ ideology is inherently incompatible with the ideas and ideals of the Left.

(Interestingly, many Leftist figures occupy key posts in the PA regime, including two ministerial posts.)

Meetings held to discuss “Leftist unity” theme

So far, several exploratory meetings have taken place in Bethlehem, Ramallah and Nablus during which representatives of the main Palestinian Leftist factions vigorously, occasionally acrimoniously, discussed the theme of “Leftist unity”.

These included the People’s Party or PPP (formerly Communist), the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine (PFLP), the Democratic Front for the Liberation of Palestine (DFLP), FEDA, and the Populist Struggle Front (PSF).

According to Leftist sources, the participants stressed the need for “a broad democratic front” encompassing all Leftist forces and independent but secular-minded figures who believe in the “democratic choice.”

“Democracy is not a resonant slogan we raise whenever we like; democracy means genuine and complete personal, political, intellectual, social and religious freedom,” said Naji Salameh, chairman of the inter-partisan follow-up and coordination committee which the participants authorized to prepare a work paper on the subject.

Salameh’s almost libertarian tone, however, didn’t seem in tune with many erstwhile Palestinian Communists who still cling to such orthodox communist concept as central democracy, class struggle, market economy and dialectical materialism.

But that was only one of the many problems rendering the realization of a true Leftist unity a distant goal, at least in current circumstances.

Problems

Taysir Aruri, a member of the People’s Party Politburo, alluded to the numerous obstacles impeding the realization of Leftist political unity in the Palestinian arena.

Speaking during a Bethlehem symposium entitled “Search for a Third Way” last week, Aruri advised his colleagues to show “piercing understanding of the profound difficulties impeding unity among the forces of the Palestinian Left.”

Aruri didn’t spell out these difficulties, but Ghassan al-Khatib, also of the PPP, identified two sets of obstacles he said were seriously hindering the realization of Leftist unity in the occupied territories.

First, he said there were real political disagreements within the Left on the peace process and the subject of Palestinian Statehood. For example, the PFLP vehemently oppose the Oslo process, often dismissing it as a “sell-out of Palestinian rights,” while FEDA (headed by PA culture and information minister Yasser Abed Rubbu), and to a lesser extent the PPP, vigorously supported the process from its inception.

Khatib predicted that these real and potential disagreements might even exacerbate, especially if the PA leadership was forced to give still more concessions to Israel in the context of the final status settlement.

“Our political arena could experience widespread disintegration in the coming months if the Palestinian leadership accepted the American-Israeli fait accompli.”

Second, there are real organizational and personal differences between various factions and their leaders having to do with esteem and prestige, which Khatib says will inhibit genuine movement toward unity.

Added to that is the wide discrepancy in the Leftists’ respective attitudes toward a host of other issues such as the role of the Palestinian working class, globalization, pan-Arab nationalism, relations with Israeli Leftist groups and peace movements.

Left to form new political body
The Leftists’ endeavour to form a new political body, though a fledgling and even uncertain, could very well revitalize the quest for genuine democracy in the self-rule areas. Leftist leaders say their ultimate goal is to build a civil, democratic society in which human rights and civil liberties are observed by the government in power. The Left can be rest-assured that the bulk of the Palestinian people, including many Islamists, share these lofty goals.

Christian and Muslim leaders condemn Jewish
attempts to attack Christian targets

Occupied Jerusalem - Christian and Muslim religious leaders have condemned attempts by Jewish extremists to attack and vandalize Christian targets in Jerusalem.

Jamil Naser, the PA-appointed symbolic governor of Jerusalem said, "Evil acts as such are not unlikely."

He called on Muslims and Christians to show "vigilance and caution."

Atallah Hanna, the Orthodox Patriarch of Jerusalem, also condemned the reported plans by some Jewish extremists to attack Christian holy places in the town.

"These evil acts are motivated by hatred and are planned and supported by groups of people that are inimical to human and moral values."

Hanna said he opposed religious extremism and called on the Israeli occupation authorities in Jerusalem to put an end to these hate groups.

On a recent Friday, the Israeli police reportedly arrested a Jewish student on suspicion of planning to attack and vandalize Christian targets, including churches, in East Jerusalem.

PA papers shun news of financial scandal in PNF

Occupied Jerusalem- PA-financed newspapers in the West Bank refrained from reporting or covering the big financial scandal in the Palestine National Fund.
The main PA papers made no mention of the embezzlement of $6.5 million by the erstwhile president of the fund, Yasser Arafat's friend Jeweid al Ghusein.
The Jerusalem-based daily, al Quds, was the only paper to report the grand embezzlement, and even its coverage of the affair was often euphemistic and half-hearted.
Al Ghusein, who resides in the United Arab Emerates, claimed he "borrowed the money with Yaser Arafat's consent."
Palestinian sources close to the PLO have dismissed PA efforts to recover the money as "futile and pretentious."
One source who asked that his name remain anonymous said "embezzlement and outright theft are the modus operandi of PLO finances, and Ghusein is only a little thief compared to the others, some of whom occupy important posts at the helm of the PA."
