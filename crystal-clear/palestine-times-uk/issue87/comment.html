Time To Challenge American Hegemony

So, the Great Satan, the perfect label the late Imam Khumeini attached to the United States, did it again, this time against two impoverished Muslim countries, Sudan and Afghanistan.
The declared justification for this cowboy-minded aggression was "to hit terrorism", especially in the aftermath of the bombing of the two American embassies in Nairobi and Dar el Salaam.
However, the more likely motive for the bombing of the two Muslim countries was Bill Clinton's apparent desire to divert attention from his domestic troubles, mainly his adulterous relationship with former White House intern, Monica Lewinsky.
Never the less, this shouldn't alone account for American savagery against Muslims.
To begin with, there is absolutely no evidence linking Sudan, especially the bombed-out pharmaceutical plant in Khartoum, to any form of "terrorism" or alleged production of chemical weapons.
Indeed, why would the Government of Sudan so openly and so earnestly invite the UN or other international bodies to inspect the bombed-out site if it ever had anything to hide? In fact, it is the American Government that is resisting inspection of the site by an impartial international body, which could only mean that the Americans want to avoid appearing as naked liars in the full view of the world.
Never the less, it would be naive to think the Americans, with all their intelligence-gathering abilities, didn't know that. They surely did, but decided to go ahead and hit Sudan anyway. Why is that?
The reason is amply clear. The Zionist-dominated Clinton Administration despises all Muslims, Governments and individuals, who take their faith seriously, e.g. by refusing to be subservient to American hegemony.
Indeed, this is first and foremost the reason behind American enmity and vitriolic hostility towards countries such as Sudan and the Islamic Republic of Iran. It is also the same morbid perception that prompted the American Congress to dispatch two congressmen to Indonesia recently to warn President Habibi against "slackening restrictions on Islamists lest democracy be undermined."
What democracy? We all know too well in this part of the world that the United States, through its successive administrations-republican and democrat-has been impeding democratization in the Arab world in favour of despotic and autocratic regimes that trample on their peoples' will.
This shameful policy apparently stems from the premise that if Arab-Muslim masses were allowed to choose their leaders in fair and democratic elections, a new breed of leaders would come to power who would be more responsible to their people than they are to the White House, which means that American economic and strategic interests in the region would be endangered.
So despotism in the Arab world and cheap oil for America go hand-in-hand. In other words, if you want to have cheap oil, you shouldn't think about democracy, and sustaining despotism in the Arab world is the only way through which American interests are safeguarded.
Obviously, this is nothing but evil-incarnate. Protecting, sustaining and seeking to perpetuate despotism in the Arab world (despotism is essentially incompatible with the principles America claims to adhere to), along with the blind and scandalous American support for Jewish colonization of Palestine and torment of the Palestinian and Lebanese peoples, is and will continue to be translated into "terrorism" against American targets around the world.
This is not something good or bad. It is inevitable, given American morbid determination to ignore the collective feelings of hundred of millions of Muslims around the world who have come to believe that the United States is truly evil and truly satanic, if only because it supports and protects evil and satanic regimes in many Muslim countries.
None the less, one should probably not think that Muslims and Americans are to be doomed to endless confrontations of violence-breeding violence. However, in order for the United States to revert to its senses, Muslims (especially the masses since the regimes are hopelessly dead) must put up fierce resistance to American aggression and hegemony.
America must understand, or be made to understand, that Muslim rights, dignity and freedom are not expendable. America must change its scandalous approach to the two main subjects of concern to Muslims: the Palestinian issue and democracy in the Arab world.
This should mean that continued American support for Israeli territorial aggrandizement and colonization at the Palestinians' expense and continued American backing of the impotent but despotic regimes in the Arab world will continue to backfire.
