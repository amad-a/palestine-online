Human Rights organizations censure Arafat

for closing Gaza institutions

Occupied Jerusalem-PT(29 September)

Human rights organizations have renewed their criticisms of Palestinian Authority chairman Yasser Arafat for closing down sixteen charitable and educational institutions in the Gaza Strip in submission to Israeli and American demands.

The Palestinian Human Rights Monitoring Group (PHRMG) has labeled the closure of the civilian Islamic institutions "a flagrant violation of human rights and a serious transgression on the pillars of civil society."

Basem Eid, Director-General of the PHRMG said in a telephone interview on Monday 29 September the closure of the Gaza institutions amounted to "a blind submission to Zionist dictates."

He added that the draconian measure was "totally unjust," opining that "it will increase rather than reduce violence."

Eid, who is gathering details on each of the closed institutions, said the only people who are now going be hurt by the closure are the poor and orphan who have no where else to turn to for daily subsistence.

"Some of these institutions used to provide the poor with daily food supplement, so I don't understand what purpose does it serve to starve the poor, the orphan and the underprivileged."

Eid ridiculed PA claims that the closure of the institutions was aimed at serving Palestinian interests.

"It is extremely hard to believe that depriving children from going to schools and closing kindergartens serves Palestinians' interests."

Furthermore, Eid pointed out that the closed institutions are located in the Gaza Strip where Arafat's authority is stronger, not in the West Bank where the latest spate of bombings were presumably planned and executed.

He said this suggests that the closure was politically motivated, and had nothing to do with any genuine security considerations.

On Sunday, the another human rights organization, al Damir (conscience) lambasted the PA for "succumbing to Israeli demands."

The organization issued a written statement describing the closure of the Gazan institutions as "a clear assault on justice and human rights."

On Friday, PA chairman Yasser Arafat reportedly told a group of Hebron civic leaders that he realized that the closure of the Gaza institutions was unjust.

However, Arafat added that "I have no choice in light of the intense pressures I am facing."

behind assassination attempt on Misha'al

Occupied Jerusalem -PT(28 September)

A junior Jordanian intelligence source revealed on Sunday that the two Canadian "tourists" who attempted to assassinate Hamas political bureau chief in Amman on Thursday were Canadian Jews from Toronto and bore Israeli as well as Canadian passports.

The source, who arrived in the West Bank on Friday and who demanded anonymity, added that several Islamist leaders were on the cell's hit list including, Hamas spokesman Ibrahim Ghoshe, Musa Abu Marzuk, Muhammed Nazzal and Khalid Misha'al.

Moreover, the source pointed out that Israel and the United States were exerting "lots of behind-the-scene pressure" on the Jordanian government in order "not to make a big fuss about what happened"

Arafat's police close down 16 Gaza institutions

 

Occupied Jerusalem-PT(26 September)

In a step that is ostensibly meant to appease Israel and the United States, Palestinian Authority police closed down 16 social, charitable and educational institutions in Gaza Thursday night.

The closed institutions are reportedly run by Islamic elements sympathetic to the Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas), Israel's arch enemy.

The institutions include, inter alia, the Islamic Charitable Society which provides freed food and clothing for the poor especially during the month of Ramadan, the multi-activity Center known as al Mujamma'a al Islami, the Muslim girl society, and the Holy Land society.

Moreover, PA police closed down a private TV station, known as the Al Ufuq (horizons) in Nablus which broadcast Quranic readings.

Palestinian sources admit that there is no direct or indirect linkage between the closed institutions and any political or military activities related to Hamas.

A high-ranking PA official intimated that the closure was only meant to "mitigate the intensive pressure being exerted on us by the Israeli and the Americans."

"We told our brothers that we didn't mean to hurt them...they didn't commit anything wrong after all...we just want to silence those SOB's for a while."

Earlier, PA police arrested 12 Islamist activists in Nablus, including Isa Abu al Ez, owner of the Ufuq TV station.

Meanwhile, the Israeli army continued its campaign against suspected Islamic supporters throughout the West Bank.

On Friday 26 September, Israeli troops arrested Ismael Al Awawdeh, head of the Engineering Department at Dura municipality and Hashem Rajoub Secretary General of the Islamic Charitable Society in Dura.

According to Israeli sources, more than 700 Palestinians have been arrested since the 31 July suicidal bombing in West Jerusalem.

At least 500 people were sent to detention camps in central Palestine where they are detained as hostages without charge or trial.

Elsewhere, in Dura thousands of townspeople performed the congregational Friday prayer in the main street after the Israeli army closed down the town's main mosque.

The imam, Sheikh Nayef Rajoub, described the closure of the mosque as "the most evil form of terrorism."

"Can you think of a form of terrorism more diabolical than preventing believers from praying at the house of God?" Rajoub said, addressing several thousand worshipers.

Rajoub said Israel was no longer fighting Hamas, accusing the Israeli occupation army "of waging war on Islam, on the mosques, and on our Prophet Muhammed."

The Israeli army has closed down over 50 mosques in the West Bank since the two recent suicidal bombings in Jerusalem.

The Israeli authorities recognize the measure to be unfair. However, they argue that Israel can not fight Palestinian resistance effectively without "inflicting collective pain and punishment on the larger Palestinian population, Hamas' ultimate base."

Hamas: Israeli intelligence is behind assassination

attempt in Amman

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (25 September)

The Islamic Resistance Movement (Hamas) has accused Israeli intelligence of master-minding an assassination attempt on Khalid Misha'al, the head of the Hamas Political Bureau, that took place in Amman the morning of 25 September.

Hamas leader and spokesman in Gaza, Dr. Abdul Aziz al Rantisi said he had no doubt whatsoever about the identity of the perpetrators, saying "Khalid Misha'al has one and only one enemy in this world and that is the Zionist criminals."

Rantisi said in a telephone interview from his Gaza home on the afternoon following the incident that he would not like to be hasty in drawing conclusions, adding that if indeed it turned out that Israeli agents were behind the assassination attempt, it would mean that Israel has "chosen to open wide a new door of confrontation with Muslims everywhere."

Another Islamist spokesman, who asked for anonymity, said "Israel is playing with fire, and fire doesn't burn only Islamist leaders."

The assassination attempt on Khalid Misha'al's life reportedly took place as he was on his way to work in Amman. According to Islamist sources in the Jordanian capital, the incident was preceded by Mr. Misha'al sensing that he was being followed by an unidentified car carrying five men. Upon reaching his destination and alighting from his vehicle, two of the men from the unfamiliar car were close behind him as he reached the entrance of his building. As they got closer to him, he noticed that one of the men was carrying a small, unidentified device which the assailant attempted to deploy close to Mr. Misha'al's ear. However, one of the two security guards looking after Mr. Misha'al was able to strike the assailant's arm just before detonation. There was a loud noise and a sort of electric shock that made Mr. Misha'al lose his sense of balance. It is still unknown what the effect of this device would have been had the detonation been complete and without interference. The two men attempted to flee the scene but were captured by the security guards who subdued them until the arrival of the police and their eventual arrest. The other three accomplices escaped in their vehicle. According to the security guards, all five are believed to be non-Arab nationals. In the interim, Mr. Misha'al was rushed to hospital and is still under observation.

Earlier this month, the internal Israeli ministerial council on security adopted a number of secret recommendations against Hamas.

According to Israeli sources, the recommendations included "reactivating Mossad death squads in Jordan," an allusion to carrying out assassinations of Islamist leaders in Jordan.

Two years ago, the murder of the Palestinian Islamist guerrilla Yahya Ayyash by the Israeli intelligence sparked off a series of deadly urban bomb attacks in Israel in which over 100 Israeli soldiers and civilians were killed.

Following Hitler's footsteps:

Victims of Jewish ethnic cleansing languish in homelessness

Occupied Jerusalem -PT(23 September)

Hundreds of Palestinian victims of the Israeli policy of ethnic cleansing in East Jerusalem have entered their second month at a makeshift refugee camp at the Suwwana neighborhood, across the Mount of Olives.

The camp, say Palestinians, only epitomizes the increasingly acute ordeal of Jerusalemite Arabs whom the Israeli seek to drive out "by hook or crook" in order to check Arab population growth in Jerusalem.

Today, the "Jerusalem Camp of Steadfastness" is "home" to as many as seventy Palestinian families, including at least 35 children below the age of eight.

So far, the Israeli municipal authorities have behaved totally callously towards the refugees, refusing to "recognize the camp" nor provide refugees with electricity and water supplies.

However, Israeli callousness doesn't seem to deter the refugees or weaken their resolve.

"We are here to demonstrate to the whole world the racist, Nazi nature of the Israeli authorities which refuse to treat us as human beings," said Um Ahmed, a woman refugee at the camp.

"Look! they just don't want us to be in Jerusalem because we are not Jews...It is like the Nazis who wanted to have a pure Arian Germany."

Another woman, Maha Jayyousi, cited a different reason for becoming a refugee in her own town.

"For 15 years my husband and I have been trying to obtain a building license from the Israeli municipal authorities, but to no avail...they told us 'if you don't like it, you can leave the town.. and we would help you leave if and when you decide to do so," she said.

Jayyousi recognized that "life here is difficult" and that "things are particularly difficult for the children."

"Life here is difficult, but being contiguous to Jerusalem makes us forget all hardship."

Virtually all refugees vow to stay at the camp until "our just demands are met."

"We are not asking for the impossible.. we just want to be treated on equal footing with other human beings," said Waheed al Resheq, pointing out to his married son Muhammed, also a refugee, whose residency right had been withdrawn by the Israeli interior ministry.

"See, they took away his identity card because he lived for six months at the Ram outskirt after he couldn't find housing in East Jerusalem."

Abdullah Burqan, another refugee at the camp, voices essentially the same grievances.

"The Israelis have one goal, that it is to force us out of Jerusalem; and since they can't just collectively expel us outside the town for public relations considerations, they create a situation in which our life in the town becomes unbearable, so that we would leave voluntarily."

The refugees hope that media coverage of their plight would prompt Israel to reconsider its stance on their cause. However, this doesn't seem to forthcoming.

Palestinians reject the Ras-al-Amud "Joke"

Occupied Jerusalem -PT(20 September)

A general state of tension continued to prevail in Israeli-occupied East Jerusalem despite the conclusion on Thursday night of a "compromise agreement" between the Israeli government and the Jewish settlers who earlier had seized two Arab houses at the Ras al Amud neighborhood.

The agreement, which Palestinians branded as "silly joke" stipulated that the three Jewish families who had entered the premise be evacuated and replaced by ten students belonging to the ultra fanatic Gush Emunim movement.

Israeli sources described the new settlers as "potential murderers" and "indoctrinated in hate of everything Arab."

Palestinians at all levels flatly rejected the "silly joke," labeling it as "a deal between thieves."

Faisal Husseini, who holds the Jerusalem portfolio in the Palestinian Authority, described the "deal" as "a naked trick" and "a crime greater than its predecessor."

PA minister of Information, Yasser Abed Rabbu, said the "Jewish-Jewish deal" was like an understanding between "field gangsters and mafia heads."

Abed Rabbu denounced the American stance on the matter, saying it provided a political cover for colonialist Jewish activities in Arab Jerusalem.

Earlier, PA Chairman Yasser Arafat rejected the deal between the Netanyahu government and settler leaders.

"This is a big trick, a public relations ploy to perpetuate Jewish colonialism at Ras al Amud," Arafat reportedly told a meeting of the Arab League foreign ministers in Cairo on Saturday.

Meanwhile, Jewish settler leaders lauded the "deal" as "a victory for our cause."

"The families have left; the people who remained are here to guard the property and make renovations, because at some point the families will come back here..this is only temporary," said settler leader Hagit Harel, a spokesman for the group of settlers who seized the building last week.

Another settler, Shimon Gaznov, said he and five fellow settlers planned to get to work soon on putting up partitions and laying ceramic tiles.

Meanwhile, more Palestinian protests are planned for this week outside the premise.

On Saturday, Palestinian school children in East Jerusalem staged a demonstration demanding "the removal of settlers from our midst."

In other development, Palestinian Islamist sources discounted Israeli claims concerning the apprehension of two armed Islamic cells in East Jerusalem.

An Islamist source in the West Bank town of Hebron said the Israeli claims to that effect "were fraught with exaggeration" and "aimed at boosting the morale of the Israeli public following recent bombings in West Jerusalem and Israeli army losses in South Lebanon."

Israeli interrogators torture Palestinian detainee to death

 

Occupied Jerusalem- PT (17 September)

Israeli Intelligence agents have tortured a 31-year-old Palestinian political detainee to death, Israeli and Palestinian sources said Wednesday.

The detainee, Marwan Hasan Ibrahim Ma'ali, of the village of A'jja near Jenin was strangled to death at the hands of three Israeli Shabak interrogators at the Megiddo detention camp.

An Israeli army spokesman said Ma'ali died in "vague circumstancesî and ìmay have committed suicide."

However, a number of his friends reportedly strongly denied the Israeli claim, saying "it was a routine ruse to conceal the crime."

"Marwan didn't commit suicide, he was murdered by the criminal thugs" said one inmate from Hebron, referring to Shabak interrogators.

According to his family, Ma'ali never had mental or psychological problems.

His friend, Ragheb, from A'jja flatly denied the Israeli insinuation that Ma'ali committed suicide.

"Why would he commit suicide. He was in good health, he was married and father of three children, and had no social or psychological problems," said Ragheb.

Isa Karaqe'i, head of the Palestinian Prisoner Club, said "I'm a hundred per cent sure that Shabak agents murdered Marwan.. he was not the kind of person who would commit suicide."

"This (the suicide claim) is not the first, nor would be the last claim of its kind by those evil murderers...They always perpetrate their crimes and then seek to conceal them in a variety of sinister ways."

According to the Palestinian Human Rights Monitoring Group, Ma'ali was arrested on 4 August following the 31 bombing in West Jerusalem. An administrative detention order for two months was issued for him. His family was not allowed to visit him, despite an illness that Ma'ali suffered from. The family tried to pass medical documents related to the illness through the Red Cross, but without success.

Ma'ali's body was taken to the Abu Kabir forensic institute for an autopsy. He was due to be buried Wednesday night at his village.

Ma'ali was detained without charge or trial, like about a thousand other Palestinians currently held hostage by the Israeli government, not knowing why they are being detained nor if and when they would be let go.

Ma'ali leaves behind him a pregnant wife and three children.

Israel is the only country in the world that legalizes the use of torture against detainees. So far, as many as 15 Palestinians died of torture in Israeli custody since the signing of the Oslo Agreement four years ago.

The grave hint coincided with revelations, reportedly made last week by the Israeli intelligence and communicated to the United States, that the two suicidal bombers who carried out the 31 July double-bombing in West Jerusalem came from abroad, a view all along held by PA chairman Yasser Arafat. Arafat's dilemma

The exacerbation of the "suicidal phenomenon" must have been in Arafat's mind when he met in Cairo earlier this week with Egyptian President Husni Mubarak and King Hussein of Jordan. According to aides, Arafat told Mubarak and Hussein that Netanyahu's policies in the West Bank were pushing the Palestinian public opinion to the boiling point and that, given this, he (Arafat) was finding it increasingly difficult to fight violence let alone isolate Hamas without risking being viewed as an Israeli collaborator along Antoin Lahed's lines.

Arafat's argument seemed to be largely justified. Interestingly, this view seems to be shared by a growing number of Palestinians who have already lost or about to lose hope of a genuine and just peace "as long as things remain as they are."

This state of hopelessness and "frozen rage" does explain Arafat's relative reluctance to launch massive arrests among Hamas supporters in the West Bank and the Gaza Strip.

Indeed, it seems that Arafat has come to realize that Netanyahu may be after him and after the Palestine Authority as much as he is after Hamas and the Islamic Jihad. Hence, Arafat's hesitation to succumb to Israeli dictates.

Added to this is the re imposition by Israel of the month-long closure on Palestinian population centers, which effectively reduced towns and villages into "closed off ghettoes." Moreover, the re imposition of the "closure," ostensibly prompted by the triple suicidal bomb attack in West Jerusalem on 4 Sept, placed the originally weak Palestinian economy in an unprecedently precarious situation, forcing the PA cut on public spending and to postpone until further notice the payment of salaries to its 100,000 or so military and civilians employees.

The logic behind Israel's draconian measures against the Palestinians is that by ìfurther narrowing their horizons and tightening the noose around them, they would be prompted to turn against Hamas, which brought on them Israel's wrath."

However, the draconian Israeli measures, often accompanied by brutal mistreatment of ordinary Palestinians at ubiquitous Israeli checkpoints and roadblocks, seem to boomerang against Israel. The seizure of the occupied territories and the cordoning-off of Palestinian towns and villages only serves to direct Palestinian indignation towards the occupiers not Hamas.
