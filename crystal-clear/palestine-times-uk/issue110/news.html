Israel insists on retaining Jerusalem,
Arafat likely to cave in to pressures

Occupied Jerusalem – From Khaled Amayreh

It was almost certain by Palestine Times’ press time that the marathon talks between Israel and Palestinian Authority leader Yasser Arafat would end in failure. According to Palestinian sources familiar with the talks, little or no progress has been made on any of the major issues, such as the status of Jerusalem, the refugees and the borders between the Zionist regime and a prospective Palestinian State.

“We haven’t reached agreement on any of the fundamental issues including the status of Jerusalem and the refugees,” said PLO representative to the U.S., Hasan Abdul Rahman.

He added that Israel was still refusing to come to terms with the United Nations resolutions pertaining to the Palestinian issues which he said must be observed and carried out if an agreement was to be reached at Camp David.

Abdul Rahman’s downbeat mood was also echoed by Arafat’s aide and confidant in Gaza, al-Tay’eb Abdul Rahim, who said in an interview on 24 July with the PA-run radio, the Voice of Palestine, that Barak was essentially sticking to his recalcitrant positions and seeking to evade international legitimacy.

“The Palestinian delegation has achieved no progress on the important issues of the negotiations, such as the status of Jerusalem, settlements, the return of refugees and borders,” he said in a speech at the Egyptian consulate in Gaza on 23 July.

Abdul Rahim argued that the Palestinian delegation to the talks was insisting that Israel would have to accept in principle at least the need for full Israeli withdrawal to the borders of 4 June 1967 as well as come to terms with UN resolution 194 on the refugees.

He added that within these principles, the Palestinian leadership was willing to show flexibility for the sake of reaching a just and durable peace with Israel.

The Palestinian official did not elaborate on what he meant by “flexibility” and whether, for example, Arafat would be willing to accept Israeli annexation of Jewish settlements in the West Bank and Gaza or the repatriation of a symbolic number of Palestinian refugees as proposed by Israel and the United States.

Meanwhile, the Zionist regime continued to leak reports about alleged Israeli “concessions” to the Palestinians concerning the future status of Jerusalem.

However, it is amply clear that none of these concessions–dubbed by the Israeli media as “painful”, “far-reaching” and other similar hyperbolic labels–meet the minimum Palestinian demands, much less aspirations.

So far, Israel has reportedly proposed to grant Palestinians a certain degree of autonomy in a number of East Jerusalem’s neighbourhoods and “safe passage” to the Haram al-Sharif along with allowing the Palestinian Authority to raise its flag atop al-Masjidul Aqsa.

Nonetheless, the ultimate sovereignty and control would remain in the Zionists’ hands, meaning Muslims would be able to worship at Islam’s third holiest shrine only in the shadow of Israeli guns.

The United States–the nominal honest broker who is also Israel’s strategic ally–is effectively adopting Israel’s positions.

Earlier, before Clinton’s sojourn to Japan, the Americans presented to the Palestinians what was described as a “bridging proposal” on Jerusalem that echoed Barak’s position.

The proposal, which was, at least ostensibly, rejected by Arafat, suggested that the fate of the Old Town of Jerusalem be postponed for two years, while Israel and the PA would have “shared” sovereignty over the two Arab neighbourhoods in the interim.

The Americans reportedly told the two sides that they either have full agreement or no agreement at all.

One American official compared the negotiations to pushing a rock up a hill. If full agreement formally bringing an end to the 52-year-old conflict between Israel and the Palestinians is not achieved, then whatever progress has been made on various issues could be wiped out as the rock rolls back down the slope.

Anxiety back home
Meanwhile, the bulk of Palestinians at home and in the Diaspora continued to voice mounting anxiety at the prospects of Arafat caving in to combined American and Zionist pressure.

On 23 July, a group of prominent Palestinian leaders and figures, among former delegates to the Madrid Peace Conference, in particular Dr. Haidar Abdul Shafi, signed a petition that stresses the adherence of the Palestinian people to their legitimate, national and inalienable rights.

The petition, addressed to the PA delegation at Camp David as well as to the international and Arab public, demanded that any agreement that might be reached at Camp David be subject to a Palestinian public referendum at home and abroad so that Palestinians can determine their own destiny.

Signatories stressed that a just and lasting peace in the region would not prevail without full Palestinian sovereignty over all territories within the 4 June 1967 borders, including East Jerusalem, the indispensable capital of the independent Palestinian State as well as the full implementation of the right of return for the refugees.

The petition was added to a number of even more strongly-worded petitions by Islamist and national forces, warning the Palestinian delegation in general, and even Yasser Arafat directly, against compromising Palestinian rights.

Indeed, Palestinian anxiety mounted considerably after it was rumoured that only the issue of Jerusalem remained the main obstacle impeding the conclusion of an agreement, which many Palestinians interpreted as a virtual sell-out of the refugees’ right of return on Yasser Arafat’s part.

Some PA officials sought to dissipate reports to that effect, saying Arafat was still committed to UN resolution 194.

However, given the almost hermetic media blackout on the talks, and also the scantiness of Arafat’s reactions to Israeli and American leaks to that effect, there seems to be no justification to give the Palestinian leader the benefit of the doubt, at least for the time being.

Muslim authorities reiterate Islamic stance
on Palestine, Jerusalem, refugees

 Occupied Jerusalem – PT

Muslim Ulema, or religious scholars, in Palestine have issued a series of fatwas (Islamic edicts) prohibiting Muslims from conceding any part of Palestine and/or al-Quds al-Sharif to the Zionists even under the rubric of a peace treaty.

Sheikh Ikreman Sabri, the Mufti of Jerusalem and the whole of Palestine, issued a fatwa on 23 July, ruling that it would be haram (sinful) for Muslims in Palestine to accept compensation or indemnification in return for their land which was seized by the Zionists when the Jewish State was created in Palestine in 1948.

Sabri said the fatwa was based on a similar decree by Islamic authorities in the 1930s strictly forbidding any sale of the land of Palestine to Zionists.

“Because all of Palestine is holy land, any acceptance of compensation would be regarded as selling the land and that is inadmissible. Those who do not want to return have no right to compensation, whatever their reasons,” he said.

However, the Mufti opined that it was permissible for the refugees and their descendants to receive compensation for their movable property and similar losses, and also for their suffering.

“The right of compensation should be linked and qualified. Palestinian refugees have the right to return to their homeland and to get compensation for their own suffering and that of their children and grandchildren,” Sheikh Sabri said.

Earlier, Saudi, Jordanian and Egyptian Ulema issued similar fatwas, forbidding Muslims to give up land rights in Palestine in return for money, saying that whoever willfully agrees to sell Muslim land to unbelievers would commit a grave sin against God, his messenger (peace and blessings be upon him) and the community of believers.

On 22 July, a group of Muslim Ulema in Palestine also issued a manifesto rejecting the Camp David talks between Israel and the Palestinian Authority.

They called the talks at Camp David an exposed attempt to liquidate the Palestinian cause, including the paramount issue of al-Quds al-Sharif and the equally significant right of return of the refugees. The latter is enshrined in UN resolution 194, which grants the estimated 4 million Palestinian refugees and their descendants repatriation and restitution.

The manifesto was signed by a group of prominent Islamic figures from the West Bank, Gaza and what is now Israel, including Hamas founder and spiritual leader Sheikh Ahmed Yassin.

The manifesto declared the following: First, that Palestine in its entirety is holy Muslim Wakf land and that no person, group, organization or State is authorized to concede any part of its soil to the enemy.

Second, that the estimated four million Palestinian refugees have an absolute and inalienable right to return to their ancestral homeland and to their villages, towns and homes from which they were expelled at gunpoint when Israel was created in 1948.

Third, that whoever concedes on the right of return and accepts “the permanent settling of the refugees in the Diaspora, in return for compensation, or without it, would be considered an outcast and enemy to Allah, his messenger, and the community of believers. This is so, the manifesto said, because, the Prophet (peace and blessings be upon him) said, “Whoever transfers the possession of Muslim land to Kafirs (unbelievers with whom Muslims are at war) will possess a resting place in hell.”

Fourth, that any agreement, pact or deal that concedes on Muslim rights would be viewed as null and void, and that the Muslim Umma will not be bound by it, neither in the present nor in the future.

The manifesto followed a massive rally by Hamas in the Gaza Strip, during which Islamist leaders in Palestine reasserted their opposition to selling out Muslim rights in Palestine.

Addressing the rally in downtown Gaza, Dr. Abdul Aziz al-Rantisi, the number-two man in Hamas, declared that Palestinian Authority leader Yasser Arafat would be committing political suicide if he buckled under American pressure and accepted a compromise on al-Quds and the refugees’ right of return.

“We exhort the President to come back now, because nothing positive could come out of this summit at Camp David.”

Rantisi warned Arafat that the Palestinian people will tear into pieces any agreement with Israel that fails to meet the aspirations of the Palestinian people.

He added that neither Arafat nor the Palestinian people have any right to give up even one atom of Palestine because “Palestine and al-Masjidul Aqsa don’t belong to the Palestinians alone but to the whole Muslim world from Jakarta to Casablanca.”

Meanwhile, Iran’s supreme Islamic leader Ayatullah Ali Khamenai told Islamic Jihad leader Ramdan Abdullah that any agreement between Israel and the Palestinian Authority that lent recognition and legitimacy to the usurpation and arrogation of Muslim rights in Palestine would be utterly rejected by Muslims all over the world.

Abdullah reportedly vowed that his organization will resist any capitulatory agreement prejudicing Muslim rights in Palestine, particularly the right of return and the restoration of al-Quds al-Sharif, the Muslims’ first Qibla (the direction toward which Muslims turn their faces during prayer) and the third holiest Islamic shrine, directly after Mecca and Medina.

Has Arafat surrendered to American-Zionist dictates?

Occupied Jerusalem – Special to PT

It seems increasingly clear that Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat, the sole decision maker in the PA regime, is budging to American pressure on virtually all the vital issues of the Palestine question.

According to reports leaked from Camp David, where Arafat was said to have been under virtual arrest, the Palestinian leader reportedly voiced a willingness to retreat from the erstwhile declared Palestinian constants, which include the following:

- Full Israeli withdrawal to the pre-1967 lines

- East Jerusalem must be the capital of a prospective Palestinian State

- The fulfilment of the refugees right of return in conformity with UN resolution 194

- The dismantling of Jewish settlements in the West Bank and Gaza Strip.

Needless to say, these “constants” represent the “bottom line” of the position of the PLO, as they have repeatedly been confirmed and reasserted, the last time during the PLO Central Council meeting early in June.

Indeed, these constants represent no more than a small part of the Palestinian people’s historical rights and give Israel the lion’s share of Palestine.

Reports from Camp David indicated that Arafat effectively budged on all the above-mentioned constants.

On withdrawal to the 1967 borders, Arafat reportedly consented to the annexation by Israel of major settlement blocks in the West Bank and Gaza Strip. And, as it is known, there are now as many as 200 Jewish settlements in the occupied West Bank and Gaza Strip covering up to 40% of the former and 35% of the latter.

This means that Arafat may have only settled for nearly 60% of the West Bank and 65% of the Gaza Strip. If true, the Palestinian leader would be accepting the Israeli stance which states that the West Bank and Gaza Strip were “disputed territories”, rather than “occupied territories”, as enshrined in UN resolutions 242 and 338.

And by agreeing to sidestep and effectively ignore the UN legitimacy, Arafat would actually be caving in to Israeli intransigence at a time when the whole international community urge for the implementation of all pertinent UN resolutions on the Palestinian question. Hence, it is difficult to understand how Arafat could turn his back to these resolutions when the whole world, including the United States, still consider them the legal reference for any settlement of the Arab-Israeli conflict.

More to the point, the reported Israeli offer to hand over up to 90% of the West Bank to Arafat was just another tactical deception. Israel and the PA, as we all know, don’t agree on a common definition of what the West Bank is: For while the PA considers the West Bank to include all the territories seized by Israel from Jordan, West of the river, the Israeli government doesn’t consider “Greater Jerusalem” as part of the West Bank.

And since Greater Jerusalem and the surrounding settlements make up 25%-30% of the West Bank, this means that Israel is offering Arafat only 90% of the 75% of the West Bank. In other words, the Israeli offer would give the Palestinians in reality less than 3,400 square kilometres of the 6,220 square kilometres that make up the total land area of the West Bank and Gaza Strip.

Needless to say, this would amount to less than 13% of historical Palestine. Predictably, this would be viewed by most if not all Palestinians as a disgraceful sell-out of Palestinian, Arab and Muslim rights, given the scandalous and conspicuous obliquity whereby the Jews would get up to 87% where the Palestinians, the indigenous people of the land, would only get the remaining13%.

In addition, Arafat, who always vowed he wouldn’t give up even one atom of East Jerusalem, seemed to be caving in on this issue of cardinal importance. Reports from Camp David suggested that Arafat was willing, in principle at least, to exclude the Armenian quarter and the so-called Jewish quarter in the Old Town, in addition to the Al-Buraq Wall, which the Jews had transformed into the “Wailing Wall” and later on to the “Western Wall.”

Arafat’s ostensible willingness to accommodate Zionist demands, by accepting a certain compromise on Jerusalem, is particularly ominous. In fact, virtually all Palestinians and Muslims view any concession on Jerusalem and the Aqsa Mosque as a straightforward act of treason and treachery. In other words, it is unlikely that Arafat would concede on Jerusalem and remain alive at the same time. Jerusalem is simply an integral central part of the collective Palestinian, Arab and Muslim psychology, and any submission to Zionist intransigence on the city would be inherently unacceptable to Muslims around the world.

Indeed, Arafat must be aware of the dangers inherent in conceding on Jerusalem. He reportedly told President Clinton that “I cannot concede on this matter; they would kill me, and then you would have Sheikh Yassin to negotiate with.”

Another scandalous aspect of Arafat’s negotiating position on Jerusalem is the fact that he agreed to confine ‘the bargaining” to only East Jerusalem. Arafat and his negotiating team reportedly made no mention of West Jerusalem, 70% of which is actually owned by Palestinians. Thus, no mention was made of the Arab neighbourhoods of West Jerusalem, namely Talibiyya, Malha, Lifta, Ein Karem and Deir Yassin, among others, which the Zionists took over and depopulated in 1948.

In short, Arafat has effectively retreated from his earlier red lines on Jerusalem, a disgraceful act that would cost him dearly, sooner or later, unless he reverts to Arab-Muslim consensus on this issue of utmost sensitivity.

On the settlement issue, Arafat has also budged to the Israeli dictates.

According to some Palestinian sources close to the PA, Arafat readily consented to an Israeli proposal whereby the Zionist regime would annex the bulk of Jewish settlements in the West Bank. If true, Arafat would enable the Zionists to reap the harvest of their occupation for the last 30 years. It would also ravage Arafat’s dream and that of the other marketers of the Oslo nightmare of seeing the settlements re-populated by Palestinian refugees from the Diaspora.

Finally, Arafat apparently agreed to virtually revoke the refugees’ right of return. Palestinian sources at Camp David had pointed out that the issue of Jerusalem remained the “main obstacle to reaching a framework agreement with Israel.” In fact, one understands from this announcement that the two sides reached a concordance on this issue, which either means that Israel agreed to carry out UN resolution 194 and allow the refugees to return to their former homes in 1948 Palestine, or that the Palestinian Authority agreed to simply forget about the right of return. If true, this position by the leader of the PLO would amount to a blatant crime against the refugees’ inalienable right to return to their former homes and villages from which they were physically expelled at gunpoint by Zionist gangs in 1948. The outlook of this crime is made even bleaker considering that the raison d’�tre of the PLO was to attain the right of return. The PLO was a refugees’ movement, and undoing the right of return by the PLO leader would be a nightmarish paradox of incalculable proportions.

The right of return issue is in a certain sense even more crucial than the issue of Jerusalem, since this right is personal and collective in nature and is also inalienable and explicitly enshrined in resolution 194. And being so, the right of return can’t be canceled, negated, suspended or conceded by anybody, save by the very person directly concerned, namely the individual refugee himself or herself.

In fact, Arafat and his hangers-on, such as Muhammad Dahlan, have been trying for sometime to signal their willingness to concede on the refugees’ rights, but without saying so publicly and openly.

A couple of months ago, Dahlan, who is Chief of Preventive Security in Gaza, reportedly told a refugee committee to forget about the right of refugees because “the bulk of the refugees don’t want to return anyway and that Israel would never accept the return of five million refugees.”

This treacherous statement on the part of Dahlan, which observers believe couldn’t have been voiced without receiving a certain yellow or green light from Arafat, is triggering deep and widespread indignation among Palestinians at large and refugees in particular.

One refugee leader in Balata, near Nablus, on the West Bank gave a stern warning to Arafat, advising him not to play with fire.

“This is a sacred issue, and Arafat should stay away from it. He must understand that if he concedes on the right of return, we’ll kill him.”

Even some Fatah leaders, in particular Husam Khader of the Balata refugee camp near Nablus, warned the PA leader that “this is a red line” and “we will not be bound by any agreement you sign that doesn’t meet our aspirations and rights.”

In short, it seems that Arafat must realize that any “peace” deal with Israel that doesn’t achieve real liberation and freedom for the Palestinians and doesn’t enable millions of Palestinian refugees to return to their homeland will be worthless and torn into pieces and shreds, and eventually buried with Arafat when he dies.

Arafat's tourism ministry to organize nudity contest

Occupied Jerusalem- The Palestinian Authority's tourism ministry announced it would organize this summer a nudity contest under the rubric of a "beauty queen contest."

The Semi-official Palestinian press today featured large advertisements to that effect, inviting Palestinian girls to take part in the contest to register their names from July 20 to August 10.

Palestinian sources said the contest to choose Miss Palestine would coincide with preparations to declare Palestinian statehood in September.

The newspaper al-Ayyam featured a topless girl in its add, emblazoned with the phrase "take part in the Miss Palestine contest now."

A spokesman for the organizers of the contest said the participants would eventually be asked to wear "swim suits", and would represent Palestine in the Miss-World contest.

Asked if he was worried about possible reactions from Islamic and conservative circles who view this kind of activity as a form of promiscuity, the spokesman said "the PA will provide protection for our activities."

Since its installment more than six years ago, Arafat's regime has sought to encourage many negative western lifestyles, such as drinking of alcoholic beverages, gambling, and even prostitution.

Palestinians, settlers clash in Hebron

Hebron- Scores of Palestinian youths and Jewish settlers clashed in downtown Hebron on 23 July after some settlers stoned Palestinian cars travelling through the Shuhada street.

Palestinian sources said settlers sought to prevent Palestinian motorists from using the main thoroughfare linking downtown with the rest of Hebron.

The settlers of Hebron are considered among the most extremist of all settlers in the West Bank and they make no secret of their ultimate goal of obliterating the Arab Muslim identity of Hebron.

In 1994, a settler terrorist, Baruch Goldstein, murdered 29 Arab worshipers at the Ibrahimi Mosque while praying at dawn at the Ibrahimi Mosque.

There around 250 Jewish settlers living in Hebron amid over 180,000 Palestinians.

The settlers are protected round the clock by nearly two thousand Israeli soldiers.

More Palestinians killed by Israel
as result of PA lawlessness

Occupied Jerusalem – PT

Tension swept over much of the West Bank and Gaza Strip during the first fortnight of July following the killing of a Gazan woman by Israeli soldiers and the death of two Palestinian boys in Hebron and Ramallah by explosive devices left over by the Israeli army.

On 7 July, shortly after midnight, Israeli soldiers manning a garrison outpost outside the Jewish settlement Kfar Darom in the southern part of the Gaza Strip fired heavily at a civilian Palestinian car travelling on the Rafah-Gaza main road, instantly killing 33-year-old I’tidal Mua’mar and seriously injuring her husband and their two children. The cab driver was also moderately injured in the shooting.

Initially, an Israeli army spokesman sought to justify the shooting by claiming that the military outpost came under fire, prompting the soldiers to return fire.

However, later on, and after it became clear that the Israeli account of the incident was concocted, the Israeli government apologized, though halfheartedly, to the PA, admitting the soldiers made a mistake.

PA officials and media described the killing as “cold-blooded murder” and blamed it on “the venomous anti-Palestinian incitement” among Israeli soldiers and settlers.

PA security officials in the area urged the Israeli government to remove “trigger-happy soldiers from the outpost” and try the perpetrators for their “hideous crime.”

However, Israel (as usual) effectively ignored PA complaints, as none of the soldiers involved in the incident were transferred to another post, let alone tried–a characteristic pattern of Israeli behaviour under such circumstances.

Palestinians taking part in Mua’mar’s funeral called for “revenge” and urged the Palestinian Authority to “give us protection.”

“We must defend ourselves; we just can’t accept this situation where they shoot and we get killed...we must defend ourselves,” said Mua’mar’s brother during the funeral.

The killing of Mua’mar was preceded by the death of two Palestinian boys as a result of detonated munitions left over by the Israeli army: one in Yatta (near Hebron) and the other in Beit Lukia (near Ramallah) on 5 and 6 July respectively.

Sixteen-year-old Khalil Yousef Abu-A’ram of Yatta was killed as he stepped on an explosive device while grazing his flock near the village.

At least three other Yatta boys were killed in similar circumstances in the last three years.

The following day, another Palestinian high school student from the village of Beit Lukia, situated near the former armistice line west of Ramallah, was also killed when an explosive device detonated while he was working on his family’s land.

Palestinian villagers in the area accused Israeli settlers of deliberately planting such devices in order to drive them into abandoning their land.

As in Yatta, several boys and shepherds had been mutilated or killed by explosions of this nature.

According to the Israeli human rights group, B’Tselem, 83 detonations of abandoned explosive devices have taken place since 1987, resulting in the death of 33 Palestinians, among them 25 children, and the injury of 90 others, among them 69 children. B’Tselem also cited an incident in which one Jewish child from the settlement of Ma’ali Adumim was also killed as a result of an explosion of abandoned munitions.

Palestinian female prisoners
in Israeli jails complain of maltreatment

Ramleh- Four Palestinian female prisoners in Zionist jails have complained to lawyers of the Ramleh prison administration’s maltreatment.

Sona Al-Ra’ee, Nasreen Taha, Suad Ghazal and Suad Abu Laban have accused the prison administration of preventing them from receiving visits by second-degree relatives and of obstructing visits by first-degree relatives by intentionally delaying them for hours.

For her part, Abu Laban said that she was punished with solitary confinement for several weeks while Taha said that she was threatened with handcuffing her hands during the rest hours outside their cells because she tried once to rig up a shade that protected them from the sun’s heat.

The prisoners said that they were regularly called offensive names and mistreated by the jailers in addition to unnecessary searches of their rooms twice weekly, which included confiscating their property.

They also charged the prison administration with preventing entry of any new books and newspapers and of giving them lesser food rations.

Mofaz warns against kidnapping
Zionist soldiers in the north

Occupied Jerusalem- Zionist chief of staff General Sha’ul Mofaz warned against the possible abduction of Jewish soldiers from the northern areas then taking them into Lebanon.

Mofaz underlined that it was difficult to completely close the borders and that attempts to kidnap soldiers or civilians in the border area with Lebanon are a possibility. He called on the Jewish settlers not to approach the border areas that were not yet duly protected, though he affirmed that his army was intensively patrolling those areas.

Mofaz, who was speaking to inhabitants of the northern settlements, said that those who throw stones at Israeli soldiers at the borders with Lebanon were encouraged by Hizbullah, which was not interested in prevailing calm in the border area.

He pointed out that Lebanon would witness public elections in August and Hizbullah was interested in increasing their political weight, so there was no need to indulge in confrontations with it in that period.

Palestinian workshop calls on the
PA to eliminate settlements

Ramallah- Participants in a Palestinian workshop in Ramallah unanimously agreed that the Jewish settlements had bad effects on the Palestinian economy and environment and even destroyed Palestinian infrastructure.

The workshop was held in Ramallah last month. A number of participants delivered speeches charging that the Jewish settlements were an obstacle to the declaration of the Palestinian State because they prevented unity of Palestinian lands.

They pointed to the settlements’ control of Palestinian waters and destruction of the agricultural and economic sectors. They also focused on the negative impact of those settlements on the environment, noting that they chose mountaintops, which feed water reservoirs.

Campaign to collect two million signatures
in Jordan rejecting refugees’ resettlement

Amman- Jordanian professional syndicates started a campaign last month to collect two million signatures rejecting the resettlement of Palestinian refugees in the diaspora and insisting on their right of return and compensations at the same time.

Al-Arab Al-Yawm daily quoted Saleh Armouti, chief of the syndicates’ council, as saying that the campaign would aim to mobilize popular rejection of resettlement and compensation.

He also pointed out that the council was discussing a proposal to organize a popular march that would head to King Hussein Bridge, the link between Jordan and the West Bank, demanding an endorsement of the refugees’ right of return and compensation, in addition to condemning capitulation agreements with the Zionist regime.

The council had issued a statement denouncing the Camp David talks and affirming that the road towards liberating Palestine, including the issue of Jerusalem, along with other occupied Arab lands was through the launching of Jihad and reviving armed struggle by striking Zionist targets as well as American and Western economic interests in the Arab region.

Butler briefs Israeli intelligence
on weapons inspection in Iraq

Occupied Jerusalem- The former chief of the UN inspection team on weapons of mass destruction, Richard Butler, briefed the Israeli intelligence services on efforts to force Iraq to get rid of its weapons of mass destruction.

Butler, who arrived in Palestine on 17 July, took part in the weekly meeting of the Israeli Knesset’s defense and foreign affairs committee.

Following the meeting, Butler told reporters in West Jerusalem that Iraq was reviving its armament programme.

“There is no doubt that Saddam Hussein is back in the business of developing his biological, chemical and nuclear weapons.”

The Israeli State-run radio said Butler briefed Israeli intelligence officials on the situation in Iraq and UN efforts to prevent the Iraqi regime from developing new weapons of mass destruction.

Iraq has long accused Butler of working for the Israeli intelligence.

Butler’s activities in the Zionist regime, including the information he volunteered to give to the Israeli intelligence services, will apparently give credence to Iraq’s accusations.
