Israel targets the Palestinian political leaders

Occupied Jerusalem – From Khalid Amayreh

In a grave escalation of Israel’s undeclared war on the Palestinians, the Israeli occupation army has begun targeting for assassination political leaders of the Palestinian resistance groups.

The new policy found expression on 31 July when American-made apache helicopter gun-ships fired several electronically guided missiles at a building in downtown Nablus that housed a media office affiliated with the Palestinian Islamic resistance movement, Hamas.

The deadly rockets reduced the building to rubble and twisted metal, instantly killing eight civilians, including two prominent Islamist political leaders (Jamal Salim and Jamal Mansour), two journalists, two academics and two children.

The bodies of the victims, which were shown on TV screens around the world, were incinerated and mutilated beyond recognition.

Islamist sources in Nablus described as “cheap disinformation” Israeli media claims that Jamal Mansour, 42 and father of five children, was associated with the Hamas military wing, the Izzedin al-Qassam Brigades.

“The Israelis know quite well that he had nothing to do with the Hamas military wing; all he did was media work,” said Sheikh Hasan Yousef, an Islamist leader in Ramallah and a close friend of both Mansour and Salim.

Salim, 45, and father of seven children, was a lecturer at the Najah University and apparently had no connections with the Hamas military wing.

The two killed journalists, Othman Katamani, 30, and the newlywed Fahim Dawabshe, 22, were reportedly conducting an interview with Salim and Mansour at the time of the bombing.

Both were ordinary journalists with an Islamic orientation but had no affiliation with Hamas.

The two other adults killed in the bombing were Omar Mansour, a cousin and bodyguard of Jamal Mansour, and Muhammed Bishawi, a human rights activist who worked for the Nablus-based society “Solidarity International,” which researches and documents human rights violations in Palestine.

The most tragic of the victims are Ashraf Abdul Munim, 8, and his brother Bilal, who were buried under rubble while sitting outside a grocery store down the street.

The killing triggered unprecedented bitterness and indignation throughout the West Bank and Gaza Strip as tens of thousands of Palestinians spontaneously took to the streets shortly after the bombing, denouncing the carnage and demanding revenge.

Some of the slogans voiced also denounced Arab regimes for failing to offer tangible political support for the Palestinians and especially for refusing to exert pressure on Israel’s strategic ally, the United States.

Predictably, Hamas reacted to the murder of two of its most prominent and intelligent political leaders by vowing to avenge their blood sooner rather than later.

Gaza spokesman for Hamas, Abdul Aziz al-Rantisi, described the killing in Nablus as “exceeding all red lines.”

“From now on, all Israeli politicians, Knesset members and ministers, from the criminal Sharon downward, will be legitimate targets for our martyrdom operations.”

Fatah leaders, too, had a similar reaction, calling the carnage “a new hideous massacre added to Sharon’s long list of massacres against our people.”

However, some of Fatah indignation was directed at the PA leadership for failing to “provide real protection for our people who are being killed and maimed round-the-clock.”

These strong words came from Husam Khader, a popular Fatah leader in the Nablus region who in the past warned PA leader Yasser Arafat against caving in to Israeli and American pressure on the question of the right of return for Palestinian refugees.

In an interview with an Arab satellite television station an hour after the carnage, Khader lashed out at Arafat for “behaving as if things are normal.”

“We have 70,000 guns in storage under PA disposal; my question to Abu Ammar (Arafat) is: why don’t we use them to defend ourselves? Should we wait until they (the Israelis) liquidate half of our people?”

The horrible killing in Nablus ended a week of murder and “quiet assassinations” carried out by the Israeli occupation army that claimed the lives of at least 18 Palestinians and scores of others, including four children who were badly wounded and disfigured by indiscriminate Israeli artillery bombardment of civilian neighbourhoods in Gaza and Hebron.

On 29 July, another wholesale “quiet assassination” took place at the village of Tubas just outside Nablus. The Israeli army detonated via remote control a large bomb which Israeli agents had planted at a junkyard where six Fatah activists were about to have their dinner.

The powerful blast mutilated the bodies of the six youths, severing the head of one of them and scattering their flesh and limbs over a 50-metre radius.

The killing was bitterly condemned by the PA, which called on the world community to “protect us from the neo-Nazis.”

“Without jury, without trial, without arrest, without charge, without anything they decide that somebody ought to die, and they send their murderers and kill them,” said Nablus Governor Mahmoud Alul.

Retaliation
The assassination of that many Palestinians, including unmistakable political leaders of the Palestinian political movements, in such a short time is expected to put tremendous pressure on Hamas, Fatah and other resistance groups to mount what one Hamas spokesman called “qualitative retaliatory attacks.”

“We must strike terror in their hearts, otherwise they will kill us all, one by one,” said the spokesman who asked for anonymity.

The bloodied escalation of the Israeli rampage against the Palestinians is also likely to prompt the PA to be more outspoken in demanding that influential Arab States with good ties to the U.S. exert pressure on the Americans to rein-in Israeli savagery.

A few days ago, PA official Sa’eb Ureikat told the Voice of Palestine that “our problem is not only with Israel and the United States; it is also with the Arab regimes that refuse to tell the U.S. that enough is enough.”

“There are thousands of American companies operating in the Arab world; if the Arabs stopped drinking Coca Cola, this alone would force the Americans to review their blind support for Israel.”

Muslims thwart Jewish attempts to lay
“Temple cornerstone” at Haram al-Sharif

Occupied Jerusalem – From Khalid Amayreh

Thousands of Muslims converged on Jerusalem’s Haram al-Sharif on Sunday, 29 July, in response to an earlier call by Muslim officials and succeeded in thwarting a high-profile attempt by a Jewish terrorist group to lay a “cornerstone for the third Jewish temple” at the esplanade of the Aqsa Mosque, Islam’s third holiest shrine.

Thanks to the presence of so many Muslims, and due to stern warnings from Muslim leaders in Palestine and abroad, the messianic Jewish group was forced to conduct its “cornerstone-laying ceremony” outside the confines of the old town and to make it as brief as possible.

The terrorists, who were protected by thousands of Israeli troops and policemen, brought a rock on a truck to the Maghariba Gate, set it down for a while and then took it back to West Jerusalem.

The symbolic act, said Jewish terrorist leader Gershon Solomon, was meant to show Jewish determination to destroy Islamic holy places in Jerusalem in order to expedite the “rebuilding” of the Jewish temple.

Earlier, thousands of Muslims from Jerusalem itself and 1948-Palestine (not a single Muslim from the West Bank and Gaza was allowed to reach Jerusalem since the two territories have been hermetically closed for the past ten months) arrived at the Haram al-Sharif to repulse the terrorists from desecrating Islamic shrines.

Islamist leaders in Palestine and abroad had warned that Muslims would under no circumstances allow Jews to desecrate their holy places.

“The Jews know well that any aggression against Islam’s third holiest place will lead to grave consequences including bloodshed,” said a statement released by the Muslim Ulema League, which has a membership of hundreds of Muslim religious scholars.

Egypt, Jordan, Morocco, Yemen and Iran, among other countries, voiced strong protests at Zionist provocation, calling the stone-laying ceremony a “naked aggression.”

The strong Muslim stance prompted the Zionist regime to assure Muslims that “the matter was blown out of proportion” and that the Temple Mount Faithful will not be allowed to enter the Haram al-Sharif.”

However, both Muslim and PA officials in Palestine rejected Israeli assurances.

Mahmoud al-Zahar of Hamas said the Zionists couldn’t be trusted. He accused the Israeli government and official religious establishment of conniving with the terrorist group of the Temple Mount Faithful to test the extent of Muslim forbearance.

“They want to test our determination before taking the next step,” said al-Zahar in a press interview on 29 July.

Moreover, PA official Yasser Abed Rabbo accused the Zionists of seeking to gain time.

He said the Israeli government was trying to prepare the international public opinion for another attempt at desecrating the holy places of Jerusalem.

He argued that the problem was the Israeli occupation, adding that “as long as the occupation structure remains intact, the dangers facing Muslim and Christian holy places will not disappear.”

Bloodshed at holy site
As Muslims repulsed a group of Jewish fanatics from desecrating the holy places a few hours after the stone-laying ceremony ended in failure, hundreds of Israeli occupation soldiers and policemen, in full rioting gear, stormed the normally tranquil holy place, triggering violent clashes with peaceful worshipers.

The Zionists fired rubber-coated metal bullets, stun grenades and tear gas canisters into the crowd consisting of thousands of men, women and children who were getting ready to perform their congregational noon prayer. In addition, crack border policemen used clubs rather savagely to beat the defenseless worshipers and journalists and cameramen who came to cover the showdown.

The worshipers, infuriated by the brazenness of the Zionist provocation, sought to defend themselves and and their holy place by hurling stones and shoes at the encroaching Zionists.

And by the time of the noon prayer, the toll was 70 Palestinians injured, including people with serious eye injuries and brain concussions, according to medical sources in Jerusalem.

The Zionists State-run radio spoke of 15 policemen getting injured.

Muslim Wakf officials accused the Zionists of provoking the clashes by allowing Jewish extremists to come close to the gates of Haram al-Sharif.

“I hold the Israeli occupation government fully responsible for this mad violence; they should have realized that allowing the terrorists to provoke the Muslims would invite a strong Muslim response,” said Sheikh Muhammed Hussein, the most senior Wakf official at the Aqsa Mosque.

Tension eventually subsided toward the afternoon when the Zionist police left the site, thus allowing Wakf officials to calm down angry protesters.

However, it is likely that any “calm” in Jerusalem would be short-lived, given the determination of Jewish terrorist groups to ignite the flames of hatred and violence in Jerusalem and throughout Palestine.

Indeed, most messianic Jewish groups believe that it is imperative that violence and bloodshed on a very large scale take place in order to induce and accelerate the advent of the Jewish messiah or redeemer.

Meanwhile, tens of thousands of Palestinians marched through the West Bank and Gaza on 29 July to protest Jewish designs against Islam’s third holiest place.

As many as 15,000 Hamas supporters marched in Gaza, vowing to defend and protect al-Aqsa mosque with “blood and soul.”

“We call upon Muslims around the world to be vigilant and to open their eyes in defense of al-Aqsa Mosque…the Jews are continuing their sick efforts to destroy our holy place, the first Qibla and the third holiest Islamic site,” said a statement issued by Hamas.

The statement called on Muslims everywhere to show the Zionists by words and deeds that “we will not stand idle while our holy places are attacked and targeted for destruction.”

Israeli officials, seeking to downplay the entire episode for public relations purposes, claimed that the Temple Mount Faithful was a small and marginal group and that it didn’t pose a threat to the Haram al-Sharif and Islamic holy places thereon.

However, it is amply clear that the group enjoys the support and backing of hundreds of thousands of Israelis, including government officials and cabinet ministers such as Rahba’am Zeivi, Avigdor Liberman and other ministers from right-wing and religious parties in the Sharon coalition.

In fact, the right-wing Israeli newspaper, the Jerusalem Post, published an on-line poll on 30 July showing that as many as 69% of its readers support the designs and goals of the terrorist group.

This shows that Muslims ought to be vigilant, irrespective of what the Zionists and despotic Arab and Muslim governments, for whom the dignity of Muslims means nothing, say about this grave assault on one of Islam’s holiest places.

In fact, an Umma (community) that can’t protect its honour and dignity from Zionist vandalism and insolence doesn’t have the right to life in a world that believes only in force.

Sharon opts for “quiet assassinations”

Occupied Jerusalem – From Khalid Amayreh

The Zionist regime stepped up its undeclared war on the essentially unarmed Palestinian population, employing a new method known as “quiet assassinations” aimed at murdering as many activists as possible and as quietly as possible for the purpose of avoiding negative international reactions.

The new virulent policy, adopted in mid-July during a special session of the Israeli Inner Council on Security, assumes the form of murdering “wanted activists” at point-blank range or planting high-intensity bombs in their homes and cars, and then claiming that they were killed as a result of “internal vendettas” or “work accidents” while preparing explosive devices intended to be used against Israel.

In so doing, the Israeli army hopes it will kill two birds with one stone: first making sure the “terrorists” are liquidated and, second, creating a modicum of vagueness surrounding the murder, thus avoiding international criticisms.

The Israeli occupation army (IOA) began putting the new virulent policy into effect on 19 July when Shin Bet agents placed and detonated via remote control a large bomb in Fatah’s main office in downtown Hebron, killing 34-year-old Raja’e Abu Rajab, a father of three.

The Israeli media carried an IOA statement denying any involvement in the murder and parroted the charge that Abu Rajab was actually killed in a “work accident” while preparing a bomb intended for use against the Israelis.

Of course, there was not one iota of truth in either claim.

The campaign of murder went on as Israeli helicopter gun-ships on 22 July fired several missiles at the home of Ibrahim Jaber, who is involved in Islamic youth and charitable activities, destroying the house completely. Jaber himself was not hurt as he was praying at the nearby mosque at the time of the bombing.

Hamas spokesman in Jenin, Khalid al-Haj, described the bombing as “a criminal assassination attempt on the life of a person because of his political beliefs.”

“They (the Israelis) are acting like street thugs and gangsters….Israel is a criminal State and its leaders are war criminals who ought to be prosecuted for their crimes sooner or later.”

The following day, 23 July, Israeli undercover soldiers, disguised as Palestinian labourers, attempted to assassinate an Islamic Jihad activist in Bethlehem.

The masked soldiers, armed with guns fitted with silencers, fired several bullets at Khalid al-Heremi, 29, while walking well inside area (A) which is supposed to be under full control of the PA.

Fortunately, the man was only moderately injured. The Israeli terrorists fled toward the nearby army outpost at the Bilal Ibn Rabah Mosque which the Israeli government had converted into a synagogue despite strong Muslim opposition.

The IOA spokesman said in a terse statement that he had no knowledge of the incident, adding that “it was probably an internal Palestinian scuffle.”

Two similar “quiet” assassination attempts took place in Nablus, this time targeting PFLP activists and resulting in two of them being injured, one seriously. Again, the Israeli spokesman dismissed the two incidents as “work accidents.”

The murders continued and with little media coverage from the Israeli side. On 23 July, the Israeli army raided Anin, near Jenin in the northern part of the West Bank, placing the small hamlet under curfew.

Carnage at Edna
The latest spate of killings and murders by the Israeli occupation army was preceded by the heinous murder of the Etmeizi family by Jewish terrorists at the village of Edna, 10 miles west of Hebron, on 19 July.

Demolishing homes.. Sharon’s message to the Palestinians

Occupied Jerusalem – PT
As the Israeli propaganda machine continued to lay the blame on Palestinian Authority Chairman Yasser Arafat for the continuation of violence and bloodshed in the West Bank and Gaza Strip, the Israeli government was busy demolishing more Arab homes in East Jerusalem and elsewhere in the occupied territories early in the month of July.

The new rabid onslaught against natural Palestinian demographic growth began in the early morning hours of Monday, 9 July, when 15 army bulldozers, escorted by a contingent of heavily armed soldiers and border policemen, raided the Shufa’at refugee camp, just north of Jerusalem.

The raid bore all the marks of a military operation. Heavily armed soldiers took positions at strategic places and a helicopter gun-ship was hovering above, ostensibly ready in case the tormented Palestinians decided to resist “operation peace for Jerusalem.”

As the bulldozers rumbled menacingly toward their targets (17 newly constructed homes sheltering 17 abject refugee families), the nearly distraught proprietors sought desperately to prevent the bulldozers from shattering the dream of their life–a concrete structure sheltering their families.

Soon the bulldozers “went into action,” pulverizing one home after the other while the soldiers were busy beating the furious homeowners and angry protesters, Jews and Arabs alike, who arrived at the refugee camp to express their solidarity with the victims and indignation at the crime.

At one point, the driver of one of the bulldozers threatened to crush a Palestinian and an Israeli peace activist who lied down before the huge earth-moving machine in a desperate attempt to prevent the destruction of the houses.

At the end of the day, the 17 houses were reduced to rubble and the lives of 17 families were shattered for a long time to come as they started a new life of homelessness because of the Israeli apartheid policy toward non-Jews.

“I don’t know what to do; where will these children go now?” asked Jamal Subhi al-Faqih, whom the soldiers didn’t even allow to get his furniture and belongings out before the demolition.

“I can’t build a new house; I’m not even able to pay off the debts of the demolished home…there is so much oppression in this world.”

The Israeli occupation authorities claimed the houses were built without a license, a routine pretext used by the Jewish mayor of Jerusalem, Ehud Olmert, to keep Arab demographic growth at the very minimum.

The victims at Shufa’at acknowledge the fact that their homes were built without their obtaining a license beforehand. However, they earnestly insist that it was virtually next to impossible for a Palestinian in Jerusalem to extract a building license from Olmert’s parsimonious hands.

“I applied for a license 15 years ago, but since I’m an Arab and not a Jew, they told me I would have to wait,” said Abdul Rahman Alkam.

Asked how much longer the Israeli municipal council asked him to wait, he said, “Maybe until I die.”

Palestinian Authority official Yasser Abed Rabbo described the demolitions as “a nefarious and callous crime aimed at subjugating our people and breaking their will to survive and withstand their oppression and persecution.”

“Destroying peoples’ homes is the ultimate violence; how can we convince those tormented people that peace with Israel is possible? These crimes are only deepening the hatred.”

Palestinian Legislative Council member Hatem Abdul Qader, who also arrived at the scene to comfort the victims, dismissed the Israeli reasoning for demolishing the homes as “a dirty joke.”

“The license story is a big Israeli lie, and nobody knows this fact better than the Israelis themselves; the naked truth is that they want the land to go to the Jews for expanding Jewish settlements, and the Palestinians can go to hell.”

Expansion of settlements
According to Palestinian and Israeli press sources, the wholesale destruction of the 17 houses at Shufa’at, along with the planned demolition of scores of other houses, is apparently aimed at confiscating the land where the demolished homes stood for expanding the Jewish settlement of Pisgat Zeiv and creating Jewish demographic continuity with the French Hill settlement north of Jerusalem.

More demolitions
On 10 July, the Zionist occupation army destroyed as many as 20 additional houses at the Yibna refugee camp in Rafah. The destruction of the homes left as many as 200 men, women and children homeless.

The Israeli army bragged about the barbarian act, suggesting it was carried out to break the will of the Palestinian people.

“We destroyed the homes so that the Palestinians will understand that we have the upper hand,” said a gleeful Zionist spokesman.

The latest destruction in Rafah brings to 200 the residential homes the Zionists have destroyed in the Rafah area alone during the past nine months.

Since the beginning of the Palestinian intifada, the Israeli army destroyed as many as 600 Palestinian homes throughout the West Bank and Gaza Strp, mainly as a reprisal for the Palestinian intifada.

The brutal measure resulted in thousands of men, women and children becoming refugees, some for the third time, and homeless in their own homeland.

Home demolition had been adopted as an “official policy” since the early days of the Israeli occupation in 1967, and human rights groups estimate that as many as 30,000 Palestinian homes were destroyed since then.

For its part, the Israeli government virtually pays no attention to the callous practice.

Even Israeli Foreign Minister Shimon Peres, a Nobel Prize winner, didn’t object to the latest demolitions as a matter of principle, e.g. as an act of brutal immorality.

“These dramatic demolitions are not received well in the world; I’m afraid we will have a difficult time explaining them to the world,” said Peres.

Arafat losing control over public
The largely unmitigated Israeli repression of the overall Palestinian population is making it extremely difficult for Yasser Arafat to convince his people that they have anything to gain from the present “cease-fire” which is rich in form but very poor in substance.

Indeed, with the Sharon government upholding its policy of subjugation, assassination, abduction and home demolition toward the Palestinian people, Arafat will be utterly unable, even if willing, to control the Palestinian public.

But weakening Arafat and corroding his authority may well be Sharon’s ultimate policy toward the PA since it could perceivably enable the certified war criminal to declare that “Arafat doesn’t control his people,” and eventually allow him to dislodge from the peace process altogether.
