Comment

Give me freedom or I’ll give you death!

The Zionist regime, along with the United States, which submissively acts at Israel’s beck and call, is actively seeking these days to liquidate the Palestinian question under the false rubric of “a final peace agreement with the Palestinians.”

At their disposal they have Yasser Arafat, the Palestinian autocrat who seldom pays attention to the feelings and views of his people and often acts in connivance with the Zionists and the Americans against Palestinian national interests.

The sinister plan is to enable the Zionists to arrogate nearly all of Palestine, including al-Quds al-Sharif (Jerusalem) and to perpetuate the dispersal of some four million Palestinian refugees who have been waiting for justice for half a century, but to no avail.

And in order to make the deal less odious and offensive than it really is, a package of promotional gimmicks are attached. These include a promise of a deformed Palestinian political entity made up of disconnected and truncated strips of land in the West Bank and surrounded by Jewish settlements on all sides, an entity that Arafat would be able to call “an independent Palestinian State” but without Jerusalem as its capital!

The gimmicks’ basket is also likely to include other inducements, including a respectable sum of American dollars, which would likely be used by Arafat to bribe some Palestinians into joining his boat and accepting the “peace of the brave.”

Apart from the travesty, things will not change much on the ground. The Israeli army would stay entrenched in the West Bank, and Israeli occupation soldiers manning checkpoints and roadblocks would continue to torment and humiliate Palestinians travelling between the truncated bantustans of “Free Palestine”, and PA officials, from Arafat to the smallest clerk, would continue to complain of Israel’s refusal to carry out the signed agreements.

Moreover, the four million helpless souls languishing in squalid refugee camps would be left languishing for justice, while a few will be allowed into Arafat’s reservations on the West Bank and Gaza Strip, “in fulfillment” of the right of return.

Needless to say, a fierce opposition to this capitulation would explode everywhere among Palestinians, especially among the refugees. And Arafat and his security apparatus would be further empowered by the Americans and the Zionists to repress all active expressions of that opposition to the imposition of the Israeli fait accompli.

These seem to be the main features of the next phase of the “peace process.” Consequently, the Palestinian people and their free forces must take note of the looming dangers and be prepared to deal with the disaster before it happens.

And the way to forestall and thwart the conspiracy being devised now is to form the broadest possible national front to face, challenge and foil the impending new Nakba.

Hence, all efforts must be mobilized to make the Americans, the Zionists and especially Arafat reconsider this path leading to a sham peace agreement.

Palestinians in the millions, Arabs and Muslims alike, as well as free people from around the world, ought to tell Arafat that should he put his signature to a capitulation document as such, he would go down in history as a traitor who colluded with the enemy against his people and who betrayed his people’s aspirations to further his personal ambitions.

Indeed, by putting his signature to such a disgraceful agreement, whether it be called transitional, interim or permanent, the Palestinian leader would effectively isolate himself from the rest of the Palestinian people and would effectively place himself in a hostile position vis-ï¿½-vis the people. In this case, the actual and potential implications aren’t difficult to predict.

As for the Zionist regime and its American subordinates, it should be made sufficiently clear to them that an imposed settlement would be utterly rejected by the Palestinian people whether at home or in the Diaspora.

It should also be made abundantly clear to the Zionists that the Palestinians would resume active armed struggle for freedom and liberation, irrespective of all other considerations.

In any case, the Palestinians would have little to lose as the Zionists continue to seek to perpetuate the usurpation of their land and rights.

The Americans say “give me freedom or give me death.” The Palestinian slogan ought to be “give me freedom or I give you death.”

The Zionists ought to take note of this.
