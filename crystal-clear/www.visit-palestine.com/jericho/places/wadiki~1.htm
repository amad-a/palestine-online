Wadi Kilt & Monastery of St. George (Deir al-Qelt)
                    

Wadi Qelt is a natural rift in the hills with high, sheer rock walls, extending 45 km between Jerusalem and Jericho. Hermits inhabited the Wadi since the third century. Today, it is a wonderful place for hiking tours, especially in the winter. The Monastery of St. George, Deir al-Qelt, is carved out of the rock and clings to the canyon walls like a fairytale fortress. Built in the fifth or sixth century, the monastery was destroyed during the Persian invasion of Palestine. Most of the present monastery dates back to the 1901 restoration by the Greek Orthodox Church.
