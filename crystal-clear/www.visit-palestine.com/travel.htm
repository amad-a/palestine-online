 Bethlehem… As the birthplace of Jesus, the picturesque town of Bethlehem has been thrust onto the world stage. Two thousand years ago, wise men and shepherds gathered in Bethlehem to worship a child that promised peace on earth and goodwill for all. Today Bethlehem promises a celebration for the Millennium that is rich in meaning - a journey to human heritage and a gathering of humanity for the future. Bethlehem lies 10 km south of Jerusalem, 765 m above sea level, on the edge of the Bethlehem Wilderness. It provides stunning views of the mountain range of Jordan beyond the Dead Sea. A fascinating ancient landscape of vine yards, olive trees and tiny villages surrounds the town which is famous for its olive-wood hand carvings, its mother-of-pearl jewellery and its distinctive and rich embroidery, as well as its friendly people. traces of history Located on an ancient caravan route, Bethlehem has always been a mosaic  of many cultures. Not only pilgrims but also numerous foreign rulers have left their trace on the town. Walking through Bethlehem's old city is like reading a book about history. Cannanite, Byzantine, Arab, Islamic, Persian, Crusader, Turkish and British cultures influenced traditions and architectural styles. 

 In recent times European architecture of Western religious institutions and the local Palestinian style merged to form a precious and challenging contemporary heritage. a glimpse into the past The district of Bethlehem is divided into three ancient towns, which, together, represent  the cradle of Christianity: Bethlehem, Beit Jala and Beit Sahour. The historic cores of these towns are currently undergoing substantial conservation, restoration and rehabilitation in preparation for the forthcoming millennial celebrations. exploring Bethlehem Pilgrims and visitors alike will have the chance to explore old Bethlehem while walking along its various routes. Tours such as "Harat Beit Lahem" (The Quarters of Bethlehem) will take the visitor through ancient alleyways, passing under romantic arches and by houses of architectural interest that date back to the seventeenth century. The "Route of the Pilgrims to Shepherds' Field" follows the historic track of the first pilgrims who walked from the Church of the Nativity to the Shepherds' Field to worship at the place where the star of Bethlehem was first seen. sites to see In and around Bethlehem a number of exciting sites are waiting to be discovered: The Church of Nativity, one of the oldest churches in the world, built by St. Helena in 334 above the Grotto of the Nativity; the Shepherds' Field in Beit-Sahour, where the Angel announced the Good News to the Shepherds; Solomon's Pools with the Ottoman Fortress, that provided Bethlehem and Jerusalem with water throughout the past 2000 years and the Desert Monasteries which are the living witness of early Christianity. the Living Faithful For 2000 years now, Christianity has enjoyed a continued presence in Palestine. Palestinian Christians are the "Living Faithful", who were the  first faithful to follow the teachings of Jesus Christ. 

The coming of Islam in the 7 th century did not alter the situation for the people of Bethlehem who remained Christian in their majority until this century. The demographic changes over the last two hundred years have not hindered peaceful co-existence among a people who profess different religions, they have worked together toward a better future for all. For centuries, Bethlehem has embodied hope in the minds of humankind. Whether we have had the privilege of visiting the town or not, its historic and sacred meaning shines like a lodestar in our consciousness. It reminds us of the bonds created by belief, of faith's ability to heal humanity, of the power of prayer. Kofi Annan, Secretary – General of the United Nations Bethlehem celebrates the year 2000 Bethlehem, where Jesus Christ was born, has been attracting pilgrims and visitors ever since this historic event. Today, at the threshold of a new millennium, Bethlehem is keenly aware of the legacy it has inherited as the spiritual centre for all Christians and for humanity at large. For the occasion of the Millennium, Bethlehem and all of Palestine is honing all efforts to celebrate the beginning of the coming millennium by honoring the place where modern times began. 

From Christmas 1999 to Easter 2001, Bethlehem will host the traditional religious celebrations of the local churches and be the venue for  a series of cultural and artistic events with participants from all over the world. Special church services, processions and vigils will underscore the spiritual significance of the occasion. Guest performers of international stature will come to Bethlehem and join local talents in order to present to Palestinians and all those who will come to celebrate with them in Palestine a variety of cultural and artistic events. Key celebrations will be broadcast globally enabling people all over the world to share in the celebrations in Bethlehem. However, nothing can equal a visit to Bethlehem - the place where Christianity was born and blossemed and  to where the first faithful welcomed the first pilgrims. Palestinian hospitality is as generous now as it was two millenniums ago.   Some important events: Festive Christmas service on December 24th 1999 and 2000 Catch the Star -  an international live celebration from Manger Square The New Years World Party with a highspot "the Concert of Hope" Easter Procession  and international musical events in 2000 and 2001 Historic Procession from the Shepherds Field to Bethlehem International Conferences, 2000 Years of Architecture, MENA Conference, and Dialogue of Hope Palestinian Folklore Festival Sound and Light  Show on the Life of Jesus Christ Where Jesus walked - Hiking in the Holy Land International Choir Competition The Major Regional Economic Conference International Youth Gatherings
